3 · Three-graph separation

OPDA keeps three graphs strictly apart (ODR-0004 §3a, CI-enforced): the class graph (opda-classes.ttl + module TBox), the shapes graph (opda-*-shapes.ttl — constraints that target classes, never owl:imports them), and the annotation graph (opda-*-annotations.ttl — DPV co-annotations, AI hints). Inference materialises into a fourth, derived graph (…/pdtf/graph/inferred/entailment) and never pollutes the canonical three.

4 · Inference / validation boundary

Infer the relations whose closure the author wants (subclass/subproperty/inverse/symmetric/transitive — the 7 frozen rules of config/opda-rdfs-plus.rules, a sound but RL-incomplete fragment, not an OWL 2 RL reasoner). Validate the constraints the author wants checked (domain/range/cardinality/identity) as SHACL — the 273 sh:targetSubjectsOf → sh:class domain/range shapes (ODR-0029 R3). The closure adds 0 triples over the flat schema; it is kept as a governed boundary + negative gate (no owl:sameAs, no spurious EPCCertificate ⊑ Property), not for current output.

5 · Governance & decision-provenance

Every term carries dct:source back to a PDTF schema-leaf-path or glossary row. The ontology's own provenance is the ODR/ADR/council corpus: the PDTF→ontology programme is retired (ODR-0003, 2026-06-14); the descriptive layer is reconciled complete (ODR-0028); the inference/validation boundary is ODR-0029 (Council session-039); this reference document is ADR-0041 (Council session-038) and the /manual→/model rename is ADR-0042.
