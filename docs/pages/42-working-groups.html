DPMSG working groups

The Digital Property Market Steering Group does its substantive work through six working groups. OPDA leads or co-leads four of them. Each group has a remit and a folder of materials in the archive.

Six DPMSG working groups feed Steering. OPDA-led groups are highlighted in the table below.

The six groups

GroupRemitOPDA roleMaterials
Steering Governance body — meets monthly, takes formal decisions, allocates work to the other five groups. OPDA Chair (Maria Harris) sits on it. steering-group/materials/
Comms & PR External narrative, press, member-facing communications, Smart Data report contributions. OPDA-led. Output includes the DPMSG Data Standards Interoperability Spring 2025 report. working-groups/comms-pr/
Engagement Stakeholder management — members, government, industry. Runs the monthly all-member update. OPDA-led. Produces the monthly all-member update slide decks. working-groups/engagement/
Policy Public-policy positioning — DMCC implementation, MHCLG consultation responses, Smart Data Scheme design submissions. OPDA contributes; chaired in rotation. working-groups/policy/
Regulator Engagement with regulators — FCA, ICO, NTSELAT (and its successor), CLC, SRA, RICS, CILEx Regulation. OPDA participates; CLC takes the lead given its sandbox role. working-groups/regulator/
Technical The substantive standards work — schemas, OpenAPI, accreditation criteria, conformance test suites. OPDA-led. Owns the @pdtf/schemas package and the trust-framework spec repo. working-groups/technical/

Notable working-group output

  • Comms & PR: DPMSG Data Standards Interoperability Spring 2025 Report — the most cited public output of the DPMSG to date.
  • Comms & PR: data_standards_for_smart_data.pdf — companion explainer.
  • Engagement: three monthly member-update decks (Feb / Apr / May 2026) — see Meetings & decisions.
  • Technical: Smart Property Data Trust Framework Project Plan.md — the live cross-WG plan.
