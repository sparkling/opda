# Executive Briefing: H&M Group Enterprise Digital Twin

## The Provocation

H&M does not need a better dashboard of what happened last week. H&M needs a
live operating model of the enterprise: what is happening now, what is likely to
happen next, what options exist, what each option will cost, and which actions
should be triggered.

The opportunity is to leapfrog conventional data-platform maturity by building a
linked-data digital twin with AI agents as the decision and execution layer. The
twin becomes the shared world model. Agents become the active operating muscle.
Humans remain accountable for strategy, taste, risk appetite, guardrails, and
exceptions.

## Why H&M, Why Now

H&M Group is already publicly describing the right strategic direction:

- More efficient purchasing and shorter decision paths.
- Better ability to capture trends.
- Supply chain improvements and closer supplier cooperation.
- Better inventory efficiency.
- Ongoing integration of store and online channels.
- More data-driven decision-making and use of AI.

Public reports show the business case is material. In full-year 2025 H&M Group
reported SEK 228.285 billion in net sales, SEK 121.821 billion gross profit, and
SEK 18.395 billion operating profit, with an 8.1 percent operating margin. In Q1
2026 the group reported stock-in-trade of SEK 34.608 billion, down 16 percent
year on year, with stock-in-trade at 15.6 percent of rolling 12-month sales.
Online sales were just over 30 percent of sales, and the group had 4,050 stores
at 28 February 2026.

The scale is large enough that small improvements matter. A 50 basis point gross
margin improvement on 2025 sales would be worth around SEK 1.1 billion before
secondary effects. A 1 percent revenue uplift at the 2025 gross margin would
carry around SEK 1.2 billion of gross profit. A 5 percent release of Q1 2026
stock-in-trade would free around SEK 1.7 billion of working capital.

Those are not forecasts. They show the order of magnitude.

## Strategic Thesis

Fashion retail is becoming a speed-of-decision industry.

The winners will not simply forecast better. They will operate with a live model
of demand, products, capacity, inventory, logistics, channels, stores, suppliers,
constraints, carbon, quality, and margin. They will run scenarios continuously.
They will use agents to convert signal into recommended action and, where
appropriate, into controlled execution.

For H&M, this is the path from long-cycle planning to AI-native responsiveness:

- Sense emerging demand and trend signals earlier.
- Simulate product, supplier, logistics, markdown, and allocation options before
  committing.
- Allocate inventory dynamically across stores, ecommerce, markets, and
  fulfilment nodes.
- Increase in-season purchasing and replenishment where demand is proven.
- Reduce waste, lost sales, stockouts, and avoidable markdowns.
- Give product, supply, logistics, and sales leaders the same operational truth.

## What the Digital Twin Is

The proposed digital twin is not a 3D visualisation project. It is an enterprise
state model.

At its core is a semantic model that connects:

- Product entities: style, option, SKU, size, colour, collection, material,
  lifecycle state, bill of materials, compliance attributes, sustainability
  attributes.
- Demand entities: market, channel, store, customer segment, trend signal,
  search, basket, return, campaign, seasonality, weather, event.
- Supply entities: supplier, factory, production line, capacity, lead time,
  quality, cost, risk, compliance, material availability.
- Inventory entities: stock on hand, in transit, allocated, reserved, returned,
  aged, blocked, markdown candidate.
- Logistics entities: purchase order, shipment, container, carrier, route,
  port, warehouse, distribution centre, last-mile method.
- Decision entities: forecast, scenario, recommendation, approval, action,
  result, exception, policy.

The twin is live because it is fed by event streams, system integrations, RFID
and inventory signals, supplier updates, logistics events, ecommerce telemetry,
store activity, and external signals.

## What Makes It Agentic

AI agents sit on top of the twin as role-specific decision workers:

- Trend-to-assortment agents detect trend signals, map them to product families,
  estimate opportunity size, and recommend design, sourcing, or re-cut options.
- Inventory allocation agents identify stock imbalances, simulate transfers,
  and recommend store/ecommerce reallocation.
- Replenishment agents monitor sell-through, lead time, and supplier capacity to
  recommend in-season buys.
- Supplier agents monitor capacity, compliance, quality, risk, and cost changes.
- Logistics agents simulate routing, consolidation, delay mitigation, and
  channel promises.
- Product lifecycle agents manage product decisions from concept to retirement,
  including returns, resale, recycling, and end-of-life.
- Scenario agents answer executive questions such as "What happens if demand in
  Germany spikes 15 percent for this capsule?" or "What if sea freight delays
  increase by two weeks?"

The key shift is this: agents do not guess from ungrounded text. They operate
against a governed linked-data model of H&M's products, nodes, constraints,
history, policies, and live state.

## Market Evidence

Digital twins are already moving from industrial settings into retail and
logistics.

- Lowe's built an interactive store digital twin using NVIDIA Omniverse,
  combining spatial data, product location, and historical order information to
  support store operations.
- Walmart publicly describes real-time AI, automation, self-healing inventory,
  and agentic AI as part of its operating model.
- Inditex reports continued evolution of store technology including Click &
  Collect silos, sorters, assisted self-checkouts, pick-up points, AI Try-On,
  and integrated physical/digital experience.
- McKinsey reports digital twins can support real-time visibility, scenario
  planning, and major decision-speed improvements. Its supply chain work cites
  retail digital twin examples with improved distribution centre utilisation and
  lower fulfilment cost.
- BCG reports early supply chain digital twin adopters have seen 20-30 percent
  forecast accuracy improvements and 50-80 percent reductions in delays and
  downtime in industrial contexts.

The fashion-retail examples are still immature. That is the opening. H&M can be
an early mover in an enterprise twin for fashion, where the twin is not just
visibility, but an AI operating layer.

## Board-Level Business Case

The programme should be evaluated through five value pools:

1. Revenue capture: fewer stockouts, faster trend response, more relevant
   assortment, better local availability, stronger ecommerce/store fulfilment.
2. Gross margin: lower markdowns, better initial allocation, more in-season
   purchasing, less stranded stock, smarter pricing and promotion timing.
3. Working capital: lower average stock, faster inventory turns, better
   allocation of scarce stock, fewer slow-moving pockets.
4. Operating cost: less manual planning work, fewer expedites, more efficient
   logistics, better warehouse utilisation, reduced exception handling.
5. Strategic optionality: supplier flexibility, better near/far sourcing mix,
   faster product lifecycle decisions, stronger resilience to tariffs,
   geopolitical disruption, regulation, and demand volatility.

An indicative mature-state annual recurring impact range of SEK 2.5-9.0 billion
in profit contribution is plausible if H&M can industrialise the twin and
agents across product, inventory, supply chain, logistics, and commercial
execution. This is not a forecast. It is a scenario range built from small
percentage improvements applied to H&M's 2025 financial baseline.

## The Strategic Programme

Programme name:

H&M Enterprise Digital Twin and Agentic Operating System.

Programme mission:

Build the live enterprise model and agentic decision layer that lets H&M sense,
simulate, decide, and execute faster than the fashion market moves.

Four strategic bets:

1. Build the H&M semantic twin as the enterprise world model.
2. Make the twin operational, not analytical: every scenario should be linked
   to possible actions.
3. Deploy agents into decision workflows, not as isolated copilots.
4. Use the programme to force simplification of data, systems, and ownership
   through value use cases, not abstract governance theatre.

## What Changes for Leaders

The current operating model likely asks leaders to reconcile conflicting
reports, defend forecasts, and negotiate across silos. The target model asks
leaders to define objectives, constraints, and risk appetite, then use the twin
to evaluate options and execute faster.

Examples:

- Product leaders see trend, sell-through, supplier capacity, material
  availability, and margin implications in one decision space.
- Supply leaders see supplier, factory, order, logistics, compliance, and risk
  state in one decision space.
- Sales leaders see stock health, local demand, store layouts, ecommerce demand,
  and replenishment options together.
- Finance leaders see the cash, margin, markdown, and working-capital effect of
  operating decisions before they are made.

## Risks

The largest risk is not technical. It is building a twin that looks impressive
but does not change decisions.

Other risks:

- A twin built as another data lake layer will inherit data debt without
  changing accountability.
- Agents without action guardrails will create trust and control problems.
- A platform-only programme will move too slowly and lose executive attention.
- A use-case-only programme will create more point solutions and miss the
  enterprise operating model.
- Governance imposed before value is visible will be resisted.

## Immediate Ask

Approve a strategic mobilisation phase with three outputs:

1. A 12-week executive twin prototype focused on one high-value flow:
   trend-to-allocation or in-season stock reallocation.
2. A target enterprise model for product, inventory, supplier, logistics,
   channel, and decision entities.
3. A sequenced investment case for scaling to enterprise operating model.

The prototype should not be a throwaway demo. It should be the first vertical
slice of the real platform.
