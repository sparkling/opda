## Q1

**Split G by UFO axis at all, or keep ODR-0008 monolithic?**

**Position: ratify the split FRAMEWORK; keep ODR-0008 monolithic until the criterion fires.** This is not a new decision — it re-affirms what Session 008 already ratified. ODR-0008 §Q2a chose **"monolithic-with-named-triggers"**: ODR-0008 stays one module, and the spawn-rule fires on "(a) UFO meta-category crystallisation — when ≥1 sub-module's leaf-set populates such that Quality / Mode / Substance-Kind-label distinctions are operationally load-bearing, spawn ODR-0008a/b/c by UFO axis with named stewards (Allemang on `property-qualities`; Guizzardi/Pandit on `property-modes`; Kendall on `legal-estate-attributes`)." R2 is the standing review of *that* trigger; it does not invent the split, it confirms the split is the right shape and that its trigger has **not** yet fired.

The split is the right shape because the three axes are genuinely different query surfaces, *when* they are populated. A `property-qualities` module collects datatype/SKOS-valued Qualia of the physical thing (`builtForm`, `currentEnergyRating`, `numberOfFloors`, `centralHeatingFuelType`) — these are Quale-in-Region per ODR-0011 §8a (Masolo et al. 2003 *WonderWeb D18* §4.3); their consumers ask "what is this property *like*." A `property-modes` module collects existentially-dependent Modes — disclosures, encumbrance-conditions, obligations that exist only because some relator does (Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4, UFO Mode); their consumers ask "what *obtains about* this property/estate, and under what PII/lawful-basis regime" — which is exactly why ODR-0023 §32 names **ODR-0018 (Mode PII)** as R2's realizing record. `legal-estate-attributes` collects the facts that attach to the *estate*, not the physical thing (`tenureKind`, `groundRent`, `councilTaxBand`) — a different subject altogether. Three subjects, three regimes, three consumer questions: the axis split is sound *as a framework*.

But monolithic-now is correct under derive-don't-declare. With zero leaves yet classified, splitting today would *declare* three modules whose membership we cannot derive — the opposite of the discipline. A module with an empty or one-element leaf-set is overhead, not modularity: it adds an `implements`/`depends-on` edge, a steward, a session, and a `kind: pattern` A9 obligation, while buying no query the monolith cannot already answer. ODR-0008 already attaches every descriptive fact to `opda:Property`/`opda:LegalEstate` and pushes per-form variation to ODR-0010 profiles; SPARQL over those classes answers every descriptive competency question today *without* an axis boundary. So: keep the framework, defer the structure.

**Vote:** FOR — ratify the three-axis split FRAMEWORK + named stewards; keep ODR-0008 monolithic until the criterion fires.

## Q2

**If split, what is the operational trigger per axis (when is `property-qualities` vs `property-modes` load-bearing)?**

**Position: the trigger is a competency query that needs *axis-specific entailment*, not axis-specific membership.** §Q2a's phrase "operationally load-bearing" is the right test but it needs an operational definition, or it will be argued forever. I propose the **load-bearing test**: *an axis spawns its sub-module only when a named consumer query (reviewable query text) needs an entailment or constraint that holds for that axis and not the others — i.e. the query's answer-set changes depending on the axis distinction.* This is the 2020 *Working Ontologist* criterion applied to modules: a distinction is real iff turning it off changes an answer. If you can answer every query by treating all of G as one flat bag of properties on `opda:Property`, the axis is decorative and MUST NOT spawn.

Per-axis instantiation of the test (the entailment each axis would have to be carrying its weight on):

- **`property-qualities` (mine) — Quality/Quale entailment.** Load-bearing when a consumer query needs **quality-region reasoning** that the flat bag cannot give: e.g. "return every property whose `currentEnergyRating` is *worse than* D" requires the ordered Quale-region of the EPC scheme (ODR-0011 §8a Quale-in-Region; the scheme *is* the region), or "compare two properties on the *same* quality dimension" requires that `builtForm` and `constructionType` be recognised as Qualia of one Property-quality space. **Concretely:** the trigger fires when a BASPI5/RDS round-trip query asks for **ordered-band or cross-quality comparison** that a flat `xsd:string` property cannot satisfy and a Quality-typed module can. Absent that query, qualities stay in the monolith — flat datatype properties already answer "what is the EPC band" without a `property-qualities` module.
- **`property-modes` (Guizzardi/Pandit) — Mode/PII-regime entailment.** Load-bearing when a query needs **Mode-vs-Quality distinction to resolve a lawful-basis or existential-dependence answer**: e.g. "which disclosed facts about this leasehold carry a *Consent* lawful basis distinct from the property's *PublicTask* HMLR facts" (ODR-0018 §Context — variant-conditional PII regimes). A Mode is existentially dependent on a relator; that dependence is queryable (who is the obligation *to*; under what basis). The trigger fires when **ODR-0018-style class-level DPV co-annotation must discriminate Mode-bearing leaves from Quality-bearing leaves** — i.e. the PII regime differs *by axis*, not just by leaf.
- **`legal-estate-attributes` (Kendall) — subject-of entailment.** Load-bearing when a query must attach a fact to the **estate** rather than the **physical property** and the two answer differently (a freehold and a leasehold over the same bricks have different `groundRent`/`tenureKind`; ODR-0005's Property-vs-LegalEstate identity distinction made queryable at the descriptive layer).

The unifying rule: **a sub-module spawns only when the axis carries an entailment a consumer query depends on.** This converts "operationally load-bearing" from a vibe into a falsifiable test — the same move ODR-0008 §Q6a already made for predicate *hierarchies* (the reasoner-independence test: UNION-over-children must equal entailed-parent, else the hierarchy is decorative). I am applying §Q6a's logic one level up, to *modules*.

**Vote:** FOR — per-axis trigger = a named, reviewable consumer query whose answer-set depends on that axis's entailment (quality-region for qualities; Mode/PII-regime for modes; estate-subject for legal-estate).
