# Session 023 ‚Äî Dean Allemang (Queen; position)

I chaired S008, which authored ODR-0008 (`Declare-once-reconcile-overlays`). This session asks the *prior* question S008 deferred to implementation and the 2026-05-30 build pass then exposed: before we curate, **what gets imported at all, and at what granularity?** My answer, from my published modelling discipline, is unambiguous. A model earns its terms by the competency questions they answer, not by mirroring the source schema's shape. The S021 "mechanical 935-leaf walk" mirrors the shape. Category-based import models the questions. I vote **FOR** the proposition, with one non-negotiable rider: Category G must still be modelled *well* ‚Äî collapsing the other six categories is the disciplined move precisely so that the ~181 genuine concepts get the per-leaf attention they deserve.

---

## Q1 ‚Äî Diagnosis: conceptual richness, or form-ergonomics + repeated micro-structure?

This is repeated micro-structure, not conceptual richness ‚Äî and PDTF's granularity is *correct for a form transport* but the *wrong altitude for a TBox*. Those are two different claims and the panel must keep them apart, because Davis is right that the second does not impugn the first.

My modelling rule (Allemang, Hendler & Gandon, *Semantic Web for the Working Ontologist*, 3rd ed. 2020, Ch. 3, "It's a Federation, Not a Corporation" / the modelling-discipline thread) is **"model the data you have, for the questions you need to answer."** The operative clause is the second one. The data the schema *has* is 1,493 annotated leaves; the questions you need to answer do not number 1,493. S023-EVIDENCE ¬ßB settles the factual side: the 1,493 leaves collapse to **337 distinct final segments, 241 occurring exactly once**, and **56% (840/1,493) are one of ~16 generic recurring tails** ‚Äî `details`√ó269, `price`√ó99, `comments`√ó96, `isIncludedExcludedOrNone`√ó89. The project's own `audit.json` records that `yesNo` is "referenced **1,135 times**. Not 1,135 unique concepts." That is the diagnosis in the corpus's own words.

The `details`√ó269 case is the cleanest tell. 269 leaves named `details` are not 269 descriptive concepts; they are the *same* free-text "please elaborate" slot, instantiated 269 times under 269 different yes/no questions (S023-EVIDENCE ¬ßB pattern 3, the disclosure pattern, 80 parent nodes). In my framework that is one annotation property ‚Äî at most `rdfs:comment`-grade ‚Äî bound to a question, not 269 entries in the descriptive TBox.

Crucially this is **not** a charge of "poor modelling" against PDTF, and I want that on the record because it is where Davis and I actually agree. JSON Schema for a form transport *should* carry `boilerImmersionHeater.isIncludedExcludedOrNone` as a distinct path ‚Äî the form needs to render that field, capture that value, round-trip that instance. Form ergonomics demand the granularity. What it does not demand is that each of those 1,493 paths becomes a permanent `owl:DatatypeProperty` IRI in the published ontology. The schema is right for its altitude; the TBox lives at a different altitude. ODR-0008 already said this ‚Äî S008 Q3, recorded in the ODR Context: *"that nesting is form ergonomics, not ontology ‚Äî flatten it."* Q1 generalises the same finding from *nesting* to *leaf-count*.

The build pass (ADR-0028's deferral note, 2026-05-30) is the empirical proof the diagnosis bites: naive last-segment naming "would collapse 1,521 distinct attributes into ~351 colliding properties," and "Declare-once (ODR-0008) reconciles the *same* attribute across *overlays*, **not** every base leaf sharing a final segment." The mechanical walk cannot even *name* its outputs without either colliding or inventing 1,521 synthetic disambiguated IRIs ‚Äî and 1,521 path-derived IRIs is the form shape declared as ontology, which is exactly what ODR-0008 Q3 rejected.

**Vote: FOR** ‚Äî the explosion is form-ergonomics + repeated micro-structure; PDTF granularity is right-for-a-form-transport, wrong-altitude-for-a-TBox.

## Q2 ‚Äî The category taxonomy A‚ÄìG as the decision-cut

The A‚ÄìG cut is the right decision-cut, and it is the natural completion of my own S008 rulings ‚Äî it is ODR-0008 ¬ßQ4a (class-promotion), ¬ßQ5a (datatype-vs-SKOS binding), and ¬ßQ6a (flat-default) lifted from per-leaf adjudication to an up-front *routing* of the whole layer. I support it, with two boundary refinements and the UFO meta-category leanings (which I state per category because A9 requires every `pattern`-grade commitment to name its UFO category, and these treatments are exactly that).

Citation for the cut itself: *Working Ontologist* 3rd ed., Ch. 10 (SKOS) and the modelling-patterns material establish that **the unit of modelling is the recurring pattern, not the individual field** ‚Äî you model `skos:Concept` schemes and reusable property patterns once and let instances reference them. A‚ÄìG is that principle applied as a triage:

- **A ‚Äî Disclosure / free-text tails (~407).** UFO: not a Quality at all ‚Äî it is `rdfs:comment`-grade *text about* an assertion. One reusable annotation property (`opda:disclosureDetail`), bound to the question it elaborates, not promoted per-question. This is the strongest collapse and I hold it firmly.
- **B ‚Äî Evidence / attachment envelope.** UFO: **Object** (a Document/evidence individual). Reuse ODR-0009 Evidence + PROV-O; ~3 properties. Settled elsewhere; do not re-mint.
- **C ‚Äî Reused status enums (378 ‚Üí 54 value-sets).** UFO: **Quale-in-Region** ‚Äî an enumerated value-space. ODR-0011 SKOS schemes, one shared property per value-space. This *is* my S008 ¬ßQ5a binding table, generalised: ¬ßQ5a already routed `currentEnergyRating`, `councilTaxBand`, `builtForm` to "SKOS scheme; Quality of `opda:Property`," and routed `yesNoNotKnown` to "`owl:DatatypeProperty` with `sh:in` ‚Äî no SKOS scheme." C executes ¬ßQ5a at category scale.
- **D ‚Äî Checklist items (fixtures & fittings, ~315 = 89 items √ó 3).** UFO: the *items* are **Objects/individuals** in a controlled list (a SKOS scheme of fixture items), and the three repeated fields (`inclusionStatus`, `comment`, `price`) are properties of the checklist-line, not 315 TBox properties. This is reference data, and reference data does not belong in the TBox as 315 predicates.
- **E ‚Äî Repeated report/result structures (~200).** UFO: **Object + Quality, prov-bearing.** One `opda:SearchResult` / `opda:RiskAssessment` class (~6 properties) + a peril/dataset SKOS scheme. This is the direct extension of ODR-0008 ¬ßQ4a, whose three-criterion test already promoted `Survey`/`EPCCertificate`/`Search`/`Valuation`/`Comparable` to classes on the **authority-retrieved-provenance** criterion (a). A search/risk *result* is authority-retrieved and prov-bearing ‚Üí it promotes to a class by my own ¬ßQ4a(a), not to ~200 flat datatype properties.
- **F ‚Äî Identity / address / contact / geo sub-fields (~133).** Already modelled upstream ‚Äî ODR-0015 (Address), ODR-0006 (Agents); geo deferred. Reuse, do not re-import.
- **G ‚Äî Genuine descriptive attributes (352 instances ‚Üí 181 distinct names).** UFO: **per-leaf** ‚Äî Quality / Quale-in-Region / Mode, adjudicated leaf by leaf. This is the real ODR-0008 layer. This is where the per-leaf walk *belongs*, and the curation budget the other six categories free up is spent *here*.

**Boundary refinements.** (1) The A/G boundary is the one to watch, and it is where Davis's vigilance earns its keep ‚Äî a `details` tail that, in one specific form, is the *only* carrier of a genuine fact (not mere elaboration) must be promoted out of A into G. The test is a competency question, not the segment name: *does any consumer ask for this value as a fact about the Property?* If yes ‚Üí G. If it only ever elaborates a yes/no already captured ‚Üí A. (2) The D/G boundary: a fixtures *item* that is also a genuine property fact elsewhere (rare) reconciles via ODR-0008 ¬ßQ1a rather than being double-modelled.

**Vote: FOR** ‚Äî A‚ÄìG is the right decision-cut and the natural lift of ODR-0008 ¬ßQ4a/¬ßQ5a/¬ßQ6a from per-leaf to up-front routing; refine the A/G boundary by competency question.

## Q3 ‚Äî Whole or part? (the core decision)

**Part ‚Äî import by category.** This is the crux and my vote is the strongest FOR on the ballot. Importing all annotated leaves 1:1 as flat datatype properties is mirroring the source schema; that is the single discipline my published work exists to refute.

The governing citation is *Working Ontologist* 3rd ed., Ch. 3 ‚Äî the **AAA slogan: "Anyone can say Anything about Any topic,"** under the **Open World Assumption.** The reason these matter here is precise and often missed: under AAA + Open World, **the absence of a per-leaf datatype property does not mean the fact cannot be stated.** A consumer who wants to say something about `boilerImmersionHeater.price` can do so against the D-category checklist pattern (a `FixtureItem` keyed to the boiler, bearing a `price`); they are not blocked by the TBox declining to mint `opda:boilerImmersionHeaterPrice`. A closed-world, relational-schema instinct says "every field must be a column or the data has nowhere to go." RDF is not relational. The pattern carries the fact; the 315th predicate is not load-bearing. This is the conceptual core of why "collapse most" is *not* "lose data."

Second citation, the cost of getting it wrong: *Working Ontologist* 3rd ed., Ch. 3 / Ch. 6 on **IRIs as durable global identifiers.** The build pass establishes (ADR-0028 deferral note) that the ~900 datatype-property IRIs "are published, stable identifiers; an auto-derived scheme cannot be cleanly reversed." Minting 900 permanent IRIs from a mechanical last-segment walk ‚Äî when 1,521 leaves collapse to ~351 distinct segments and the existing 23 were each *hand-curated* with semantic short names and hand-assigned domains ‚Äî is not "completeness." It is committing the federation to ~900 irreversible identifiers, most of which name a form-field rather than a concept, none of which any competency question requested. That is the opposite of "model the data you have, for the questions you need to answer."

The proposition's arithmetic is the disciplined reframing: "evaluate 935 leaves" ‚Üí "ratify ~5 structural treatments + curate ~181 G-leaves" ‚Äî an ~80% reduction in the *expensive* operation, with the freed budget redirected to modelling G properly. I would put the headline as: **we are not importing less of the domain; we are importing the domain at the right grain and declining to import the form's plumbing as if it were the domain.**

**Vote: FOR** ‚Äî import by category (collapse A‚ÄìF; per-leaf only G). 1:1 import mirrors the source schema, the one thing my methodology refuses.

## Q4 ‚Äî Recurring micro-patterns (A, B, E)

Reusable property/class patterns ‚Äî emphatically not per-leaf datatype properties. And here is where my "derive, don't declare" principle does its sharpest work, because it answers Davis's round-trip objection *without* declaring 900 properties.

- **A (disclosure tail):** one `opda:disclosureDetail` annotation property. Citation: *Working Ontologist* 3rd ed. ‚Äî the discipline that **free text describing an assertion is metadata about a statement, not a domain Quality.** 269 `details` ‚Üí 1 property; the *which-question-it-elaborates* is carried by the binding (the `dct:source` path / the SHACL shape that places it), not by 269 distinct predicates.
- **B (evidence envelope):** reuse ODR-0009 Evidence + PROV-O. Already a `pattern` ODR; importing attachment leaves as datatype properties would duplicate a ratified class. No new work.
- **E (search/risk result):** one `opda:SearchResult`/`opda:RiskAssessment` class with ~6 properties + a peril/dataset SKOS scheme. This is *forced* by my own ODR-0008 ¬ßQ4a(a): authority-retrieved, prov-bearing ‚Üí class. The "one result structure √ó ~24 datasets, recursively nested" (S023-EVIDENCE ¬ßB pattern 2) is the textbook case for *one* class instantiated per dataset, with the dataset identity in a SKOS scheme ‚Äî not ~200 flat predicates that would have to be named `floodingFloodRiskRiskIndicator`, `coalMiningResultSummary`, and so on.

**Derive, don't declare ‚Äî the operative form here.** ODR-0008 ¬ßQ6a (which I authored) gives the exact test that adjudicates Davis's "but a consumer asks for `boilerImmersionHeater.price`" worry: the **reasoner-independence / UNION test.** A parent-or-pattern level answer is legitimate only if "UNION-over-children must equal the entailed-parent answer-set." Apply it here: the form *structure* (which leaf sits under which question) is **derivable by query** over the pattern + `dct:source` paths + the SHACL profile shapes ‚Äî it does not need to be *declared* as 900 standalone TBox predicates. The structure is recoverable; declaring it is decorative under an entailment-off SPARQL endpoint. That is "derive don't declare" stated precisely: the form structure can be *derived/queried*; it must not be *declared* as 900 properties.

**Vote: FOR** ‚Äî A‚Üíone annotation property; B‚Üíreuse ODR-0009; E‚Üía prov-bearing class + scheme (forced by ODR-0008 ¬ßQ4a). Structure is derived/queried, not declared.

## Q5 ‚Äî Checklist + enums (D, C)

- **D (fixtures & fittings, ~315 = 89 items √ó 3):** a SKOS scheme of fixture items + ~3 properties (`inclusionStatus`, `comment`, `price`) on the checklist-line ‚Äî **reference data**, not 315 datatype properties. Citation: *Working Ontologist* 3rd ed., Ch. 10 (SKOS), the controlled-vocabulary discipline ‚Äî **a controlled list of items is a `skos:ConceptScheme`, not a set of TBox predicates.** 89 boiler/radiator/heater chattels are `skos:Concept`s in a `FixtureItem` scheme; "is this one included?" is a property of the line referencing the concept. This is the single highest-leverage collapse after A: 315 ‚Üí 3 + a scheme.

- **C (54 enum value-sets):** ODR-0011 SKOS schemes with shared reused properties ‚Äî this is **literally my S008 ¬ßQ5a binding table**, which already ruled `(Excluded,Included,None)`-style category-likes to "SKOS scheme; Quality of `opda:Property`," while keeping pure flags (`yesNoNotKnown`) as "`owl:DatatypeProperty` with `sh:in` ‚Äî no SKOS scheme." The ¬ßQ5a discriminator stands: **a value-space that is a genuine category ‚Üí SKOS (ODR-0011); a one-shot boolean/flag ‚Üí `xsd:string + sh:in`.** 378 enum leaves ‚Üí 54 value-sets ‚Üí a bounded set of SKOS schemes (the genuine categories) plus inline `sh:in` (the flags). The burden-of-promotion rule from ¬ßQ5a ("burden of SKOS promotion on the proposer per leaf") governs the residue.

One caution against *over*-collapsing C: do not merge two value-sets that happen to share members but answer different questions (e.g. an `(Attached, To follow)` evidence-status is not the same Quale-region as an `(Included, Excluded, None)` inclusion-status even where a form reuses tokens). SKOS scheme identity follows the *question*, not the token string ‚Äî the same A/G discipline, applied to enums.

**Vote: FOR** ‚Äî D‚Üífixture-item SKOS scheme + 3 props (reference data); C‚Üí54 value-sets to ODR-0011 SKOS schemes + shared props per ODR-0008 ¬ßQ5a; flags stay `sh:in`.

## Q6 ‚Äî Coverage, round-trip & residual scope (Davis's crux)

Category-based import **does** satisfy BASPI5 round-trip + consumer queries without 1:1 leaves, and Category G (~181 distinct names) **is** the right bounded per-leaf target. This is the question Davis owns and I want to meet it on its merits, not wave it away.

**Round-trip.** ODR-0008 ¬ßQ7a (which I authored at S008) already settled the architecture that makes this work: per-form structure lives in the **SHACL overlay profiles** (ODR-0010), not the base TBox. Boundary clause 1: "base TBox `0..*` for every descriptive property; per-form `sh:minCount` lives in ODR-0010 profile shapes." Boundary clause 2: enum unions merged at build-time per profile. The round-trip gate ‚Äî "loaded profile validates data *and* regenerates the form" (ODR-0008 Consequences) ‚Äî is discharged by the *profile* carrying the per-form leaf set, against the *pattern/class/scheme* base. The base does not need 900 predicates for the profile to know that BASPI5 question N renders fixture-item *boiler* with an inclusion-status field. The structure that regenerates the form is in the profile + the `dct:source` path, exactly where S008 Q7a put it. Citation: *Working Ontologist* 3rd ed., Ch. 13 (SHACL) ‚Äî **SHACL shapes describe the expected structure of data for a context**; that is the right home for per-form structure, and it is reused across forms rather than frozen into base predicates.

**Consumer addressability.** Under AAA + Open World (Ch. 3), a consumer asking for `boilerImmersionHeater.price` is answered by the D-pattern: the `FixtureItem` scheme keys the boiler; the checklist-line bears `price`; the `dct:source` carries the instance path for exact addressing. ADR-0028's own deferral note confirms instance addressing survives: "`dct:source` carries the instance addressing." So the specific leaf is *reachable* ‚Äî it is just reached through a pattern + a scheme + a path, not through a dedicated 1:1 predicate. Coverage is preserved; what changes is the *grain* of the identifier, not the *reach* of the query.

**This is where Davis is right and where he over-reaches.** He is right that coverage is a gate ‚Äî a TBox that silently dropped a fact a consumer needs would fail, and the A/G boundary (Q2) is exactly the vigilance that prevents it: every genuine fact must land *somewhere* (G, a class, or a pattern), and the ¬ßQ4a held-as-live promotions (`Building`/`Room` ‚Äî his own S008 dissent) stay re-openable on a named round-trip query. Where he over-reaches is conflating *coverage* with *one-property-per-leaf*. Those are different gates. Coverage asks "is every fact reachable?" ‚Äî answer: yes, via patterns + profiles + paths. One-property-per-leaf asks "does every form-field have its own TBox predicate?" ‚Äî and the AAA/Open-World answer is that a federation does not need that, and the IRI-permanence cost (ADR-0028: ~900 irreversible identifiers) makes paying for it actively harmful.

**Residual scope.** Category G ‚Äî **181 distinct names** ‚Äî is the right, bounded per-leaf WG target. It is bounded (12% of 1,493), it is genuine (the `builtForm`/`yearOfBuild`/`councilTaxBand` facts ODR-0008 was *written* for), and it is the only category where per-leaf UFO adjudication (Quality vs Quale-in-Region vs Mode, per ¬ßQ5a) earns its cost. Collapsing A‚ÄìF is what *makes the G curation affordable*: instead of 935 mechanical-then-unsound emissions, the WG does ~5 structural ratifications + ~181 real modelling decisions. That is the disciplined target.

**Vote: FOR** ‚Äî round-trip is satisfied by SHACL profiles (ODR-0008 ¬ßQ7a) + `dct:source` paths, not 1:1 leaves; G (~181) is the right bounded residual; coverage ‚âÝ one-property-per-leaf.

## Cross-talk ‚Äî engaging Ian Davis (DA: completeness / round-trip / publish-as-is)

Ian, your dissent is the most important one on this panel and I do not want it withdrawn cheaply, because the half of it that is right is load-bearing.

**Where you are right.** Three places, and I have written all three into my votes. (1) *Coverage is a gate.* A TBox that drops a fact a consumer needs is broken, full stop ‚Äî your standing S021 dissent on "completeness-as-a-gate" is correct as far as it goes, and it is why I made the A/G boundary (Q2) a *competency-question* test, not a segment-name test: any `details` tail that is the sole carrier of a genuine fact gets promoted to G. Nothing genuine is allowed to fall through the collapse. (2) *Round-trip is the MVP gate.* BASPI5 must regenerate. I am not relaxing that; I am pointing at where S008 ¬ßQ7a already put the machinery that discharges it ‚Äî the profile, not the base predicate set. (3) *Your `Building`/`Room` held-as-live from S008 ¬ßQ4a stands*, re-openable on the first BASPI5 round-trip query that exercises sub-Property reasoning. Your vigilance is *in* the design, not argued away by it.

**Where you over-reach ‚Äî the conflation.** Your completeness-gate quietly equates two things that are not the same: **coverage** (every fact is reachable) and **one-property-per-leaf** (every form-field has its own permanent TBox predicate). The first is a real gate and we pass it. The second is a *relational/closed-world reflex* ‚Äî the instinct that if a field is not a column, the value has nowhere to live. RDF is a federation under AAA and the Open World Assumption (*Working Ontologist* 3rd ed., Ch. 3): the value lives against the pattern, addressed by `dct:source`, reachable by SPARQL over the `FixtureItem` scheme. Demanding a 1:1 predicate to "guarantee" reach is solving a problem the data model does not have ‚Äî and the bill for solving it is ~900 permanent, irreversible IRIs (ADR-0028's own finding) that mostly name form plumbing. You would be buying addressability you already have, at the price of a federation cluttered with form-shaped identifiers no competency question asked for.

**The publish-as-is point, met head-on.** "Publish the leaves, let consumers find them" sounds like the BBC `/programmes/` instinct ‚Äî expose the data, let the graph grow. But `/programmes/` published *concepts* (brands, episodes, versions) at the grain users queried, not the CMS's every internal field. The PDTF analogue of `/programmes/`-discipline is **G + the patterns**, not the 1,493 form paths. Publishing 900 last-segment-derived predicates is not "publish the data"; it is *publish the form's internal structure as if it were the vocabulary* ‚Äî and the build pass proved you cannot even do that cleanly (1,521 leaves ‚Üí ~351 colliding segments; the existing 23 were hand-curated, there is no mechanical extension). So the very thing the publish-as-is position wants ‚Äî a clean, dereferenceable, complete vocabulary ‚Äî is *better served* by category import: ~181 clean G IRIs + a handful of reusable patterns and schemes, every one of which a consumer query can actually motivate.

My ask of you, Ian: hold your dissent precisely where it bites ‚Äî name the *specific* competency query, if you have one, that a category treatment fails to answer. If it exists, that leaf is G (or a class), and you have improved the cut. If it does not exist, the 1:1 predicate it would have justified was never load-bearing. Either way the federation is better for asking.
