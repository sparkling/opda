**Baker (Q2 — my core question):** **REJECT the new scheme; AFFIRM reuse of `opda:TransactionStatusScheme`. Ballot: FOR reuse / AGAINST minting a coarse scheme.** This is reuse-before-mint at its plainest, and the proposed coarse scheme fails three SKOS-governance tests at once:

1. **Reuse-before-mint (the standing OPDA doctrine).** `opda:TransactionStatusScheme` already exists, is 1 of 47 emitted schemes, is ADR-0010-named, and every member carries `prov:wasDerivedFrom` its data-dictionary `status` enum value (`opda-vocabularies.ttl:2647–2684`). Its own `skos:definition` already declares it "Phase labels for the lifecycle of a Transaction." A coarse parallel scheme would be a **near-duplicate of a published scheme covering the same axis on the same bearer** — exactly the proliferation SKOS catalogue stewardship forbids. There is no warranted-distinctness here: the five canonical phases (Listed/Offered/Accepted/Exchanged/Completed) are the *finer, source-derived* set; a coarse re-cut is a lossy re-labelling of terms we already publish, not a new conceptual axis. (SKOS Reference §3; DCMI Usage Board / ISO 25964-1:2011 §8 — one authoritative scheme per axis.)

2. **The coarse tokens collide head-on with a *different bearer's* scheme — and S032's bearer-distinctness is the whole point.** I verified both schemes. `opda:ParticipantStatusScheme` (`:281`) has notations **`Proposed / Invited / Active / Removed`**; the proposed coarse Transaction tokens (`Proposed / Active / Exchanged / Completed`, per the removed exemplar comment) reuse **`Proposed` and `Active` verbatim**. Minting a coarse Transaction scheme with those tokens would put *the same notation strings* under two schemes whose entire reason for separate existence (S032 §Refinement, 5–0–0) is that they bear on **two different things** — `opda:Transaction` (the Relator) vs. the participant's role-play. Identical surface tokens across two bearer-distinct schemes is a catalogue-hygiene hazard: it invites silent cross-scheme confusion in queries, badges, and dereferenced pages, and it erodes the bearer-distinctness S032 paid for. Reusing `TransactionStatusScheme` keeps the two bearers' value-spaces lexically disjoint (`Listed/Offered/Accepted/Exchanged/Completed` vs `Proposed/Invited/Active/Removed`), which is the correct, hygienic outcome. **Confirmed: the two bearers stay distinct per S032** — and that is precisely *why* the coarse scheme must be rejected. (S032 Q1/Q3 5–0–0 + §Refinement; SKOS Reference §S27–S31 one-primary-scheme integrity.)

3. **One-primary-scheme integrity (Isaac's S032 constraint, my co-signed S011 line).** ODR-0011 §1a holds `skos:inScheme` exactly-1 per concept. A coarse `Active` would either duplicate the participant `Active` concept (forbidden) or be a third near-synonym floating beside two existing ones — the duplicate-concept proliferation S032 explicitly blocked when it refused per-role schemes. The remedy S032 already named for "a different cut of the same terms" is a `skos:Collection`, never a new `skos:ConceptScheme`. If a consumer ever genuinely needs a coarse *view* (e.g. Marketing→UnderOffer→Exchanged→Completed roll-ups), that view is a `skos:Collection` of `skos:member`s over the existing five, or an `sh:in` subset — **decorative-if-minted-as-a-scheme**, in Allemang's S032 phrase.

Bearer confirmation (mine to give from the SKOS side): the value-space binds the **Transaction Relator** (Milestone-Phase axis, S032), and it is `opda:TransactionStatusScheme`. **Cross-talk — Kendall (Queen):** your S032 DA attack established that a single scheme per axis survives precisely *because the corpus carries no role-divergent lifecycle data*; the same evidentiary discipline kills a coarse mint here — there is no second, coarser axis in the source, only a looser exemplar label. **Cross-talk — Davis:** this is the don't-mint verdict in SKOS terms — the strongest non-mint is that the scheme is *already minted and source-grounded*. **Cross-talk — Gandon:** reuse also serves dereferenceability — the five `…/scheme/transactionStatus/{Listed…}` term IRIs already resolve; a coarse mint would publish a second, competing set of phase IRIs for one bearer, which is the catalogue-incoherence your lens and mine both reject.

**Baker (Q3 — exemplar value-mapping):** **REVISE — ballot FOR the canonical mapping below.** The removed exemplar tokens were lower-case free-text (`"active"`, `"completed"`) and a comment string (`proposed/active/exchanged/completed`) that match **neither** scheme's `skos:notation`s exactly — which is the §S15 notation-discipline failure that justified removing them. Mapped onto the canonical `opda:TransactionStatusScheme` notations:

- `chain-of-transactions.ttl` / `lease-extension`'s **`"active"`** → there is no `Active` notation in `TransactionStatusScheme` (that token belongs to `ParticipantStatusScheme`). A Transaction that is live but pre-exchange maps to **`Offered`** if an offer is on the table, else **`Listed`**; absent any milestone or offer evidence in `chain-of-transactions`, the honest canonical value is **`Listed`** (the entry phase), or — preferably — **omit the property** rather than assert a phase the source does not attest (generator-first / data-grounded discipline). I do **not** invent an `Active` Transaction notation; doing so would re-import the very collision Q2 rejects.
- **`"completed"`** → **`Completed`** (`…/scheme/transactionStatus/Completed`, `:2651`) — a clean case-normalisation to the canonical CamelCase `skos:notation`. `lease-extension` and `simple-transaction-with-milestones` both map here.
- `simple-transaction-with-milestones.ttl` additionally has reified milestones (instruction→…→registration); its **phase** value is still `Completed` on the Transaction, and its **milestone** events bind `opda:MilestoneScheme` — the two are orthogonal and both already exist.

The load-bearing SKOS point: `"active"`/`"completed"` were ungoverned literals; the fix is to bind them to existing **`skos:notation`** values (case- and scheme-correct), *not* to widen any scheme to admit the loose tokens. **Cross-talk — Davis:** the mapping mints nothing — every target notation is already emitted. Citation: SKOS Reference §S15 (`skos:notation` typed, the controlled lexical handle) + §S14 (one `prefLabel @en`); my own Baker+Pandit S011 Q2 cardinality table (ratified 4–0); `opda-vocabularies.ttl:2651,2669` (the canonical `Completed`/`Listed` notations).

**Baker (Q4 — should `ufoCategory` dereference?):** **AFFIRM — ballot FOR.** Yes, `/pdtf/ufoCategory` and the `UFOCategoryScheme` term pages SHOULD dereference, *even though the property is inert and never reasoned*. Inertness is a **reasoning** property; dereferenceability is a **catalogue/publication** property — they are orthogonal, and conflating them ("it's inert, so don't publish it") is the category error to avoid, not the publishing. The linked-data first principle is that any IRI a published artefact mentions SHOULD resolve to a useful description (the Cool-URIs / linked-data-principle baseline); `opda:ufoCategory` is used on ~39 classes and 47 schemes and is referenced in ODR-0030 R7b and ODR-0031 §Confirmation, so a 404 at `/pdtf/ufoCategory` is a dangling published term — a catalogue defect — regardless of its OWL semantics. This is not scope-creep: ODR-0031 R4 already settled that the pages are **read-side SPARQL projections** that "cannot entrench" (5–0–1 FOR), and ODR-0031 §Confirmation *lists the term page as a confirmation criterion*. So the council has already decided this; Q4 is enforcement, not re-litigation. Crucially, for *my* lens, `UFOCategoryScheme` is a `skos:ConceptScheme` of nine `skos:Concept`s with `skos:closeMatch`/`exactMatch` to gUFO — **that is exactly the kind of vocabulary term SKOS expects to be dereferenceable and browsable**; an inert annotation property pointing into a real SKOS scheme is the *most* publishable shape, not the least. **Cross-talk — Gandon:** fully with you — a referenced-but-unresolvable term is the dereferenceability failure your lens names; the deploy-fix's `hasResource()` guard stopped the dangle from breaking shape links but left the term itself missing, which is a half-fix. **Cross-talk — Guizzardi:** publishing the page does not re-assert UFO grounding — the disclosure ("UFO-informed, not UFO-grounded") rides on the page, so dereferencing strengthens the honesty doctrine rather than over-claiming. Citation: ODR-0031 R4 (read-side projections; 5–0–1) + §Confirmation (term page is a criterion); ODR-0030 R7b (the disclosure mandate); SKOS Reference §10 (mapping properties — these are publishable, browsable terms); Berners-Lee, *Linked Data* design note / *Cool URIs for the Semantic Web* (W3C Note 2008 — published vocabulary IRIs SHOULD dereference).

**Baker (Q5 — how should the site model surface annotation properties?):** **REVISE — ballot FOR a distinct `entryKind` + inert badge + disclosure-as-spine.** Yes — and the way to publish an inert facet *without* it masquerading as a first-class reasoned term is precisely a distinct catalogue affordance. Concretely: (a) extend `scripts/ontology-model.mjs` to extract `owl:AnnotationProperty` into the model under a **separate `entryKind`** (e.g. `annotationProperty`) — distinct from `objectProperty`/`datatypeProperty`/`class` — so `allResources()`/`ontology-model.json` include it and `/pdtf/ufoCategory` resolves; (b) the term page carries an explicit **inert badge** reading "classified-under / advisory — not reasoned" (never "is-a"), per ODR-0031 R4's badge rule; (c) the **page spine is the three-part DOLCE disclosure** (ODR-0030 R7b), so the first thing a reader sees is the honesty caveat, with the `UFOCategoryScheme` value-list (the nine concepts + their gUFO `closeMatch`) rendered as the controlled vocabulary it is. This is the SKOS/DCMI catalogue-typing instinct: a catalogue distinguishes term *kinds* so consumers don't mistake an editorial annotation for an asserted axiom — the `entryKind` *is* that type discriminator. It also generalises cleanly to the annotation-property family (the IAO crosswalk, any future advisory predicate) without bespoke per-term plumbing. **Cross-talk — Gandon:** the distinct `entryKind` is what makes the dereferenced page *honest about its own status* — it resolves (your requirement) and it self-labels as inert (the no-over-claim requirement), satisfying both lenses in one affordance. **Cross-talk — Guizzardi:** the disclosure-as-spine is the structural guarantee your "UFO-informed not UFO-grounded" doctrine asked for — it cannot be skimmed past. Citation: ODR-0031 R4 + R8 anti-patterns (pages must not read as a grounded UFO commitment); ODR-0030 R7b (disclosure on the term page); BRIEF verified findings (the generator extracts neither `owl:AnnotationProperty` today, hence the 404); SKOS Reference §1.3 / DCMI Usage Board (typed catalogue entries — distinguish concept/scheme/annotation surfaces).
