# Session 043 — Guizzardi (UFO / OntoUML; gUFO)

Lens: phases as anti-rigid sortals partitioning a kind by contingent intrinsic state; Relators; the class-vs-value and endurant-vs-perdurant distinctions; reify-a-stage-as-event vs carry-it-as-attribute; and the coherence of an inert UFO-meta-category annotation. Citations are load-bearing.

---

**Guizzardi — Q1 (is transaction phase an anti-rigid Phase carried as an attribute, or a perdurant event/milestone?). Verdict: REVISE. Ballot: FOR — *amendment*: a Transaction carries a direct lifecycle-phase property whose bearer is the Relator and whose value is the current Phase; the reified `opda:Milestone` events are retained as the perdurants that *effect* the phase transitions, not as a substitute for the phase.** These are two different ontological categories and the model needs both. A Phase is an *anti-rigid sortal that partitions a kind by a contingent change in the bearer's intrinsic state* — the bearer is *in* the phase, the phase is a synchronic snapshot of an endurant (Guizzardi 2005, *Ontological Foundations for Structural Conceptual Models*, Ch. 4, §4.3, Phase vs Role; the partition is by intrinsic, not relational, contingent state). A Milestone, as the exemplar already types it (`a prov:Activity, opda:Milestone`; `simple-transaction-with-milestones.ttl:74,88,108,128,148`), is a **perdurant/event** — an occurrent with temporal parts that *brings about* a state change. The event is *not* the state: an event happens and is over; the phase is a standing condition the Relator occupies *between* events. Collapsing the phase into the event is exactly the perdurant/endurant category error UFO exists to prevent — you cannot ask a completed `prov:Activity` "what state is the transaction in now?", because the activity is past and the question is about the present standing of the endurant. The decisive evidence is in the brief's own findings: `chain-of-transactions.ttl` and `lease-extension-transaction.ttl` carry **no milestones at all** yet manifestly have a lifecycle standing (the chain exemplar even derives `chainStatus` from per-transaction status: "any-blocked → chain-blocked", `chain-of-transactions.ttl:21,66`). If the milestone events were the *only* phase representation, these two transactions would have *no expressible current phase* — an absurd result for a Relator that is plainly "active" or "completed". The phase is the queryable, monotone present-state; the milestone is the dated occurrent in the provenance layer (ODR-0009). ODR-0007 §Rules already commits to exactly this dual treatment — "Milestones and status as anti-rigid Phases … A transaction is 'in marketing', 'under offer', 'exchanged', 'completed' the way a phase is entered and left" AND "a milestone *transition* is represented once as a lifecycle Phase change here and **referenced (not re-modelled)** as a `prov:Activity`" (ODR-0007 §Rules, "Milestones and status as anti-rigid Phases"; "Lifecycle/provenance seam"). **Cross-talk — to Davis (DA, "milestones suffice; mint nothing"):** Davis is right that we mint no new *machinery* and right to be parsimonious, but "milestones suffice" conflates the occurrent with the state. The two no-milestone exemplars are the falsifier: an event-only model leaves them stateless. The phase property is not a second copy of the milestone — it is the *fons et origo* present-tense projection the milestone's completion writes to once. To Moreau (PROV, event-vs-state): we agree the *transition* is a `prov:Activity`; my point is narrower and complementary — the *post-state* the activity `prov:generated` is an endurant Phase on the Relator, not another activity. PROV gives the verb; UFO gives the standing condition the verb leaves behind.

**Guizzardi — Q2 (reuse `opda:TransactionStatusScheme` or mint a new coarse scheme; confirm bearer = the Transaction Relator per S032). Verdict: AFFIRM (reuse; bearer = Relator). Ballot: FOR.** Two distinct sub-claims, both settled by prior council and by UFO bearer-analysis. **(a) Bearer.** The bearer of the *milestone/transaction* lifecycle phase is the `opda:Transaction` **Relator** — this is not re-opened, it was decided 5–0–0 at [session-032](../../session-032-status-scheme-grain.md) and recorded in ODR-0011 §8a: "Transaction **milestones** / `legalForms` → ONE `opda:MilestoneScheme` (Phase label; bearer = `opda:Transaction`, the Relator)" (ODR-0011 §8a status-scheme-grain note). The session-032 refinement I co-authored is precise: **two distinct Phase-bearers** — the milestone-Phase bears on the Relator; `participantStatus` bears on the *participant's role-play* (the qua-individual / Mode existentially dependent on the Relator). My S032 rationale stands verbatim: "the bearer of `participantStatus` is the role-PLAY (qua-individual/Mode), existentially dependent on and individuated by the *single* `opda:Transaction` Relator … 'Active' is one Phase universal under many bearers" (Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4; gUFO 2019). So the property at issue in Q1/Q2 is the *transaction*-phase, and its bearer is unambiguously the Relator. **(b) Scheme.** Reuse the **already-emitted** `opda:TransactionStatusScheme` (Listed / Offered / Accepted / Exchanged / Completed); do **not** mint a coarse parallel scheme. Minting a second scheme over the same phase-axis would create the duplicate-concept lattice that the ODR-0011 §1a `ConceptInExactlyOnePrimarySchemeShape` one-primary-scheme integrity constraint forbids — the same constraint that defeated per-role schemes at S032 (ODR-0011 §1a; session-032 Isaac's grounding, SKOS Reference 2009 §S27–S31). One Phase partition over one kind ⇒ one scheme; a Phase partition is a *single* division of the kind's state-space into mutually-exclusive, jointly-exhaustive phases (Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4 — a phase-partition is exhaustive and disjoint by construction), so two schemes over the same axis is not a modelling choice, it is an incoherent double partition. The five notations are already `prov:wasDerivedFrom` the data-dictionary `status` enum (brief finding A), i.e. source-grounded — minting coarser values would *lose* that provenance for no gain. **Cross-talk — to Baker (SKOS scheme reuse):** I concur with Baker's reuse instinct on SKOS-mechanism grounds; I add the UFO warrant that makes it non-optional — the phase-partition's exhaustive-and-disjoint structure means the scheme is the *extensional realisation of one partition*, so "reuse vs mint" is not balanced options but coherent-vs-incoherent. To Davis: note that reuse-not-mint is the maximally parsimonious answer to Q2 and should satisfy the DA's "mint nothing" floor for the *scheme* even though I diverge on Q1's property.

**Guizzardi — Q3 (canonical mapping of the removed `active` / `completed`). Verdict: REVISE. Ballot: FOR — *amendment*: `completed` → `Completed`; `active` → `Offered` as the conservative default, flagged for operator confirmation, NOT silently to `Accepted`.** This is partly a SKOS/data question (Baker's and Davis's lane) but it turns on a UFO point I must make. `completed` maps cleanly and unambiguously to the scheme notation **`Completed`** — same phase, terminal state, no judgement needed. `active`, however, has **no isomorphic phase** in `Listed / Offered / Accepted / Exchanged / Completed`: "active" is not a member of *this* partition at all — it is a member of the *participant*-status partition (`Proposed | Invited | Active | Removed`, ODR-0007 §Context; the `opda:ParticipantStatusScheme`), a **different bearer** (the role-play, not the Relator). That the removed exemplar triples borrowed `active` onto a Transaction is precisely the bearer/partition confusion the deploy-fix `4905c6b` was right to remove. Because "active" denotes only "the transaction is live/ongoing" and the live phases of the transaction partition are Offered → Accepted → Exchanged, mapping it requires a *decision the source data does not license*. The honest mapping is the weakest commitment consistent with "ongoing": **`Offered`** (the transaction is on the market and in play) — and it must be surfaced as an operator-confirmable mapping, not asserted, because picking `Accepted` or `Exchanged` would *invent* milestone information the exemplar never carried. This is the S026 latent/unexercised-disposition discipline the council already enforces (session-032 §Queen's adjudication — "modelling a per-role machine the source lacks is exactly the speculative over-modelling the Council forbids"). **Cross-talk — to Davis (DA):** here the DA's parsimony cuts *my* way and I yield to it — we must not over-specify `active` into a precise milestone. Map `completed→Completed` firmly; mark `active→Offered` as a provisional, operator-ratifiable mapping. To Baker: the cross-scheme leakage of `active` (a participant-status concept landing on a Transaction) is exactly the co-reference hazard the one-primary-scheme IC guards against — the fix is bearer-discipline, not a new mapping concept.

**Guizzardi — Q4 (is a dereferenceable page for an inert documentary `opda:ufoCategory` annotation ontologically coherent?). Verdict: AFFIRM. Ballot: FOR.** It is not only coherent — it is the *correct* treatment, and the apparent tension dissolves once you separate two senses of "first-class". An `owl:AnnotationProperty` carries **no model-theoretic consequence** (OWL 2 *Structural Specification*, W3C Rec 2012, §10.1 / §5.5 — annotations are not part of the logical axiom set and license no entailment); that is its *inferential* status. Dereferenceability is an entirely orthogonal, *documentary* property — it is about whether an IRI, when looked up, returns a description (the Linked Data "follow-your-nose" expectation; and OPDA's own ADR-0044 ontology-as-web-pages). A term being inert to the reasoner says nothing about whether it deserves an HTTP-resolvable description; conflating "inferentially inert" with "not worth dereferencing" is a category error. Indeed the *whole content* of `opda:ufoCategory` is a documentary claim — the predicate's own `scopeNote` is a three-part DOLCE-lineage disclosure (Masolo et al., WonderWeb D18, 2003) stating the categories are UFO's and descend from DOLCE's Quality/Quale/Region (`opda-annotations.ttl:124`). A claim that is *constitutively documentary* is exactly the kind of resource that *most* needs a dereferenceable page: the page is where the disclosure lives so a consumer can discover that this facet is "UFO-informed, not UFO-grounded" and never reasoned over. ODR-0031 already mandates this — Rule 4: "Keep `/pdtf/ufoCategory` … as **read-side SPARQL projections** over the annotation graph … **Ship the ODR-0030 Rule 7(b) three-part DOLCE disclosure** … badges read 'classified-under', never 'is-a'" (ODR-0031 §Rules R4). The page is coherent **provided** it is a *read-side projection that renders the inertness*, not a presentation that dresses the facet as a reasoned `rdf:type`. The brief's verified defect — that `scripts/ontology-model.mjs` extracts everything *but* `owl:AnnotationProperty`, so `/pdtf/ufoCategory` 404s — is therefore a *broken promise of ODR-0031 R4*, not evidence that the page is scope-creep. The 404 should be fixed. **Cross-talk — to Gandon (dereferenceability / OWL annotation):** I expect Gandon and I to converge — an annotation property is `owl:AnnotationProperty` precisely so it dereferences-without-entailing, which is the Linked-Data ideal: a resolvable IRI that adds documentation and zero logical commitment. To the latent Davis "scope-creep" worry: the discipline that keeps it from creep is the *honesty framing* (ODR-0030 Rule 7 "UFO-informed", badges "classified-under") — the page documents a design-time governance record, it does not assert a grounded ontological commitment. That is the entire point of quarantining gUFO referenced-not-imported (ODR-0030 Rule 1).

**Guizzardi — Q5 (how should the site model surface annotation properties so they are not mistaken for first-class reasoned terms?). Verdict: AFFIRM (the proposed distinct `entryKind` + inert badge + disclosure-as-spine). Ballot: FOR.** This is largely an engineering/IA question (Gandon's lane) but it has a UFO-honesty dimension I will speak to and otherwise defer. The three proposed devices are each load-bearing and I endorse all three. (i) A **distinct `entryKind`** for annotation properties is the machine-readable analogue of the endurant-vs-documentary distinction: a `Substance Kind` class, an `owl:ObjectProperty`, and an inert `owl:AnnotationProperty` are *ontologically different sorts of thing* and must not share the rendering that implies "reasoned term" — the generator currently extracts `owl:Class`/`owl:ObjectProperty`/`owl:DatatypeProperty`/`sh:NodeShape`/`skos:Concept`/`skos:ConceptScheme` and an annotation property is none of those, so a new kind is the honest fix (brief finding B). (ii) An **inert badge** rendering "classified-under, never is-a" is mandated verbatim by ODR-0031 R4 and realises the ODR-0030 Rule 7 anti-overclaim doctrine — the badge is the UI surface of "UFO-informed, not UFO-grounded with guarantees". (iii) **The disclosure as the page's spine** is correct because, as argued in Q4, the disclosure *is* the resource's content — an inert facet's page should foreground *why it is inert and what authority it defers to* (the DOLCE lineage, the gUFO `closeMatch`, the quarantine), not bury it. One UFO caveat for the spine: the page must present the value-axis as the **9-concept `opda:UFOCategoryScheme`** (each concept carrying its OntoClean signature + gUFO `closeMatch`, `opda-annotations.ttl:32–117`), and must **not** re-present the register-deference scheme axis (the `"Quale-in-Region"` / `"Phase label"` strings) as "UFO doing categorial work" — that axis was split off and relabelled by ODR-0030 Rule 2 / ODR-0031 R1, and showing it as a UFO category on this page would re-introduce the double-duty defect ODR-0031 corrected. **Cross-talk — to Gandon:** I defer to Gandon on the projection mechanics (SPARQL-over-annotation-graph so the page cannot entrench, per ODR-0031 R4) and ask only that the rendered facet stays bound to the `skos:notation` of a `UFOCategoryScheme` concept so the page's "category" is always the governed resource, never a free string. To Baker: the `entryKind` should let the page link the badge through to the `opda:UFOCategoryScheme` concept (a real SKOS resource with `skos:definition` + `closeMatch`), which is the dereferenceable home of the alignment Baker would want reused, not duplicated.
