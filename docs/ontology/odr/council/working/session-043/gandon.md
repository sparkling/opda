**Gandon — Q2 (reuse `TransactionStatusScheme` vs mint a new coarse scheme).** **Verdict: REVISE. Ballot: ABSTAIN** (out-of-lens; defer to the transaction experts on grain). The narrow linked-data principle I can ground: **reuse-before-mint** is the web-architecture default — Berners-Lee rule 4 ("include links to other URIs") and the AWWW "do not mint a new URI for a thing that already has one" maxim. A scheme that is *already emitted and dereferenceable* (`TransactionStatusScheme`: Listed/Offered/Accepted/Exchanged/Completed) has a lower web-cost than a fresh mint, all else equal — minting duplicate concept IRIs for the same conceptual axis is exactly the proliferation the council warned against for gUFO in ODR-0031 R1. So *if* the experts find the five existing notations fit the axis, reuse; mint only if the grain genuinely differs (a real new concept deserves a real new URI). I do not adjudicate which. Cross-talk to **Baker**: this is your SKOS-grain call — `skos:broader` to coarsen an existing scheme is cheaper than a parallel scheme, and keeps one dereferenceable concept tree rather than two competing ones.

**Gandon — Q4 (should the inert `opda:ufoCategory` dereference at `/pdtf/ufoCategory`?). Verdict: AFFIRM. Ballot: FOR.** Yes — and ODR-0030 Rule 7(b) + ODR-0031 §Confirmation **already settle it**; this question is *whether the corpus honours a standing decision*, not whether to take a new one. Two principles, held apart, both point the same way:

1. *Retrieval layer (Berners-Lee, four rules; httpRange-14).* `opda:ufoCategory` is a minted `https://opda.org.uk/pdtf/` HTTP URI (`opda-annotations.ttl:118`). Rule 2 — "use HTTP URIs so people can look them up" — and rule 3 — "when someone looks up a URI, provide useful information" — are unconditional for any minted name. A 200-class URI is *httpRange-14 class-of-its-own*: it denotes an information-or-non-information resource for which a representation must be served. **A minted opda: URI that 404s is a broken promise** ("Cool URIs don't 404", TAG/W3C 2008). The brief confirms it 404s today: the generator (`scripts/ontology-model.mjs` L123–185) extracts `owl:Class`/`owl:ObjectProperty`/`owl:DatatypeProperty`/`sh:NodeShape`/`skos:Concept`/`skos:ConceptScheme` and **not `owl:AnnotationProperty`**, so `ufoCategory` is absent from `allResources()` and the page is missing. ODR-0030 R7(b) ("a dereferenceable `/pdtf/ufoCategory` term page") and ODR-0031 §Confirmation (it names the term page as a confirmation criterion) are therefore *literally unmet in the shipped site*. The deploy-fix `hasResource()` guard (`src/lib/ontology-model.ts:231`) is a correct *interim* — it stops shapes dangling into a 404 — but it papers the gap; its own doc-comment concedes "the predicate's own dereferenceable disclosure page … is a separate annotation-graph surfacing, tracked for the model generator." That tracked work is exactly Q4. The promise was made when the URI was minted and re-affirmed by two ODRs; the only honest discharge is to serve the page.

2. *Model-theoretic layer (OWL 2 §10.1 / §5.5).* Dereferenceability is **orthogonal** to logical force. An `owl:AnnotationProperty` "has no effect on the logical … consequences of an ontology" (OWL 2 Structural Specification §10.1) — it is inert under any regime, not merely under ODR-0029's accidental shallowness (ODR-0031 R2; ADR-0045 driver "inertness intrinsic, not contingent"). The crucial point against DA Davis: **inertness is a reason the page is safe, not a reason to withhold it.** `rdfs:label` is itself an `owl:AnnotationProperty` (RDF Schema 1.1 §5.1; OWL 2 §10.1 lists it among the built-in annotation properties) — and it dereferences at `http://www.w3.org/2000/01/rdf-schema#label`, serving a real, useful representation, while contributing exactly zero model-theoretic consequence. The web has dereferenced inert annotation terms for two decades; that an opda: term carries no logical force is no bar to its retrieval. Withholding the page would make OPDA's own inert predicate *less* of a first-class web citizen than `rdfs:label` — an inconsistency with no warrant.

Cross-talk to **Davis (DA)**: I take your honesty concern seriously and I think it is *fully* answerable without a 404. You argue an inert facet must not "masquerade as a first-class /pdtf term" and that a guarded non-link is the honest move. But the masquerade risk is a *presentation* defect, not a *dereference* defect — and httpRange-14 already separates "what the URI denotes" from "what representation you serve." The honest answer is **dereference + disclose**, not **withhold**: serve the page, and make the page itself say, loudly and machine-readably, "annotation property; no logical force; never reasoned" (that is Q5). A 404 is not honesty — it is silence, and silence on a URI you minted and promised twice (R7b) reads as breakage, not candour. Your "guarded non-link" is right for *edges into* the term from shapes (the `hasResource()` guard already does this correctly); it is wrong as the *terminal* answer for the term's own URI. The disclosure ODR-0030 R7(b) mandates is *the* mechanism that converts your masquerade worry into an honest, self-describing page. So I read us as 90% aligned: guard the inbound links (done), and serve an honestly-badged page at the URI itself (Q4/Q5). Cross-talk to **Kendall (Queen)**: this needs no new decision — it is an execution gap against R7(b) + ODR-0031 §Confirmation; the disposition is "implement the standing rule," and the *how* is Q5.

**Gandon — Q5 (HOW to surface annotation properties without their reading as first-class reasoned terms). Verdict: REVISE. Ballot: FOR** (with the amendment below). The mechanism, grounded:

**Amendment (the exact change).** Add a seventh resource kind to the extraction + the typed accessor, distinct from the six reasoned kinds:

- *Generator* (`scripts/ontology-model.mjs`): add a query over `GRAPH ?g { ?p a owl:AnnotationProperty }` reading from `opda-annotations.ttl`, carrying `rdfs:label`, `rdfs:comment`, `dct:source`, and — load-bearing — the `skos:scopeNote` (the three-part DOLCE disclosure, `opda-annotations.ttl:124`) and the linked value-space scheme (`opda:UFOCategoryScheme`). This is the one graph the script does **not** currently read for predicates; reading it is graph-transparent and does not touch the reasoned union (ODR-0004 §3a stays green — annotations carry no `sh:`/`owl:Class`).
- *Accessor* (`src/lib/ontology-model.ts`): a new `entryKind: 'annotation'` in `AnyResource`, surfaced by `allResources()`, so `/pdtf/[...name].astro` (its `getStaticPaths` reads `allResources()`) emits `/pdtf/ufoCategory` automatically and `hasResource('ufoCategory')` returns `true` (retiring the special-case guard's *raison d'être* for this term, though the guard stays correct for genuinely-page-less predicates).
- *Page* (a new `AnnotationDetail.astro`, dispatched in `[...name].astro`): the page **spine is the DOLCE disclosure** (ODR-0030 R7(b)(i)–(iii): the categories are UFO's; UFO's quality categories descend from DOLCE's Quality/Quale/Region — Masolo et al., WonderWeb D18, 2003; the majority of tagged properties fall under that apparatus), with a prominent inert badge.

**The badge wording, grounded in the spec, not in anonymous "best practice."** The badge must read **"Annotation property — documentary; carries no logical consequence; never reasoned over"**, citing OWL 2 §10.1 inline. This is the §10.1 fact stated plainly, and it is the exact analogue of ODR-0031 R4's class-level rule "badges read 'classified-under', never 'is-a'." For the *value* relationship, the page must show the `closeMatch`→gUFO edge as **a mapping that never reaches the reasoner** (SKOS Reference §10, W3C Rec 2009 — `skos:closeMatch` is a *mapping* property asserting documented similarity, not equivalence/subclassing; it has no entailment into the OPDA TBox), reinforcing ODR-0031 R2's "absolute red line."

**Distinct `entryKind` — yes; do not overload an existing kind.** Folding annotation properties into the `'property'` kind (alongside object/datatype properties) would be the category confusion Davis rightly fears: object/datatype properties *do* carry domain/range and *can* be SHACL-keyed; an annotation property cannot (OWL 2 §5.5 — annotation properties are disjoint from the logical property hierarchy; an axiom annotation is not a class/property axiom). A distinct `entryKind` makes the page template's *structure* — disclosure-spine + inert-badge + non-reasoned-mapping — carry the honesty, so the surface cannot drift back into reading as a reasoned term. This is the "engineering act IS the ontological act": the typed `entryKind` *is* the machine-checkable statement "this is documentary."

Cross-talk to **Davis (DA)**: this is the constructive form of your dissent — the distinct `entryKind` + the explicit "no logical force, never reasoned" badge is precisely the anti-masquerade guarantee you want, realised *on a served page* rather than via a 404. We get your honesty and Berners-Lee's "don't 404" simultaneously; the disclosure is not decoration, it is the page's reason to exist. Cross-talk to **Guizzardi**: the page is where your UFO categories finally dereference as *OPDA's* documented view (the `skos:definition` OntoClean signatures already shipped in `opda:UFOCategoryScheme`, `opda-annotations.ttl:39–116`) without OPDA ever asserting `gufo:` axioms it cannot warrant — the `closeMatch` is shown, the `rdf:type` is never minted. Cross-talk to **Kendall (Queen)**: the read-side projection requirement (ODR-0031 R4 — pages render off either a string or a concept value, "so they cannot entrench") is satisfied by reading the annotation graph through the same committed-model pipeline; note for the disposition that ADR-0045 §Confirmation already flags "regenerating `src/data/ontology-model.json` … deferred (needs `make build-data`)" — so this Q5 change rides the *same* regen, and the byte-identity/three-graph gates must stay green across it (the annotation-graph read adds no triple to any reasoned graph).
