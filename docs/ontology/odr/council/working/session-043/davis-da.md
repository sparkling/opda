# Davis (DA) — Session 043 position

Role: Devil's Advocate. Lens: publish-first, scope-discipline, minimal vocabularies, deployment realism — BBC `/programmes/` ontology (2009); `data.gov.uk` linked-data design patterns; "don't mint terms you don't need." I attack both "mint it now" framings. A guarded non-link and a SPARQL projection that returns nothing are *honest signals*, not defects to paper over.

---

**Davis (DA) — Q1 (direct lifecycle-phase property vs reified milestones).**
**Verdict: AFFIRM** the no-mint reading (phase is adequately modelled by reified `opda:Milestone` events). **Ballot: AGAINST** any motion to add a direct lifecycle-phase property to `opda:Transaction`. *Rationale + citation:* The BBC `/programmes/` ontology shipped a Brand→Series→Episode→Version spine where state ("a programme is now broadcastable") was *derived from* the events that had occurred, never stamped as a redundant parallel string — because a denormalised status field drifts the moment an event lands and the field is not updated. That is the deployment lesson I am bound to argue from. Here the verified facts make the case stronger than mine usually is: `simple-transaction-with-milestones.ttl` already reifies the full chain (`opda:Milestone`/`prov:Activity`: instruction→offerAccepted→exchange→completion→registration, each with `prov:atTime` or `prov:startedAtTime`/`prov:endedAtTime` and a `prov:Plan` companion). The maximal phase fact — *which phase is current* — is `(SELECT the latest Milestone by prov:atTime)`, a one-line SPARQL projection. A `opda:transactionPhase "exchanged"` literal alongside that is precisely the denormalisation that drifts: two writable surfaces for one fact, no invariant tying them. The brief's own note is the clincher — `chain-of-transactions.ttl` and `lease-extension-transaction.ttl` carry **no milestones**. For *those*, an invented "active" is **fabricated state**: the data does not record a marketing/offer/exchange event, so a projection returning nothing is the *honest* answer, and synthesising "active" asserts a lifecycle position the source never attested. The whole `4905c6b` fix was the removal of exactly such borrowed `opda:status` tokens; re-minting a transaction-scoped phase property would re-import the defect under a new name. *Cross-talk (rebut Guizzardi):* if Guizzardi argues from S032 that the Milestone-Phase is *categorially* borne by the Transaction Relator and therefore wants it materialised as a property, I answer that S032's own ruling already located that Phase **on reified milestone events** ("a milestone *transition* is represented once as a lifecycle Phase change … and **referenced (not re-modelled)** as a `prov:Activity`", ODR-0007 §Rules "Lifecycle/provenance seam"). The category being real does not license a second materialisation of it; UFO tells you *what kind of thing* the phase is, not that you must denormalise it onto a string. **Disposition: HOLD.** I will not pretend the Relator-bears-Milestone-Phase categorial point is wrong — it is right — but it does not reach my conclusion. **Named RE-OPEN TRIGGER:** a verified consumer query that cannot be answered by projection over reified milestones *and* an exemplar where milestones exist to project from — i.e. a real bottleneck, measured, not anticipated. Absent both, mint nothing.

**Davis (DA) — Q2 (if a property: reuse `TransactionStatusScheme` vs mint a coarse scheme; confirm bearer).**
**Verdict: REJECT** the mint-a-new-scheme option; **conditional REVISE** if Q1 is overridden. **Ballot: AGAINST** minting any new coarse scheme; **ABSTAIN** on reuse-vs-nothing (it is moot under my Q1 AGAINST). *Amendment, stated exactly (binds only if the council overrides Q1 and adds a property):* "Bind the property to the existing `opda:MilestoneScheme` (`opda-vocabularies.ttl:239` — the ratified instruction→offerAccepted→exchange→completion→registration Method/plan codes), with bearer = `opda:Transaction` (the Relator, per S032). Do **not** mint a new scheme, and do **not** bind `opda:TransactionStatusScheme` until its `skos:definition` is corrected." *Rationale + citation:* "Don't mint terms you don't need" (the `data.gov.uk` cookbook discipline I authored against) bars a *third* status scheme when two already sit unused — `opda:TransactionStatusScheme` (Listed/Offered/Accepted/Exchanged/Completed) and `opda:MilestoneScheme`. But reuse here exposes a live defect the panel must not ratify around: `opda:TransactionStatusScheme`'s shipped `skos:definition` reads *"Phase labels for the lifecycle of a Transaction **Substance Kind**"* (`opda-vocabularies.ttl:365`) — and `opda:ParticipantStatusScheme` likewise says *"lifecycle of a Participant **Substance Kind**"* (`:281`). S032 (5–0–0) and ODR-0007 §Rules fix the Transaction as a **Relator**, not a Substance Kind, and assign it the **Milestone-Phase**, *not* a "TransactionStatus" — these schemes' own definitions contradict the bearer ruling the brief asks me to "confirm." Reusing a scheme whose definition mis-categorises its bearer would launder that contradiction into a new property. *Cross-talk (rebut Kendall):* Kendall, in S032, opened the per-role-lifecycle attack and **withdrew on the data** — the corpus carries `participantStatus` as one un-role-discriminated field, zero per-role enums. That withdrawal is precedent *against* enriching transaction status now: the same SET-test applies — show genuine *definitional divergence* in the source before minting, and there is none (the data-dictionary `status` enum is the only surface). If Kendall now argues FIBO-style for a richer transaction lifecycle scheme, the answer is her own S032 withdrawal condition: model what the source records, not the conveyancing world's richer mental model. **Disposition: HOLD.** **Named RE-OPEN TRIGGER:** correction of the two schemes' `skos:definition` strings to name the Relator/role-play bearer (per S032) — until that ships, no property may bind either scheme, because binding propagates a mis-categorisation.

**Davis (DA) — Q3 (exemplar value-mapping: what do the removed `active`/`completed` map to).**
**Verdict: REVISE.** **Ballot: FOR** the following mapping *only as documentary back-fill, not as a re-minted property.* *Amendment, stated exactly:* "(a) `simple-transaction-with-milestones` and `lease-extension-transaction`: the removed `completed` maps to **the presence of a `completion`/registration `opda:Milestone`** (already reified in the simple exemplar) or, for the lease extension, to the `opda:LeaseExtensionEvent`'s `prov:atTime "2024-09-30"` completion timestamp it already carries — i.e. `completed` is *projected from events already present*, mapped to nothing new. (b) `chain-of-transactions`: the removed per-Transaction `active` maps to **no canonical value** — it is dropped, because those Transactions reify no milestone and the data attests no phase; the file's surviving `opda:chainStatus "active"` (`chain-of-transactions.ttl`, on the `opda:TransactionChain`, *not* the Transaction) is a *derived* chain-level rollup, explicitly defined in that file's `skos:scopeNote` as 'any-blocked → chain-blocked', and is the only place 'active' legitimately lives." *Rationale + citation:* This is the publish-first discipline applied to migration — you map a removed value to the *fact already in the graph*, never to a freshly minted slot, because the latter recreates the redundancy you just deleted. The asymmetry is the whole point: `completed` has a real event to project from; `active` does not, so honouring "active" for the chain Transactions would fabricate state (cf. my Q1). The brief verifies the removed tokens "match **neither** existing scheme's notations exactly" (the comment said `proposed/active/exchanged/completed`; `TransactionStatusScheme` is `Listed/Offered/Accepted/Exchanged/Completed`) — that mismatch is *evidence the tokens were never governed*, which argues for projection-or-drop, not for stretching a scheme to fit ungoverned strings. *Cross-talk (rebut Moreau):* Moreau will rightly insist that a completion is a `prov:Activity`/`prov:InstantaneousEvent` with `prov:atTime`, and that this is where "completed" belongs — I agree entirely, and that is exactly my mapping (a): `completed` ⟶ the reified milestone's time, not a status literal. Where I go further than PROV alone: for the chain Transactions Moreau's apparatus has *nothing to attach to* (no activity reified), so the honest PROV-shaped answer is silence, not a synthesised event. **Disposition: HOLD.** **Named RE-OPEN TRIGGER:** if the WG supplies source data that records a transaction-level phase *independently of* any milestone event (a genuine standalone `status` observation in the PDTF feed), then `completed`/`active` get a governed home — until then they project or drop.

**Davis (DA) — Q4 (should inert `opda:ufoCategory` dereference at `/pdtf/ufoCategory`).**
**Verdict: REVISE** (dereference, but *not* as a first-class `/pdtf` term page). **Ballot: AGAINST** a `/pdtf/ufoCategory` page rendered by the same template/`entryKind` as reasoned terms; **FOR** a disclosure surfacing that is visibly *not* first-class. *Rationale + citation:* My genuine opposition is to the category confusion. `opda:ufoCategory` is `owl:AnnotationProperty` (`opda-annotations.ttl:118`) — by OWL 2 §10.1 it carries **no model-theoretic consequence**; it is inert by construction, the reasoner never sees it. The data.gov.uk linked-data design pattern I argue from is that a dereferenceable `/{vocab}/{term}` URI is a *publishing promise*: "this is a term of the vocabulary you reason and validate with." `/pdtf/ufoCategory` rendered identically to `/pdtf/Transaction` makes exactly the promise the artefact cannot keep — it presents an inert provenance facet as a first-class reasoned predicate. The deploy-fix already encoded the honest signal: `hasResource()` (`src/lib/ontology-model.ts:231`) *deliberately* excludes `ufoCategory` from the resource set so shape links don't dangle, and the code comment (`:222-229`) states plainly that the disclosure page "is a **separate** annotation-graph surfacing" — the engineers already drew the first-class/inert line. **However**, I do not get to REJECT outright, because ODR-0030 **Rule 7(b)** and ODR-0031 §Confirmation are *binding records* that mandate the dereferenceable disclosure, and ODR-0031 R4 already fixes the safe mechanism: a **read-side SPARQL projection over the annotation graph**, never an entrenched first-class entry. A disclosure page that leads with "UFO-informed, not UFO-grounded," badges "classified-under" (never "is-a"), and is built as a projection is *not* the category confusion I oppose — it is the honest non-first-class surfacing the deploy-fix comment already anticipated. So my opposition is satisfied by *how*, not *whether* (→ Q5). *Cross-talk (rebut Gandon):* Gandon will argue from linked-data orthodoxy that *every* published predicate SHOULD dereference (httpRange-14 / "follow your nose"), and that an `owl:AnnotationProperty` is still a first-class RDF resource entitled to its URI. I concede the URI must resolve — that is why I REVISE rather than REJECT. But "resolves" ≠ "rendered as a reasoned term." Gandon's own *Semantic Web for the Working Ontologist* (3e, 2020) is meticulous that annotation properties sit *outside* the entailment regime; the page must *say so* on its face, or dereferenceability becomes overclaim. A 404 (the status quo) under-delivers on R7b; a clone of the `Transaction` page over-claims; the projection-with-disclosure is the only honest middle. **Disposition: WITHDRAW** the REJECT-the-page push. *Verbatim rationale that won me over:* ODR-0030 Rule 7(b) + ODR-0031 §Confirmation are co-signed binding records mandating the disclosure page, and ODR-0031 R4's "read-side SPARQL projection … render whether the value is a string or a concept (so they cannot entrench)" removes the entrenchment risk that was my sole objection. I hold no live dissent on *whether* it dereferences. **Named RE-OPEN TRIGGER (carried to Q5):** if the page ships rendered by the first-class term template / a generic `entryKind` indistinguishable from reasoned terms, or without the Rule 7(b) DOLCE disclosure as its spine, the category confusion I oppose has shipped and I revert to REJECT.

**Davis (DA) — Q5 (how should the site model surface annotation properties).**
**Verdict: AFFIRM** ODR-0031 R4's read-side-projection design, **REVISE** to nail the engineering invariant. **Ballot: FOR**, with the amendment below. *Amendment, stated exactly:* "(1) `scripts/ontology-model.mjs` (which today extracts only `owl:Class`/`owl:ObjectProperty`/`owl:DatatypeProperty`/`sh:NodeShape`/`skos:Concept`/`skos:ConceptScheme`, lines 125–180) gains a **separate** extraction for `owl:AnnotationProperty` that lands in a **distinct collection**, surfaced through a new `entryKind: 'annotation'` in `src/lib/ontology-model.ts` (the union at `:201–215` and `allResources` at `:208`) — it MUST NOT be folded into the `'property'` arm (`:211–212`) that carries reasoned object/datatype properties, because that is the exact conflation Q4 forbids. (2) The page template for `entryKind: 'annotation'` leads with an **inert badge** ('annotation — not reasoned, OWL 2 §10.1') and renders the **Rule 7(b) three-part DOLCE disclosure as the page spine**, not as a footnote. (3) It is built as a read-side projection over `opda-annotations.ttl` per ODR-0031 R4 — the page renders whether the value is a string or a concept, so it cannot entrench a minted IRI. (4) `hasResource()` and the link-guard stay as-is for *reasoned*-graph link integrity; the annotation entries are a parallel set, so shape `sh:path`/`sh:targetSubjectsOf` links still do not dangle." *Rationale + citation:* This is minimal-vocabularies applied to the *site model*: a new `entryKind` is the smallest change that lets one inert facet dereference without teaching the reasoned-term renderer to lie about it. It is also exactly what the existing code comment (`ontology-model.ts:222-229`) flags as "tracked for the model generator" — I am specifying the tracked work, not inventing scope. The badge + disclosure-as-spine is the published equivalent of the BBC ontology's discipline of never letting a derived/advisory annotation render as a core class. *Cross-talk (rebut Baker):* Baker (SKOS governance) may push for the `ufoCategory` value to be promoted to a minted `opda:UFOCategoryScheme` concept-IRI so the page renders rich SKOS structure (definitions, `closeMatch`). I push back: ODR-0031 R7(b) leaves the *predicate range* (string+`sh:in` vs concept-IRI) an **open operator call**, and R4 requires the page to render *either* — so the site model must NOT assume the IRI form. Building the projection to require a minted concept-as-value would prejudge the operator's residual and entrench the mint the session-040 DA (Cagle) holds a live trigger against. The `UFOCategoryScheme` concepts already ship (`opda-annotations.ttl:33–116`) carrying the OntoClean signatures and gUFO `closeMatch`; the page can *project* those by following `opda:ufoCategory`'s value or its `sh:in` set without the predicate's range being promoted. **Disposition: WITHDRAW** any objection to surfacing annotation properties at all. *Verbatim rationale that won me over:* surfacing is mandated (R7b/§Confirmation) and the read-side-projection + distinct-`entryKind` design makes the surfacing honest and non-entrenching, which is the entirety of what I was defending. **Named RE-OPEN TRIGGER:** if the implementation folds annotation properties into the reasoned-`property` `entryKind`, or renders them through the first-class term template without the inert badge + DOLCE-disclosure spine, the surfacing has become the overclaim — revert to Q4's REJECT.
