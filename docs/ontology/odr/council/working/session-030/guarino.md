# Session 030 (R3) — Nicola Guarino (OntoClean / DOLCE mereology) working vote

- **Voice:** Nicola Guarino (ISTC-CNR). **Lens:** OntoClean (Guarino & Welty 2002/2009) — Rigidity, Identity (+I/+O), Unity (+U), Dependence; DOLCE parthood + *features* (holes, rooms).
- **Frame:** ODR-0023 **R3**, directing-authority override. "No gates" = no deferral-by-calendar. I decide on the **merits**. I set the part-promotion IC test in S026; I now apply it without conjunct (b) operating as a *gate*, but conjunct (b) survives as an **ontological fact about the data**, not as a scheduling excuse.
- **The OntoClean question, stated precisely:** A +U part earns its **own Kind** only if it carries a **data-realisable** identity criterion (an IC a SHACL key or a co-reference predicate can actually *check* on the corpus). A +U part that is **−O-on-the-data** — a part whose individuation is exhausted by "the n-th sub-region of its bearer" — is a DOLCE **feature** (a room, a hole, a bump): existentially dependent on its host, individuated *by* its host, and therefore correctly a **Quale-bundle of its bearer**, not a free-standing endurant. The whole of R3 turns on which of these Room is, and on whether Building's genuine +O is *exercised* or merely *latent*.

---

## Q1 — `opda:Room`: does a +U feature with no data-realisable identity EARN a Kind, or is it a Quale-bundle on its bearer?

**REJECT** (do not mint `opda:Room` as a Kind; affirm it as a **by-value Quale-bundle** on its bearer).

A room is the textbook DOLCE **feature**: +U (it has a determinate spatial unity — a bounded volume), but its identity is **parasitic on its host** — "the master bedroom of *this* property" is individuated by the property plus a label, never by anything intrinsic to the room. I checked the corpus directly: the repeating group at `/propertyPack/buildInformation/floorplan/rooms[]` (59-hawkley-gardens) carries `roomName`, `description`, `length`, `width`, `units`, `dimensionDetails` — and **no positional token, no stable per-room identifier, no UPRN-equivalent**; ordering is bare array position. ODR-0024 R10 already adjudicated `roomName` as **−I** (non-rigid label, "Bedroom 2" can re-attach next survey). So Room's would-be IC is **−O on the data**: there is nothing to re-identify a room *by* across two surveys except its bearer and a mutable name. That is precisely a feature, not a Sortal — it fails the OntoClean +O test, so it does **not** earn a Kind. It is a measured-Quale bundle (`length`/`width` Qualia + a `roomName` label) borne by its host — modelled as **by-value structure** (the `opda:MonetaryAmount` shape, ODR-0024 R3, is the precedent: a value-structure node with a node-shape, *no* class-level identity). Minting `opda:Room` would assert a re-identifiable endurant the data cannot witness — an implicit-identity defect of exactly the kind ODR-0005 was built to cure, run in reverse.

## Q2 — `opda:Building`: its +O IC is genuine — is it *exercised* in the corpus today, or latent?

**AFFIRM** the IC is genuine (+O); **but the honest finding is: LATENT, not exercised** — so do not mint a `Building` Kind now.

I stand by S026: Building's +O is **real**, and the witness is ODR-0005 §3a hard-case 4 ("Replacement") — a built structure can persist when `opda:Property` identity changes (knock-down-rebuild without registry discontinuity preserves Property; conversely the *structure* can be replaced while the plot/Property persists). That divergence proves a built structure carries a **segregated unity-and-persistence IC** distinct from Property's spatial-material IC. Conjunct (a) of my test remains **MET**. But I must be honest under "decide on the merits": that IC is **typeable but not re-identified anywhere in today's corpus**. The data has `builtForm` and `numberOfFloors` as flat Qualia *on* `opda:Property` (verified: `opda-property.ttl` lines 152, 464); there is **no `opda:Building` instance, no cross-Property building-sharing assertion, no `prov:wasDerivedFrom` chain over a structure** in any exemplar (verified: zero hits). A genuine-but-latent IC earns a Kind only once something **realises** it — a building shared across Properties, or a structure re-identified across dated surveys. Today nothing does. So: **typeable, not yet re-identifiable**; the +O is genuine and dormant. Mint nothing; preserve the zero-cost re-home path (the dormant `PropertyTypeScheme`→subclass binding, S026 Consequences).

## Q3 — mereology: temporary parthood? transitivity across differing ICs?

**AFFIRM** (with two OntoClean cautions that are *themselves* reasons not to mint now).

(1) **Temporary parthood is real here and is an argument against a naive Kind.** A room is a part of a building *at a time* (renovation merges/splits rooms; ODR-0005 §3a subdivision/merger splits the host). DOLCE handles this with **time-indexed parthood** (`P(x,y,t)`), which a flat by-value bundle on the current bearer models for free — whereas a free-standing `opda:Room` Kind would *demand* the temporal-parthood machinery (OWL-Time, reified part-events) to stay honest, machinery the corpus gives no reason to carry. (2) **Parthood is NOT transitive across differing ICs.** Room ⊑part Building ⊑part Property does **not** license Room ⊑part Property as a single relation: the Room↔Building parthood is feature-of-host (functional/spatial unity), while Building↔Property crosses a Kind boundary (physical-structure IC vs spatial-material-plot IC). Collapsing them is the classic OntoClean transitivity fallacy (Varzi). This is decisive for Q1/Q2: the part-chain is **heterogeneous**, so each link must be modelled in its own register — a further reason Room is a Quale-bundle (homogeneous with its host's Qualia) and Building, if ever minted, takes an explicit non-transitive `opda:structureOf` relation, never an inherited `hasPart`.

## Q4 — `length` / `width` / `roomName`: OntoClean typing

**AFFIRM** (this is settled by ODR-0024 R10 and I confirm the OntoClean grounds).

`length` and `width` are **DOLCE Qualities** inhering in the room-feature, valued in a length **Quale** (a Region) — directly analogous to the `builtForm` Quale-in-Region pattern (ODR-0008 §Q5a row 1). `units` ("meters") is the **metric of the quality-space**, not a separate attribute — it co-types with the magnitude (the same coupling `opda:MonetaryAmount` enforces between `amount` and `currency`, ODR-0024 R3). `roomName` is **−I, −R** — a non-rigid label / mode-of-presentation, NOT an identity principle (ODR-0024 R10, affirmed). None of the three is +O; none individuates; therefore the cluster `{length, width, units, roomName}` is a **Quale-bundle**, and the correct home is a **by-value structure node** borne by the host (no class-level identity), with `length`/`width` as `xsd:decimal` magnitudes over a length Quale and `roomName` as a bare non-rigid `xsd:string` label. `dimensionDetails`/`description` collapse to `opda:disclosureDetail` per ODR-0024 R2 (free-text tails carry no IC).

## Q5 — SHACL identity-key (if any) + realisation

**REJECT a `dash:uniqueValueForClass` identity key** for Room or Building (there is no IC for one to enforce); **AFFIRM** a structural by-value SHACL node-shape.

There is **no identity key to mint**, and asserting one would be the anti-pattern ODR-0005 names explicitly (a key inert when its discriminator is absent; here the discriminator never exists — rooms have no UPRN-analogue, and `roomName` is −I so `dash:uniqueValueForClass roomName` would fire false violations on two "Bedroom 2"s and miss a renamed room). Realisation = a **by-value structure** exactly on the `opda:MonetaryAmount` template (ODR-0024 R3): an anonymous (or skolemised) node bearing required `length`/`width` (`sh:datatype xsd:decimal`, both `sh:minCount 1`), `units` `sh:in` a unit scheme (reuse before mint — `UnitOfAreaScheme` exists for *area*; a length-unit scheme is the small honest add, not `UnitOfAreaScheme` overloaded), and an optional `−I` `roomName`. The bearer is `opda:Property` (the floorplan hangs off the property today; there is no `Building` node to hang it on, and inventing one to be the bearer would mint the very latent Kind Q2 declines). The repeating group `rooms[]` becomes an **unordered `sh:node` collection** on `opda:Property` via one object property (`opda:hasRoomDimension` or equivalent), value-typed to the structure shape — **NOT** a `Room` Kind, **NOT** flat indexed datatype properties (`room1Length`… would be the namespace-landmine R4 warned against).

## What I DO mint, with its identity criterion — and would-earn conditions for what I don't

**MINT (this session, by-value, NO class-level identity):** a single **by-value structure** for room dimensions — the `{length, width, units, roomName}` Quale-bundle — borne by `opda:Property`, on the `opda:MonetaryAmount` template (ODR-0024 R3 precedent). **Its "identity":** *by-value / structural only* — two dimension-bundles are the same value iff their magnitudes, unit, and label coincide; they have **no endurant identity of their own** and re-identify nothing across surveys. This is the correct OntoClean home for a −O +U feature: a Quale-bundle of its bearer, not a Sortal. (This closes the only 3 of 239 uncovered leaves — `length`/`width`/`roomName` — *without* the false promise of a re-identifiable Room.)

**DO NOT MINT:** `opda:Room` (Q1 — a DOLCE feature, −O-on-data, fails the +O Kind test) and `opda:Building` (Q2 — +O genuine via the Replacement witness, but **latent**: nothing in the corpus re-identifies a structure).

**WOULD-EARN conditions (identity facts, NOT calendar gates):**

- **`opda:Building` earns its Kind** when the corpus *realises* its already-genuine +O — i.e. ANY of: (i) one structure asserted as shared across **two distinct `opda:Property` instances** (a building straddling/spanning Properties — the +O made manifest); OR (ii) a structure **re-identified across two dated surveys** via a stable structure-token (an explicit building reference that survives `roomName`/dimension change); OR (iii) a `prov:wasDerivedFrom` chain over a **structure** independent of the Property chain (Replacement materialised as data, not just as the §3a rule). On any one firing: mint `opda:Building` as a UFO Substance Kind / DOLCE PhysicalObject, IC = **structural unity + persistence independent of plot-Property identity** (the Replacement witness, now data-backed), related to `opda:Property` by an explicit **non-transitive** `opda:structureOf` predicate (Q3), with the room-dimension bundle re-homed onto it.

- **`opda:Room` earns its Kind** only on the strictly stronger event: the data acquires a **data-realisable room IC** — a stable per-room identifier (a positional/persistent token, which **BASPI5 does not have today**, ODR-0024 R10) that is +O *and* survives renaming — AND a consumer query that **re-identifies a specific room across surveys by that token** (not one that merely lists or measures rooms). Absent a +O token, no query can earn it: re-identification has nothing to bind to. Until then Room stays the by-value Quale-bundle minted above.

**Net:** REJECT Room-as-Kind; Building's IC AFFIRMED-genuine but LATENT (mint nothing); mereology AFFIRMED with non-transitivity + temporal-parthood cautions that *reinforce* by-value; Q4 typing AFFIRMED (Qualities + −I label); Q5 — no identity key, AFFIRM a by-value node-shape. The override discharged the *deliberation*; the merits say the **data carries no part-identity to promote** — so I promote the value-structure and name the exact identity facts (not dates) that would flip each Kind.
