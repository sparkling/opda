# Session 030 (R3) — Working notes: Tom Baker

> **Tom Baker** (DCMI — Dublin Core Metadata Initiative; DCMI Usage Board; metadata-standards governance; SKOS concept-scheme stewardship; namespace/URI policy). Session 030, 8-voice Full Council, `agent-fan-out`. Lens: DCMI metadata-standards discipline, vocabulary governance, namespace/URI policy, SKOS concept-scheme hygiene. **Reuse external registers via mappings before minting.**

**Standing on the merits, not the gate.** Per the convening instruction, "no gates" means **decide NOW**, not defer. My remit is the vocabulary/namespace questions the structural voices (Guarino, Davis, Knublauch, Allemang, Cagle) defer to me: is a new `opda:UnitOfLengthScheme` warranted for two leaves, or does reuse-before-mint (ODR-0011 §Rules) and QUDT-deferral (ODR-0002 §Out-of-scope) govern; and what URI/label policy binds any new term. I rule at the **register level** — the same level ODR-0011 §8a discharges A9 (b).

Inputs read (scope-bound): [session-026 guarino-r3](../session-026/guarino-r3.md) + [davis-da-r3](../session-026/davis-da-r3.md) (HOLD on Room/Building; interim datatype/structured on `opda:Property`); [ODR-0024 R3/R10](../../ODR-0024-curated-category-g-walk-dispositions.md) (`opda:MonetaryAmount` by-value structure + `opda:CurrencyScheme`; `roomName` non-rigid, `opda:Room` held); [ODR-0011 §Rules + §8a](../../ODR-0011-enumeration-vocabularies.md) (SKOS-per-enum, no floor; **reuse external register via `skos:exactMatch`/`closeMatch` where one governs the domain**; seven-category UFO framework); [ODR-0005 §3a](../../ODR-0005-property-land-identity-crux.md) (Property IC; hard-case 4 Replacement); [ODR-0008 §Q4a/§Q5a/§Q6a](../../ODR-0008-property-descriptive-attributes.md) (three-criterion class gate; range-from-data-dictionary-type; flat-default).

**Two corpus facts decide the unit question before any theory does.** I verified both in the emitted TTL:

1. **`opda:area` declined a unit scheme.** `opda-property.ttl:120-126` — `opda:area` is bare `xsd:decimal`, comment: *"the unit of measure is carried alongside in the source structure."* No `skos:` range, no QUDT mapping. The Council already ruled, for the *area* leaf, that a measured magnitude carries **no** unit vocabulary on the value.
2. **`UnitOfAreaScheme` exists only because the area data carries a `units` enum.** `opda-vocabularies.ttl:419-427` + concept `dct:source` resolving to `…internalArea.units.{square metres|square feet}` — the scheme was minted **from a live enum token in the data** (ODR-0024 R6 "SKOS from live enums"), and it is **not bound to `opda:area`**; it is a Category-C unit-status enum on the separate `internalArea.units` leaf. Its own `skos:scopeNote` carries the standing WG flag: *"flagged for WG on whether QUDT unit IRIs should be referenced via `skos:exactMatch`."*

The decisive asymmetry for Q4: **the `length`/`width` data carries NO unit token.** There is no `roomDimensions.units` leaf (the only sibling is `hasFloorplan`, `opda-descriptive.ttl:337`); the data-dictionary type is plain `number`. So unlike area — where a real `{square metres, square feet}` enum existed to lift into SKOS — **there is no enum in the `length`/`width` data to make a scheme from.** Minting `UnitOfLengthScheme` would be inventing a register the data does not exercise: the precise inverse of ODR-0011's reuse-before-mint and ODR-0024 R6's "mint where the data *actually carries* an enum."

## Q1 — Promote `opda:Room` to a class now?

**AFFIRM HOLD (vote: AGAINST promote).** I defer to Guarino/Davis on the identity-criterion merits and concur on the DCMI ground that touches my remit: minting a class mints a **permanent, dereferenceable URI** under `opda:` (the asymmetry I raised at S008 — downstream consumers resolve it forever), and DCMI Usage Board discipline forbids minting a governable term before a consumer commits to its conceptualisation (Guarino 1998 §3). No consumer re-identifies a Room *qua* individual today; the URI would name an identity nothing dereferences. ODR-0024 R10 already records the held status with a clean re-open trigger — that is the correct register-governance posture: **defer the mint, keep the re-home mechanical.**

## Q2 — Promote `opda:Building` to a class now?

**AFFIRM HOLD (vote: AGAINST promote).** Same DCMI reasoning, and one register-specific reinforcement: `opda-vocabularies.ttl:275` already wires the **non-disruptive upgrade path** — `PropertyTypeScheme` members "may bind to OWL sub-classes of `opda:Property` via `skos:exactMatch` when conditional Building/Room class promotions trigger." The SKOS↔OWL bridge for the eventual promotion is pre-built and dormant (exactly the `SubstanceKindLabel` discipline of ODR-0011 §8a — `skos:exactMatch`, **NEVER `owl:sameAs`**). Holding costs zero namespace budget; the term-mint is the reasoned move only when the gate fires. No new vocabulary is needed for Building either way.

## Q3 — Mereology / attachment (interim modelling)?

**AFFIRM datatype/structured on `opda:Property`; no premature URIs for parts.** From the namespace-hygiene seat: do **not** mint `opda:hasRoom`/`opda:partOfBuilding` object properties pointing at reified part individuals before the class exists — that mints relationship-predicate URIs whose range class is undefined (Guarino's "instance without an IC"; my gloss: a predicate URI with a dangling range is an un-governable term). The two leaves (`length`, `width`) are flat `xsd:decimal` datatype properties on `opda:Property` per ODR-0008 §Q5a/§Q6a until the gate fires, then re-homed onto the promoted `opda:Room`. This keeps the namespace clean: we mint `length`/`width` as Property datatype properties now (they are needed for coverage), and re-`rdfs:domain` them on re-home — a generator change, not a published-URI migration.

## Q4 — `length`/`width` units + `roomName` URI/label policy **(my ruling)**

**REJECT minting `opda:UnitOfLengthScheme`. REJECT a QUDT/UCUM/ISO mapping for these two leaves now. AFFIRM the `opda:area` precedent: emit `length`/`width` as bare `xsd:decimal`, metres-by-convention recorded in `rdfs:comment` + an overlay-level note — no unit on the value.**

Four grounded reasons, in priority order:

1. **Reuse-before-mint cannot fire, because there is nothing to reuse-*against* and nothing in the data to mint *from*.** ODR-0011 §Rules: reuse an external register *where a governed register exists for the domain*; mint an `opda:` scheme only for a controlled vocabulary the data carries. The `length`/`width` data carries **no unit token** — there is no `{m, ft}` enum to lift (unlike `internalArea.units`, which *did* carry one → `UnitOfAreaScheme`). A `UnitOfLengthScheme` with hand-authored members `{metre, foot}` would be a vocabulary **invented by the Council, not sourced from the data** — failing ODR-0024 R6 ("mint where the data *actually carries* an enum") and ODR-0011's `dct:source`-to-origin requirement (every concept must resolve to a glossary row or schema leaf; these would resolve to nothing). Two leaves do not justify standing up a governed register with a steward, a closed/open flag, a `sh:in`, and a UFO-category triple (ODR-0011 §8a) for a value-space the data never enumerates.

2. **QUDT/UCUM mapping is corpus-deferred and has no consumer.** ODR-0002 §Out-of-scope is explicit: *"QUDT for units (kWh, m², W·m⁻²·K⁻¹) — carry `xsd:decimal` + SKOS-typed units"*, deferred until *"a pipeline producing the corresponding data starts."* No such pipeline exists for room dimensions. `skos:exactMatch` to `qudt:unit/M` or `unit:M` (UCUM) is the *right* mechanism **when** the trigger fires (it is the standing WG flag on `UnitOfAreaScheme` itself) — but mapping a non-existent `opda:` length concept to QUDT is mapping *nothing* to *something*. SSSOM/`skos:exactMatch` earns its place for **external-vocabulary mappings of terms we actually hold** (ODR-0011 §Rules); we hold no length-unit term, so there is nothing to map.

3. **The `opda:area` precedent is directly on point and must govern by consistency.** `opda:area` is the senior sibling of `length`/`width` — same family (`buildInformation`), same kind (measured linear/areal magnitude as a UFO Quale-in-Region of `opda:Property`), same "unit carried in source structure" situation. The Council declined a unit scheme **on the value** for area (`opda:area`); the `UnitOfAreaScheme` exists *separately*, bound to the `units` leaf the data carries, not to the magnitude. Length/width have **no** corresponding `units` leaf, so they get **strictly less** unit machinery than area, not more. Treating two unit-less linear leaves more richly than the unit-bearing area magnitude would be incoherent register governance.

4. **Metres-by-convention is the honest, auditable record.** The data has no unit; SI + UK residential survey practice + the IPMS/RICS convention make metres the overwhelming default. Record it where DCMI puts contextual qualification: `rdfs:comment` on each property ("linear dimension in metres by convention; the source carries no unit token") and an overlay-profile `skos:scopeNote`/annotation (ODR-0008 §Q7a clause 3 — form-ergonomic guidance lives in `opda-annotations.ttl`, never the base TBox). This is reversible: if a future BASPI overlay ever emits a unit token or a consumer demands unit-typed comparison, *that* is the trigger to add a typed `skos:notation` / QUDT `skos:exactMatch` — identical to the `UnitOfAreaScheme` WG flag's own re-open condition.

**`roomName` URI/label policy.** ODR-0024 R10 already settled the semantics: `roomName` is a **non-rigid label, not an identity principle** (a room renamed "study" → "nursery" is the same room). DCMI/SKOS consequence, which I make explicit: `roomName` is therefore a **plain `xsd:string` `owl:DatatypeProperty`** — it is **NOT** a `skos:prefLabel`, **NOT** a SKOS concept, and **must NOT mint a URI per room name.** It is annotation-grade free text on whatever bearer carries it (`opda:Property` now; `opda:Room` on re-home). It cannot serve as the basis for a derived Room IRI (no `…/room/{roomName}` slugging) — non-rigid labels make unstable identifiers, the canonical DCMI anti-pattern. If Room is ever promoted, its IRI is opaque or Property-ordinal-derived (an Allemang/Cagle minting-policy question), never `roomName`-derived.

## Q5 — Realisation + any vocabulary the ADR must record

**AFFIRM the realisation, and the ADR records NO new vocabulary.** The ADR's "what to mint" list for vocabularies/URIs is, deliberately, **empty of schemes**: no `UnitOfLengthScheme`, no QUDT/UCUM import, no `roomName` SKOS scheme, no part-relationship predicates. It mints only the two flat datatype-property URIs (`opda:length`, `opda:width`) plus `roomName`'s datatype property on `opda:Property`, all by ODR-0008 §Q5a/§Q6a, all re-homeable to `opda:Room` on the held trigger. The ADR **must record the deferred mapping hook** explicitly so the re-home is mechanical: a `skos:scopeNote`-style note that length/width are metres-by-convention with a QUDT/UCUM `skos:exactMatch` available on the same trigger that governs `UnitOfAreaScheme` (ODR-0002 §Out-of-scope QUDT condition). That keeps the namespace minimal now and the upgrade path pre-wired — the same posture ODR-0024 R3 took for `opda:price` (single-currency interim, `MonetaryAmount` mapping deferred until the type existed).

## RULING ON THE UNIT-OF-LENGTH QUESTION

> **Do NOT mint `opda:UnitOfLengthScheme`.** The `length`/`width` data carries no unit token, so there is no live enum to lift into SKOS (ODR-0024 R6) and no `dct:source` origin for its concepts (ODR-0011 §Rules) — minting would invent a Council-authored register the data never exercises. **Do NOT add a QUDT/UCUM/ISO mapping now:** QUDT is corpus-deferred (ODR-0002 §Out-of-scope) and there is no `opda:` length-unit term to map *from*. **Fix metres by convention**, exactly as the senior sibling `opda:area` did (bare `xsd:decimal`, unit recorded in `rdfs:comment` + overlay note, no unit on the value). Reuse-before-mint here resolves to **mint-nothing**: emit `length`/`width` as flat `xsd:decimal` datatype properties on `opda:Property`. The QUDT/UCUM `skos:exactMatch` hook is deferred to the same trigger that governs `UnitOfAreaScheme`'s standing WG flag — a pipeline/consumer that needs unit-typed comparison — at which point a `UnitOfLengthScheme` (or a direct value-shape `skos:exactMatch`) becomes the reasoned move, not before.

**Engagements.** With **Knublauch** (SHACL): the metres-by-convention decision needs *no* base-TBox shape — a `sh:datatype xsd:decimal` property shape on `opda:length`/`opda:width` suffices; any unit assertion is an overlay `skos:scopeNote`, never a base `sh:in` (no scheme to constrain against). With **Cagle** (DA): the namespace stays minimal — three datatype-property URIs, zero new schemes, zero new classes; the only "decision" the namespace records is a *non-mint*, which is the cheapest possible governance outcome and fully reversible. With **Allemang** (reuse): reuse-before-mint here yields mint-nothing because there is neither an internal enum nor a consumer-demanded external mapping — the QUDT hook is parked on the exact trigger ODR-0002 already named, so we honour reuse-before-mint *by deferring the mapping until a term exists to carry it.*
