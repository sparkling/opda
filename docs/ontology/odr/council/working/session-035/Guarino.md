# Session-035 — Guarino working position

**Lens:** Nicola Guarino — OntoClean (Guarino & Welty 2002, 2009), the rigidity / identity / unity / dependence meta-properties; the formal critique of roles-modelled-as-rigid-subclasses; DOLCE (Masolo et al. 2003, WonderWeb D18).

**Proposition under review:** opda's evidence-class short/long-name alias pattern is an anti-pattern; rigid subclass inheritance is wrong for evidence typing; evidence-kind should be a facet; facets-vs-inheritance was never weighed.

---

## OPENING

### 0. The OntoClean apparatus I am applying

OntoClean tags every property (in the logician's sense — every unary predicate / class) with meta-properties and then forbids certain subsumption configurations:

- **Rigidity.** A property φ is **rigid (+R)** iff it is essential to *all* its instances: necessarily, ∀x (φx → □φx). It is **anti-rigid (~R)** iff it is *not* essential to *any* instance: necessarily, ∀x (φx → ◇¬φx). It is **non-rigid (−R)** iff essential to some and not others.
- **Identity (+I / −I).** A property *carries* an identity criterion (IC) if it supplies a relation that decides, for two instances, whether they are the same. A **sortal** carries an IC; a **non-sortal** (e.g. a mixin) does not.
- **The cardinal constraint (Guarino & Welty 2009, §"A Formal Support for OntoClean", the rigidity subsumption rule):** *an anti-rigid property cannot subsume a rigid property*, and equivalently **a rigid property cannot be a subclass of an anti-rigid one is permitted, but a rigid property must not be subsumed *under* an anti-rigid property only when…** — to state it cleanly: **if φ is anti-rigid (~R) and ψ is rigid (+R), then ψ cannot be subsumed by φ** (you cannot say "every Student is necessarily a Person" via "Student ⊑ Person" — that is fine; what is forbidden is the reverse: a rigid class subsuming an anti-rigid one, e.g. `Person ⊑ Student`). The diagnostic that bites *here* is the **roles-as-rigid-subclasses** anti-pattern (Guarino & Welty 2009 §4, the `Person`/`Student` and `Apple`/`Food` worked cases): when an **anti-rigid role** is modelled as a **rigid `rdfs:subClassOf` specialisation of a Kind**, the subclass *inherits the Kind's rigidity by the OWL semantics of `rdfs:subClassOf`*, which silently re-types the role as essential — a category error.

The relevant move for evidence is the third one. The question is not abstract: is **`…Evidence`** rigid or anti-rigid, and does the artefact's `rdfs:subClassOf` placement respect that?

### 1. Evidence-hood is anti-rigid — this is the load-bearing finding

Take the artefact's own paradigm case (`opda-claim.ttl` ll. 35–38, 55–62): a **grant of probate issued by HMCTS**. Before any verification activity exists, that grant is a document — an `opda:AttachedDocument`, individuated (the file comment is correct) "by its content + issuing activity." It becomes **`opda:DocumentEvidence`** *only* when a `opda:VerificationActivity` `prov:used` it to derive a `opda:Claim` (ODR-0009 §Rules, `prov:wasDerivedFrom`). The very same artefact, filed in a registry bundle that no verification ever touches, is **not** evidence — it is a bare `opda:AttachedDocument` (ODR-0024 §R7 says exactly this: "an attached document stands as evidence only under ODR-0009, never by mere attachment").

So: **necessarily, ∀x (DocumentEvidence(x) → ◇¬DocumentEvidence(x))** — for any thing that is evidence, there is a possible world (the world where the verification was never run, or was retracted) in which that same thing is *not* evidence. That is the textbook definition of **anti-rigidity (~R)**. Evidence-hood is a property a truth-bearer **acquires by standing in a relation to a verification** — in UFO terms (Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4) it is a **relationally-founded, externally-dependent** classification, i.e. precisely a **Role** (or, since the bearers are cross-sortal — a document, an electronic record, a vouch — a **RoleMixin**).

This is not a marginal reading. It is the same logical shape as Guarino & Welty's canonical `Student`: a person is a student only *qua* enrolment; remove the enrolment and the person persists, the student does not. Remove the verification and the document persists, the evidence does not. **`Evidence` is to `VerificationActivity` as `Student` is to `Enrolment`.**

### 2. Therefore the artefact's backbone is the roles-as-rigid-subclasses violation — VERDICT on Q2

The artefact asserts (l. 60):

```turtle
opda:DocumentEvidence rdfs:subClassOf opda:AttachedDocument, opda:Evidence .
```

and `opda:AttachedDocument rdfs:subClassOf prov:Entity` (l. 37), described (l. 35) as "a NEUTRAL document **Kind**." `AttachedDocument` is **rigid (+R)**: a document does not stop being a document across worlds; its IC (content + issuing activity, l. 35) is essential. `DocumentEvidence`, by §1, is **anti-rigid (~R)**.

So the axiom reads: **an anti-rigid property (`DocumentEvidence`) is `rdfs:subClassOf` a rigid Kind (`AttachedDocument`).** Under the OWL semantics of `rdfs:subClassOf` (Hayes & Patel-Schneider 2014, *RDF Semantics* — `subClassOf` is class-extension inclusion in *every* interpretation), the subclass is forced to inherit the superclass's necessary-membership profile. The model now *entails* that being-evidence is as essential to its instances as being-a-document is — i.e. it **silently re-rigidifies the role**. This is exactly the configuration OntoClean was built to flag (Guarino & Welty 2009 §4, "backbone taxonomy must be rigid; roles hang off it, they are not woven into it").

The file comment "evidence is a role a document plays, not every document's Kind" (ll. 35, 38, 58) **states the correct ontology in prose and then contradicts it in the axioms.** The author saw the anti-rigidity and then encoded it with the one construct (`rdfs:subClassOf`) that destroys it. That is the defect — and it is the *same* defect ODR-0005 named at the programme's gate: "an ontology whose central endurant has no identity criterion is not an ontology — it is a schema with RDF syntax." Here the dual failure: a role with a Kind's rigidity is not a role — it is a mislabelled subclass.

**The corpus already has the cure and applied it correctly elsewhere.** ODR-0006 models `opda:Seller`/`opda:Buyer` as **`opda:RoleMixin`** (anti-rigid, cross-sortal — Person *or* Organisation), **externally founded by the `opda:Transaction` Relator**, and *specialised by sortal sub-roles* (`PersonSeller`, `OrganisationSeller`) that carry identity (ODR-0006 §Role layer, ll. 87–92, 128). It does **not** write `Seller rdfs:subClassOf Person`. The evidence layer should mirror this exactly:

- `opda:Evidence` is a **`opda:RoleMixin`** — anti-rigid, cross-sortal (its bearers are an `AttachedDocument`, an electronic record, *or* an attestation), **externally founded by the `opda:VerificationActivity`** (the founding event, exactly as `Seller` is founded by `Transaction`).
- The bearer Kinds stay rigid and separate: `opda:AttachedDocument` (the document), an `opda:ElectronicRecord` kind, and — critically — the **Agent's attestation** for vouch.
- A document **plays** the evidence role (a `playedBy` / characterisation link to its bearer Kind), it does not **subclass** it into evidence-hood.

This is the UFO/OntoClean-correct shape and it is *already the house style*. **Q2 verdict: REVISE — recast `…Evidence` as a `RoleMixin` founded by `VerificationActivity`, bearer Kinds rigid and distinct, role *played by* bearer (not `rdfs:subClassOf`-woven into it); mirror the ratified ODR-0006 Seller/Buyer treatment. Ballot FOR the proposition on Q2.**

### 3. `DocumentEvidence ⊑ AttachedDocument` is *additionally* an identity-criterion conflation

OntoClean's second meta-property bites independently. A subclass must **inherit the IC of its superclass** (Guarino & Welty 2009, the identity-carrying / +I principle: subsumption must preserve identity criteria). `AttachedDocument`'s IC (l. 35) is *content + issuing activity*. But `Evidence`'s individuation is **not** content — two physically identical copies of the same grant of probate, used in two different verifications, are **two different evidence items** (different founding verification, different assurance judgement, different `prov:used` edge), while they are **one** `AttachedDocument` by content-identity. Conversely a `VouchEvidence` (ll. 110–117) has **no document content at all** — it is `prov:wasAttributedTo` an Agent, "an attestation, not a document." So:

- `DocumentEvidence ⊑ AttachedDocument` forces evidence to inherit a **content-based IC that does not individuate evidence** (it individuates the document). That is an IC conflation: the subclass and superclass count their instances by different relations. OntoClean forbids it.
- It is *also* the live hazard ODR-0024 §R7 already caught for the sibling `owl:equivalentClass` (`Document ≡ DocumentEvidence`): "reusing the evidence class as the bearer would entail every attached doc is Substantial-tier evidence." The `rdfs:subClassOf` direction is the safe direction *for that particular entailment* (evidence ⊑ document, not document ⊑ evidence) — but it pays for that safety with an IC violation, and it still rigidifies the role (§2). The corpus fixed the equivalence hazard and left the subsumption hazard standing.

### 4. The `owl:equivalentClass` aliases — VERDICT on Q1

Three aliases (ll. 51–53, 61, 67–69, 77, 103–108, 116; ADR-0011): `DocumentEvidence ≡ Document`, `ElectronicRecordEvidence ≡ ElectronicRecord`, `VouchEvidence ≡ Vouch`. `owl:equivalentClass` is the assertion that two classes have the **identical extension in every interpretation** — i.e. **co-extensional necessity**, the strongest identity-bridge OWL offers short of `owl:sameAs` on individuals.

Two distinct problems, which the proposition runs together and I must separate:

**(a) As an OntoClean / identity matter — the aliases are unobjectionable *as identity*, harmful *as named*.** A pure short-name/long-name alias (`Document` and `DocumentEvidence` denoting one class) is not itself a rigidity or IC violation — co-extensional classes share all meta-properties trivially. The defect is **naming**, not logic: the short name `opda:Document` is a **rigid-Kind name** ("a document") asserted co-extensional with an **anti-rigid role** name ("document *evidence*"). A reader who types an instance `a opda:Document` (the short, Kind-flavoured name) has, by the equivalence, typed it `opda:DocumentEvidence` (the role) — asserting it is evidence by the mere act of calling it a document. That is the §1 anti-rigidity error re-introduced *through the vocabulary*, and it is precisely the confusion ODR-0024 §R7 had to legislate against ("`opda:Document` MUST NOT be the bearer"). The alias makes the role *look like* a Kind to every downstream author. **It is an anti-pattern — but a naming/ergonomics anti-pattern layered on the §2 modelling anti-pattern, not an independent identity-merge.**

**(b) As an entailment matter — already neutralised, and the proposition over-reaches if it claims live harm.** ODR-0026 §R2/§R3 (proposed 2026-06-01) rule `owl:equivalentClass` **modelled-but-not-evaluated**: the load-time closure does not propagate it, and pyshacl's `inference="rdfs"` never did (it is OWL, not RDFS). So *today* an instance typed `opda:Document` is **not** inferred to be `opda:DocumentEvidence`. The catastrophic entailment ("every short-named document is Substantial-tier evidence") **does not fire** under opda's declared regime. The aliases are inert identity-bridges sitting in the TBox as documentation.

**Synthesis for Q1.** The aliases are an anti-pattern to **retire** — but for the *naming/clarity* reason (a Kind-name made co-extensional with a role-name invites the §1 error and forced ODR-0024 §R7 to exist), **not** because they cause a live unsound merge (ODR-0026 §R2 closed that). And the retirement is *contingent on* the §2 fix: once `Evidence` is a `RoleMixin` and the bearer Kinds are named honestly (`AttachedDocument`, an `ElectronicRecord` Kind, an attestation), the short names either (i) become the **bearer-Kind** names — at which point `Document` *is* the Kind `AttachedDocument`, no equivalence needed — or (ii) are dropped from the exemplars in favour of explicit role/bearer typing. **Q1 verdict: AFFIRM (the alias *as currently named and pointed at a role* is an anti-pattern to retire) — but REVISE the proposition's framing: the live danger is naming-induced role/Kind conflation, not an active equivalence-merge (ODR-0026 §R2 already de-fanged the entailment). Ballot FOR retirement.**

### 5. "Facets-vs-inheritance was never weighed" — is the proposition's strongest *or* weakest claim?

Here I rule honestly, including against the proposition.

**Where it is right:** ODR-0026 §R3 says in terms that "re-examining the alias pattern itself is an ADR-0011 question, **out of scope here**," and ADR-0011's validation log (ll. 463) records the short-name handling was "independently affirmed as within-engineering" — i.e. treated as a *naming-convention* decision, never as an *ontological* facet-vs-subsumption decision. ODR-0009 §Rules simply *asserts* "Evidence subtypes as `prov:Entity` subclasses … Do not collapse the three evidence types into one pattern" — it gives a (correct) **anti-collapse** ruling but never asks *whether the discriminator should be subsumption at all*. **So the proposition is correct that the facet alternative was never put.** That is a real methodological gap and it is exactly the kind of "credible split never adjudicated" that ODR-0001 says convenes a Council. This session *is* that adjudication.

**Where the proposition over-reaches — and I will not sign the maximal version:**

1. **"Rigid subclass inheritance is wrong for evidence typing" — true for the *bearer*↔*role* axis, false as a blanket.** The three subtypes are *not all* the same ontological animal. `DocumentEvidence` and `ElectronicRecordEvidence` are **document-like/record-like bearers in an evidence role**. But `VouchEvidence` (ll. 110–117) is **categorially different**: it is an **attestation** — in UFO terms much closer to a **Mode/Relator inhering in / founded by an Agent's speech act** (`prov:wasAttributedTo` an Agent) than to a document bearer. So the right model is *not* "one flat facet over one bearer Kind." It is: `Evidence` = `RoleMixin` founded by `VerificationActivity`; **two different bearer families** (artefact-bearers: document, electronic record; and attestation, which is Agent-founded). A naive "evidence-kind is just a `sh:in` facet on one `Evidence` class" would **erase the Vouch/document categorial difference** that ODR-0009 §Rules rightly refused to collapse and that the artefact's own comment on `VouchEvidence` ("an attestation, not a document") protects. **The facet must be a facet *over the role*, with the bearer typing kept as honest (rigid) Kinds underneath — not a facet that dissolves the bearer distinctions.**

2. **The three subtypes carry genuinely type-specific *facets* (ODR-0009: `document_details`; `record.source`; `attestation`+`voucher`; `sh:xone` dispatch).** That is real structural variation, and SHACL `sh:xone` over the role's facet-discriminator is the right home for it (ODR-0009 already chose `sh:xone`; the artefact comment l. 83 confirms). This *supports* a facet treatment of the **discriminator** while *requiring* we keep the per-type shapes — so "facet" here means "an `opda:evidenceKind` SKOS-coded facet on the `Evidence` role, dispatched by `sh:xone`," **not** "delete the subtypes." The proposition's word "facet" is right; any reading of it as "collapse to one class" is wrong and collides with ODR-0009 R5 + ODR-0026 R3's non-collapse stance.

**Q3 verdict: REVISE.** Disposition: (i) recast `opda:Evidence` as `opda:RoleMixin` founded by `opda:VerificationActivity` (mirrors ODR-0006); (ii) keep the **bearer Kinds** rigid and distinct — `opda:AttachedDocument` (document), a record-bearer Kind, and the Agent-founded **attestation** for vouch (do **not** force vouch under a document bearer); (iii) move the document/record/vouch **discriminator** to a **SKOS-coded `opda:evidenceKind` facet on the role**, dispatched by the *existing* `sh:xone` (ODR-0009) — facet *over* the role, not collapse *of* the subtypes; (iv) retire the `owl:equivalentClass` short-name aliases per Q1, replacing exemplar short names with explicit role+bearer typing or bearer-Kind names; (v) **migration is documentation-and-shape-level only** — ODR-0026 §R2/§R3 already make the closure ignore both `equivalentClass` and the subsumption, so *no live entailment changes* and there is **no byte-identity-breaking ABox migration forced** (consistent with ODR-0024 §R7's "shapes target the long name" and ODR-0026 §R3's "resolve in the shape/exemplar layer"); the change is a TBox re-authoring that the next emission (ADR-0011) carries. **Ballot FOR revision.**

### 6. Summary of verdicts

- **Q1 (alias an anti-pattern to retire?):** `AFFIRM` (retire) — ballot **FOR** — *but reframe*: the harm is a rigid-Kind-name made co-extensional with an anti-rigid-role-name, inviting the role/Kind conflation ODR-0024 §R7 had to legislate against; it is **not** a live equivalence-merge (ODR-0026 §R2 already renders `owl:equivalentClass` non-evaluated). Citation: Guarino & Welty 2009 (rigidity); ODR-0024 §R7; ODR-0026 §R2/§R3.
- **Q2 (rigid subclass vs facet/Role for evidence typing?):** `REVISE` — ballot **FOR** the proposition — evidence-hood is anti-rigid (~R); `DocumentEvidence rdfs:subClassOf AttachedDocument` (a rigid +R Kind) is the canonical **roles-as-rigid-subclasses** violation *and* an IC conflation; recast `Evidence` as a `RoleMixin` founded by `VerificationActivity`, bearers rigid Kinds, role *played-by* (not subclassing) the bearer — mirroring the corpus's own correct ODR-0006 Seller/Buyer treatment. Citation: Guarino & Welty 2009 §4 (roles-as-subclasses; identity inheritance); Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4 (RoleMixin, relational founding); ODR-0006.
- **Q3 (disposition/migration):** `REVISE` — ballot **FOR** — RoleMixin recast + rigid bearer Kinds (vouch kept as Agent-founded attestation, **not** a document) + SKOS `opda:evidenceKind` facet over the role dispatched by the existing `sh:xone` + retire aliases; migration is TBox/shape/exemplar re-authoring only (ODR-0026 §R2/§R3 ⇒ no live entailment shift, no forced byte-identity ABox migration). Citation: ODR-0009 §Rules (`sh:xone`, non-collapse); ODR-0026 §R2/§R3; ODR-0024 §R7; ADR-0011.

**Honest concession recorded against the proposition:** its blanket "rigid subclass inheritance is wrong" must be narrowed — the defect is the *role-into-Kind* subsumption, not subsumption per se; and "evidence-kind should be a facet" is right *only* if the facet rides on the role while the bearer Kinds (especially the Agent-founded Vouch attestation) stay rigid and distinct. A facet reading that collapses the three subtypes would violate ODR-0009 R5 and re-introduce the very conflation we are removing.

## REBUTTAL (single)

### A. To Davis — I concede the technicality, and it sharpens rather than blunts the case

**Conceded, openly:** Davis is right that `anti-rigid ⊑ rigid` is **not** the *directly* forbidden OntoClean subsumption. The rigidity-subsumption constraint (Guarino & Welty 2009) forbids the *reverse* — a rigid property subsuming an anti-rigid one (`rigid ⊑ anti-rigid`). `DocumentEvidence (~R) ⊑ AttachedDocument (+R)` is the permitted *direction*. If my case rested only on "the rigidity arrow points the wrong way," Davis would have refuted me. It does not.

**The live violation is the *identity-criterion* meta-property, not the rigidity arrow — and Davis's own argument is my proof.** OntoClean's second rule (Guarino & Welty 2009, the +I / identity-carrying principle): a class that subsumes others must either (a) carry **one** identity criterion that *all* its subclasses inherit and refine, or (b) be an explicitly **IC-less non-sortal** (a mixin/category). Now take Davis's claim 3 at face value — *the discriminator value determines which **superclass** (`AttachedDocument`) and which **object properties** (`attestedBy`) apply.* That is precisely a statement that **`DocumentEvidence` and `VouchEvidence` have incompatible identity criteria**: one is individuated by document content + issuing activity (it `⊑ AttachedDocument`, IC per ODR-0024 R7), the other carries **no document content at all** and is individuated by an Agent's attestation (`attestedBy → prov:Agent`, l. 131). Yet the artefact presents `opda:Evidence` (l. 80–86) as a plain `owl:Class` `⊑ prov:Entity` with three rigid `rdfs:subClassOf` children — i.e. **as if it were a common sortal Kind**. A supertype cannot be a rigid Kind-flavoured `owl:Class` *and* subsume children with mutually incompatible ICs. That is the OntoClean failure: `opda:Evidence` is a **non-sortal masquerading as a sortal**. The repair is to *name it for what Davis just proved it is* — an **IC-less, cross-categorial, anti-rigid type = a RoleMixin**. **Davis's "the value changes the property signature" is not a defence of the subclass model; it is the demonstration that `Evidence` is not a Kind.**

**Davis attacked a facet I did not propose — the Quale strawman.** Davis asks: is `evidenceKind` a *Quale*-of-Evidence (like `tenureKind` is a Quale-of-LegalEstate)? and answers (correctly) **no**, because a Quale never changes a thing's superclass or object-property signature. I agree entirely — and that is exactly why **I never proposed a flat datatype/SKOS Quale on a single collapsed `Evidence` class.** My §1/§5 proposal is the opposite of a Quale:

- the **bearer Kinds stay rigid and distinct** — `opda:AttachedDocument` (document), a record-bearer Kind, and an Agent-founded attestation (Vouch) — each keeping its own IC and its own property profile (`attestedBy` lives **only** on the vouch bearer, unchanged);
- `opda:Evidence` becomes a **RoleMixin** *played by* those bearers (characterisation/`playedBy`), not `rdfs:subClassOf`-woven into any of them;
- the `document`/`electronic_record`/`vouch` discriminator is the **`sh:xone` role-typing dispatch ODR-0009 already chose** (l. 83; ODR-0009 §SHACL) — it selects *which bearer + which per-type shape* applies. That is a **role discriminator, not a Quale.** It changes the property signature precisely *because* it is role/bearer typing, which is exactly what Davis says a Quale cannot do — so it cannot *be* a Quale, and I never said it was.

So Davis's §Q5a disanalogy is **correct and irrelevant to my actual position**: the SKOS-Quale-faceting precedent (session-029) indeed does **not** transfer to evidence — I agree it doesn't. The precedent I invoke is **ODR-0006's RoleMixin**, which transfers exactly: cross-sortal, anti-rigid, externally founded, specialised by bearer-typed sub-roles. Davis refuted the collapse-to-one-Quale strawman (which would breach ODR-0009 R5 and which I explicitly disavowed in §5). The RoleMixin-over-rigid-bearers model survives every point Davis raised — and is *strengthened* by his property-signature observation.

**On "is there an IC the facet preserves that the subclass loses?" (Davis's closing dare).** Inverted: the *subclass* model **loses** an IC — it forces `DocumentEvidence` to inherit `AttachedDocument`'s content-IC, which mis-individuates evidence (two verifications using one physical document = two evidence items, one document). The RoleMixin model **restores** the correct individuation: an evidence item is individuated by its **founding verification + the bearer it is played over**, not by document content. The facet/role does not "preserve an IC the subclass loses"; it *removes a false IC the subclass imposed*. That is the whole point.

### B. To Guizzardi — three rulings under the dependence analysis

**(Q-Phase) RoleMixin, decisively not Phase — agreed.** A **Phase** partitions a Kind by the bearer's own **intrinsic/qualitative** state (water/ice by temperature; child/adult by age) — phase membership changes when the *thing itself* changes, with no second relatum. Evidence-hood changes when a **wholly external** relatum (a `VerificationActivity`) comes or ceases to bind it; the document's intrinsic qualities are untouched. **Extrinsic relational dependence is the Role signature, not the Phase signature** (Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4; Guarino & Welty 2009 on dependence). And because the bearers are **cross-categorial** (Information-Object artefacts *and* an Agent-founded attestation), the precise UFO type is a **RoleMixin**, not a plain Role — exactly as ODR-0006 reaches for `RoleMixin` (Seller/Buyer span Person *and* Organisation). Concur with your reading; Phase is ruled out.

**(Q-founding) Founded by the `opda:Verification` *Relator*, not the Event — I rule (b).** Under the dependence analysis a Role/RoleMixin is grounded in a **Relator** (the truthmaker of the material relation that founds the role), not directly in a Perdurant/Event. The `VerificationActivity` is a `prov:Activity` (Event/Perdurant); an Event can *generate* a relator (`prov:wasGeneratedBy`) but is not itself the founding endurant. ODR-0009 already carries the latent relator: the **qualified attribution** structure (`prov:qualifiedAttribution → prov:Attribution` with `prov:hadRole`, binding Verifier + Claim + the evidence consumed) is a relator in all but name. So: `opda:Verification` **Relator** mediates Claim ↔ Evidence ↔ Verifier; the `VerificationActivity` Event `prov:wasGeneratedBy`-founds/sustains it; the `Evidence` **RoleMixin is founded by that Relator.** This keeps the ODR-0006 parallel **exact** — Seller is founded by the Transaction *Relator*, not by a transaction event — and it answers my own §2 verdict's loose phrasing ("founded by VerificationActivity"): the precise founder is the Verification Relator the activity generates. I am tightening my own position here.

**(Q-Vouch) The attestation is a *Relator*, not a Mode — and this is the one place a uniform bearer would erase a real distinction.** Dependence test: a **Mode** inheres in and is existentially dependent on **exactly one** bearer (a headache, a colour, a skill). The vouch attestation binds **two** mutually independent endurants — the **attesting Agent** (`prov:wasAttributedTo` / `opda:attestedBy → prov:Agent`, l. 131) **and** the **Claim** it supports (ODR-0009: a vouch is "an attestation, not a document derivation"). Existential dependence on **two** relata is the **Relator** signature, not the Mode signature. So the vouch's *bearer* is an **Agent-founded attestation Relator** — categorially distinct from the two artefact-bearers (`AttachedDocument`, the record Kind, both UFO Information Objects). The consequence is sharp and confirms the brief's instinct: **`VouchEvidence`'s bearer must NOT be subsumed under any document / InformationObject Kind** — there is no document there to subclass. The *same* `opda:Evidence` RoleMixin is played over all three bearer families (artefact-bearers + the attestation Relator), with `sh:xone` dispatching the per-bearer profile. A single uniform "facet over one document-shaped bearer" would silently re-shoehorn the attestation under a document Kind — the exact collapse ODR-0009 R5 forbids and the artefact's own "an attestation, not a document" comment protects. Concur with your Relator lean; the two-relata dependence argument is what settles it over Mode.

### C. Net effect of the rebuttal on my verdicts

No verdict flips; two are tightened:

- **Q2** founder is now precisely the **`opda:Verification` Relator** (generated by the `VerificationActivity`), not the Event — restoring the exact ODR-0006 founding parallel (Guizzardi Q-founding).
- **Q3** the bearer family is now explicitly **three** types under one RoleMixin: two Information-Object artefact-bearers (`AttachedDocument`, record Kind) **plus an Agent-founded attestation Relator** for Vouch — which must not subclass a document Kind (Guizzardi Q-Vouch). The `sh:xone` discriminator is a **role/bearer dispatch, not a Quale** (Davis), so the §Q5a SKOS-Quale precedent correctly does not transfer; the transferring precedent is ODR-0006's RoleMixin.
- **Davis's DA challenge is answered, not deferred:** the rigidity-arrow objection is conceded as a technicality; the real violation is IC-inheritance/non-sortal-masquerade, which Davis's own "the value changes the superclass + object-properties" observation *demonstrates*; and the Quale objection refutes a flat-facet strawman I never advanced.

## FINAL

**Position: the proposition is substantially AFFIRMED with a principled narrowing. All three verdicts FOR.**

- **Q1 — alias an anti-pattern to retire?** `AFFIRM(retire)` — **ballot FOR**. The `owl:equivalentClass` short-name aliases (`Document≡DocumentEvidence`, `ElectronicRecord≡…`, `Vouch≡…`; ll. 51–117, ADR-0011) are an anti-pattern *as named*: a rigid-Kind-name made co-extensional with an anti-rigid-role-name, inviting the role/Kind conflation ODR-0024 §R7 had to legislate against ("`opda:Document` MUST NOT be the bearer"). **Reframe of the proposition:** the harm is naming-induced role/Kind conflation, **not** a live unsound equivalence-merge — ODR-0026 §R2/§R3 already make `owl:equivalentClass` modelled-but-not-evaluated, and pyshacl `inference="rdfs"` never evaluated it (it is OWL, not RDFS), so the catastrophic "every short-named document is Substantial-tier evidence" entailment does **not** fire today. Retire the aliases as part of the §B recast: short names become **bearer-Kind** names where apt (`Document` = the Kind `AttachedDocument`, no equivalence needed) or are dropped from exemplars in favour of explicit role+bearer typing. *Citations: Guarino & Welty 2009 (rigidity); ODR-0024 §R7; ODR-0026 §R2/§R3; ADR-0011.*

- **Q2 — rigid subclass vs facet/Role for evidence typing?** `REVISE` — **ballot FOR**. Evidence-hood is **anti-rigid (~R)**: a truth-bearer is evidence only *qua* a verification using it; remove the verification and the bearer persists, the evidence does not (ODR-0024 §R7). `opda:DocumentEvidence rdfs:subClassOf opda:AttachedDocument` (l. 60; `AttachedDocument` a rigid +R Kind, l. 35) is **not** a forbidden rigidity-direction (conceded to Davis) but **is** an **identity-criterion conflation** (forces a content-IC onto a relationally-individuated role) layered over a **non-sortal-masquerading-as-sortal** `opda:Evidence` supertype. **Recast (mirroring the corpus's own ratified ODR-0006 Seller/Buyer treatment):** `opda:Evidence` = **`opda:RoleMixin`** (anti-rigid, cross-categorial), **founded by the `opda:Verification` Relator** (which the `VerificationActivity` `prov:wasGeneratedBy`-generates); bearer Kinds stay rigid and distinct; the bearer **plays** the role (`playedBy`/characterisation), it is **not** `rdfs:subClassOf`-woven into evidence-hood. *Citations: Guarino & Welty 2009 §4 (roles-as-rigid-subclasses; +I identity inheritance; non-sortal vs sortal); Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4 (RoleMixin; relational founding; Relator-grounding of Roles); ODR-0006; ODR-0009 §Rules; ODR-0024 §R7.*

- **Q3 — disposition / migration + ODR-0025/0026 + ADR-0011.** `REVISE` — **ballot FOR**. **[CORRECTED after Guizzardi's decline — see §D below; the discriminator is NOT a facet-over-the-role but the bearer-Kind typing itself.]** Disposition: (i) `opda:Evidence` → `opda:RoleMixin` founded by the `opda:Verification` Relator; (ii) the document/record/vouch trichotomy lives on **rigid bearer Kinds**, NOT on a discriminator-facet over the role — two UFO Information-Object bearers (`opda:AttachedDocument`; a latent `opda:ElectronicRecord` Kind) **plus an Agent-founded attestation Relator** for Vouch (which must **not** subclass any document/InformationObject Kind — Vouch is "an attestation, not a document"); each bearer Kind carries its own IC and its own property profile directly (`attestedBy` only on the vouch bearer; `⊑ AttachedDocument` only on the document seam); (iii) the role-bearing specialisations (`DocumentEvidence` = "an `AttachedDocument` *playing* the evidence role") use the repo's already-correct `AttachedDocument`→`DocumentEvidence` seam (ODR-0024 §R7); **`sh:xone` (ODR-0009) dispatches on bearer Kind** — the existing `evidence.type` mechanism — NOT on a SKOS discriminator over one flattened class; (iv) retire the `owl:equivalentClass` aliases per Q1; (v) **migration is TBox/shape/exemplar re-authoring only** — because ODR-0026 §R2/§R3 already make the closure ignore both `owl:equivalentClass` and the subsumption, there is **no live entailment change** and **no forced byte-identity ABox migration** (verified: 3 hand-authored exemplar lines + 3 short-name blocks in `claim.py`); the recast lands in the next ADR-0011 emission. **Held-as-live (named re-open trigger):** the corpus currently implements the role-of-Kind seam for *only* `DocumentEvidence` (l. 60, `⊑ AttachedDocument`); `ElectronicRecordEvidence` (l. 76) and `VouchEvidence` (l. 115) subclass `opda:Evidence` **directly** with **no neutral bearer Kind minted** (the short names `opda:ElectronicRecord`/`opda:Vouch` are bare aliases, not Kinds). Completing the symmetry — minting an `opda:ElectronicRecord` bearer Kind and an Agent-founded attestation bearer — is **gated on a consumer query** needing the "record-but-not-yet-evidence" / "attestation-but-not-yet-evidence" distinction (per Davis's "name a query" test; consistent with ODR-0024 §R4/§R10 and ODR-0005 held-as-live discipline). Until then the asymmetry is recorded, not silently uniform. *Citations: ODR-0009 §Rules (`sh:xone` on `evidence.type`, non-collapse R5); ODR-0026 §R2/§R3; ODR-0024 §R7 (the `AttachedDocument`→`DocumentEvidence` seam) + §R4/§R10 (query-gated bearer promotion); ADR-0011; ODR-0006 (RoleMixin emission precedent).*

**Honest concessions on record (where the proposition over-reaches, and where Davis was right):**
1. The proposition's blanket "rigid subclass inheritance is wrong for evidence typing" must be **narrowed**: the rigidity *direction* (`anti-rigid ⊑ rigid`) is not itself OntoClean-forbidden (Davis is correct); the defect is the **IC conflation + non-sortal masquerade**, i.e. the role-into-Kind subsumption, not subsumption per se.
2. "Evidence-kind should be a facet" is the proposition's **weakest** claim and I now reject it outright (corrected after Guizzardi's decline, §D): the document/record/vouch trichotomy is a partition of **bearer Kinds** (two Information-Objects + an Agent-founded attestation Relator), each with its own IC and property profile — **not** a value-space facet over one role, in *any* form (neither a flat Quale, which Davis correctly rejects, nor my own earlier "`sh:xone` discriminator over the role"). A discriminator-facet would have to *reconstruct* the bearer-Kind distinctions via conditionals (heavier, not lighter) and risks erasing the Vouch fork. The RoleMixin recast applies to **`opda:Evidence` (the role)**; the trichotomy stays on rigid bearer Kinds.
3. The proposition's claim that "facets-vs-inheritance was never weighed" is **correct and is the methodological justification for this session** (ODR-0026 §R3 deferred it to ADR-0011 as a naming question; ODR-0009 §Rules asserted the subclass model without weighing the alternative). This Council *is* that adjudication — and the adjudicated answer is **neither pole**: not a flat facet, and not the rigid `…Evidence ⊑ Kind` subsumption either, but a RoleMixin role borne over rigid bearer Kinds.

## §D — Acceptance of Guizzardi's decline (corrects my Q3-iii)

Guizzardi ruled WITH me on both questions I put (founding = the latent `opda:Verification` Relator, not the Event; Vouch = Agent-founded bearer, not an InformationObject — he leans Relator on the attestation, as do I via the two-relata dependence test) and **declined my Q3-iii** ("discriminator → single SKOS facet on one RoleMixin") on the empirical merits Davis had also pressed. **I accept the decline — he is right, and it sharpens my own Q2.** Two grounds, both sound:

1. **Heavier, not lighter.** The three subtypes carry **disjoint property profiles** (`attestedBy` only on Vouch, l. 131; `⊑ AttachedDocument` only on Document, l. 60). Collapsing the discriminator into one `evidenceKind` slot would force every type-specific property to be reconstructed as `sh:xone`/conditional on that slot — pushing branching into every shape and query. Rigid bearer Kinds carry those profiles **directly**. Minimal ontological commitment cuts *toward* keeping the bearer Kinds here, not toward a facet.
2. **It would re-introduce the very erasure my Q2 forbids.** A single `evidenceKind ∈ {doc,record,vouch}` over one bearer treats the vouch as a document-with-a-different-code — the categorial flattening of the Agent-founded attestation I insisted against. One cannot both fork the bearer (vouch ≠ InformationObject) AND flatten the discriminator to one slot; they pull opposite ways. My Q3-iii was internally inconsistent with my own Q2/Vouch ruling; Guizzardi caught it.

**Corrected position (where the three of us now land — full convergence):** `opda:Evidence` = cross-categorial **RoleMixin** (the role), founded by the **`opda:Verification` Relator**; the document/record/vouch trichotomy lives on **rigid bearer Kinds**, with the RoleMixin/`playedBy`-not-`subClassOf` pattern applied **at the seam where the evidence-role flips** — which the repo *already* implements correctly for Document (`AttachedDocument`→`DocumentEvidence`, ODR-0024 §R7) — completed by symmetry for electronic-record and the vouch attestation **gated on a consumer query** (Davis's "name a query" test; ODR-0024 §R4/§R10 discipline).

**One asymmetry I add to Guizzardi's synthesis, for honesty (verified against the TTL):** the repo's three subtypes are **not** uniformly modelled today — `DocumentEvidence ⊑ AttachedDocument, Evidence` (l. 60, role-of-Kind seam present) but `ElectronicRecordEvidence ⊑ Evidence` (l. 76) and `VouchEvidence ⊑ Evidence` (l. 115) **directly, with no neutral bearer Kind**. So the "keep the three as rigid sub-kinds" framing is precise for Document but is, for the other two, *currently* "sub-roles of `Evidence` with latent/unnamed bearers." Under the RoleMixin recast that is acceptable (a RoleMixin may have sub-roles), but the council should record it as a **known asymmetry with a named re-open trigger** (mint the missing bearer Kinds on a consumer query), not present the three as uniformly handled. This *tempers the proposition's sweep further*: the only seam proven to need the role-of-Kind treatment by a live query is the Document one, and it is already built.
