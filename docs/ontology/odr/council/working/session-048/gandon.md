# Council A — Opening — Fabien Gandon (W3C / Inria)

**Session-048 · Lens: RDF/RDFS/OWL standards-conformance, linked-data principles, "the engineering act IS the ontological act."**

> Framing premise I hold throughout. A published ontology is a *contract with the open Web of data*. The instant OPDA serialises `rdfs:domain`, `owl:unionOf`, or `owl:FunctionalProperty` into a `.ttl` and ships it under `https://opda.org.uk/`, those triples acquire **W3C-standard meaning to every consumer that did not read OPDA's ODRs** — a reasoner, a SHACL-from-OWL generator, a Protégé import, an LLM trained on the RDF corpus. OPDA's internal "we never entail this" is a property of OPDA's *loader*, not a property of the *graph*. So the engineering act of writing the triple **is** the ontological act of asserting its standard semantics to the Web. That is the lens. Every verdict below follows from it.

## Q1 — Disjoint domain/range form: (A) repeated `rdfs:domain` "any-of" + `sh:or` vs (B) `owl:unionOf` + `sh:or`

### Verdict: **REVISE** · Ballot: **AGAINST (A) / FOR (B)** for the disjunction cases, with a sharp scoping caveat.

**The standards question is not close, and it is the one squarely in my wheelhouse.** RDF Schema §3.2 (rdfs:domain) states verbatim: *"Where a property P has more than one rdfs:domain property, then the resources denoted by subjects of triples with predicate P are instances of **all** the classes stated by the rdfs:domain properties."* §3.1 says the identical thing for `rdfs:range` (W3C, *RDF Schema 1.1*, REC-rdf-schema-20140225, §3.1–3.2; the formal rules are `rdfs2`/`rdfs3` in *RDF 1.1 Semantics*, §9.2.1). I verified this against the live W3C REC this session rather than from memory.

So `opda:playedBy rdfs:domain opda:Role, opda:RoleMixin` **means, in the standard, "every subject of `playedBy` is BOTH a Role AND a RoleMixin"** — a conjunction, very probably an unsatisfiable one if Role and RoleMixin are disjoint anti-rigid sortals. Option (A) does not merely "use a non-standard convention." It **publishes a triple whose standard meaning is the logical negation of the author's intent.** "Any-of" is not an under-specification of the union; it is the *complement* of the standard reading. This is the precise inversion of the doctrine's own stated goal — ADR-0049 wants OWL axioms to be "standards-legible for external DL tooling" (§Decision Drivers, "OWL adherence") and "machine-legible documentation of modeller intent" (§Decision Drivers, "AI/LLM signal"). Option (A) makes them machine-legible as the *opposite* of the intent. The AI-signal argument **boomerangs**: an LLM that has internalised RDFS §3.2 — and any competent one has, it is in every RDF primer — reads `rdfs:domain A, B` as "A and B," exactly the wrong signal. You cannot simultaneously claim "we author OWL because it is the lingua franca that tools and models understand" and "we use this construct in a sense no tool or model will understand." That is having it both ways.

**The "but OPDA never reasons" defence does not reach this.** The non-evaluation guarantee (ODR-0025 §R2, ODR-0026 §R2) governs OPDA's *load-time closure*. It says nothing about, and cannot bind, the external DL consumer that ADR-0049 explicitly names as a beneficiary ("standards-legible for external DL tooling even though OPDA never reasons over them," ADR-0049:24). For the *internal* consumer the documentary triple is inert either way — so internally (A) and (B) are equivalent and the SHACL `sh:or` does the real work regardless (ODR-0029 §R3). The disjunction form is therefore **decided entirely by the external/standards reading**, and on that axis (A) is not neutral-but-unconventional; it is *wrong*. Berners-Lee's Linked Data principle 4 ("include links to other URIs, so that they can discover more things," *Linked Data*, 2006) presumes the things they discover carry their published meaning. Shipping an inverted axiom violates the good-faith basis of that contract.

**Why I land REVISE, not a clean "adopt (B)".** Option (B) is the *only* standards-correct union — `rdfs:domain [ a owl:Class ; owl:unionOf ( opda:Role opda:RoleMixin ) ]` is read correctly by every OWL tool, and `owl:unionOf` is itself an RDFS-compatible construct (it is just a class description; *OWL 2 Structural Specification*, §8.1.2). But I will not pretend (B) is costless, and the honest standards-conformance position must concede the corpus-discipline objection:

1. (B) introduces OPDA's **first boolean class constructor and first anonymous (blank-node) class expression** (corpus has zero today — ADR-0049:62, 82). My lens cuts *both* ways here: introducing `owl:unionOf` is also an ontological act, and a `[ owl:unionOf (...) ]` blank node is a *class that now exists in the graph*. Under standard semantics that anonymous class is a legitimate `owl:Class`; it is not free.
2. The doctrine's governing principle (ADR-0049 §Decision Outcome) is "author for documentation, validate in SHACL, never reason." `owl:unionOf` is the one R2-set member that is **purely a class-construction device** — it earns its keep *only* if something reads it as a union, i.e. only via the external DL reader. So (B) is justified **exactly and only** to the extent OPDA takes the external-legibility goal in ADR-0049 seriously.

**Therefore my REVISE is conditional and I will defend the condition in cross-talk:**
- For the **single-class** object properties (`founds`, `mediates`, and the already-correct `concernsProperty`/`hasRegisteredTitle`): plain `rdfs:domain C` / `rdfs:range C`. No constructor. Standards-correct and trivial. (This is uncontested — HANDOVER Part B-1.)
- For the **genuine disjunctions** (`playedBy`/`plays` bearer, `hasParticipant` co-domain, `hasAddress` bearer): **(B) `owl:unionOf`** is the standards-correct documentary form, *or* author **no `rdfs:domain` at all** and carry the disjunction in SHACL `sh:or` only (HANDOVER option C, locally). What is **not** admissible on standards grounds is (A): a published triple that asserts a conjunction the author means as a disjunction. Between (B) and local-omission, I prefer (B) *if* external DL legibility is a real requirement; if the panel judges external legibility aspirational rather than real, then omit `rdfs:domain` for the disjunction cases and let SHACL carry it — that is honest (it asserts nothing false) where (A) is not.

The decisive grounded point, restated for the ballot: **a non-standard convention is acceptable when it under-specifies; it is not acceptable when it asserts the negation.** (A) asserts the negation (RDFS §3.2). That is why I vote against it.

## Q2 — Author `owl:FunctionalProperty` / `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` as documentary signal, or domain/range only?

### Verdict: **REJECT** (do not author FP/IFP as documentary OWL) · Ballot: **AGAINST authoring; FOR SHACL-only.**

**This is where my lens is most uncomfortable for the proposal, and I will not soften it.** Domain/range and FP/IFP are **not the same kind of axiom**, and the doctrine's tidy "author the whole R2 set as documentation" elides a category difference that the standard makes sharp:

- `rdfs:domain`/`range` are **typing** axioms. Under standard RDFS their entailment *adds class memberships* (`rdfs2`/`rdfs3`). Excluding them is monotone-safe: you simply lose some derived types. The documentary triple, if a reasoner ever reads it, produces *more types* — possibly wrong types, but it does not corrupt **identity**.
- `owl:FunctionalProperty`/`owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` are **identity** axioms. Their *whole* standard meaning is `owl:sameAs` generation: if `P` is functional and `x P y1`, `x P y2`, then `owl:sameAs(y1, y2)` (*OWL 2 Semantics*, §2.3.5, table for `FunctionalObjectProperty` / `InverseFunctionalObjectProperty`; cf. *OWL 2 Primer* §2.3.3). There is no other thing they say. An IFP is *definitionally* "this property's value is a unique key" → distinct subjects sharing a value are the *same individual*.

**OPDA's own corpus has declared `owl:sameAs` the single most dangerous construct in a master-data ontology and banned its materialisation outright** — ODR-0005 §R5, ODR-0017 §R6, and ODR-0025 §R2 lists FP/IFP precisely *because* they "Trigger `owl:sameAs` identity-merge" (ODR-0025:85). To then *author* FP/IFP as a standing documentary signal is to **publish a latent `owl:sameAs` bomb** into the open-Web graph under the banner "we never reason over it." But the external DL consumer that ADR-0049 invites *will* reason over it, and the one inference it will draw is the catastrophic identity-merge OPDA spent ODR-0005/0017/0025 forbidding. **The engineering act of typing a property `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` IS the ontological act of asserting "this is a global unique key implying cross-record identity"** — and OPDA does not believe that for master data (bounded-context identity, no `owl:sameAs`, ODR-0005). Authoring the axiom asserts a thing the project holds to be *false*. That is a standards-conformance failure of the worst sort: not "non-standard," but "standard-meaning contradicts our model."

There is a tempting symmetry argument — "domain/range are R2-excluded and we author them, so author FP/IFP too, it's the same model-but-don't-evaluate move." **I reject the symmetry on grounded semantics.** ODR-0026 §R2 authorises authoring the excluded set *"wherever they add information — human documentation, OWL identity, and input for external DL tooling"* (ODR-0026:31, 57). For domain/range the external-DL input is *benign-if-redundant* (extra types). For FP/IFP the external-DL input is *actively unsound for the domain* (identity merge OPDA rejects). "Model-but-don't-evaluate" was always predicated on the axiom being *safe to evaluate elsewhere even if we don't* — that is the literal text "input for external DL tooling." FP/IFP fail that predicate. So the doctrine, read honestly against its own words, **excludes** FP/IFP from the authorable set rather than including them.

**The correct home for single-valuedness / key-ness is SHACL, and OPDA already has it:** `sh:maxCount 1` for functionality and `sh:uniqueValueForClass` (or DASH `dash:uniqueValueForClass`) for inverse-functional uniqueness. These say "*are* the values unique?" (closed-world check) — exactly the right modality for master data — instead of "*therefore* the subjects are identical" (open-world generative). This is the same direction-of-effect argument the council already ratified for domain/range in ODR-0029 §R3/§R4 ("validate, never infer"; *"RDFS only ever put them on the wrong side"*, ODR-0029:40). FP/IFP are even *more* on-the-wrong-side than domain/range, because their wrong side is identity, not typing.

**Concession for the record:** if the panel insists on a documentary key-signal for AI/LLM grounding, the standards-clean substitute is a **non-merging** vocabulary — `sh:uniqueValueForClass` (already enforced) plus an *annotation* (`rdfs:comment` / a custom `opda:` annotation property) saying "identifying within context," never the FP/IFP *axiom* whose standard meaning is `sameAs`. Author the *information*; refuse the *unsafe axiom*. That is exactly the move session-035 made for the evidence-class aliases (ODR-0026 §R3 amendment: keep the information via `skos:altLabel`, drop the `owl:equivalentClass` axiom). The precedent is on the books and it points at REJECT here.

## Q3 — Author `owl:disjointWith` under a scoping rule, or not?

### Verdict: **REVISE → conditional AFFIRM** · Ballot: **FOR, under a strict scoping rule + a hard carve-out.**

`owl:disjointWith` is the one R2-set member where my lens points the *other* way from Q2, and I want to be even-handed about that.

**Standards posture.** `owl:disjointWith` is monotone and, in RL/RDFS-Plus terms, *non-generative of bad triples*: it licenses an **inconsistency detection** (`x a A`, `x a B`, `A owl:disjointWith B` → clash), not a `sameAs` merge and not a spurious type. Its standard meaning (*OWL 2 Semantics*, §2.3.2, `DisjointClasses`) is exactly what OPDA already *checks* — ADR-0035's negative consistency gate detects `owl:disjointWith` violations as a validation step (ODR-0025 §R1 closing note: *"A disjointness consistency check … is permitted but is a validation gate, not an entailment"*). So here, authoring the axiom and OPDA's actual behaviour **agree**: the documentary `owl:disjointWith` says "these are disjoint," and OPDA's gate enforces precisely that. **The engineering act and the ontological act coincide** — which is the test my lens applies, and Q3 passes it where Q1(A) and Q2 fail it.

This is the crucial standards distinction the proposal should make explicit and currently blurs: among the R2-excluded set, `owl:disjointWith` is the member whose *standard semantics are aligned with OPDA's enforcement*, whereas FP/IFP's are *opposed* to it and `owl:unionOf`'s are *orthogonal* (a class description, not a rule). Treating "the R2 set" as one undifferentiated bucket to author-or-not is the original sin that produced the session-047 error; Q2 and Q3 must be decided **per-construct on their standard semantics**, not as a bloc.

**Why REVISE not bare AFFIRM — the carve-out is non-negotiable and it is itself a standards/ontology point.** Disjointness between **anti-rigid roles that share a bearer's identity is a modelling error**, not a documentary nicety. If `opda:Seller` and `opda:Buyer` are Roles a single Person can play (sequentially or even in different transactions), then `Seller owl:disjointWith Buyer` is *false* under the standard reading and would make any agent-who-was-both inconsistent — the closure's own disjointness gate would then fire on correct data. OPDA's classification doctrine already forbids subclassing across roles for exactly this reason (ODR-0027 facets-not-subclasses; the `playedBy`/`plays` bearer-disjunction comments in `opda-agent.ttl` invoke this reasoning). So the scoping rule must be the standard's own satisfiability discipline:

> **Author `owl:disjointWith` only between classes that are (1) rigid or sortal kinds — never anti-rigid Roles/Phases/RoleMixins — and (2) genuinely context-independently exclusive (no individual can be both, in any context), and (3) where the disjointness carries real data-quality stakes. Author it as a documentary axiom *and* keep the SHACL `sh:not`/`sh:xone` enforcement (ODR-0029 §R3-style dual). Never between role-kinds sharing a bearer's identity criterion.**

Conditions (1)–(3) are the prior-art ODR-0028b scoping rule named in the HANDOVER (Part B-4), and I affirm them on standards grounds: (1) is OntoClean rigidity discipline (Guarino & Welty, *Evaluating Ontological Decisions with OntoClean*, CACM 2002 — an anti-rigid class disjoint from another anti-rigid class it overlaps is an OntoClean violation), (2) is just the satisfiability condition for `DisjointClasses`, (3) keeps us from littering the graph with vacuous axioms.

**One verification caveat I carry into cross-talk** (from the HANDOVER's Phase-2 corrections, which I take as established fact for this session): OPDA's closure *lists* `owl:AllDisjointClasses` as enabled but **no rule consumes the list** — only pairwise `owl:disjointWith` fires (HANDOVER §Verification caveats). So if the scoping produces N mutually-disjoint kinds, author **pairwise `owl:disjointWith`**, not `owl:AllDisjointClasses`, or the documentary axiom and the enforced gate will silently diverge — re-introducing exactly the engineering-act ≠ ontological-act gap I am voting against everywhere else. Affirm `owl:disjointWith`; do **not** affirm `owl:AllDisjointClasses` until a rule consumes it.
