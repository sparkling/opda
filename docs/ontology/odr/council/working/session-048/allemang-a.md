## Q1 — Disjoint domain/range form: (a) repeated `rdfs:domain` "any-of" + `sh:or`  vs  (b) `owl:unionOf` + `sh:or`

**Verdict: AFFIRM (a). Ballot: FOR (a).**

This is my wheelhouse, so let me be precise about what SWWO actually says, because both options are being mis-described in the brief.

SWWO teaches that `rdfs:domain` is an **inference licence, not a constraint**: `P rdfs:domain C` licenses "anything in the subject position of P is inferred to be a C." Two facts follow that decide this question:

1. **Multiple `rdfs:domain` is conjunction-by-entailment, not "any-of."** `P rdfs:domain C1 ; rdfs:domain C2` licenses *both* inferences — every subject is inferred to be a C1 *and* a C2 (the intersection). So `rdfs:domain opda:Role, opda:RoleMixin` does NOT mean "Role or RoleMixin" under standard RDFS — it means "both at once," which for sibling meta-classes is nonsense. The brief and ADR-0049 both acknowledge this (ADR-0049 Pros/Cons of (A): *"under standard RDFS multiple rdfs:domain is intersection"*).

2. **But OPDA never runs `rdfs:domain` through the closure.** ODR-0025 §R2 / ODR-0026 §R2 exclude `rdfs2`/`rdfs3` (domain/range) from the 7-rule set; the ADR-0035 zero-domain/range-triple test *proves* no domain/range triple is ever materialised. So neither the conjunction reading nor the union reading is ever *computed*. The triple is pure documentation.

Given (2), the question collapses to: **which documentary form is the simplest, lowest-tax carrier of the intent "the bearer is a Person or an Organisation," for two audiences — a human reading the TTL, and an LLM ingesting it?**

**Repeated `rdfs:domain` (a) wins decisively, for three pragmatic reasons:**

- **Boolean-constructor-free.** The corpus has **zero** `owl:unionOf` today (ADR-0049, verified). Option (b) introduces the first blank-node class expression `[ owl:unionOf ( … ) ]` — into the TTL, into the per-section class diagrams, and into `ontology-model.json`. Every one of those surfaces now has to learn to render an anonymous union class. SWWO's modelling discipline: *don't add a construct the rest of your tooling doesn't already speak.* This is precisely the "would a senior engineer say this is overcomplicated?" smell — and the answer is yes.

- **The LLM reads (a) correctly and naturally.** An LLM tokenising `opda:playedBy rdfs:domain opda:Role, opda:RoleMixin` reads "played-by relates roles and role-mixins" — the any-of reading falls out of the comma list as natural language, exactly as the AI-signal goal wants. It does NOT silently apply DL intersection semantics; it reads documentation. The blank-node union (b) is *harder* for an LLM to follow, not easier — it has to resolve an RDF-list through a bnode. For the stated AI-signal purpose, (a) is the higher-fidelity signal.

- **The "any-of" convention is cheap to declare and already implied by the sibling SHACL.** The honest cost of (a) is one documented convention — "in OPDA, multiple `rdfs:domain` on a never-evaluated property reads as *any-of*, mirrored authoritatively by SHACL `sh:or`." That is a one-line per-module header note (Part B-2 of the handover already calls for it). The `sh:or` shape is the *authoritative* statement of the disjunction (verified: `opda:RolePlaySubjectShape`, `opda:SellerShape`, `opda:BuyerShape` all carry `sh:or` over the Person/Organisation and Role/RoleMixin unions, `opda-agent-shapes.ttl:104,132,42`); the `rdfs:domain` list is the documentary echo, never the enforcement.

**On "OWL adherence" (the pull toward (b)):** I take it seriously but I think it is overweighted here. (b)'s only advantage is "an external DL reasoner reads it right." But OPDA has *constitutionally opted out of being reasoned over* (ODR-0025 §R2, the whole doctrine ADR-0049 is stating). Authoring `owl:unionOf` so that a reasoner OPDA explicitly never runs — and warns external consumers not to run (ADR-0049's corollary) — would read it "correctly" is paying a real, permanent complexity tax to satisfy a consumer that the doctrine says does not exist. That is speculative generality: building flexibility for a use case nobody has asked for. If an external-DL consumer ever *does* materialise with a hard requirement, minting the union then is a mechanical, localised follow-on (it's ~5 disjoint properties). Don't pay now for a maybe-later.

**The honest concession I'll defend in cross-talk:** option (a) is only *sound* because OPDA never evaluates it. The instant anyone turns domain/range entailment back on, (a) silently produces the everything-is-both-a-Role-and-a-RoleMixin garbage. So (a) is correct **only as a rider on the frozen closure + the ADR-0035 proof obligation.** The doctrine ADR-0049 states and the test that enforces it are load-bearing for my vote. If those weren't airtight I'd flip to (b) or (c). They are airtight (the test exists, ODR-0026 §R2 line 62 spells out "treated as documentary, not as intersection-typing inference"), so (a) stands.

## Q2 — Author `owl:FunctionalProperty` / `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` as documentary signal — or domain/range only?

**Verdict: REVISE — author IFP/Functional ONLY where a SHACL substitute does NOT already carry the full signal; in practice that means: do NOT mint them broadly; domain/range + the existing SHACL is enough for almost every case. Ballot: AGAINST authoring them as a general documentary layer; FOR a narrowly-scoped, opt-in exception only where it adds signal SHACL can't.**

Here I diverge from the "extend across the whole R2 set" enthusiasm in ADR-0049's framing, and I ground the divergence in **OPDA's own binding precedent**, not just my priors.

**The session-035 precedent is the controlling authority.** ODR-0026 §R2 line 68 records an 8–0–0 ruling: the `owl:equivalentClass` evidence aliases were **RETIRED, not retained-as-inert**, with the explicit principle: *"a safe, evaluable, native substitute exists (`skos:altLabel` + `skos:exactMatch`), so model-but-don't-evaluate (R2) does NOT apply here: keep the information, drop the unsafe-and-redundant axiom."* That is the corpus telling itself: **"documentary OWL is justified only when nothing safer carries the same information."** R2 is a *fallback for irreplaceable signal*, not a licence to mint the whole excluded set.

Now apply that test to Functional/IFP:

- **What does `owl:FunctionalProperty` document that SHACL doesn't?** "This property has at most one value per subject." SHACL says exactly that, *evaluably and enforceably*, with `sh:maxCount 1`. SHACL is already in the pipeline (it's the validation layer); `sh:maxCount` is already used corpus-wide. So for single-valuedness, **the safe native substitute already exists** — and by the session-035 rule, the OWL axiom is the unsafe-and-redundant one. `sh:maxCount 1` is *better* signal for both humans and LLMs than `owl:FunctionalProperty`, because it's the form the corpus already speaks and it actually fires in CI.

- **What does `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` document?** "This value uniquely identifies its subject" — i.e. it's an identifying key (UPRN, title number, company number). Two problems. First, the safe substitute again exists: DASH's `dash:uniqueValueForClass` states "unique within this class," *evaluably*, and is the SHACL-native idiom for exactly this (Knublauch's DASH vocabulary — the standard SHACL-land key constraint). Second — and this is the maintenance-tax point — IFP is the single most *dangerous* documentary axiom to leave lying around in a corpus whose whole doctrine is "don't reason over us": a future reasoner reads `IFP` and infers `owl:sameAs` across every pair of subjects sharing a value. That's the cross-context-identity merge that ODR-0005 bans outright and ODR-0017 §R6 flags as an anti-pattern (cited in ODR-0026 §R2 line 62). Authoring IFP as "documentary" plants the exact landmine the doctrine spends its energy defusing.

**So my position:** the *information* (single-valued; identifying) must be captured — but in SHACL (`sh:maxCount 1`, `dash:uniqueValueForClass`), which is where it's evaluable, enforced, and safe. Authoring `owl:FunctionalProperty`/`owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` *on top* is redundant ceremony that adds a maintenance obligation (two places to keep in sync) and, for IFP, re-arms a banned entailment. By the corpus's own session-035 logic, that's the axiom to drop, not author.

**The narrow exception I'll grant (why REVISE not REJECT):** there is *one* thing `owl:FunctionalProperty` arguably signals that `sh:maxCount 1` doesn't quite — the difference between "single-valued as an **ontological fact about the world**" (a person has exactly one date of birth) vs "single-valued as a **local data-shape rule**" (we only store one preferred phone). If the council judges that distinction worth a machine-readable marker for AI grounding, I could live with authoring `owl:FunctionalProperty` *only* on the handful of genuine world-facts (identity-bearing codes, intrinsic singletons), explicitly NOT as a blanket pass over every `sh:maxCount 1`. But that's an opt-in, hand-curated, justify-each-one list — the opposite of "extend across the R2 set." And IFP I'd keep out entirely on the sameAs-landmine ground; `dash:uniqueValueForClass` carries identifying-key signal without the entailment hazard.

## Q3 — Author `owl:disjointWith` under a scoping rule — or not?

**Verdict: REVISE — author `owl:disjointWith` ONLY under a strict 3-condition scoping rule, NEVER blanket, and NEVER between anti-rigid roles sharing a bearer's identity. Ballot: FOR a tightly-scoped rule; AGAINST any broad "author disjointness across the model" reading.**

This is the one place I'm *more* willing to author than on Q2, and the reason is consistent with my lens: `owl:disjointWith` is the R2-excluded construct that **carries real signal SHACL doesn't fully replicate, and whose entailment hazard is benign under OPDA's closure.**

**Why disjointness clears the session-035 bar where Functional/IFP didn't:**

- **The SHACL substitute is only partial.** `sh:not`/`sh:xone` enforce "this node is not also a member of that class" *at validation time, per shape*. But `owl:disjointWith` documents a **symmetric, class-level ontological fact** — "no individual is ever both a Seller-Relator and a Buyer-Relator," "a Person is never an Organisation." That class-to-class statement is exactly the structural signal an LLM and a human want when reading the TBox, and `sh:not` scattered across node shapes doesn't surface it as a single legible "these two kinds are mutually exclusive" assertion. Here the OWL axiom genuinely adds documentary signal the SHACL layer doesn't concentrate. (Contrast Q2, where `sh:maxCount 1` *is* the whole signal.)

- **The entailment hazard is benign.** Under standard OWL, `disjointWith` doesn't *create* triples — it makes a graph *inconsistent* if violated. OPDA's closure doesn't run consistency checking as inference; it checks disjointness via the ADR-0035 negative consistency gate (the handover confirms: "OPDA has zero `owl:disjointWith` but *checks* disjointness via the ADR-0035 negative consistency gate"). So authoring `owl:disjointWith` documents intent without arming a triple-producing or merge-producing rule. Unlike IFP (which arms `sameAs`), disjointWith is safe to leave authored.

**But — and this is the load-bearing caveat — only under a strict scoping rule, the one the brief already names (UFO/ODR-0028b prior art):**

1. **Structural signal** — the disjointness reflects a genuine top-level kind split (Person vs Organisation; the Relator kinds), not an incidental non-overlap.
2. **Context-independent exclusivity** — it's true in every context, not "happens not to overlap in our data."
3. **Real data-quality stakes** — getting it wrong is a meaningful error worth flagging.
4. **NEVER between anti-rigid roles sharing a bearer's identity.** This is the OPDA-specific tripwire and it's non-negotiable. `opda:Seller` and `opda:Buyer` are RoleMixins over a shared bearer; the *same Person* can be seller in one transaction and buyer in another. Declaring `Seller owl:disjointWith Buyer` would be flatly false and would (under any reasoner, and under the ADR-0035 gate if it ever keyed on it) reject legitimate data. session-047 Q4's whole `playedBy` qua-individual treatment exists precisely because roles share bearer identity — disjointness belongs between the *bearer Kinds* (Person/Organisation) and the *Relator Kinds*, never the *roles*.

**So:** author `owl:disjointWith` for the genuinely-disjoint Substance Kinds (Person/Organisation) and Relator kinds where conditions 1–3 hold, paired with the existing `sh:not`/`sh:xone` enforcement (two layers, as the doctrine says) — but as a hand-curated, scoped list with each entry justified against the 4 conditions, NOT a blanket sweep. The maintenance tax is acceptable here because the signal is irreplaceable and the hazard is benign — the exact inverse of the Functional/IFP calculus.

### Q2 — Author `owl:FunctionalProperty` / `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` as documentary signal?

**FINAL VERDICT: REVISE. Ballot: AGAINST broad authoring; FOR a narrow opt-in Functional-only carve-out; IFP kept OUT entirely.**
**(Held from opening, with the IFP/Functional split sharpened by convergence with Cagle, Gandon, and Hendler.)**

The controlling authority is OPDA's own **session-035 ruling** (ODR-0026 §R2 line 68, 8–0–0): *model-but-don't-evaluate does NOT apply where a safe evaluable native substitute carries the same signal — keep the information, drop the unsafe-and-redundant axiom.* Apply it construct-by-construct:

- **`owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` — OUT, entirely.** Two independently sufficient grounds, both reinforced in cross-talk. (1) The safe substitute exists and is purpose-built: DASH `dash:uniqueValueForClass` states "unique within this class," **evaluably, scoped, and non-merging** — it flags the collision instead of fusing the entities. Authoring IFP as "documentary" plants the exact loaded gun the doctrine spends its energy defusing — safetied only by a non-evaluation doctrine the ADR itself admits was forgotten once (session-047). A wrong domain adds a wrong *type*; a wrong IFP merges *individuals*. Keep it out; carry identifying-key signal in `dash:uniqueValueForClass`.

- **`owl:FunctionalProperty` — narrow opt-in only, NOT a blanket pass.** For single-valuedness, `sh:maxCount 1` is the safe evaluable substitute already used corpus-wide, and by session-035 it is the redundant OWL axiom that should be dropped for the general case. If the council prefers zero Functional authoring and an `rdfs:comment`/`opda:` annotation for the world-fact distinction (Gandon's non-merging-vocabulary concession), I am content — the difference between us is small and either lands safely.

### Q3 — Author `owl:disjointWith` under a scoping rule?

But that global-poison hazard bites only an **external DL reasoner**; under OPDA's own closure `owl:disjointWith` triggers no global inconsistency (ADR-0035 runs a *targeted, local* disjointness check, ODR-0025 §R1). So disjointWith's only differentiated *benefit* (external-DL partition semantics) and its only differentiated *hazard* (external-DL global inconsistency) **both reduce to the same absent external reader and cancel.** For OPDA's real consumers, the axiom and the dedicated NodeShape are signal-equivalent; the axiom adds only an external-only benefit/hazard pair that nets to zero today. The simplicity lens breaks the tie decisively for the SHACL form: fewer constructs, it fires, no external-only surface, and the ADR-0035 negative gate already banks the data-quality value.

**On Hendler's counter** ("a check needs an axiom to check against — authoring `owl:disjointWith` is what *arms* the existing safe gate; today it's vacuous"): a fair point about the gate's current state, but it argues for *some* disjointness assertion the gate can fire on, not specifically for the OWL axiom over the dedicated SHACL NodeShape — and the ADR-0035 negative consistency check is itself the disjointness-detector (ODR-0025 §R1 frames it as a *validation gate*, the SHACL-side mechanism), so the dedicated `sh:not` NodeShape arms it equally. **Net:** author **no general `owl:disjointWith` layer.** Carry disjointness in (a) the ADR-0035 negative consistency gate (already present) and (b) dedicated mutual-exclusion NodeShapes (`sh:not`/`sh:xone`) where conditions 1–3 hold — structural partition + context-independent exclusivity + real data-quality stakes — and **NEVER between anti-rigid roles sharing a bearer's identity** (`opda:Seller`/`opda:Buyer` stay co-typable; that exclusivity is transaction-scoped SHACL on the qua-individuals, not a TBox axiom — the session-047 Q4 discipline). If the council rejects (b) on Q1, this rider vanishes and I am a flat AGAINST any `owl:disjointWith` authoring (SHACL-only).
