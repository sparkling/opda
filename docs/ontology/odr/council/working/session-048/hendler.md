## The semantic core (the fact that decides Q1 and frames Q2/Q3)

`rdfs:domain` and `rdfs:range` are **inferential, not constraining** properties. RDF Schema 1.1 §2.3.1 (rdfs:domain) and §2.3.2 (rdfs:range): the entailment rules `rdfs2` (`(?p rdfs:domain ?c), (?x ?p ?y) ⊨ (?x rdf:type ?c)`) and `rdfs3` (`(?p rdfs:range ?c), (?x ?p ?y) ⊨ (?y rdf:type ?c)`) *add type triples*. They never reject a mis-typed node — there is no such thing as a domain/range violation in RDFS or OWL model theory; the open-world assumption forbids it (RDF Semantics, §entailment; OWL 2 Direct Semantics, W3C Rec, the absence of any "constraint violation" notion). This is exactly the point I made in session-039 (recorded at ODR-0029 §R5: "DA Hendler WITHDRAWN (mechanism kept) + CONCEDED rename") and again at session-047 Q5 as the normative MUST-warning.

The "everything-becomes-a-Person" trap is **real** — but it is a property of *running rules rdfs2/rdfs3 over a multi-domain or single-domain-against-a-wider-population property*. It is a statement about **what a reasoner computes**. It says nothing about what an axiom *means* when no reasoner that evaluates it is in the loop.

OPDA's loop, provably, contains no such reasoner. ODR-0025 §R1 freezes the closure to exactly 7 rules; ODR-0025 §R2 lists `rdfs:domain`/`range` type entailment as *forbidden from the closure*; the rules file comments them out as `# EXCLUDED` (handover A1, citing `config/hm-owl-rl-safe.rules:49-67`); and ADR-0035 ships a **consistency test that proves the closure adds zero domain/range-derived triples** (handover A3 / ADR-0049 §Confirmation — "no `opda:EPCCertificate rdf:type opda:Property` ever appears"). The EPC case (ODR-0025 §R7) is the worked proof that the trap **does not fire** under R1.

So the load-bearing adjudication: **is authored-but-never-evaluated `rdfs:domain`/`range` sound, given the closure provably excludes it?** Yes, and not as a matter of taste — as a matter of model theory. An axiom that is never fed to the rule that would draw the unwanted conclusion draws no unwanted conclusion. `model-but-don't-evaluate` (ODR-0026 §R2) is not a hopeful convention; it is a precise statement that OPDA evaluates the closure of R1, and the R1 closure is *sound and RL-incomplete* (ODR-0029 §R5). Soundness is the only property at stake for "does authoring this break anything," and it is preserved by construction. My session-047 example silently assumed rdfs2/rdfs3 were live. They are not. That is the error.

**Corollary (and this is the doctrine ADR-0049 is right to state once):** one must not reason about OPDA from standard W3C OWL/RDFS entailment semantics. OPDA has formally opted out of them for everything outside the 7 rules. A future modeller — or an LLM trained on the OWL Primer — who re-derives "domain implies type implies the Person trap" is reasoning in the *wrong model*. Session-047 is the proof that this mistake is easy to make; the fix is to state the regime positively, not to keep stripping sound documentary axioms to placate an inference that does not run.

## Q1 — Disjoint domain/range form: (a) repeated `rdfs:domain` "any-of" + `sh:or` vs (b) `owl:unionOf` + `sh:or`

**Verdict: REVISE — adopt (b) `owl:unionOf` for the genuine disjunctions. Ballot: FOR (b).**

This is squarely in my lens, so I will not hide behind doctrine-consistency. The two options are **not** semantically equivalent, and the brief's own note concedes it:

- Under standard RDFS/OWL semantics, **multiple `rdfs:domain` triples on one property are a conjunction (intersection)**, not a union. RDF Schema 1.1 §2.3.1 is explicit that each `rdfs:domain` assertion independently licenses `rdfs2`, so `P rdfs:domain A` *and* `P rdfs:domain B` entails every subject of P is `rdf:type A` **and** `rdf:type B` — i.e. in `A ⊓ B`. (This is also stated in *Semantic Web for the Working Ontologist*, 3rd ed., Ch. 7, on the additive/intersective reading of multiple domains — the precise trap a modeller misreads as "any-of.")
- Therefore **option (a)'s "any-of" reading is not just non-standard — it is the exact opposite of what the triples mean.** `playedBy rdfs:domain opda:Role ; rdfs:domain opda:RoleMixin` *means* "every player is both a Role and a RoleMixin," which is false and, for `Role`/`RoleMixin`, very likely *unsatisfiable* if those are disjoint. An external DL reasoner pointed at OPDA's class graph (a case ODR-0026 §R2 explicitly contemplates: "External OWL-DL tooling, if ever pointed at opda's class graph, may evaluate them independently") would read (a) as a contradiction, not as a disjunction.
- **Option (b), `rdfs:domain [ owl:unionOf ( opda:Role opda:RoleMixin ) ]`, is the correct and only correct documentary union.** OWL 2 Primer §8.1 (Class Expressions → Union) and OWL 2 Structural Spec §8.1.2 (`ObjectUnionOf`): the union class is exactly "anything that is a member of at least one of these." It is the standards-legible "any-of." It is what an LLM that actually parses OWL should be given, and it is what survives an external reasoner.

The whole *stated point* of ADR-0049 is OWL-adherence + AI-signal + external-DL legibility (Decision Drivers: "axioms should be standards-legible for external DL tooling even though OPDA never reasons over them"). Choosing (a) **defeats its own driver**: you author a documentary axiom whose documented meaning is false under the very semantics you invoke to justify authoring it. "Documentary" only has value if the document is *correct*. A wrong axiom annotated with a comment saying "read this the opposite of how it's written" is worse than no axiom — it is a landmine for exactly the external consumer the ADR wants to serve, and it institutionalises the same "the triples don't mean what they say" confusion that produced the session-047 error in the first place.

On the cost side — the corpus has zero boolean constructors today, and (b) introduces the first blank-node class expression (TTL/diagram/model-JSON complexity; handover B2 / ADR-0049 §(B) cons). I take that cost seriously but it is **bounded and local**: it lands on the *handful* of genuinely-disjoint object properties (`playedBy`/`plays` bearer Role∪RoleMixin → Person∪Organisation; `hasParticipant` co-domain Seller∪Buyer; `hasAddress` bearer Property∪Person∪Organisation — handover B1 table), not corpus-wide. Every single-class case (`founds`, `mediates`, `concernsProperty`, `hasRegisteredTitle`) stays plain `rdfs:domain`/`range` and never sees a constructor. A `owl:unionOf` of named classes is **still inside OWL 2 RL** as a *class-expression-in-superclass-position is RL-restricted, but unionOf in domain/range axiom position is a standard SROIQ construct readable by every DL tool* — and since OPDA evaluates none of it, the RL-profile question is moot for OPDA's own closure (the relevant audience is the external tool, which wants full correctness).

The honest objection to (b) is the doctrine's *spirit*: "boolean constructors invite reasoning." That is a real cultural signal, and it is why I REVISE rather than AFFIRM-with-no-caveat: **(b) must ship with the same belt-and-braces the rest of the regime uses** — (i) the SHACL `sh:or` carries 100% of enforcement regardless (ODR-0029 §R3; unchanged from session-047); (ii) the permitted/excluded meta-shape (handover B5) must be amended to *admit `owl:unionOf` only in domain/range axiom position* and continue to fail `owl:Restriction`/cardinality/`oneOf`/`hasKey`/free-standing intersection-complement — so the corpus does not silently drift into DL class-modelling; (iii) the ADR-0035 zero-triple test is re-run to confirm `unionOf`-in-domain still entails nothing in the closure (it will — no rule consumes it). With those three, (b) gives the standards-correct signal *and* keeps the closure sound *and* keeps the constructor on a leash.

If the Council weights boolean-constructor-freedom (a) above external-DL fidelity (b), I can live with (a) **only if** it never carries *two `rdfs:domain` triples that are jointly unsatisfiable* — i.e. (a) is tolerable for `hasParticipant`'s `Seller`/`Buyer` if those are not asserted disjoint, but it is *actively wrong* for `Role`/`RoleMixin` and `Person`/`Organisation` if those are (Q3 may make them disjoint, which makes (a) a contradiction). That coupling is why I will press Gandon and the DA on it in cross-talk.

## Q2 — Author `owl:FunctionalProperty` / `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` as documentary signal (+ SHACL), scoped to ontological-fact single-valuedness — or domain/range only?

**Verdict: REVISE — author them, but tightly scoped; not blanket. Ballot: FOR (scoped).**

Formally, the soundness argument is identical to Q1's: ODR-0025 §R2 excludes Functional/IFP from the closure precisely because, *if evaluated*, they fire the `owl:sameAs` identity-merge (the rules-file rationale, handover B3 / ODR-0030 not ODR-0054 — the verification caveat is right that the "would infer sameAs" rationale lives in the rules file). OPDA evaluates neither sameAs nor the characteristics, and ADR-0035's negative gate covers identity-merge the same way it covers domain. So *authoring* them is sound by the same construction. There is no formal-semantics objection.

The reason I REVISE rather than AFFIRM-broadly is that the *signal value* of these two characteristics is real but narrow, and over-authoring them invites a specific harm I care about. `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` is the strongest identity-bearing axiom in OWL short of `sameAs` — IFP **is** the open-world "this value identifies its subject" claim (OWL 2 Primer §4.2; an IFP key is the canonical FOAF `foaf:mbox` pattern). On a genuine identifier — a UPRN, a title number, a companies-house number — `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` is the *most informative correct thing you can say*, and an LLM or external tool reading it learns "this is an identity key," which SHACL's `dash:uniqueValueForClass` enforces but does not *name ontologically*. That is exactly the "two layers of meaning" the doctrine claims (handover TL;DR: "OWL describes the domain; SHACL validates the data; declaring both is not duplication"). I am persuaded by that for *true identifiers*.

But the scope must be **ontological single-valuedness / identification, not mere current-data shape**. The test I would impose (and this is the OntoClean-adjacent discipline, Guarino & Welty 2009, that I'd defer to Guarino's lens on for the identity-criterion half): author `owl:FunctionalProperty` only where single-valuedness is a *domain truth* (a Property has exactly one UPRN-keyed identity; a Title has exactly one title number) — never where it is a storage artefact or a current-cardinality accident (handover C7's datatype-fidelity test transposed to characteristics: "admit a constraint only if it states a domain truth, not a storage artefact"). And `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` only where the value genuinely *identifies the subject across the domain* — i.e. exactly the predicates that ODR-0005's identity work already treats as contingent identifiers. Authoring IFP on a non-identifying property is a *false* ontological claim, and (the asymmetry with Q1) a false IFP is more dangerous than a false domain, because if any external tool *does* evaluate it, it merges individuals. So the leash matters more here.

Net: **author Functional/IFP as documentary AI-signal, gated to the identifier/single-valued-by-domain-truth set, always paired with the SHACL `sh:maxCount 1` / `dash:uniqueValueForClass` enforcement, never deleting the SHACL because "OWL says the same thing."** That is more than domain/range-only, and it earns its keep on the identifier spine. I'd reject a mandate to characterise every functional-looking datatype property.

I do **not** support authoring Symmetric/Asymmetric/Reflexive/Irreflexive/PropertyChain as *documentation* — but note that is a different bucket: the closure *does* enable Symmetric/Transitive (R1 rules 6/7), so those, if authored, would actually fire. The handover's A4 nuance is correct and I endorse it: the rule is a dormant safe-capability, not a mandate to author the characteristic; OPDA types nothing Symmetric/Transitive today and should keep it that way unless a real symmetric/transitive relation appears. That is outside Q2's Functional/IFP scope but I flag it because conflating "enabled" with "should-author" is the same class of error as session-047.

## Q3 — Author `owl:disjointWith` as documentary axiom (+ SHACL `sh:not`/`sh:xone`) under a scoping rule — or not?

**Verdict: AFFIRM — author it, under a strict scoping rule. Ballot: FOR.**

Of the three, this is the one where documentary OWL carries the *most* unique signal that SHACL structurally cannot. `owl:disjointWith` is a **TBox axiom about the classes themselves** — "no individual can be both a `Seller` and `Buyer` of the same transaction," "a `Person` is not an `Organisation`" (OWL 2 Primer §5.3, Disjoint Classes; OWL 2 Structural Spec `DisjointClasses`). SHACL `sh:not`/`sh:xone` enforces the *consequence* on instance data, but it does not state the *class-level fact*; the disjointness axiom is the thing that tells a reader (human, LLM, or external reasoner) that these categories partition. It is also the axiom that makes a (b)-style `owl:unionOf` domain meaningful — a union over disjoint classes is precisely a discriminated "exactly one of."

Soundness again is not in question: `owl:disjointWith` is on the R2-excluded list (ADR-0049 §Context lists it in the R2 set), the closure does not evaluate it, and ADR-0035 *already runs a disjointness consistency check as a validation gate, not an entailment* (ODR-0025 §R1: "A disjointness consistency check is permitted but is a validation gate, not an entailment — it produces no domain triples"). So OPDA is *already* tooled to consume authored `owl:disjointWith` as a check without entailing anything — Q3 is the smallest delta of the three.

The scoping rule is the whole game, and I adopt the three-condition form from the handover (B4) essentially verbatim, with one OntoClean guard I will not compromise on:

1. **Structural signal** — the disjointness expresses a genuine partition a consumer relies on (Seller/Buyer; Person/Organisation; the bearer-kinds behind the Q1 unions).
2. **Context-independent exclusivity** — the classes are disjoint *as kinds*, not merely "no instance happens to be both right now." (This is the rigid-kind test.)
3. **Real data-quality stakes** — a co-typed instance is a genuine error worth a SHACL violation, not a curiosity.

And the **hard exclusion** (Guarino & Welty 2002, OntoClean; Guizzardi 2005 §4.x on anti-rigidity): **never assert `owl:disjointWith` between anti-rigid Roles that share a bearer's identity.** `Seller` and `Buyer` as *Roles* are anti-rigid and *can* be played by the same Person across different transactions — so the disjointness is **not** `Seller disjointWith Buyer` at the Role-kind level; it is "not both Seller and Buyer *of the same transaction*," which is a *relational/SHACL* constraint on the qua-individuals (the `playedBy` distinct-node discipline from session-047 Q4), **not** a TBox `owl:disjointWith`. Getting this wrong would re-create, in disjointness form, the exact role/relator confusion session-047 spent Q4 untangling. So: `Person disjointWith Organisation` — yes (rigid Kinds, genuinely disjoint). `Seller disjointWith Buyer` as Roles — **no**; that exclusivity is transaction-scoped and lives in SHACL. This is a question where I'd want Guizzardi/Guarino's identity-criterion concurrence, but the rigid/anti-rigid line is clear enough that I'll vote FOR with the exclusion stated.
