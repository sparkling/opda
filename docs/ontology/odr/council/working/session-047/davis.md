# Session-047 — Davis (DA): Relationship-layer completeness-by-mandate (ODR-0032 / ADR-0048)

Devil's Advocate. Lens: publish-first; scope-discipline; deployment realism; model only what you will serve and traverse. I carry a recorded prior dissent in this very corpus — ODR-0007 "defer [OWL-Time] until a concrete consumer" (Allemang/Davis, session-001 Q2), and session-043 §Held "mint nothing — a parallel status string is denormalisation that drifts." Completeness-by-mandate is the thing I have twice voted against here. I am not play-acting the opposition; it is on the record.

## OPENING — Davis (DA)

**Davis (DA) — Q1 (Framing / un-freeze).** REJECT the framing "already-decided, just emit." It is not honest, and the dishonesty is structural, not rhetorical. ODR-0006 §"Freeze gate" (line 234) names **two** conditions: (a) ODR-0005 identity gate, **and (b) "the remaining Kind-layer choice (W3C Org vs bespoke `opda:`) is resolved in council."** ODR-0032 §R4 claims to un-freeze "only as it blocks emitting the relationship layer" and asserts the Kind-layer choice is "orthogonal" and "unaffected." It is not orthogonal on ODR-0032's own inventory: §R2 mints `opda:Name` **as a class** with `opda:hasName` (Person/Organisation → Name), and ODR-0006 files `opda:Name` under "**Kind layer**" (§"Kind layer (rigid, identity-supplying)" line 149: "`opda:Name`, `opda:Address` — structured datatypes, declared **once** here"). You cannot mint the Kind-layer's structured `Name` and the Person/Organisation→Name/Address joins while claiming the Kind-layer freeze condition (b) is untouched. The Person and Organisation classes are the W3C-Org-vs-bespoke endpoints; deciding their `hasName`/`hasAddress` shape **is** the deferred Kind-layer work, smuggled in under "relationship gap." A scoped un-freeze that reaches into the exact sub-system whose choice is still open is not scoped — it pre-empts. Ballot: **AGAINST** §R4 as worded. The attack: name three §R2 endpoints that are fresh decisions, not emissions — (i) Name-as-class vs structured datatype set; (ii) `opda:hasAddress` *reuse* vs participant-specific; (iii) `playedBy` as a real edge vs co-typing-only. If those are decisions (they are — see Q3/Q4/Q6), "implementation gap" is a misnomer and this needs the Kind-layer council ODR-0006 §234(b) demands, not a relationship-layer ODR wearing its coat.

**Davis (DA) — Q2 (Completeness criterion §R1).** REJECT §R1 as written ("reify EVERY source association … no collapse … gate-enforced"). This is over-reification, and it breaks the very analogy it claims. ODR-0022 — which §R1 cites as its precedent — was a **reduction governed by a consumer test**: 935 leaves → ~181, D-option ("promote every checklist item to a class") **rejected as over-engineering** ("a class earns its keep only on a named §Q4a consumer query"), and gate **G3 = coverage-by-test**: "a worked SPARQL query retrieves a collapsed leaf by path + a Category-G leaf by dereferenceable term." ODR-0022's discipline was *YAGNI + prove-the-query*. ODR-0032 §R1 inverts both: it is an *expansion by mandate* ("EVERY source association," "collapse is **not** available") with **no competency-question gate at all** — ADR-0048 §4 gate fails on (a) rangeless props and (b) un-emitted R2 entries and (c) "any source inter-entity association … not reified," but **nowhere** does it require a worked traversal query to demonstrate a consumer exists. That is the mechanical-lift ODR-0022 spent a whole council killing: JSON containment (`transaction contains participants[]`, `participant contains address`) lifted one-to-one into OWL object properties because the JSON nests, not because a consumer traverses. The PDTF JSON is "correct-for-a-form-transport, wrong-altitude-for-a-TBox" (ODR-0022 Context) — that verdict was about leaves, but the containment edges are *the same transport shape*. Ballot: **AGAINST** §R1 as a mandate. The attack: §R1 has no §G3. Show me the competency query each R2 edge serves, or accept that "every association" is JSON-nesting mechanically promoted — the exact anti-pattern ODR-0022 names. The relator spine (`founds`/`mediates`/`playedBy`) was deliberately "design-time, never reasoned" (ODR-0006); promoting design-time annotations to gated mandatory data because a gate can count them is letting the gate write the ontology.

**Davis (DA) — Q3 (opda:Name class-vs-value).** REVISE → REJECT class-by-default. ODR-0032's **own** §Out-of-scope (line 101) lists "structured `name`" in "the participant *attribute* backlog … datatype/PII work, not inter-entity relationships." Then §R2 (line 77) mints `opda:hasName` + `opda:Name` (structured) as an in-scope relationship. The ODR contradicts itself in the same document: `Name` is simultaneously out-of-scope backlog and a ratified §R2 object property. A name is not an inter-*entity* relationship — a `Name` has no identity criterion independent of its bearer, no lifecycle, is never shared between two people as the *same* node, and is never traversed *to* as a destination entity. By OntoClean (Guarino & Welty, which this corpus invokes repeatedly) no-IC ⇒ not a sortal worth minting as a class. `foaf:name`/`vcard` keep name as datatype properties precisely because the value-object adds a node nobody queries. Linked Data Patterns (Dodds & Davis 2012) "Literal-or-resource" and "Label everything" guidance: promote to a resource only when you will dereference or attach further statements to it. Ballot: **AGAINST** `opda:Name` as a class. The attack: this is the participant-attribute backlog (your own §Out-of-scope) wearing a relationship costume; structured name is datatype/PII work that belongs with `phone`/`email`, not in a relationship-completeness gate.

**Davis (DA) — Q4 (playedBy vs role co-typing).** REVISE. §R3 concedes the canonical encoding "remains class co-typing (`?x a opda:Person, opda:Seller`)" and defends `playedBy` only for "the cases where the bearer and the role-instance are distinct nodes." Fine — but then `playedBy` is **conditional**, not mandatory, and §R1's "reify EVERY association, gate-enforced" makes it mandatory everywhere. You cannot have both. If the canonical form is co-typing, then in the co-typed case `playedBy` is a self-edge (`?x opda:playedBy ?x`) — denormalisation that *will* drift, exactly the session-043 status-string attack I logged: a parallel representation of a fact already carried by the rdf:type set, with nothing keeping the two in sync. A gate that *mandates* `playedBy` on every Seller forces the redundant self-edge; a gate that exempts the co-typed case isn't the §R1 "every association" gate. Ballot: **ABSTAIN** pending disambiguation — `playedBy` is defensible *only* as an optional edge for the genuinely-distinct-node case (prov-attested participant), never as a §R1-mandated universal. The attack: reconcile §R1 (mandatory) with §R3 (co-typing canonical) — one of them is wrong, and if §R3 is right, `playedBy` cannot be gate-mandatory.

**Davis (DA) — Q5 (OWL vs SHACL-only + rangeless ban / Option D).** REVISE. The hard part first: mandating `rdfs:domain` + `rdfs:range` on `founds`/`mediates`/`playedBy` is not free — it causes **entailment**. `rdfs:domain opda:Transaction` on `opda:founds` means any `?x opda:founds ?y` **infers `?x a opda:Transaction`**; `rdfs:range` infers the object's type. ODR-0006 was explicit these relators are "**design-time, never reasoned**" (ODR-0032 Context cites this). Asserting domain/range converts a design-time annotation into a *reasoned* axiom that will silently re-type individuals under any RDFS reasoner — the opposite of "never reasoned." This is a real semantic change dressed as a completeness fix. Dodds & Davis (Linked Data Patterns, 2012) and the W3C RDF Schema spec (§2.3.1/§2.3.2 domain/range as *entailment*, not constraint) are explicit: domain/range are inference rules, not validation. If you want to *constrain* the bearer of `playedBy` to Person-or-Organisation, **that is SHACL's job** (and ODR-0006 §"SHACL constraints" already wrote `opda:SellerShape` with `sh:or`). So Option D is not as cleanly rejectable as ODR-0032 claims: the *constraint* belongs in SHACL; only the *navigable predicate declaration* needs OWL — and a predicate can be navigable with `rdfs:range` **omitted** where the range is a genuine union or genuinely open. The blanket "rangeless = non-conformant" ban (§R1) is too strong: it forces a single `rdfs:range` onto `playedBy` whose range is `Person ∪ Organisation` — expressible cleanly only as `owl:unionOf` (heavier OWL) or punted to SHACL `sh:or`. Ballot: **AGAINST** the rangeless ban as absolute. The attack: (i) asserting domain/range on the "never-reasoned" relators contradicts ODR-0006's own stance and causes unwanted re-typing; (ii) the bearer-union constraint is SHACL's job, already written; (iii) "rangeless ⇒ non-conformant" mis-bans the legitimately-union-ranged predicate. The right split: OWL declares the predicate, SHACL constrains its endpoints — not "every property gets rdfs:range or fails CI."

**Davis (DA) — Q6 (opda:Address reuse).** AFFIRM the reuse instinct, REJECT it being settled here. Reusing the single `opda:Address` for Person/Organisation is the *right* call — ODR-0006 §149 mandates "single Address class," ODR-0022 Category F says "reuse ODR-0015 (Address) … never re-mint." So far so good. But note what this concedes: it is a **modelling sub-choice being settled inside ODR-0032** (ODR-0032 Consequences line 55 admits this: "some endpoints require small modelling sub-choices (e.g. should `participants[].address` reuse `opda:Address` directly)"). The ODR says these "must be settled during emission" — i.e. by the *engineer running ADR-0048*, not by council. That is the smuggle again: Q6 is genuinely easy, but Q3 (Name) and Q4 (playedBy) and Q5 (domain/range entailment) are **not**, and the same "settle during emission" clause hands all of them to the implementer. Ballot: **FOR** Address-reuse on the merits, **AGAINST** the "settle during emission" delegation that bundles the hard sub-choices with the easy one. The attack: if Address-reuse is a council-grade modelling decision (it is — it's in ODR-0006's Consequences and ODR-0015), then so are Name, playedBy, and domain/range — you don't get to call one "already decided" and the others "settle during emission." Decide them here, in the open, or defer the layer.
