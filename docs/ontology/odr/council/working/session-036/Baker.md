# Session-036 — Tom Baker (DCMI; namespace/URI governance; SKOS concept-scheme stewardship)

**Proposition (directing-authority directive):** *"Always prefer classification over inheritance where you can"* — prefer a governed SKOS scheme + coded value on one class over an OWL subclass tree.

**Lens:** governance + extensibility cost over a long retention horizon. The question I answer is not "is a facet more elegant" but **"which structure costs less to govern, audit, and extend across the 12-year-plus retention window opda's PII-bearing schemes already commit to"** (ODR-0011 §5a Pandit retention amendment).

---

## OPENING

### Q1 — The principle, and the governance test that bounds it

**The principle is correct, and it is largely a *restatement of opda's own settled trajectory* — not a new constraint.** opda has been ruling for "coded value over class-per-value" since ODR-0011 was accepted (2026-05-27). The directive does not introduce a doctrine; it *names a doctrine opda already lives by* and asks the council to make it a first-class rule rather than a recurring case-by-case finding. The precedent is dense and one-directional:

- **ODR-0011 §Alternatives** explicitly **REJECTED** "OWL enumerated classes (`owl:oneOf` / subclassing)" as a modelling vehicle for value-spaces: *"conscripts a reasoner to police membership of a hand-curated list — machinery without a purpose — and misclassifies vocabulary terms (`Detached`) as either individuals or classes."* That is the directive, stated as a rejection, eleven months of council-time ago.
- **ODR-0024 R4 ("namespace landmines")** rejected minting five range-less `opda:primary`/`opda:private`/… school-type *classes/properties* in favour of **one `opda:schoolType` datatype property over an `opda:SchoolTypeScheme`** SKOS scheme. R6 re-ranged `ownerType` → `opda:OwnerTypeScheme` rather than subtype it. The phrase "namespace landmines" *is* the catalogue-hygiene cost the directive is trying to avoid: every minted class is a URI you must govern, dereference, version, and never silently retire, for the life of the namespace.
- **ODR-0008 §Q5a** binding table sends every `Quale-in-Region` value-space (`builtForm`, `currentEnergyRating`, `councilTaxBand`, `ownershipType`, `centralHeatingFuelType`, `heatingType`) to a **SKOS scheme**, not a subclass. Only `tenureKind` (Freehold/Leasehold/Commonhold) carries the dual layer — and *only because* the legal-estate subclasses bear distinct structure.

So my Q1 ballot affirms the principle. But the directive as phrased ("always … where you can") smuggles in the hard question — **where *can* you?** A directive that flattens every subtype into a bare facet would be wrong, because opda has cases where a subtype carries a property signature SHACL must enforce. The honest output of this session is not "facet wins" but **a governance test that says *which of three structures* each axis earns.**

#### The three-tier governance test (my proposed rule)

| Tier | Structure | Warranted when | Governance / extensibility cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Facet-only** | A `skos:Concept` in a governed scheme + a coded-value property (`opda:evidenceType`-style) on **one** class. No subtype. | The value-space carries **no distinct property signature** — the kinds differ only in *which value they bear*, not in *what other properties they license*. | **Lowest.** Extends by adding one `skos:Concept` (+ `dct:source`, `skos:prefLabel@en` per ODR-0011 §2a). **No class-mint, no ODR per value.** A SHACL `sh:in` (closed) or its absence (open) is regenerated mechanically (ODR-0011 §5a `sh:in` regeneration discipline). This is the directive's home ground. |
| **2 — Dual layer** | The subtype **retained** as an `owl:Class` structure-bearer **+** a governed SKOS concept, bridged by `skos:exactMatch` (the ODR-0011 §8a "Substance Kind label" row). Per-kind obligations validated by `sh:qualifiedValueShape` keyed on the **coded value**. | The subtype carries a **distinct property signature SHACL must enforce** (a property only that subtype may bear, or a cardinality only it must meet). | **Medium.** Banks the governance win for the *value-space* (new values are concepts), but pays a **one-time class-mint** for each kind that genuinely has a signature. That residual cost is the **price of SHACL-enforceable structure, not waste** — see Q2. |
| **3 — Bare subclass tree** | An OWL subclass hierarchy with **no** governed value-space facet. | Neither a governable value-space *nor* a coded SHACL key is wanted — and the subtypes are rigid sub-Kinds with identity criteria the data realises. | **Highest, and almost never justified in opda.** Each value is a class-mint *and* (historically) needed alias glue to wire human-facing short names — the very `owl:equivalentClass` glue **session-035 retired 8–0–0**. opda's RDFS-only closure (ODR-0026) makes `sh:targetClass` dispatch entailment-fragile, so even the validation argument for bare subclasses is weak. |

**The test in one line:** *prefer Tier 1; escalate to Tier 2 **iff** a subtype carries a SHACL-enforceable property signature; reach Tier 3 essentially never.* "Prefer classification over inheritance" = **default to Tier 1, and where you must keep a class, keep it as a Tier-2 structure-bearer under a governed facet — never a Tier-3 bare tree, and never a bare facet that erases an enforceable signature.**

**Why the limit is non-negotiable (the honest concession).** A pure facet that *replaces* a structure-bearing subclass loses the property signature the validator keys on. The live opda kernel is `opda:attestedBy rdfs:domain opda:VouchEvidence` (`opda-claim.ttl:121`) — a property **only** vouch evidence bears, because a vouch is an Agent-founded attestation, not a document derivation. If you dissolve `VouchEvidence` into a bare `evidenceType="Vouch"` facet with no class, you have nowhere to hang the "MUST be `attestedBy` a `prov:Agent`" obligation as a class-scoped shape, and you re-admit the `sh:xone`-conditional-soup ODR-0009 §R5 forbids. The dual layer (Tier 2) is the governed answer to exactly this: keep the class for the signature, govern the *kind value-space* as a scheme, bridge with `skos:exactMatch`. This is **not** a retreat from the directive — it is the directive obeyed *correctly*, because the SKOS concept and the OWL class are permitted to coexist (SKOS Reference §3.5.1: a resource may be both a `skos:Concept` and an `owl:Class`; the spec makes "no statement" forcing one over the other).

> **VERDICT Q1: AFFIRM — ballot: FOR.** The principle restates opda's ratified trajectory (ODR-0011 §Alternatives rejecting `owl:oneOf`/subclassing; ODR-0024 R4/R6 "namespace landmines"; ODR-0008 §Q5a SKOS-for-Quale-in-Region). Codify it as the **three-tier governance test**: default Tier-1 facet; Tier-2 dual layer *iff* a SHACL-enforceable property signature exists; Tier-3 bare subclass essentially never. The catalogue-hygiene rationale: a governed scheme extends by **one curated `skos:Concept` + `dct:source`** with no ODR per value (W3C SKOS Reference §1.4; ODR-0011 §2a/§4a), whereas every minted class is a governed-forever URI ("namespace landmine," ODR-0024 R4) that historically needed the retired alias glue (session-035).

### Q2 — Evidence application: is `EvidenceMethodScheme` the right governed home, and do the subclasses earn `skos:exactMatch` retention or should some be facet-only?

**This is already settled and *implemented in-tree* — session-035 landed the dual layer, and I verified the artefact directly.** My charge frames Q2 as an open application question; it is in fact a *confirmation* question, because session-035's REVISE is fully realised in the emitted TTL. The relevant facts, verified:

- **`opda:EvidenceMethodScheme` is the governed home, and it is now correctly the *kind* axis.** `opda-vocabularies.ttl:1108` — `skos:prefLabel "Evidence Kind"@en`; `dct:source` the OIDC4IDA spec; `opda:ufoCategory "Substance Kind label"` (the §8a row that *carries* the `skos:exactMatch`-to-subclass mandate). Its three members `{Document, Electronic-Record, Vouch}` are the OIDC4IDA `evidence.type` values, sourced verbatim per ODR-0011 §4a regulator-citation discipline. session-035's mislabelling finding ("its members are `evidence.type`, not method") was **applied** — the `prefLabel` is now "Evidence Kind," not "method."
- **All three subclasses earn `skos:exactMatch` retention — and do.** `opda-claim.ttl:55/65/98` — `opda:DocumentEvidence skos:exactMatch <…/evidenceMethod/Document>`, and the same for ElectronicRecord and Vouch. **Never `owl:sameAs`** (ODR-0011 §8a; ODR-0005 Anti-pattern §5). The short name is now a `skos:altLabel` (`:54/64/97`), not a class.
- **The `owl:equivalentClass` aliases are gone — verified zero live axioms.** A corpus grep returns four `owl:equivalentClass` strings in `opda-claim.ttl`, and **all four are inside `rdfs:comment` text narrating the retirement** — not one is a live axiom. session-035's Q1 (8–0–0 retire) is fully executed.
- **`opda:evidenceType` is minted and SHACL-validated.** `opda-claim.ttl:108` — the coded discriminator; `opda-claim-shapes.ttl:38` — `EvidenceTypeValueShape sh:targetSubjectsOf opda:evidenceType` with `sh:in {Document, Electronic-Record, Vouch}`. This is the **entailment-free value-space key** (the `opda:ownerType`/`sh:targetSubjectsOf` idiom, ODR-0024 R5/R6) — it fires with **no RDFS/OWL entailment dependency**, which matters because opda's closure does not evaluate `rdfs:domain`/`equivalentClass` (ODR-0026 §R2).

**Do all three subclasses earn the dual layer, or should some be facet-only?** This is the sharp governance question my charge asks, and the answer is **mixed — and the in-tree state already gets it right, with one residue:**

- **`VouchEvidence` — Tier 2 EARNED, unambiguously.** It bears `opda:attestedBy → prov:Agent` (`:121`), a property *no other evidence kind bears*, enforced by `VouchEvidenceShape` (`opda-claim-shapes.ttl:73`, `sh:class prov:Agent ; sh:minCount 1`). It is also a categorially distinct thing — typed `opda:Relator` (`:91`), an Agent-founded attestation, not an Information-Object sibling. Demoting it to facet-only would orphan `attestedBy` and lose the categorial fact. **Keep the class.** This is the live justification for the *entire* dual-layer tier.
- **`DocumentEvidence` — Tier 2 EARNED, on the bearer-Kind structure.** It is `rdfs:subClassOf opda:AttachedDocument, opda:Evidence` (`:53`) — it inherits the neutral document bearer's signature (filing metadata: `documentDate`/`documentTypeCode`/`filedUnder`/`retrievedOn`, ODR-0024 R7). The `⊑ AttachedDocument` edge is a real structural commitment SHACL and the IC story depend on. **Keep the class.**
- **`ElectronicRecordEvidence` — the honest marginal case, leans facet-only on *today's* signature.** It is `rdfs:subClassOf opda:Evidence` **only** (`:63`) — no `attestedBy`, no `⊑ AttachedDocument`, no property *only it* bears in the current artefact. By my own Tier-1/Tier-2 boundary, **a kind with no distinct property signature is a Tier-1 facet-only candidate.** On a strict reading, `ElectronicRecordEvidence` is carried as a class today only for *symmetry* with its two siblings, not because it earns the class on signature.

I do **not** propose retiring `ElectronicRecordEvidence`, for three governance reasons, but I want the asymmetry **recorded honestly** rather than papered over:

1. **Set-completeness over a regulator-cited closed axis.** The three are the OIDC4IDA `evidence.type` enumeration — a *closed, externally-governed set*. Keeping all three classes co-present preserves a clean 1:1 `skos:exactMatch` lattice (concept ↔ class for every member) rather than a ragged 2-of-3. For a catalogue meant to round-trip against the OpenID spec, the regularity has audit value (ISO 25964-1 §8 thesaurus-maintenance hygiene: a mapping set that covers its source vocabulary uniformly is cheaper to re-verify than one with documented exceptions).
2. **The signature is *latent, not absent*.** OIDC4IDA electronic records carry source/check-method structure (the `check_details`/`source` sub-objects) that opda has simply not yet emitted. The class is the place that structure will land when a consumer query pulls it. Retiring the class now and re-minting it on first demand is *more* namespace churn than keeping a thin-but-warranted bearer — the opposite of the catalogue-hygiene win.
3. **Asymmetric retirement would itself be a governance event.** Removing one of three sibling classes, re-typing exemplars, and re-pinning the `skos:exactMatch` lattice is precisely the class-lifecycle cost the directive is trying to *avoid*. The cheap, hygienic move is to leave the realised dual layer alone and *record the marginal status of `ElectronicRecordEvidence` as a held note* — its class is held on **set-completeness + latent-signature** grounds, re-examined only if the OIDC4IDA axis is ever re-cut or a "no electronic-record structure will ever land" determination is made.

**One implementation residue I surface (not a Q2 verdict change, a Q3 routing item).** `opda:Evidence`'s `rdfs:comment` (`:71`) promises *"per-kind obligations are validated by `opda:EvidenceFacetShape` (`sh:qualifiedValueShape`; ADR-0012)."* No `opda:EvidenceFacetShape` and no `sh:qualifiedValueShape` exists in `opda-claim-shapes.ttl` — the per-kind dispatch is currently realised as a **`sh:targetClass opda:VouchEvidence`** class-scoped shape (`VouchEvidenceShape`) plus the value-space `EvidenceTypeValueShape`. That is *functionally* the dual-layer dispatch session-035 specified, but the comment names a shape that does not exist — the same "annotation asserts semantics the artefact does not perform" defect-class session-035 itself flagged (the inert alias + the phantom `sh:xone` comment). Either emit `EvidenceFacetShape` (the `sh:qualifiedValueShape`-keyed-on-`evidenceType` form the comment promises) **or** correct the comment to name the shapes that exist. This is a §Findings item, routed below.

> **VERDICT Q2: AFFIRM — ballot: FOR.** `opda:EvidenceMethodScheme` (now "Evidence Kind," `ufoCategory "Substance Kind label"`) IS the right governed home — verified in-tree, sourced verbatim to OIDC4IDA per ODR-0011 §4a. The dual layer is **already realised and correct**: `skos:exactMatch` (never `owl:sameAs`) on all three subclasses (`opda-claim.ttl:55/65/98`), `opda:evidenceType` SHACL-keyed entailment-free (`EvidenceTypeValueShape`, `sh:targetSubjectsOf`). **`VouchEvidence` (`attestedBy` signature + `Relator` category) and `DocumentEvidence` (`⊑ AttachedDocument` bearer signature) earn Tier-2 retention decisively; `ElectronicRecordEvidence` is the marginal case** — facet-only by a strict signature reading, but **held as a class on set-completeness over the closed OIDC4IDA axis + latent (`check_details`/`source`) signature**, with retirement itself being the more expensive governance event. Record the marginal status; do not retire.

**The session-035 implementation is complete, clean, and ratification-track — I verified every limb.** There is nothing to *fix* in the evidence layer for this directive; session-036 is not re-litigating session-035. #### Codification: amend ODR-0011, not a new principle ODR

I argue **against** a free-standing "principle ODR" and **for** amending **ODR-0011 §8a**, with a one-line cross-reference from ODR-0008 §Q5a. Reasons, on governance grounds:

1. **The doctrine already has a home.** ODR-0011 §8a is the *seven-category UFO framework* that already distinguishes **"Substance Kind label"** (bound to an OWL sub-class hierarchy via `skos:exactMatch`) from the five value-space categories that are facet-only (`Role label`, `Phase label`, `Quale-in-Region`, `Method/plan code`, `Quality Region`, `Quality Value`). **The three-tier test is already latent in that table** — §8a *already* encodes "most categories are facet-only; the `Substance Kind label` category is the dual layer." session-036 makes the *decision rule* explicit ("escalate to the dual layer iff a SHACL-enforceable property signature exists") rather than leaving it implicit in which category a scheme is assigned. A new ODR would *fork* a doctrine that ODR-0011 already owns — a referential-integrity hazard (two records claiming the same rule), exactly the catalogue-hygiene anti-pattern I'd otherwise be arguing against.
2. **A principle ODR with no new artefact is a record that asserts more than it performs.** opda's own session-035 through-line is "build the missing thing, don't just assert it." A standalone "prefer classification" ODR would carry no new TTL, no new shape, no new scheme — it would restate ODR-0011 §Alternatives + §8a + ODR-0024 R4/R6 and add a citation. That is an *index entry*, not a decision record. The hygienic move is to **sharpen the existing record's §8a** with the explicit three-tier escalation test and the worked evidence application as the canonical example.
3. **`odr-review` lint already reads §8a.** ODR-0011's "`odr-review` lint extension contract" verifies `opda:ufoCategory` presence + value-in-the-seven-category-vocabulary + the `skos:exactMatch`-to-subclass cross-check for `Substance Kind label` schemes. The enforcement machinery for the rule **already points at §8a.** Codifying there means the rule is *mechanically checked* on the next lint run; codifying in a new ODR orphans it from the lint.

**Concrete amendment I propose to ODR-0011 §8a** (routed as a disposition; the WG/Modelling Sub-Committee ratifies):

- Add an explicit **three-tier escalation rule** to §8a: *for any value-space, default to Tier-1 (facet-only: a scheme + a coded-value property on one class); escalate to Tier-2 (the `Substance Kind label` dual layer — subclass retained + `skos:exactMatch`) **iff** the subtype carries a distinct property signature SHACL must enforce; Tier-3 (bare subclass tree, no governed facet) requires a named identity criterion the data realises AND no governable value-space — essentially never in opda.*
- Name the **evidence layer as the canonical worked example** of the dual layer (`EvidenceMethodScheme` + the three `…Evidence` subclasses, `skos:exactMatch`-bound, `opda:evidenceType`-keyed), cross-citing session-035 and session-036.
- Record the **`ElectronicRecordEvidence` marginal-class note** (Q2): a sibling held as a class on set-completeness + latent-signature, not on a present signature — the documented exception that proves the rule.
- Carry the **held-as-live dissent** from session-035 unchanged: the facet must remain a *bound label over retained structure-bearers*; it must **not** become a SHACL dispatch discriminator that dissolves the subclasses into `sh:xone`-soup (ODR-0009 §R5). Re-open trigger: a named uniform-evidence consumer query.

#### 1. Cagle's disjunction — I adopt it; my Tier-2 condition was one notch too narrow

My opening stated the dual-layer trigger as a single condition: *"Tier-2 dual layer iff a subtype carries a SHACL-enforceable property signature."* **Cagle is right that this under-warrants, and the narrowness would bite `VouchEvidence`.** The corpus proof he cites is exact and I verified it: `opda-agent-shapes.ttl` **ships both realisations side by side** — `opda:Person`/`opda:Organisation` are full Kinds with `sh:targetClass` shapes carrying their own structure (`PersonIdentityKeyShape:53`; the Organisation `hasAssertedCapacity` shape `:78-83`), *because they have independent identity*; but the owner-**type** discrimination is keyed value-side (`OwnerTypeValueShape sh:targetSubjectsOf opda:ownerType` + `sh:in`, `:47-51`, whose own message says it holds *"without an overlay profile"*), with **no** minted `opda:PrivateIndividualOwner` subclass anywhere. One file, both layers, the boundary drawn exactly where the disjunction predicts.

So I **revise my Tier-2 condition to a disjunction** (Cagle's formulation, which I endorse):

> **Tier-2 dual layer (retain the subclass) is warranted IFF the subtype carries EITHER (i) a SHACL-enforceable distinct property signature OR (ii) a distinct identity criterion / UFO category that a coded value structurally cannot carry. Otherwise Tier-1 facet-only (no class).**

Limb (ii) is the one I under-weighted. A `skos:Concept` `evidenceType="Vouch"` can say *which value*; it **cannot** say *"this is a `opda:Relator`, not an Information-Object."* That categorial fact is class-bearing independently of whether `attestedBy` were enforced — and `VouchEvidence` is emitted `a opda:Relator` (`opda-claim.ttl:92`, verified). Dropping a Relator/distinct-IC class to a flat code is the **categorial-erasure anti-pattern** (Sales & Guizzardi, OntoUML anti-pattern catalogue) and would contradict the live `opda:Relator` emission. From the *governance* seat this disjunction is **strictly better**, not a dilution: it prevents the directive from being read as "flatten every subtype," which would erase identity criteria that no value-space can reconstruct. Under the disjunction, `VouchEvidence` is **over-determined** (both limbs fire — `attestedBy` *and* `Relator`); `DocumentEvidence` fires limb (ii) via the `⊑ AttachedDocument` bearer-Kind seam; and **`ElectronicRecordEvidence` fires neither on today's artefact** — which is exactly where my Q2 marginal-class note already landed. Cagle and I reach the identical disposition on all three, now with a cleaner rule.

**Net Q1 refinement:** the three-tier test stands, with the Tier-2 condition stated as Cagle's disjunction. This does **not** change any verdict; it states the rule so it cannot be misread as facet-absolutism.

The reversal is principled, not concessive: the directive under debate is *prefer the cheaper-to-govern structure*, and that same logic disciplines **against** cosmetic IRI churn. This is an ODR-0011 §5a *minor-edit* (relabel → `dct:modified` only, no relation), not a §5a deprecation (which fires only on a *referent* change — a concept split/merge — which has not happened). **Disposition refinement (Q3):** close the ODR-0009 §Deferred "gated rename `EvidenceMethodScheme`→`EvidenceKindScheme`" as **"no-op — withhold permanently,"** not "deferred-pending-a-driver." ## FINAL POSITION SUMMARY

**The directive "always prefer classification over inheritance where you can" is AFFIRMED on all three questions — as a *rebuttable default with a stated escalation test*, not an absolute.** It is, on the governance axis, a *generalisation of opda's own settled trajectory*: ODR-0011 §Alternatives already rejected `owl:oneOf`/subclassing for value-spaces; ODR-0024 R4/R6 already chose schemes over subtypes to avoid "namespace landmines"; ODR-0008 §Q5a already routes every Quale-in-Region to SKOS. session-036's contribution is to **state the rule explicitly** rather than re-derive it case by case.

**The rule (codify in ODR-0011 §8a):** default **Tier-1 facet-only** (governed `skos:Concept` + coded value on one class; extends by +1 concept, no ODR per value, no class-mint); escalate to **Tier-2 dual layer** (subclass retained + `skos:exactMatch`-bound governed concept; per-kind obligations `sh:qualifiedValueShape`-keyed on the coded value) **IFF the subtype carries EITHER a SHACL-enforceable distinct property signature OR a distinct identity criterion / UFO category a coded value cannot carry**; reach **Tier-3 bare subclass** essentially never. The coded value is the stable, entailment-free SHACL key (ODR-0024 R5/R6; ODR-0026 RDFS-only closure makes `sh:targetClass`-on-a-subclass entailment-fragile); the class is structure layered on top only where a limb fires.

**Evidence is the canonical worked example, and it is already realised correctly in-tree** (verified): alias retired → `skos:altLabel`; `EvidenceMethodScheme` repurposed to the kind axis (`Substance Kind label`, IRI correctly *pinned* not renamed); `opda:evidenceType` SHACL-keyed entailment-free; `skos:exactMatch` (never `owl:sameAs`) on all three subclasses. The dual layer is warranted for `VouchEvidence` (over-determined) and `DocumentEvidence`; `ElectronicRecordEvidence` is the documented marginal member.

**The limit, named honestly (the DCMI/Usage-Board caution):** "prefer classification" must **not** be read as "dissolve every subtype into a bare facet." Where a subtype bears a distinct property signature SHACL enforces (`attestedBy`) **or** a distinct identity criterion / UFO category (`opda:Relator`), the governed answer is the **dual layer** — a `skos:exactMatch`-bound subclass under a governed scheme — **not** a bare facet that erases the structure, and **not** a bare subclass tree that loses the governable, regulator-cited, extensible value-space. That is the §8a "Substance Kind label" row, and it is the price of SHACL-enforceable structure, paid once per genuinely-structured kind — not waste.
