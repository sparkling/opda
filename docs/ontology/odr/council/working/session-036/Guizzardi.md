# Session-036 — Guizzardi (Devil's Advocate) working position

- **Role:** Devil's Advocate — Giancarlo Guizzardi (gUFO / OntoUML; UFO rigidity taxonomy Kind/Subkind/Role/Phase/Relator/Mode/Quality; Guizzardi 2005 *Ontological Foundations for Conceptual Modeling*; Sales & Guizzardi OntoUML anti-pattern catalogue).
- **Proposition under attack (directing-authority directive):** *"Always prefer classification over inheritance where you can"* — i.e. prefer a coded SKOS facet on one class over an OWL subclass tree.
- **Stance:** GENUINELY OPPOSED, on published methodology. Not contrarian theatre — a blanket classification preference commits the **low-level/atomic-class-collapse** anti-pattern (Sales & Guizzardi, OntoUML anti-pattern catalogue) whenever the thing being flattened is a rigid sortal carrying its own identity criterion and a disjoint property signature. "Where you can" is doing illegitimate work: the *whole question* is when you can, and ontology — not consumer convenience — answers it.

## OPENING

### The category error in the proposition

"Always prefer classification over inheritance" conflates two ontologically distinct constructs that the corpus *already* distinguishes and that ODR-0011 §8a *already* legislated apart:

1. **A Quality / Quale value** — a value in the quality-space of a single Kind. `builtForm`, `councilTaxBand`, `currentEnergyRating`, `ownershipType`, `addressVariant`, `tenureKind`'s *value carrier*. Here classification is not merely *preferable*, it is *mandatory* — these are NOT subclasses (`opda:Detached` is not a class; ODR-0011 §Alternatives rejected exactly that as "machinery without a purpose; misclassifies vocabulary terms as classes"). The proposition is **redundant** for this case: the council already prefers classification here, unconditionally.

2. **A rigid Subkind supplying its own identity-and-structure** — a sortal that (a) is rigid (instances cannot cease to be of that type without ceasing to exist), (b) carries its own identity criterion, and (c) bears a **disjoint property signature** absent from its siblings. Here a coded value *destroys information*: it erases the disjoint property profiles and erases the UFO category distinction. The proposition is **wrong** for this case.

The proposition's defect is that it offers a *single* answer ("prefer classification") to a question whose answer is *type-dependent*. ODR-0011 §8a is the proof the corpus already knows this: its seven-category framework routes **Quale-in-Region / Quality Value** schemes (`builtForm`, `ownershipType`, `addressVariant`) to a **flat coded facet with no class**, and routes **Substance Kind label** schemes (`tenureKind` = Freehold/Leasehold/Commonhold) to **a coded facet `skos:exactMatch`-bound to a retained OWL sub-class hierarchy** ("NEVER `owl:sameAs`"). Two different ontological categories, two different remedies, both already ratified. A blanket "always prefer classification" *overwrites* that adjudication with a slogan.

### The load-bearing test must be ONTOLOGICAL, not query-driven

The directive asks for a "load-bearing inheritance" test. I accept the framing but I reject any test phrased as *"is there a consumer query that dispatches on `rdf:type`?"* — that is the YAGNI/demand-pull test (Davis's instrument in S035), and it is the wrong instrument for a *categorial* question. A consumer query can be satisfied by a coded value via `skos:exactMatch` (S035's whole reconciliation: dispatch on `rdf:type` OR coded value, same answer). So "no query needs the class" does NOT entail "the class is not load-bearing" — the class can be load-bearing *ontologically* while no current query exercises it (cf. ODR-0024 §R10 `opda:Building`: a real +O identity criterion, latent but genuine; the council declined to mint the empty Kind, but it never said the identity criterion was *fictional*).

**Proposed load-bearing test (the ⊥-test — three conjuncts, ALL required to retain a subclass over a coded facet):**

1. **Rigidity (R+).** The discriminator is a *rigid* sortal under the UFO rigidity meta-property (Guizzardi 2005 Ch.4 §4.2): an instance is necessarily of that type across all worlds in which it exists. (Fails for anti-rigid Roles/Phases — those are correctly *not* subclasses; they are RoleMixins/Phases, e.g. `opda:Evidence` itself, S035.)
2. **Own identity criterion (IC).** The subtype supplies or specialises a *principle of identity* its siblings do not share — the OntoClean `+I` test (Guarino & Welty 2009). A code that merely *labels* a Quality value carries no IC (`builtForm` Detached has none); a Subkind that individuates differently does.
3. **Disjoint property signature (Δ).** The subtype is the rigid `rdfs:domain` of ≥1 property its siblings categorially cannot bear (`opda:attestedBy` Vouch-only, `opda-claim.ttl:121`; `⊑ opda:AttachedDocument` Document-only, `:53`). A single coded value cannot home disjoint property profiles without re-admitting the `if evidenceType=X then require P` conditional soup ODR-0009 §R5 forbids.

If **R+ ∧ IC ∧ Δ** all hold, inheritance is load-bearing and a flat coded facet is the **atomic-class-collapse anti-pattern**. If any conjunct fails, classification is preferable (and §8a already routes it).

This test is *not* "prefer classification unless a query needs the class." It is "prefer classification unless the discriminator is a rigid sortal with its own IC and a disjoint signature." The difference is the entire dissent.

### Why the framing "prefer classification" is itself hazardous

Even where the *outcome* is a coded facet, the *slogan* invites the inverse error the corpus has already been burned by: ODR-0024 §R4/R7 "namespace landmines" (`opda:primary`/`opda:private` as range-less object properties), ODR-0024 §R7 the `Document ≡ DocumentEvidence` conflation, S035's `owl:equivalentClass` alias. A *principle* should name the **invariant** (rigid sortal + IC + disjoint signature ⇒ class), not a *default direction*. A default direction gets cargo-culted; an invariant gets reasoned. This is my standing objection to Q1 as worded.

## Per-question OPENING positions

### Q1 — Adopt "prefer classification over inheritance unless load-bearing" as a principle (+ the load-bearing test)?

**Opening: REVISE.** I will not affirm the principle *as a directional default*. I AFFIRM a principle stated as an **invariant**: *model a discriminator as a coded SKOS facet UNLESS it is a rigid sortal with its own identity criterion and a disjoint property signature, in which case model it as a subclass (and, where a governed value-space is also wanted, ALSO carry the coded facet `skos:exactMatch`-bound — the §8a Substance-Kind-label dual layer).* The "unless load-bearing" clause is acceptable ONLY if "load-bearing" = the ⊥-test (R+ ∧ IC ∧ Δ), NOT "a consumer query exists." With that substitution the principle is sound and merely **restates ODR-0011 §8a** at the methodology level. Without it, REJECT — it is a slogan that will be misapplied to collapse rigid Subkinds.

### Q2 — Collapse the three evidence subclasses into classification, or is inheritance load-bearing (esp. Vouch-Relator)?

**Opening: REJECT the collapse — inheritance is load-bearing, decisively for Vouch.** Run the ⊥-test:

- **VouchEvidence** (`opda-claim.ttl:91-99`): R+ (an attestation is necessarily an attestation), IC (individuated by the *attestation event* — "a vouch has no document content," S035 A9 IC), Δ (`opda:attestedBy → prov:Agent` Vouch-only, `:121`). Moreover it is a **different UFO fundamental category** — an Agent-founded **Relator** (two-relata existential dependence Agent↔Claim; Guizzardi 2005 Ch.4 §4.5), already emitted `a opda:Relator` (`:92`). A single `opda:Evidence` + `evidenceType="Vouch"` treats a Relator as "a document with a different code" — the exact categorial erasure S035 (8–0–0) and ODR-0009 §R5 forbid. **Classification CANNOT carry a category change.** A SKOS code says *which kind of value*; it cannot say *this is a Relator, not an Information-Object*.
- **DocumentEvidence** (`:48-56`): R+/IC/Δ all hold — `⊑ opda:AttachedDocument` (Document-only, `:53`), bearer-Kind IC (content + issuing activity, ODR-0024 §R7). Retain.
- **ElectronicRecordEvidence** (`:58-66`): R+ holds, Δ is *currently thin* (no Document-only property of its own yet; its neutral bearer-Kind is the deferred S035/§R7 symmetry item). This is the ONE subtype where the collapse argument has a *foothold* — and I concede it is the weakest of the three. But it partitions by provenance **origin** (API-retrieved structured record vs paper artefact vs attestation), which is rigid, and ODR-0009's per-subtype obligation (`record.source.name` required, §"SHACL over the PROV structure") is its latent Δ. Retain pending the symmetry completion; do not collapse the strong two to normalise the weak one.

**Disposition: the dual layer (S035's settled outcome) is correct and the proposition's "facet INSTEAD OF inheritance" is REJECTED.** Keep the subclasses as structure-bearers; the `opda:evidenceType` facet is the governed kind-*label* (`skos:exactMatch`-bound, `:55/65/98`), NOT the dispatch discriminator.

### Q3 — Disposition vs the in-tree S035 implementation + ODR-0009/0026.

**Opening: AFFIRM the in-tree state; REJECT any re-collapse.** The TTL already implements S035 correctly: `opda:Evidence a opda:RoleMixin` (`:69`), `opda:VouchEvidence a opda:Relator` (`:92`), three subclasses retained with `skos:exactMatch` (`:55/65/98`), `opda:evidenceType` as the governed facet (`:108-114`), `rdfs:domain` documentary under ODR-0026 §R2 (`:113`). ODR-0009 §Amendment (S035) and ODR-0026 §R3 amendment are consistent and ratified 8–0–0. Session-036's proposition, applied literally, would *reverse* a 2026-06-01 unanimous council decision **one day later** with no new evidence and no named ontological argument — only a general preference. The bar to re-open (ODR-0009 §Amendment re-open trigger: "a named consumer query needing all evidence through one uniform property set, OR a proposal to collapse enforcement into `sh:xone`-over-`evidenceType`") is **not met** by "we prefer classification in general." HOLD against re-collapse; the only legitimate Q3 output is to record the *methodology principle* (Q1, invariant form) that explains *why* S035 retained the subclasses.
