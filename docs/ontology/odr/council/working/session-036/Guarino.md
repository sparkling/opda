# Session-036 — Nicola Guarino — Working Position

**Lens:** OntoClean (Guarino & Welty 2009). The meta-property algebra — rigidity (±R), identity (±I/+O), unity (±U), dependence (±D) — and the formal subsumption constraints those impose. I am the adjudicator of the *load-bearing boundary*: when subsumption is ontologically sound versus when a putative subclass is really a Role, Phase, or value and must be classified.

**Directive under review:** *"Always prefer classification over inheritance where you can"* — i.e. prefer a coded facet on one class over an OWL subclass.

---

## OPENING

### The one-sentence answer

The directive is a **heuristic pointing at a real and common error** (over-subclassing anti-rigid and value-like distinctions), but stated as *"always"* it is **false as a principle** and must be REVISED to its OntoClean-precise form. Inheritance is not a stylistic preference to be minimised — it is the **only** correct vehicle for one specific ontological situation, and classification is the only correct vehicle for the complementary situation. The boundary is not negotiable by taste; it is fixed by the meta-properties of the distinction being drawn. So the rule is not "prefer classification"; it is **"classify unless the distinction is a rigid sortal carrying its own identity criterion, in which case you MUST subclass — and the discriminator is then a *consequence* of the Kind, not a substitute for it."**

### Q1 — The principle and the OntoClean load-bearing test

#### What the directive gets right

The error it targets is real and I have ruled against it repeatedly in this corpus. Modelling a **Role** as a subclass (`opda:Proprietor ⊑ opda:Person`) is an OntoClean violation: a Role is anti-rigid (−R) and **carries no identity of its own** (−I — "a Proprietor has no identity *qua* Proprietor", ODR-0005 §Anti-patterns, my S005 ruling). A subclass asserts that instances *are necessarily* members — but an anti-rigid property has instances that are only *contingently* members, so the IS-A is false in every world where the bearer stops playing the role. The bearer does not cease to exist when it stops being a seller; if "Seller" were a subclass of "Person", the person would have to. That is the formal contradiction OntoClean's rigidity constraint catches: **a +R property cannot subsume a −R one as a rigid IS-A, and an anti-rigid distinction has no business being a class in the subsumption backbone at all** (Guarino & Welty 2009 §4, the rigidity constraint). For those distinctions, a **coded facet / role-relator** is not just acceptable — it is *mandatory*, because subclassing them is the abstraction error of freezing a contingent state into an essential type.

So where the distinction is a **Role, Phase, or Quality value**, the directive is correct and I AFFIRM that half: classify.

#### Where the directive over-reaches — and why it is dangerous

The directive's failure is that it treats the *output* (one class + a coded facet) as universally preferable, when the **ontological cost of collapsing a Kind into a facet is the silent destruction of an identity criterion and a rigid backbone**. This is not a cosmetic loss. It is the precise inverse error of the one it cures.

A **Kind** (or Subkind) is, in OntoClean terms, **+R (rigid), +I (carries its own identity criterion), +U (its own unity criterion)**, and it is a **sortal** (it tells you *how to count* its instances). When you have such a thing and you demote it to a coded value on a parent, you discard exactly the information that made it a Kind:

- You discard the **identity criterion**. A coded value `kind="X"` is a *Quality* of the bearer; it does not carry an IC. But the sub-Kind's whole ontological content may *be* its distinct IC. (This corpus already legislated precisely this: `opda:Property`, `opda:LegalEstate`, `opda:RegisteredTitle` are three Substance Kinds *because each supplies its own IC* — ODR-0005 §2a, "Sortal, Rigid, supplies own IC". You cannot replace `RegisteredTitle` with `propertyKind="registered"` on a single Property class without throwing away "title-number lineage", a genuinely different identity principle from "spatial-material continuity". The proposition's "always prefer classification" would mandate exactly that collapse, and S005 ruled it an *ontology vs. "a schema with RDF syntax"* failure.)
- You discard **rigidity / the modal backbone**. A subclass says *necessarily-a-member*; a facet says *currently-coded-thus*. For a rigid sortal those are different claims and only the first is true.
- You discard **disjoint unity profiles**. Sub-Kinds with different `+U` (a court instrument is unified by its issuing act; an API record by its retrieval transaction) cannot share one bearer's unity without equivocation.

**Therefore the load-bearing test for inheritance (the OntoClean form of the directive):**

> **Subclass IFF the candidate distinction is `+R ∧ +I` — a rigid sortal that supplies its own identity criterion (a Kind/Subkind), AND it carries a unity/property profile (`+U` or a disjoint discriminating-property signature) the parent lacks. Otherwise classify (coded facet). And there is a *third* exit, which the directive entirely omits: if the distinction is `+R` but is `−I` and *externally founded* — i.e. existentially dependent on a mediating relationship (`+D`) — it is a **Relator**, which is neither a subclass nor a classification value but a reified relationship in its own right.**

Restated as the decision procedure, in my meta-property terms:

1. **Is it rigid?** (Does every instance keep the property in every world in which it exists?)
   - **−R (anti-rigid: Role/Phase) → CLASSIFY.** A coded facet (or, if relational, a Role founded by a Relator). Never a rigid subclass. *(Directive correct here.)*
   - **+R → continue.**
2. **Does it supply its own identity criterion distinct from the parent's?** (+I — a *new* way to individuate/count that the parent does not give.)
   - **−I (it is a value/attribute of the bearer, individuated by the bearer's IC) → CLASSIFY.** This is a *Quality* or *Quality-value*, e.g. a colour, an assurance tier, an acquisition-method code. A coded facet is exactly right; a subclass here is the over-subclassing the directive rightly fears.
   - **+I → continue.**
3. **Externally founded / existentially dependent on a relationship?** (+D, two-place — the thing exists only *qua* a mediating relation between two relata.)
   - **+D → RELATOR.** Reify the relationship. Not a subclass; not a classification value either (a value cannot carry the two-relata existential dependence). *(Directive blind to this.)*
   - **−D → it is a genuine Subkind → SUBCLASS.** Inheritance is mandatory and a coded facet would be an abstraction error.

This is *one* test, not three competing ones: it is the standard OntoClean meta-property cascade (Guarino & Welty 2009, Tables 1–2 and §4 constraints), read as a routing function. The directive collapses steps 1–3 into "prefer the facet", which is right only at step-1-and-2-fail and wrong at step-2-pass and step-3-pass.

**Crucial corollary the directive misses entirely:** when step 3 routes to SUBCLASS, the coded facet does **not disappear** — it reappears as a *derived, redundant-by-design discriminator* whose value is **entailed by** the Kind, bound by `skos:exactMatch`, not as the *substitute* for it. Classification and inheritance are then **dual, not exclusive** (FIBO practice; SKOS Reference §3.5.1 — a resource may be both an `owl:Class` and a `skos:Concept`, the spec forcing neither). The honest framing is therefore not "classification *over* inheritance" but **"classification *alongside* inheritance, with the subclass as the home of identity and the facet as the queryable label."** That is exactly what session-035 ruled and what the in-tree `opda-claim.ttl` now emits.

**`Q1: REVISE` — ballot: FOR** (revise to the OntoClean load-bearing test). The directive's heuristic is sound where it bites (−R Roles, −I value-attributes) but as an unconditional "always" it licenses the destruction of identity criteria on rigid sortals and is blind to the Relator exit; the load-bearing rule is the `+R ∧ +I ∧ (+U|disjoint-signature)` subclass test with a `+D → Relator` branch (Guarino & Welty 2009 §4; applied in-corpus at ODR-0005 §2a, ODR-0006, ODR-0024 §R7).

### Q2 — Evidence: collapse to classification, or which subclasses are ontologically mandatory?

I ran this test in session-035 and the in-tree artefact (`opda-claim.ttl`) now embodies the result. Applying the Q1 cascade to each node, the answer is **nuanced and the directive's "collapse to one classified class" is REJECTED** — but so is the naïve "three rigid subclasses" reading. The honest verdict is three different categorial answers:

**The genus `opda:Evidence` itself is NOT a rigid Kind — it is a RoleMixin (−R).** Step 1 fails. Evidence-*hood* is **anti-rigid**: a probate document is evidence only *qua* a `VerificationActivity` using it (the `Student`/`Enrolment` shape — Guarino & Welty 2009 §4 on RoleMixins; my S035 holding, now emitted as `opda:Evidence a opda:RoleMixin`, `opda-claim.ttl:69`). The directive's instinct — "don't make evidence-hood a rigid sortal backbone" — is *correct at the genus*, and the corpus honours it: `Evidence` is a cross-categorial RoleMixin founded by the verification, not a rigid superclass over substances. So far the directive wins.

**But the three sub-distinctions do NOT all behave alike.** Here is the load-bearing split, and it is the heart of my disagreement with a flat "classify everything":

- **`DocumentEvidence` and `ElectronicRecordEvidence`** — these are **role-specialisations whose *bearers* are rigid Information-Object Kinds with their own IC**. The decisive evidence is already in-corpus: `opda:AttachedDocument` is a *rigid Kind* with an explicit IC — "**individuated by its content + issuing activity**, NOT by `documentTypeCode`/`documentDate`" (`opda-claim.ttl:35`; ODR-0024 §R7). `DocumentEvidence ⊑ AttachedDocument ∩ Evidence` (`:53`) — it is the role-bearing subclass of a genuine Kind. An electronic record is individuated by its **source register + retrieval activity** — a *different* IC (provenance origin: `prov:wasDerivedFrom` an API vs. a filed artefact). These two have **disjoint discriminating-property signatures** (a `DocumentEvidence` carries filing metadata `documentDate`/`filedUnder`; a record carries `record.source`). Under my Q1 test the *bearers* are `+R ∧ +I ∧ +U` with disjoint signatures — **so the structure they carry is subclass-worthy and a flat facet would be an abstraction error** (it would force `attestedBy`/`record.source`/`filedUnder` into one undiscriminated property soup — the conditional-soup ODR-0009 §R5 forbids). The *evidence-role* over them is anti-rigid (RoleMixin), but the partition by **provenance origin is rigid** (a thing does not migrate from document-origin to API-origin; cf. Guizzardi's rigidity test on origin-partitions). **Retain the subclasses.**

- **`VouchEvidence` is categorially different again — it is a `+D` Relator, not a subclass *and not a classification value*.** This is the sharpest point and where I must be honest that the directive *over-reaches in both directions*. A vouch is `prov:wasAttributedTo` an Agent (`opda:attestedBy rdfs:domain opda:VouchEvidence → prov:Agent`, `:116–122`); it is an **attestation binding Agent ↔ Claim — two-relata existential dependence**, the signature of a UFO Relator (Guizzardi 2005 Ch.4 §4.5; my S035 holding, now emitted `opda:VouchEvidence a opda:Relator`, `:92`). Step 3 of my cascade fires: **+D → Relator.** A Relator is **not** a coded facet value — a value-space slot cannot carry two-place existential dependence on an Agent and a Claim. So "collapse Vouch to `evidenceType='vouch'` on one Evidence class" is an **ontological category error**: it would dissolve the `attestedBy` dependence into a property of an Information Object that has no Agent-founding. This is the one place I say plainly: **the directive's "always classify" is not merely sub-optimal here, it is wrong in kind.** A Relator is its own thing.

**So the answer to Q2 is the dual layer, exactly as the in-tree artefact has it:**

- **Keep `opda:Evidence` as a RoleMixin** (anti-rigid genus — the directive's correct instinct at the genus level). ✓ emitted `:69`.
- **Keep the three subtypes as structure-bearers**, because (i) Document/Record sit over rigid Information-Object bearer Kinds with disjoint IC and disjoint discriminating-property signatures, and (ii) Vouch is a `+D` Relator that *cannot* be a classification value. ✓ emitted `:53,63,96`.
- **Carry the OIDC4IDA acquisition-*kind* (Document / Electronic-Record / Vouch) as the coded facet `opda:evidenceType`**, `skos:exactMatch`-bound to each subclass (`:55,65,98,108–114`). This is the *classification layer* the directive wants — and it is correct **as a derived, queryable discriminator**, NOT as the dispatch substitute. The acquisition-method axis, considered purely as a value, *is* `−I` (it is a Quality-value, the OIDC `evidence.type` enum) — so a coded facet is the right vehicle *for the kind-label*; my point is only that it must sit **alongside** the subclasses, because the subclasses carry IC and structure the label does not.

The literal proposition — "make evidence-kind a facet *instead of* the subclasses" — **collapses a rigid bearer-IC partition and a Relator into one undiscriminated value-typed class.** I REJECT that. The corpus is already correct.

**`Q2: REVISE` — ballot: FOR** (dual layer; reject "facet instead of"). Collapse the *genus* to a RoleMixin (anti-rigid — directive correct); retain the three subtypes (Document/Record = role-subclasses over rigid +I Information-Object bearers with disjoint unity/signature; Vouch = `+D` attestation Relator — *not* a classification value); carry acquisition-kind as the `skos:exactMatch`-bound `opda:evidenceType` facet *alongside*, never *instead of* (Guarino & Welty 2009 §4; ODR-0005 §2a; ODR-0006; ODR-0009 §R5; ODR-0024 §R7; session-035 8–0–0).

### Q3 — Disposition vs the in-tree session-035 implementation + ODR-0009/0026

The in-tree `opda-claim.ttl` **already implements the session-035 dispositions correctly**, and they are the correct OntoClean answer:

- `opda:Evidence a opda:RoleMixin` (`:69`) — anti-rigid genus. ✓
- `opda:VouchEvidence a opda:Relator` (`:92`) with `opda:attestedBy → prov:Agent` (`:116–122`) — `+D` reified attestation. ✓
- `DocumentEvidence ⊑ AttachedDocument, Evidence` (`:53`); `AttachedDocument` with explicit content+issuing-activity IC (`:35`) — rigid `+I` bearer Kind, role-specialised. ✓
- `opda:evidenceType` coded facet (`:108–114`) + three `skos:exactMatch` links (`:55,65,98`) — classification *alongside* inheritance, the dual layer. ✓
- `owl:equivalentClass` short-name aliases **retired** — ODR-0026 §R3 amendment confirms (`ODR-0026:43`). This was the *right* deletion: the alias made a **rigid-Kind-name (`Document`) co-extensional with an anti-rigid-role-name (`DocumentEvidence`)**, inviting the very Kind/Role conflation OntoClean forbids (my S035 Q1 holding). Retiring it removes the conflation hazard; `skos:altLabel` carries the synonym without the false identity. ✓

**On ODR-0026's "model-but-don't-evaluate" (§R2):** I want to be precise that the evidence case is **not** an instance of R2 and the amendment (`ODR-0026:43`) says so correctly. R2 keeps `rdfs:domain`/`range` as *unevaluated documentation* because there is no safe substitute. The `equivalentClass` alias was different: a safe, evaluable, native substitute existed (`skos:altLabel` + `skos:exactMatch`), so the axiom was *dropped*, not retained-as-inert. That distinction matters for the directive: **the cure for an over-strong axiom is sometimes a coded label (here), but the cure for a missing Kind is never a coded label — it is the Kind.** The directive conflates these; ODR-0026 §R2/§R3 read together draw the line correctly.

**One honest caveat against my own side / the directive's residue.** The directive *does* have a live foothold even after Q2, and I record it as the standing dissent (carried from S035, DA Davis): the `opda:evidenceType` facet must remain a **bound kind-label over retained subclasses**, and SHACL enforcement must ride the retained subclasses / disjoint discriminating properties — it must **not** become `sh:xone`-over-`evidenceType` that dissolves the bearers into conditional soup (ODR-0009 §R5; §"Held-as-live dissent", `ODR-0009:211`). The re-open trigger is unchanged: a named consumer query needing all evidence through one uniform property set. Were such a query to arrive, the *enforcement* could key on the value **without** retracting the subclasses — `skos:exactMatch` makes the two dispatch routes interoperable. That is the directive's legitimate maximal reach: it may govern *enforcement-keying*, never *identity-bearing structure*.

**`Q3: AFFIRM` — ballot: FOR** (the in-tree implementation + ODR-0009 S035 amendment + ODR-0026 §R2/§R3 are correct and require no change). The artefact already embodies the dual-layer OntoClean answer; the only standing item is the held-as-live enforcement-keying dissent + its re-open trigger, which is correctly recorded at `ODR-0009:211`. No new ODR is needed for evidence; session-036's contribution is the *generalised principle* (Q1), which should be recorded as a reusable OntoClean modelling rule (the load-bearing test) so the next "shouldn't this be a facet?" is adjudicated by the cascade, not re-litigated. (Guarino & Welty 2009 §4; ODR-0009 §R5 + S035 amendment; ODR-0026 §R2/§R3.)

## FINAL POSITION

### Q1 — The principle + the OntoClean load-bearing test
**`Q1: REVISE — ballot: FOR`** — Reject the unconditional "always prefer classification." Adopt the **OntoClean load-bearing cascade** (Guarino & Welty 2009 §4), now the panel's canonical form, stated as an **invariant** (per Guizzardi/Allemang — name the invariant, not a directional slogan that gets cargo-culted):

> **(1) −R (anti-rigid Role/Phase) → CLASSIFY** (coded facet; or a Role founded by a Relator if relational). **(2) +R ∧ −I (a Quality-value individuated by the bearer's IC) → CLASSIFY** (the directive's true and largest target — `tenureKind`/`ownerType`/`builtForm`). **(3) +R ∧ +I ∧ +D (externally founded, two-relata existential dependence) → RELATOR** (neither subclass nor classification value — the exit the directive is blind to). **(4) +R ∧ +I ∧ −D → SUBCLASS** (inheritance mandatory; a coded facet would be an abstraction error discarding the IC).**

Rigidity is the **gate** (necessary, not sufficient); **Δ (disjoint signature) ∨ distinct-IC ∨ distinct-lifecycle/PII** is the modeller-facing *operative* earning-test (Allemang/Cagle/Kendall ordering); **+I is the categorial warrant** that explains why the test works and that a *latent* IC (thin-Δ) does not forfeit. Where step 4 fires, the coded facet **reappears alongside** the subclass as a `skos:exactMatch`-bound derived discriminator (dual layer; FIBO; SKOS §3.5.1) — classification *alongside* inheritance, never *instead of*.

**Second leg — the orthogonal-axis / multiplicity constraint (Guizzardi, accepted; grounded by me as single-IC-per-sortal).** The cascade above is a **per-axis** test ("is THIS discriminator subclass-worthy?"). It does not bound how many subclass axes a class may carry at once. That is a *separate*, independent constraint: **at most ONE +I axis may be the inheritance backbone; every other axis is carried as classification (a coded facet) or as a non-sortal partition that supplies NO competing IC — never as a second inheritance dimension.** OntoClean root (deeper than the "Cartesian-explosion / ODR-0024 §R4 landmine" face): the **single-identity-criterion-per-sortal discipline** (Guarino & Welty 2009) — a sortal carries one IC; no individual may be subsumed under two distinct identity-*supplying* supertypes with competing ICs, so **the rigid sortal taxonomy is a TREE, not a LATTICE**. `DocumentEvidenceSubstantial` (evidence-kind × assurance) would assert two competing identity-supplying memberships → forbidden. *Independence is precise:* on the **evidence artefact**, assurance-tier is itself −I (`opda:AssuranceLevel` = `ufoCategory "Quale-in-Region"`, `:30`), so the per-axis cascade *already* classifies it at step 2 → here the multiplicity leg is **confirmatory**. Its genuinely independent bite is the **two-+I-axes** case the cascade structurally cannot adjudicate (both pass step 4): the multiplicity leg picks one backbone, classifies the rest. Both legs belong in the principle ODR.

Codification: amend **ODR-0011 §8a** (which already owns the Substance-Kind-label↔subclass rule and where the dangling-`tenureKind`-prose defect lives) + cross-ref ODR-0008 §Q5a — **not** a new top-level ODR (Kendall's simplicity argument; the rule is what three ODRs already half-state). *(Guarino & Welty 2009 §4 + single-IC discipline; ODR-0005 §2a; ODR-0011 §8a + §Alternatives; ODR-0024 §R4/§R10; ODR-0026 §R2.)*

Vouch's rigid anchor is the *Relator itself* (a Relator is rigid) → no bearer needed. **ElectronicRecord's rigid anchor is a *latent* bearer Kind — there is NO neutral `opda:ElectronicRecord` Kind (verified; S035 Finding-5 asymmetry).** Its latent +I is the IC of that **not-yet-minted neutral bearer** (the deferred §R7 symmetry). So the **re-open trigger is sharpened: mint the neutral `opda:ElectronicRecord` bearer (complete the §R7 symmetry) — which simultaneously supplies the rigid anchor, witnesses the IC, and grounds the `record.source` Δ** (this *supersedes* the looser "emit `record.source.name`" trigger; minting the bearer is the categorial root, the shape is its consequence). Until then documentary-retained — the role-subclass is the placeholder for the pending bearer; SKOS §3.5.1 gives no warrant to *dissolve* a class whose structure is pending rather than absent.
  - **Sharpened by Cagle cross-talk (two levels of redundancy + convergent trigger):** I **concede** Cagle's level-correct point — *as emitted today*, the `ElectronicRecordEvidence` class node carries no triple-level payload the `Electronic-Record` scheme concept lacks (no disjoint property; `record.source` unbuilt; `filedUnder`/`documentDate`/`retrievedOn` are on `AttachedDocument`, the *Document* bearer), so its `skos:exactMatch` IS, **at the information level**, the S035 alias-redundancy in a SKOS hat. But redundancy splits into two levels: **information-level** (Cagle — true now: empty class node) vs **ontological** (me — the class is *warranted* by a latent +I, a world-fact not a byte-payload). The class is **informationally-empty-today but ontologically-warranted** — and ODR-0026 §R2 is *exactly* the construct for that state (authored, unevaluated, identity-bearing, zero closure cost — the `rdfs:domain` treatment). So "facet-only/documentary-pending" (Cagle) and "documentary-retain on latent +I" (me) **name the identical artefact state.** **Hard convergence on the trigger:** Cagle (from SHACL) — "ER stays facet-borne unless a *distinct-identity record-source bearer appears*" (the §R5 obligation lands value-keyed as `sh:qualifiedValueShape`, so only a distinct-identity bearer rescues the *class*); me (from OntoClean) — "mint the neutral `opda:ElectronicRecord` bearer." **Same event, reached from opposite directions.** Residual delta = the *default* only: retain-documentary (me; dominates on churn — delete-then-re-mint is TBox churn + IRI-disruption risk) vs facet-only (Cagle; agnostic on the world-fact). It turns on the recorded fulcrum — *is the source register identity-constitutive for an authoritative record?* (I rule yes: an HMRC record ≠ a credit-bureau record of identical bytes). If the bearer will *never* appear, facet-only wins — a clean recorded re-open condition, not a present disagreement.
  - **Guard against misreading (Isaac-Miles/Baker citation discipline) — subclass-worthiness is PER-MEMBER, not PER-SET.** Retention of the three is **not** warranted by "the OIDC4IDA axis is a closed, regulator-fixed set" (set-completeness/symmetry is the over-retention this cascade exists to resist — `tenureKind` is closed and regulator-fixed too, and is −I → facet-only). Each member is retained on its **own** +I (Vouch +D; Document +I-via-bearer; ElectronicRecord +I-via-latent-bearer); that all three clear it is a *consequence*, not the *reason*. The citable principle is therefore **"identity trumps property-signature parsimony" (per-member +I), NOT "closed/rigid-origin set ⇒ retain the whole set."** Origin-rigidity is the +R *gate*, not the warrant (Guizzardi withdrew "origin ⇒ subclass"); the warrant is +I / distinct individuation.

**The pivotal ruling (answer to Kendall's "is the line between Vouch and the other two?"): there are TWO cuts at two meta-property grains.** Kendall's sort-test sees only the first.
1. **Sort cut (Vouch):** Relator (+D) vs Information-Object. Decisive for Vouch — a value-slot cannot carry +D.
2. **IC cut (Document vs ElectronicRecord):** the finer cut the sort-test misses. **Sameness of UFO sort does NOT entail sameness of identity criterion** — `opda:Person` and `opda:Organisation` are the *same sort* (Substance Kind) yet distinct Kinds with distinct ICs (no one collapses them to a `personhoodKind` facet). Step-4's hinge is **+I, which is finer than sort.** So Doc-vs-ER turns not on "same sort?" (yes, trivially) but on "**same principle of individuation?**" — and the operative test is: *does the discriminator change the principle of individuation, or merely label a value while individuation stays fixed?*
   - **`tenureKind` — individuation INVARIANT** (a Freehold and a Leasehold estate are individuated identically, by the `RegisteredTitle`/HMLR-record IC; tenure is a quality whose identity is fixed elsewhere). **Genuinely −I → classify.** Kendall right.
   - **Evidence acquisition-origin — individuation MOVES** (a Document is individuated by content+issuing-activity; an ElectronicRecord by source-register+retrieval-activity — there is no standalone artefact, the record *is* the register-state-at-retrieval; an HMRC record ≠ a credit-bureau record of identical bytes). **Different individuation principles → +I, which `tenureKind` lacks.** The `tenureKind` analogy *fails* for Doc/ER: tenure leaves individuation fixed; evidence-origin moves it.

So **3 retained, not 1**, via two different cascade routes: Vouch by +D→Relator; Document/Record by +R∧+I∧−D→subclass. The honest fulcrum (recorded for synthesis): *if* an electronic record were held to be individuated merely by content+digest once digested (like a document), acquisition-method would be −I and the collapse correct — I rule the source register is identity-constitutive for an *authoritative* record, but concede this is arguable, which is exactly why ElectronicRecord lands on documentary-retention-with-a-trigger, not settled-keep. Document is settled-keep (its IC is witnessed via the inherited `AttachedDocument` signature + §R7 seam); ElectronicRecord's IC is genuine but unwitnessed (latent).
