### Q1 ‚Äî Adopt the principle + the test

I affirm the principle, and I want to fix its single weak word. "*Where you can*" is unfalsifiable ‚Äî it invites taste. The corpus already owns a crisp, ratified promotion gate; the directive should be re-expressed as its dual.

**The principle (Cagle form):**
> **Default to classification.** A discriminating "kind" axis is modelled as a coded SKOS facet (`opda:ConceptScheme` + a datatype property + `sh:in` via `sh:targetSubjectsOf`) ‚Äî **one class, a value that varies** ‚Äî UNLESS the value earns a class by clearing a structural-need test. A type that supplies no identity or structure beyond its discriminating value **is a structured value, not a class** (my maxim; this is the W3C SHACL idiom the corpus already ships at `ownerType`).

**The test (promote-to-subclass iff ANY):** opda already has it ‚Äî ODR-0008 ¬ßQ4a (`ODR-0008:86`), the leaf‚ÜíClass promotion gate. I propose adopting **¬ßQ4a as the general subclass-admission test**, with one SHACL-specific limb made explicit (¬ßQ4a was written for *leaf*-to-class; a "kind"-value to subclass needs the property-signature limb spelled out):

A "kind" value promotes from facet-member to OWL subclass **iff ANY of**:
- **(a) distinct property signature** ‚Äî the subtype bears a property no sibling bears, *and* that property must be SHACL-enforced *per subtype* (a `sh:qualifiedValueShape` / per-target obligation the supertype shape cannot state); OR
- **(b) distinct identity criterion** ‚Äî the subtype individuates differently from its siblings (a different IC under OntoClean / UFO); OR
- **(c) distinct lifecycle or PII regime** ‚Äî ¬ßQ4a (b)/(c) (issued/superseded/withdrawn; an ODR-0018 sensitivity boundary).

Absent all three, the axis is a facet. **Negative test (the falsifier):** if the only thing the subclass tells you is *which member of the scheme this is*, delete the tree and keep the facet ‚Äî you have re-encoded a `skos:Concept` as an `owl:Class`, the exact defect ODR-0011 ¬ßAlternatives (`ODR-0011:259`) and ODR-0024 ¬ßR4 (`opda:primary`/`private`/‚Ä¶ "are namespace landmines") already reject. This is multiple-inheritance hygiene, not aesthetics: the moment a *second* orthogonal axis appears (evidence-kind **√ó** assurance-tier ‚Äî both live in this corpus, `EvidenceMethodScheme` √ó `AssuranceLevelScheme`), a subclass-per-value tree forces the Cartesian product into the class hierarchy (`DocumentEvidenceSubstantial`, `VouchEvidenceLow`, ‚Ä¶). Faceting exists precisely to keep orthogonal axes orthogonal ‚Äî two coded properties, not N√óM classes. That is Ranganathan's analytico-synthetic core, and it is why the SKOS/SHACL stack is built the way it is.

`Q1: AFFIRM ‚Äî ballot: FOR ‚Äî adopt "default to classification; promote to subclass only on a demonstrated structural need (distinct property signature SHACL must enforce per-subtype, OR distinct IC, OR distinct lifecycle/PII)". This generalises ODR-0008 ¬ßQ4a (ODR-0008:86) from leaf-promotion to kind-promotion, and matches the emitted reality at tenureKind/ownerType/propertyType/builtForm. ODR-0011 ¬ßAlternatives + ODR-0024 ¬ßR4 are the standing precedents; SHACL Core sh:targetSubjectsOf+sh:in is the enforcement idiom (opda-agent-shapes.ttl:51).`

### Q2 ‚Äî Collapse the evidence subclasses, or keep some load-bearing?

Here I apply my own test honestly, subtype by subtype ‚Äî because the test is worthless if I won't run it against the case I want to win. session-035 kept all three; I argued there for the facet. Running the ¬ßQ4a gate on each:

- **`VouchEvidence` ‚Äî KEEP (clears limb (a) AND (b), decisively).** This is the strongest pro-subclass datum in the corpus and I concede it without reservation. It bears `opda:attestedBy ‚Üí prov:Agent` (`opda-claim.ttl:116‚Äì122`, `rdfs:domain opda:VouchEvidence`), a property **no sibling bears**, and that obligation is SHACL-enforced *per subtype* ‚Äî `VouchEvidenceShape sh:targetClass opda:VouchEvidence` ‚Üí `attestedBy sh:minCount 1` (`opda-claim-shapes.ttl:66‚Äì70, 87‚Äì92`). And the IC differs: a vouch is `prov:wasAttributedTo` an Agent ‚Äî an attestation Relator (two-relata existential dependence, Agent‚ÜîClaim; `a opda:Relator`, `opda-claim.ttl:92`), individuated by the attestation, **not** by document content. Document/Electronic-Record are `prov:wasDerivedFrom` Information Objects individuated by content+issuing-activity. **Vouch is a different fundamental category, carrying a distinct, separately-enforced property.** That is not "a discriminating value" ‚Äî it is identity + structure. It earns its class. (This is exactly why session-035's "do NOT collapse" ‚Äî ODR-0009 ¬ßR5, `ODR-0009:206‚Äì207` ‚Äî is *ontologically* correct, not merely cautious. I will not re-litigate it.)

- **`DocumentEvidence` ‚Äî KEEP, but on a *thinner* warrant (limb (b), borderline).** It clears the gate via its bearer-Kind IC, not a distinct enforced property. `opda:DocumentEvidence ‚äë opda:AttachedDocument, opda:Evidence` (`opda-claim.ttl:53`) ‚Äî it sits at the `AttachedDocument ‚Üí DocumentEvidence` role seam ODR-0024 ¬ßR7 deliberately built (an attached document plays the evidence role; the neutral bearer Kind is distinct). That dual parent is genuine structure a facet cannot express: "is-an-attached-document AND is-evidence" is two rigid memberships, not one coded value. So it stays ‚Äî but note the honest weakness: it bears **no DocumentEvidence-specific enforced property** today (no `DocumentEvidenceShape` exists; grep confirms). Its warrant is the bearer-Kind seam, not limb (a).

- **`ElectronicRecordEvidence` ‚Äî this is the one that does NOT clearly clear the bar.** Run the test ruthlessly: it has `rdfs:subClassOf opda:Evidence` and nothing else (`opda-claim.ttl:58‚Äì66`) ‚Äî **no distinct property** (no `record.source` obligation was ever emitted; ODR-0009 ¬ßR5's promised `electronic_record ‚Üí record.source.name` `sh:minCount 1` is *unbuilt*, confirmed: `opda-claim-shapes.ttl` has no such shape), **no distinct bearer Kind** (unlike Document, it has no `AttachedDocument`-style neutral Kind ‚Äî session-035 Finding 5, the "bearer-Kind asymmetry," `session-035:118`), and its IC does not visibly differ from the generic derived-Information-Object IC. **On the emitted artefact, `ElectronicRecordEvidence` supplies no identity or structure beyond `evidenceType = "Electronic-Record"`.** By my own maxim, *that* is a structured value wearing a class's clothes.

So my honest answer to Q2 is **not** "keep all three" and **not** "collapse all three." It is **graduated**:

> Keep `VouchEvidence` (clears (a)+(b), uncontested). Keep `DocumentEvidence` (clears (b) via the ODR-0024 ¬ßR7 bearer-Kind seam). **Flag `ElectronicRecordEvidence` as not currently clearing the bar** ‚Äî it is a facet member masquerading as a class. It earns its subclass the moment ODR-0009 ¬ßR5's promised `record.source.name` per-subtype obligation is actually emitted (limb (a)); until then it is carried on **documentary** warrant only (ODR-0026 ¬ßR2 ‚Äî authored-but-not-evaluated, like a `rdfs:domain`), with a re-open trigger to either emit the obligation or collapse it to the facet.

This is the directive applied with teeth: I am willing to name a subclass *I* would otherwise be tempted to keep, and say it has not yet paid for its place. The facet (`opda:evidenceType` + `EvidenceTypeValueShape`, `opda-claim-shapes.ttl:38‚Äì42`) **already carries the full Document/Electronic-Record/Vouch discrimination** for every consumer that dispatches on value ‚Äî so collapsing ElectronicRecord loses *nothing* a consumer can observe today.

`Q2: REVISE ‚Äî ballot: FOR ‚Äî graduated, not uniform. Keep VouchEvidence (distinct enforced property opda:attestedBy + Relator IC ‚Äî the strongest pro-subclass datum, conceded). Keep DocumentEvidence (ODR-0024 ¬ßR7 AttachedDocument bearer-Kind seam, dual-parent structure).
