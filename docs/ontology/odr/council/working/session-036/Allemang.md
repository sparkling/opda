# Session-036 — Dean Allemang working position

- **Role:** Panellist — Dean Allemang (co-author *Semantic Web for the Working Ontologist*, Allemang & Hendler, 3rd ed. 2020; RDFS-Plus pragmatism; "model only what earns its keep"). Cost/benefit arbiter of the GENERAL RULE.
- **Proposition under review (directing-authority directive):** *"Always prefer classification over inheritance where you can"* — prefer a coded SKOS facet on one class over an OWL subclass tree.
- **Stance:** The directive's **default direction is right** and matches my standing guidance against subclass-explosion. But "always" and "where you can" are doing illegitimate work; the rule must carry a crisp **earn-its-keep exception** or it degrades into the opposite anti-pattern — a coded value that hides a genuinely different *thing*, forcing every consumer into conditional logic a subclass would have given them for free.

---

## OPENING

### The working ontologist's default *is* classification — and the directive is mostly preaching to the choir

I will start by affirming the thing the directive gets right, because it is most of the directive. *Semantic Web for the Working Ontologist* (Ch. 10, "Counting and Sets," and Ch. 13's modelling-discipline material) makes the case plainly: **a class is a commitment, and most distinctions a modeller is tempted to mint a class for are not commitments — they are values.** When I see a modeller reaching for `:Detached rdfs:subClassOf :BuiltForm`, `:BandA rdfs:subClassOf :CouncilTaxBand`, `:RatingG rdfs:subClassOf :EnergyRating`, the working-ontologist reflex is correct and unconditional: **those are not subclasses, they are coded values**, and minting a class for each is the classic over-modelling that bloats the TBox, multiplies the query surface, and buys nothing. ODR-0011 §Alternatives already rejected exactly this ("conscripts a reasoner to police membership of a hand-curated list — machinery without a purpose; misclassifies vocabulary terms as classes"). ODR-0011 §8a's seven-category framework routes **Quale-in-Region** and **Quality Value** schemes (`builtForm`, `councilTaxBand`, `currentEnergyRating`, `ownershipType`, `addressVariant`) to a **flat coded facet with no class at all**. For that entire family — which is the *bulk* of the corpus's enumerations — the directive is not just correct, it is **already the ratified law**. A working ontologist would say: good, the slogan agrees with us here, and here it costs nothing to follow.

So my first finding is a concession to the directive: **for the dominant case (values dressed as kinds), classification is cheaper to consume, cheaper to query, cheaper to extend, and the corpus already does it.** I will not let the dissent obscure that the directive is *right about most of the territory*.

### Where it stops being free: the cost test for the exception

The directive's flaw is not its direction; it is the word **"always."** A coded value is cheaper than a subclass tree **right up until the coded value has to stand in for a thing that carries its own structure** — and at that point the cost ledger flips. This is the part of the directive that, left unqualified, becomes dogma. Let me put the exception the way I'd put it in the book, in **cost terms** (which is my lens; Guizzardi will put the same line in *ontological* terms, and I'll come to where we agree and where I'd codify differently):

> **Default to the coded-value (classification) model. Promote a distinction to a subclass only when the subclass earns its keep — when it homes asserted properties that consumers actually query, or carries a distinct identity, such that collapsing it to a code would force consumers to reconstruct that structure with conditional logic.**

The operative phrase is **"earns its keep."** A class earns its keep when keeping it is *cheaper for the people downstream than not keeping it.* Two concrete cost-signals tell you the class has earned it:

1. **It homes a disjoint property the siblings categorially cannot bear.** If `opda:attestedBy` has `rdfs:domain opda:VouchEvidence` (`opda-claim.ttl:121`) and *only* vouches can bear it, then collapsing `VouchEvidence` into `Evidence` + `evidenceType="Vouch"` doesn't remove the asymmetry — it just **moves it from the schema into every consumer's code**. Every consumer now writes `if evidenceType == "Vouch" then expect attestedBy else expect …`. That is precisely the `if/then`-on-`evidence.type` conditional that ODR-0009 §"SHACL over the PROV structure" exists to *capture in shapes* and that ODR-0009 §R5 forbids collapsing into one undiscriminated pattern. A coded value here is not *simpler* — it is the **same complexity, relocated to where it's most expensive** (N consumers instead of 1 ontology). The working ontologist's rule "model only what earns its keep" cuts **for** the subclass here, because the subclass is *load-bearing structure*, not decoration.

2. **It carries a distinct identity criterion.** `opda:AttachedDocument` is "individuated by its content + issuing activity" (ODR-0024 §R7, `opda-claim.ttl:35`). That is an identity principle, not a label. A code cannot carry an identity criterion; it can only *name* one that lives elsewhere. If the identity is real and consumers re-identify on it, the bearer class earns its keep.

If **neither** signal is present — the distinction homes no disjoint property and carries no distinct identity — then it is a value, and the directive's default wins, unconditionally. That is `builtForm`, `councilTaxBand`, and the rest of §8a's Quale family. **Most distinctions fail the test, which is exactly why the default is right.**

### My one substantive divergence from the DA's framing — and it is a framing point, not an outcome point

Guizzardi (DA) has written the ⊥-test as a **three-conjunct ontological invariant**: Rigidity (R+) ∧ own Identity Criterion (IC) ∧ disjoint property signature (Δ). I have read his opening and I **agree with every disposition it reaches** — including the decisive one on Vouch. But I want to register a working-ontologist's caution about *codifying the test in purely ontological terms*, because the book's whole stance is that **a methodology principle has to be usable by a modeller who is not a formal ontologist**, and "is this discriminator a rigid sortal under the UFO rigidity meta-property?" is not a question a working data-modeller can answer on a Tuesday without a UFO seminar.

Here is the cost-vs-ontology tension stated honestly:

- **Guizzardi's rigidity conjunct (R+) is doing real work I cannot replicate from cost alone.** I concede this up front. Rigidity is what tells you `opda:Evidence` *itself* must NOT be a rigid subclass tree — it is an anti-rigid **RoleMixin** (a bearer is evidence only *qua* a verification using it; `opda:Evidence a opda:RoleMixin`, `:69`). My cost test would not, on its own, have caught that the *role* must be modelled differently from the *bearer kinds*; the rigidity meta-property does. So I do **not** propose to replace his test with mine.
- **But my cost conjuncts (disjoint-property Δ, queried-identity IC) are the ones a working ontologist can actually *apply* — and they happen to be two of his three conjuncts.** Δ and IC are his conjuncts 3 and 2. They are *checkable against the artefact*: grep for a property whose `rdfs:domain` is the subtype and whose siblings can't bear it (`attestedBy`, `:121`); check whether the class asserts an identity principle (ODR-0024 §R7). A modeller can do that. Rigidity they largely cannot.

**My proposed reconciliation (for Q1 / codification):** state the principle with the cost-framed earn-its-keep test as the **operative, modeller-facing rule** (Δ ∨ IC — *does this distinction home a disjoint queried property, or carry a distinct identity?*), and attach the **rigidity meta-property as the upstream ontological warrant** that explains *why* the test works and catches the role-vs-kind case the cost test alone would miss. That is not a rejection of Guizzardi's test — it is the same test, ordered so the cheap-to-check conjuncts lead and the formal-ontology conjunct backstops. The book's readers get a rule they can run; the ontologists get the rigour underneath. I think this is a *friendly amendment* to his ⊥-test, not a counter-proposal, and I'll test that with him in cross-talk.

I explicitly **reject** any test phrased as *"is there a consumer query that dispatches on `rdf:type` today?"* — Guizzardi is right that this is the wrong instrument, and for once the cost-arbiter agrees against the demand-pull instrument. `skos:exactMatch` makes `rdf:type` dispatch and coded-value dispatch return the same answer (S035's reconciliation), so "no query touches the class today" does **not** prove the class is free to delete. The cost that matters is the cost *if you collapse it* — the conditional logic forced onto consumers — not whether a query happens to name the class this week. A queried-disjoint-property is a present cost signal; absence-of-query is not a present licence to collapse.

### Why "always prefer classification" is hazardous even when the answer is "classification"

A working ontologist learns to distrust slogans because **slogans get cargo-culted and invariants get reasoned.** "Prefer classification" as a *default* is fine guidance; "*always* prefer classification" as an *unqualified rule* is the mirror-image of the subclass-explosion error it's trying to cure. The corpus has already been burned by *both* directions: subclass-overreach (the retired `Document ≡ DocumentEvidence` alias, S035; the `opda:primary`/`opda:private` "namespace landmines," ODR-0024 §R4) *and* the temptation to flatten genuinely distinct things. The remedy is not to swing the pendulum to "always classify" — it is to name the **invariant** (earn-its-keep: disjoint-queried-property ∨ distinct-identity, with rigidity as warrant) so a modeller reasons each case instead of applying a reflex. I'd put it in the book as: *the question is never "class or code?" in the abstract; it is "what does this distinction cost a consumer if I make it a code?"*

## Per-question OPENING positions

### Q1 — Adopt the principle + the earn-its-keep exception test?

**Opening: AFFIRM the principle as a *default* + REVISE the test to a cost-led, ontology-backstopped form.**

I AFFIRM: *default to the coded-value / classification model.* This is correct, matches *Semantic Web for the Working Ontologist*'s "model only what earns its keep," and is already ratified for the §8a Quale/Quality-Value families. I REVISE the directive's wording from "**always** prefer classification" to "**prefer classification by default, promote to subclass when it earns its keep**," because "always" is dogma. The earn-its-keep exception, modeller-facing form: *promote a distinction to a subclass when it homes a disjoint property consumers query (Δ) OR carries a distinct identity (IC); otherwise model it as a coded facet.* The **rigidity meta-property (R+) is the upstream warrant** that explains why the test holds and catches the anti-rigid role-vs-kind case (`opda:Evidence` RoleMixin) the cost test alone would miss — this is my friendly amendment to Guizzardi's ⊥-test, not a competitor to it. With the cost-led test as operative and rigidity as warrant: AFFIRM. As a bare directional slogan with "always" and no exception: REJECT. *Allemang & Hendler 2020 Ch. 10/13; ODR-0011 §8a; ODR-0011 §Alternatives.*

### Q2 — Evidence: collapse to classification, or keep load-bearing subclasses?

**Opening: REJECT the collapse — the subclasses earn their keep; decisively for Vouch.** Run the cost test:

- **VouchEvidence** (`opda-claim.ttl:91-99`): homes `opda:attestedBy → prov:Agent`, Vouch-**only** (`rdfs:domain opda:VouchEvidence`, `:121`) — a disjoint property the siblings categorially cannot bear. Collapsing to `evidenceType="Vouch"` does not remove this asymmetry; it relocates it into N consumers as `if Vouch then expect attestedBy` — the conditional soup ODR-0009 §R5 forbids and §"SHACL over the PROV structure" captures in shapes instead. **Cost flips hard against the collapse.** And — conceding Guizzardi's point that cost alone can't see this — it is also a *different UFO category* (`a opda:Relator`, `:92`; an Agent-founded attestation, two-relata dependence). A coded value can say *which kind*; it cannot say *this is a Relator, not an Information-Object*. Both lenses converge: **retain.**
- **DocumentEvidence** (`:48-56`): homes `⊑ opda:AttachedDocument` (Document-only, `:53`) and carries a distinct identity (content + issuing activity, ODR-0024 §R7). Δ and IC both present. **Retain.**
- **ElectronicRecordEvidence** (`:58-66`): the honest weak case. Its Δ is currently *thin* — no ElectronicRecord-only emitted property yet (the neutral-bearer-Kind symmetry is the deferred S035/§R7 item). Its latent Δ is ODR-0009's per-subtype obligation (`electronic_record` requires `record.source.name`, §"SHACL over the PROV structure"). On pure present-cost I'd flag this as the one subtype where the collapse argument has a foothold — but I will **not collapse the strong two to normalise the weak one**, and the partition-by-provenance-origin (API-retrieved structured record vs paper artefact vs attestation) is rigid (Guizzardi's R+), so retain pending the symmetry completion. **Retain, with the thin-Δ caveat recorded.**

**Disposition: the dual layer (S035's settled outcome) is correct.** Keep the `…Evidence` subclasses as structure-bearers; the `opda:evidenceType` facet (`:108-114`, `skos:exactMatch`-bound `:55/65/98`) is the governed kind-**label**, NOT the dispatch discriminator. The proposition's "facet *instead of* inheritance" is REJECTED for evidence; the proposition's *default* survives only for the kind-label *axis*, which is exactly where S035 already applied it. *ODR-0009 §R5 + §Amendment (S035); ODR-0024 §R7; Allemang & Hendler 2020 (cost of conditional logic).*

### Q3 — Disposition vs the in-tree S035 implementation; codify where?

**Opening: AFFIRM the in-tree state; codify the Q1 principle at the methodology level; do NOT re-collapse.**

The TTL already implements S035 correctly and the in-tree state passes both my cost test and Guizzardi's ⊥-test: `opda:Evidence a opda:RoleMixin` (`:69`), `opda:VouchEvidence a opda:Relator` (`:92`), three retained subclasses with `skos:exactMatch` (`:55/65/98`), `opda:evidenceType` documentary-`rdfs:domain` facet (`:108-114`), ODR-0026 §R3 amendment + ODR-0009 §Amendment both ratified 8–0–0 on **2026-06-01**. Applying session-036's proposition *literally* would reverse a unanimous council decision **one day later** with no new evidence and no named cost or ontological argument — only a general preference. The ODR-0009 §Amendment re-open trigger ("a named consumer query needing all evidence through one uniform property set, OR a proposal to collapse enforcement into `sh:xone`-over-`evidenceType`") is **not met** by "we prefer classification in general." HOLD against re-collapse.

The legitimate Q3 output is **codification of the methodology principle (Q1)** — the earn-its-keep rule that *explains why* S035 retained the subclasses — not a re-litigation of the evidence model. **Where to codify (post-cross-talk, strengthened):** the principle is `kind: pattern` / methodology-level and belongs as an **amendment to ODR-0011 §8a**, not a standalone ODR — because §8a is *already* the corpus's "coded facet alone vs coded facet + bound subclass" adjudication, AND because (verified in cross-talk, see below) **§8a's own worked exemplar `tenureKind` is a live instance of the very defect the principle cures**: it mandates OWL subclasses + `skos:exactMatch` bindings that the artefact never emitted. Adopting the directive in earn-its-keep form therefore does not merely *generalise* §8a — it **forces a correction of a live §8a defect**. The codification edit is two-in-one: (a) restate the §8a Substance-Kind-label row as *coded facet on the one Kind by default; subclass + `skos:exactMatch` ONLY where the R+ gate passes AND (Δ ∨ IC) holds*, dropping the unconditional cross-scheme "MUST carry `skos:exactMatch` to the corresponding sub-class" check (it is currently false in the artefact and would cargo-cult modellers into minting the empty subclasses ODR-0024 §R10 refused for `opda:Building`); (b) correct the two false annotations (§8a prose + `opda-property.ttl:699` comment) to state `tenureKind` is a coded facet that earns no subclass. *ODR-0011 §8a; ODR-0009 §Amendment re-open trigger; ODR-0026 §R3; ODR-0024 §R10.*

## Post-cross-talk amendment — the §8a `tenureKind` defect (verified)

Kendall's keystone, verified against the artefact, materially strengthens the Q1/Q3 codification case and is recorded as an independent finding (route regardless of the evidence outcome):

- **Leg 1 — mandated subclasses absent.** No `opda:Freehold` / `opda:Leasehold` / `opda:Commonhold` `owl:Class` exists anywhere in `source/` (grep, exit 1, zero matches). ODR-0011 §8a's prose ("bound to OWL sub-class hierarchy via `skos:exactMatch`") and its cross-scheme consistency check ("each scheme member MUST carry `skos:exactMatch` from the SKOS concept to the corresponding OWL sub-class") mandate classes the generator never emits.
- **Leg 2 — `skos:exactMatch` not carried.** `opda-vocabularies.ttl:2664-2686`: the three `TenureKindScheme` concepts carry `skos:prefLabel`/`skos:definition`/`dct:source`/`skos:inScheme`/`skos:notation` and **no `skos:exactMatch`**. The emitted artefact silently declines §8a's MUST — the *correct* modelling outcome, but it means the artefact contradicts §8a's prose.
- **Leg 3 — false annotation.** `opda:tenureKind` is `owl:DatatypeProperty` (`opda-property.ttl:697`) + `rdfs:domain opda:LegalEstate` (`:701`) + `sh:in` on `LegalEstate` (`opda-property-shapes.ttl:96-97`) — a coded-only facet — yet its `rdfs:comment` (`:699`) asserts "Each member binds to its corresponding OWL sub-class via `skos:exactMatch` per ODR-0011 §8a." It does not.

**Why `tenureKind` correctly resolved to a facet (the principle, applied):** it fails earn-its-keep on **both** conjuncts — no Freehold-only property (Δ fails), and a freehold converted to leasehold is the *same* legal estate, so a tenure subclass would be a **rigidity violation** (anti-rigid; fails the R+ gate). The artefact got the model right and the documentation wrong. This is the **same S035 defect class** (annotation/axiom asserting structure the artefact never performs) re-instantiated in §8a's own worked exemplar.

## Convergence-close (Guizzardi reply 2 — adopted, no further round)

Guizzardi accepted the cost-led / rigidity-warrant form and closed his side ("no further rounds"). Two refinements adopted into the codified principle (they tighten, not reopen):

1. **The R+ warrant is NORMATIVE with veto power, not decorative.** A modeller who finds (Δ ∨ IC) satisfied but the distinction is *anti-rigid* (a Role — Seller, Evidence-hood) must still be **stopped** by the gate. Codified form, adversary-proofed: *"promote to subclass iff (Δ ∨ distinct-IC ∨ distinct-lifecycle/PII) AND NOT anti-rigid; rigidity is the upstream warrant that (i) explains why the operative test works and (ii) vetoes the −R case the cost test alone would wave through."* This keeps the modeller-facing operativeness of (Δ ∨ IC) **and** the role-vs-kind backstop with teeth. The full codified principle also carries Guarino's +D→Relator exit and the orthogonal-axis warrant (classify axes 2..n always).
2. **ElectronicRecord — honest status sharpened (Guizzardi/Guarino, adopted over my own "promissory-Δ").** The test gates on **+I (categorial identity — read off the *world*)**, not Δ (realisation — read off the *shapes file*). ElectronicRecord **IS +I**: source-register + retrieval-activity individuation, which holds whether or not a shape exists. So it is a **warranted subclass that is realisation-incomplete, not promissory** (the §R10 `opda:Building` latent-IC mirror) — its IC limb carries the warrant even though its bearer Kind / `record.source` / shape are unbuilt (S035 Finding-5) and its Δ is the unbuilt ODR-0009 `record.source.name` obligation. **The demotion trigger is therefore +I failing the same-IC-different-channel defeater** ("is this just a document arriving via a different channel?") — which ER **survives** — **NOT** "Δ stays thin after the §R5 obligation emits." (My earlier "promissory-Δ" mis-attached the trigger to the Δ limb; a *generically*-shaped `record.source.name` obligation would wrongly demote a genuinely +I Kind under that wording, which the +I form forecloses.) Disposition unchanged: retained on **documentary warrant (ODR-0026 §R2)**, retention free (`skos:exactMatch` ⇒ no consumer-visible difference), the re-open trigger authorising **EMISSION** (bearer-Kind → realises IC; `record.source.name` → realises Δ), **not deletion**. This adopts the IC/+I limb of my own R+→(Δ ∨ IC) test — a clarification of which limb carries ER, not a change to the test.

The conjunctive/disjunctive split does not bite once Guizzardi withdrew his AND: the operative test is disjunctive (Δ ∨ distinct-IC ∨ lifecycle) **beneath** the rigidity-veto gate. Three-way convergence (Allemang / Guizzardi / Kendall) is complete; loop closed.
