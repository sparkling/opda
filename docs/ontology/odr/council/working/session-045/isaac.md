## Verified findings I rely on (checked against the committed artefacts, not just the brief)

I read `public/ontology/artefacts/source/opda-vocabularies.ttl`, `opda-shapes-merged.ttl`, both extractors, and the committed `ontology-model.json` / `ontology-graph-elements.json`. Three facts the brief's "0 broader ‚Üí flat stars" framing under-states:

1. **The schemes are NOT undifferentiated stars ‚Äî they assert top concepts.** The source TTL carries **16 `skos:topConceptOf`** triples (CurrencyScheme, PerilScheme: "all 12 perils are top concepts"). Per the **SKOS Reference ¬ß8.6 (`skos:hasTopConcept` / `skos:topConceptOf`)**, declaring top concepts is the *correct* way to root a scheme; a scheme where every concept is a top concept with no `broader` is a **legitimate flat scheme** (SKOS Primer ¬ß2.3). The scopeNotes are explicit and principled ("the data dictionary attests no enumerated sub-peril value-set, so all 12 perils are top concepts"). This is documented authoring, not an accidental gap.

2. **The extractor SILENTLY DROPS those 16 top-concept relations.** `scripts/ontology-model.mjs:184` queries only `?s skos:hasTopConcept ?top` (scheme‚Üíconcept). The source asserts the **inverse** `skos:topConceptOf` (concept‚Üíscheme) ‚Äî and there are **0 `skos:hasTopConcept`** triples. SKOS Reference ¬ß8.6.2 defines `skos:topConceptOf` as a *sub-property* of `skos:inScheme` and the inverse of `skos:hasTopConcept`; an extractor that does not materialise the inverse loses real structure. Result: model shows `topConcepts: []` on all 49 schemes. The graph therefore cannot show a scheme's roots even where the source declares them.

3. **The class‚Üívocabulary binding the model documents in prose is NOT in the RDF.** Definitions/scopeNotes say members bind to OWL (sub)classes "via `skos:exactMatch` (NEVER `owl:sameAs`)" ‚Äî but there are **0 actual `skos:exactMatch` triples** asserted anywhere in the source. And the **`sh:in` enumerations bind bare string literals** (`"Document" "Electronic-Record" "Vouch"`), **not** the `skos:Concept` IRIs (`‚Ä¶/scheme/evidenceMethod/Document`). So the scheme and the shape are *two parallel encodings of one value-space with no RDF edge between them*. The class‚Üîscheme linkage the brief wants to "recover" largely **does not exist as data** ‚Äî it lives in NL annotations and in duplicated `sh:in` strings.

## OPENING ‚Äî per-question positions + ballots + citations

### Q1 ‚Äî Class‚Üívocabulary bridge (draw class‚Üíscheme from `sh:in` / `‚ÜíConcept`)
**Verdict: REVISE. Ballot: AGAINST the `sh:in`-derived edge as proposed; FOR a different, faithful bridge.**

Two distinct objections, both grounded:

- **(a) A `sh:in` enumeration cannot faithfully yield a class‚Üí*scheme* edge here, because `sh:in` does not reference the scheme or its concepts.** SHACL Core ¬ß4.6.1 (`sh:in`) constrains a node's value to a member of a *given RDF list of terms*; in this ontology those terms are **plain strings**, not the `skos:Concept` IRIs. Drawing `class ‚Üí PerilScheme` off a `sh:in ("Climate" "CoalMining" ‚Ä¶)` shape is inferring an edge to a resource the constraint never mentions ‚Äî a fabricated relation, not a recovered one. That is a category error twice over: (i) a SHACL constraint is a *validation* statement, not an asserted ontological relation (SHACL Core ¬ß1.2 ‚Äî shapes describe conditions data must meet); (ii) even granting a derived predicate, the *target* (the scheme) is not in the constraint.
- **(b) The clean, faithful idiom already has a name in my spec, and it is missing from the data.** The W3C-sanctioned way to say "this coded attribute's values are the members of this scheme" is to **make the value an `skos:Concept` and assert `skos:inScheme`** (SKOS Reference ¬ß8.1‚Äì8.2), and to relate the *class* to the *scheme* either by ranging the property at the concepts (`rdfs:range` of a coded object-property = the concept class / scheme membership) or by an explicit annotation property. The model extractor's `usesSchemes` already checks `rdfs:range ‚àà schemes` (`ontology-model.mjs:274‚Äì276`) ‚Äî it returns 0 **because no coded property is typed that way**: the coded values were modelled as `sh:in` strings, bypassing the concepts entirely.

**So my REVISE is: do not synthesise a class‚Üíscheme edge from `sh:in`. Fix the *source* so the bridge is real** ‚Äî either (i) author `sh:in` over the **concept IRIs** (`sh:in ( <‚Ä¶/peril/Climate> ‚Ä¶ )`) so the constraint genuinely references the scheme's concepts, or better (ii) introduce an explicit `opda:usesScheme` (sub-property of nothing load-bearing; a documented annotation) asserted on the class, and *then* the extractor draws it. An edge must trace to an asserted triple. Surfacing a constraint-derived guess as a relation would make the graph lie. (SKOS Reference ¬ß1.3: SKOS is for asserting the organisation of concepts; SHACL is orthogonal.)

### Q2 ‚Äî Unconnected classes (relator-mediation / type-to-gUFO edges)
**Verdict: ABSTAIN (defer to Guizzardi/Hendler/Knublauch).** This turns on UFO relator semantics and OWL/gUFO typing, not on SKOS. I hold no SKOS-grounded position on whether to surface relator-mediation edges among the 25 orphan OWL classes. I will only note, *in support of the doctrine*, that the SKOS layer's flatness (Q3) and the OWL layer's lack of `subClassOf` are **two different design facts** and must not be conflated in argument: a flat SKOS scheme is correct-by-spec; a disconnected OWL TBox is a separate question I leave to the foundational-ontology voices.

### Q3 ‚Äî Layer separation vs one canvas (**my decisive question**)
**Verdict: REVISE ‚Üí strongly toward separation. Ballot: AGAINST the single unified node-link canvas for the SKOS layer; FOR presenting SKOS separately as a tree/list keyed on `inScheme`/`topConceptOf`.**

- A **force-directed node-link graph is the wrong instrument for a SKOS vocabulary.** A concept scheme is a (poly)hierarchy / faceted term list, navigated by `broader`/`narrower`/`inScheme` (SKOS Reference ¬ß8). The canonical, accessible renderings are **trees, sunbursts, indented lists, or alphabetical term registers** ‚Äî not a physics simulation. Forcing 323 concepts + 49 schemes through Cytoscape, where `inScheme` is the *only* edge, yields exactly what the operator saw: **49 disconnected stars that dominate ~90% of the nodes and drown the 40-class OWL backbone**. The SKOS Primer (¬ß2, ¬ß3) and every production thesaurus UI I have built or reviewed (Europeana's multilingual vocabularies; the EuroVoc / AGROVOC / GEMET publishing pattern) present concept schemes as **browsable hierarchies/lists**, because that is what `broader`/`narrower`/`topConceptOf` *are for*.
- **The unification is a layer-confusion.** **SKOS Concepts are NOT OWL classes** ‚Äî SKOS Reference ¬ß1.3 and the SKOS/OWL note are explicit that `skos:Concept` instances are *units of thought / terms*, asserted in the **A-box-like assertional layer relative to the SKOS data model**, whereas `owl:Class` lives in the domain **T-Box**. Putting `owl:Class`, `skos:Concept`, `sh:NodeShape`, and gUFO meta-categories on one untyped canvas conflates **terminological/domain classes** with **controlled vocabulary values** with **validation shapes** ‚Äî three different epistemic kinds. They should be **explicitly typed by an `opda:layer` dimension and shown as toggleable, grouped layers** (the REVISE), and ideally the SKOS layer should get its *own* idiom (tree/sunburst) rather than node-link at all (the stronger position).
- **Concretely:** keep the OWL class graph as a class diagram / node-link of `owl:Class` + object properties (that is the right instrument for a T-Box). Render the SKOS layer **separately** as a per-scheme tree rooted on `topConceptOf` (once the extractor stops dropping it ‚Äî see my finding #2), falling back to a flat member list where the scheme is legitimately flat. Do **not** merge them on one canvas; if a combined "everything" view is kept, gate SKOS behind a layer toggle that is **off by default**, as the page already half-does.

### Q4 ‚Äî Surfacing the mass / "too few entities"
**Verdict: AFFIRM (SKOS portion). Ballot: FOR "40 classes is correct-by-doctrine, communicate better".**

On the SKOS-specific slice of Q4: the **323 concepts are correctly NOT promoted to OWL subclasses** ‚Äî coded value-spaces belong in SKOS, not in the class tree (SKOS Reference ¬ß1.2; SKOS Primer ¬ß1 ‚Äî SKOS exists precisely so KOS terms need not be ontology classes). So the "thinness" of 40 classes is partly *because* the value-mass was correctly pushed into SKOS, which is right. The remedy is **presentational, not structural**: surface the 323 concepts in their own scheme-browser (Q3), and show per-class which schemes it draws on (Q1, *once the binding is real*). I ABSTAIN on whether datatype properties / SHACL shapes should be surfaced as nodes ‚Äî that is Knublauch's and Cagle's call; my view is only that SKOS concepts must be browsable as a vocabulary, not scattered as graph nodes.
