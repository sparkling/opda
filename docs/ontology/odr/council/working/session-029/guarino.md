# Session 029 (R2 reconvened) — Nicola Guarino, foundational-rigor check

**Lens:** OntoClean (Guarino & Welty, "An Overview of OntoClean", *Handbook on Ontologies*, 2nd ed., 2009) and DOLCE — the meta-properties rigidity (+R/−R/~R), identity (+I), unity (+U) and dependence (+D) as formal constraints on the *correctness of a taxonomy and its partitions*. My charge here is narrow and, I think, decisive: to separate two questions the proposition runs together. Is "is a Quality" vs "is a Mode" a **rigid typing of an attribute-universal** — something one may legitimately assert with `rdf:type`? And separately: is that distinction a sound **backbone partition key**, fit to be carved into permanent w3id.org module IRIs? OntoClean's central methodological lesson is that these are *not* the same test. A property can be perfectly rigid (+R) and still make a bad subsumption or partition backbone if its assignment is not *settled for every member* and if it does not carry the *identity and unity* the partition is supposed to track. My verdict is that the Quality/Mode distinction is real and, in the clean cases, rigid — and that this is precisely *not enough* to license the split. A backbone partition must be clean for **all** its members; the corpus's own flagship cases (`priceQualifier`, `ownershipType`) show it is not. I therefore stand with Davis on the disposition, but for a foundational reason he states only pragmatically: the violation is an OntoClean partition violation, not merely an absent consumer query.

## Q1 — Load-bearing? **REJECT**

Load-bearingness is not my native axis — Allemang owns the query test and I defer to it — but OntoClean supplies an independent corroboration, so I do not abstain. The §Q6a "reasoner-independence" test (UNION-over-children must equal the entailed-parent answer-set, else the hierarchy is decorative) is the *taxonomic* face of exactly the discipline OntoClean imposes: a distinction earns structural status only when it does formal work. No tabled query consumes a Quality-vs-Mode entailment; turning the axis off changes no answer-set. **(ungrounded as to the query economics — that is Allemang's evidence)**, but on my own ground I add: even were a query tabled, it could be satisfied by an `rdf:type` filter without a module boundary, so Q1 cannot by itself carry the spawn. REJECT, concurring with Allemang's threshold and Davis's reading of it.

## Q2 — Valid partition key? **REJECT** — the distinction is rigid where it is clean, but the partition is not clean for all members, and that is fatal.

This is my question, and I want to be exact, because the careless answer ("it re-sorts, therefore it is anti-rigid, therefore reject") is *wrong* and would mislead the panel. Quality and Mode are not roles. Per DOLCE and Guizzardi's own UFO, a Quality (e.g. the EPC band inhering in `opda:Property`) and a Mode are both **rigid** universals: `currentEnergyRating` does not *cease* to be a quality-kind individual under any counterfactual in which its bearer survives — there is no possible world in which that universal is instantiated yet fails to be a quality. By the OntoClean definition (Guarino & Welty 2009, §"Rigidity": a property φ is rigid, +R, iff necessarily every instance of φ is necessarily an instance of φ), the *type assignment is rigid in the clear cases*. So I tell Guizzardi plainly: you are right that the meta-category is real and rigid, and Davis overstates if he calls it anti-rigid (~R). It is not role-like. **One may sound-mindedly assert `gufo:Quality a` on the EPC/council-tax leaves.**

But rigidity of the typing is the wrong test for a *partition backbone*, and here is where Davis's "re-sorts under refinement" objection is correct once restated in OntoClean terms. A partition key must satisfy three conditions, not one: it must be (i) rigid, (ii) **carry a single identity criterion (+I) and unity criterion (+U) uniform across the partition cells**, and (iii) be **defined — assigned to exactly one cell — for every member**, with no member straddling two cells and none migrating cell under refinement (Guarino & Welty 2009, the constraints "+I cannot subsume −I", "incompatible identity criteria cannot share a subsuming type", and the disjointness obligation on a partition). The corpus violates (iii) and signals a violation of (ii):

- **`priceQualifier` is tagged in §Q5a as "Mode / Quality Value" — one leaf, two cells.** That is not a borderline I can adjudicate away; it is a *straddler at the source of truth*. A partition in which a member belongs to two cells at once is, by definition, not a partition. A w3id.org boundary literally cannot be drawn through it: either `property-qualities` and `property-modes` both claim it (overlap → not disjoint) or neither does (incomplete cover). Both break the backbone.
- **`ownershipType` is a Quale-in-Region whose `rdfs:domain` is `opda:LegalEstate`** (§Q5a row 1's "(or `opda:LegalEstate` for ownership)" branch; ODR-0024 R1). So it is a *quality* by UFO type yet a *legal-estate-attribute* by bearer — Allemang's axis and Kendall's axis claim the same leaf. This is the diagnostic signature of an **identity-criterion conflict (ii)**: the proposed partition is trying to ride two *different* identity-bearing principles at once (the inhering quality-kind vs the bearer Kind's identity), and OntoClean forbids a single backbone from conflating incompatible +I sources.

So my ruling on Davis's objection, with the precision the instruction asks for: the leaves do **not** re-sort because the *typing* is anti-rigid — it is not. They re-sort because the *partition key is under-determined and bearer-entangled at the margins*, and a backbone partition must be clean for **all** members, not most. A near-clean partition is, for module-boundary purposes, a broken one — the same way a taxonomy with one +I/−I subsumption violation is unsound however many clean links it also has (Guarino & Welty 2009). REJECT as a partition key. The distinction earns `rdf:type`; it does not earn a namespace.

## Q3 — Module reality. **REJECT** — c is not a module; a/b lack the typing pass their partition presupposes.

OntoClean reinforces each of Davis's three sub-findings on foundational grounds. **ODR-0008c** ("legal-estate-attributes") partitions by `rdfs:domain opda:LegalEstate` — i.e. by the *bearer Kind's* identity criterion, which is already carried by the domain assertion in the emitted TTL. A module here would re-encode an existing +I partition as an IRI boundary, buying no new identity or unity; it is `SELECT ?p WHERE { ?p rdfs:domain opda:LegalEstate }`, not a sortal module. **ODR-0008a/b** presuppose a Quality-vs-Mode `rdf:type` partition over the 69 `opda:Property` leaves that the walk *did not compute* (ODR-0024 R12 typed by bearer, not by UFO axis). To mint the namespace before that typing exists is to assert a partition with no partitioned content — the inverse of method: structure must follow the identity/unity analysis, never precede it. And the split reaches at most ~94 of 164 leaves; the ~70 others already sit under bearer Kinds owned by ODR-0006/0007/0008d. A backbone that partitions under 60% of its domain and delegates the rest is not a modularization of the universe of descriptive attributes — it is a partial overlay on three Kinds, and OntoClean asks a backbone to be *exhaustive over what it claims to sort*.

## Q4 — Disposition. **REJECT the UFO-axis split; DEFER with a sharpened, OntoClean-grounded trigger.**

Keep ODR-0008 monolithic; do not spawn a/b/c. I concur with Davis's disposition and sharpen the trigger from the partition side. The candidate axis that is *actually* clean here is **bearer Kind** (Property vs LegalEstate vs Search …) — because each bearer Kind supplies its own +I/+U, and the walk already partitioned losslessly along it via `rdfs:domain`. That is Kendall's attachment-class reading, and it is the identity-respecting alternative the instruction invited me to prefer: it sorts by a *sortal that carries identity*, which is what a backbone partition is *for*, whereas "is-a-Quality vs is-a-Mode" sorts attributes by their inherence-category, which is a rigid *typing* but not a *sortal-identity* backbone. **Sharpened spawn trigger (both conjuncts required):** spawn ODR-0008a/b/c only when (i) a named consumer query needs an entailment true on one UFO axis and false on another (Allemang's test), **AND** (ii) the leaves it ranges over carry a committed gUFO `rdf:type` partition in the corpus such that **every** such leaf is assigned to exactly one axis with no straddler — i.e. `priceQualifier` and `ownershipType` have each been adjudicated to a single cell by a stated rigid rule. Conjunct (ii) is the OntoClean disjointness/identity gate; without it the boundary is drawn through a non-partition.

## Q5 — Preserve the insight? **REVISE** — assert it as `rdf:type` on the clean leaves; this is the *correct* home, not a consolation prize.

The whole force of my Q2 is that the Quality/Mode distinction is *rigidly true of the clear leaves* — so it belongs in the ontology, asserted as `gufo:Quality` (and, where settled, `gufo:Mode`) via `rdf:type` on the uncontested leaves (the EPC/council-tax/built-form Quale-in-Region set), exactly as ODR-0008d typed the authority-retrieved artefacts as UFO Information Objects *by `rdf:type`, not by module boundary* — the governing precedent (BACKGROUND §6). Crucially, and against any move to over-formalise: **omit the straddlers** (`priceQualifier`) and bearer-entangled re-sorters (`ownershipType`) from the typing until a rigid rule adjudicates them — asserting a contested `rdf:type` would manufacture the very identity conflict I am flagging. I agree with Davis that SHACL is the wrong reach (a shape presumes a validator that does not exist). An `rdf:type` assertion records a rigid fact; it commits no permanent IRI, is recoverable by query, and is reversible if a typing later proves bearer-dependent. That is OntoClean discipline: state what is metaphysically settled, withhold what is not, and never let a clean majority license a partition the margins cannot support.

---

**Net:** The Quality/Mode distinction is **real and rigid (+R) where it is clean**, and Guizzardi is right that it is not role-like — Davis's "anti-rigid" framing, taken literally, is too strong. But rigidity of a *typing* is not soundness of a *partition backbone*. A backbone partition must carry uniform identity (+I) and unity (+U) and be defined for **every** member with no straddler; `priceQualifier` (two cells) and `ownershipType` (quality-by-type, legal-estate-by-bearer) show the UFO axis fails the disjointness and identity gates on the corpus's own flagship cases. One unclean member voids a backbone. **REJECT the module split; keep ODR-0008 monolithic; assert the rigid cases with `gufo:Quality`/`gufo:Mode` `rdf:type`, omitting the contested leaves until a rigid adjudication rule exists.**
