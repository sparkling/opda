# Session 029 (ODR-0023 R2) — Elisa Kendall, `legal-estate-attributes` steward

*Opening.* I am the steward the spawn-rule names for ODR-0008c, so the panel is owed my honest read of whether the module I would own is real — and it is not, not as a UFO-axis module. My discipline is FIBO's: you modularize an ontology along **business-domain / subject-matter boundaries** and you **reuse aggressively before you mint** (Bennett 2013, *FIBO*; Kendall & McGuinness 2019, *Ontology Engineering* §5, on modularization governed by competency and subject matter, not by metaphysical kind). FIBO has thousands of qualities and relators scattered across its modules; it has never once minted an "all-the-qualities" module set against an "all-the-modes" module set, because meta-category is not a subject-matter boundary and carries no reuse story. The walk did not type a single leaf `gufo:Quality` or `gufo:Mode` — it typed by **bearer Kind** (`rdfs:domain`), which is the one observable, subject-matter-adjacent key in the corpus. So the question in front of me is whether to wrap three permanent w3id.org namespaces around an analytic distinction the corpus never computed, for ~94 in-scope leaves of which a domain filter already isolates 25. As the steward, I decline. The genuinely useful cut here is the one I have held as live since S008 — **attachment-class**, Property-borne vs LegalEstate-borne — and it is *already realised* by `rdfs:domain`, needs no UFO axis, and needs no module spawn. I align with Allemang and Davis: REJECT the UFO-axis spawn. I go one step further than Allemang on Q2 and name what, if anything, I would actually modularize.

### Q1 — Load-bearing? Does a NAMED consumer query need the Quality-vs-Mode (or legal-estate) entailment as a typed set?

**REJECT.** No tabled query needs the typed set, and as the `legal-estate-attributes` steward I will not pretend mine does: every legal-estate question on the table — tenure of this estate, ground rent, service charge, ownership type — is answered by predicate-and-bearer (`?e opda:groundRent ?v`, `?e a opda:LegalEstate`), never by selecting `?x a gufo:Quality`. FIBO's competency-driven method (Kendall & McGuinness 2019, §4 on competency questions as the unit of justification) is explicit that a distinction earns a structural commitment only when a required answer turns on it; the entailment here has no consumer behind it. I adopt Allemang's S025 criterion as governing and find it unmet on my own axis as squarely as on his.

### Q2 — Valid partition key? Is UFO meta-category a stable key fit for a permanent w3id.org module boundary?

**REJECT** as a module-boundary key (AFFIRM only as analytic commentary). A w3id.org boundary is a near-permanent published reuse contract, and FIBO's hard lesson is that you draw it where the *subject matter* is rigid, never where a meta-theory is (Bennett 2013; Kendall & McGuinness 2019 §5.2 on stable modular boundaries). This corpus refutes the UFO key on its own flagship leaves: §Q5a types `priceQualifier`/`marketingTenure` "**Mode / Quality Value**" — a straddle the table records with a slash — and `ownershipType`, *my* leaf, sits ambiguous between Quale-in-Region (row 1) and the Mode reading. A key that puts my own steward-leaf on the slash between two published modules is not rigid; it forces re-homing across IRIs every time the metaphysical reading shifts. The stable key is `rdfs:domain` — the bearer Kind — which is a subject-matter proxy and is exactly what the walk used.

### Q3 — Module reality (my central question). Does ODR-0008c collapse to `rdfs:domain opda:LegalEstate`? Do a/b need a typing pass not done? What of the ~70 other-class leaves?

**AFFIRM the collapse, and I say it as the steward who would otherwise own the module.** ODR-0008c **is** `rdfs:domain opda:LegalEstate` — the 25 legal-estate leaves are *already* partitioned by domain in `opda-property.ttl` (ODR-0024 R1: "`opda:LegalEstate` tenure/lease/charge attrs"), recoverable today by `SELECT ?p WHERE { ?p rdfs:domain opda:LegalEstate }`. "legal-estate-attributes" as a module would be a namespace wrapper around a domain filter that already returns the set — in FIBO terms a module with no independent reuse surface, which Kendall & McGuinness 2019 §5 treats as ceremony, not modularization. So **ODR-0008c is not a module; it is a query**, and what it actually expresses is my **attachment-class** distinction (LegalEstate-borne vs Property-borne) — which is realised by `rdfs:domain`, not by the UFO axis. ODR-0008a/b are not even drawable: the 69 `opda:Property` leaves carry no `gufo:` type, so a Quality/Mode split has no leaf-set to divide until a typing pass the walk did not perform actually runs. And the UFO axis reaches at most the ~94 Property+LegalEstate leaves — the other ~70 sit on `opda:Search`/`Transaction`/`NearbyFacility`/`Organisation`/`RiskAssessment`, already routed to ODR-0008d / ODR-0006 / ODR-0007. A cut that covers under 60% of the emitted leaves is a partial overlay, not a modularization.

As steward I formally **retire the expectation that ODR-0008c spawns on the UFO trigger** and re-route my held-as-live to its original, sharper condition. **REVISE → preserve as lightweight, query-free annotation; never as module structure or enforced entailment.** Keep the UFO reading exactly where it lives now — the analytic `skos:scopeNote`/`rdfs:comment` prose already in the corpus — optionally extended to the straddlers (`priceQualifier` "Mode/Quality Value"; `ownershipType` "Quale of LegalEstate", flagged as contested). I align with Davis's floor: per-leaf `gufo:rdf:type` is acceptable **only on uncontested leaves** and explicitly **omitting the straddlers/re-sorters** until adjudicated; do **not** mint SHACL shapes (a shape implies a validator that does not exist) and do **not** fake a partition with `dct:subject`. FIBO records analytic provenance as annotation precisely so it stays recoverable by query without freezing a permanent IRI commitment (Kendall & McGuinness 2019 §6 on annotation as the vehicle for non-load-bearing classificatory metadata). The honest preservation is documentary: the framework + criterion stand in S025/ODR-0023 R2, the straddler evidence sits in §Q5a, and the Q4 trigger tells the next reviewer exactly what data + query would convert the insight into a module.
