# Session 041 — Giancarlo Guizzardi

*Lens: the UFO category system as meta-types instances are classified into (identity / rigidity / dependence as discriminators); gUFO as the OWL rendering that makes those categories **referenceable IRIs** precisely so they need not be spelled as prose; and the gUFO authors' own OWL-2-DL result as the warrant that the categories must not be reasoned over.*

Grounding texts I argue from throughout: Guizzardi, *Ontological Foundations for Structural Conceptual Models* (2005), Ch. 4 §4.4 (Relator/qua-individual/mediation; the Kind/Role/RoleMixin/Phase sortal apparatus); Guizzardi et al., "Towards Ontological Foundations for Conceptual Modeling: The UFO Story," *Applied Ontology* 10(3–4), 2015 (UFO as a system of categories discriminated by identity/rigidity/existential-dependence); Almeida, Guizzardi, Sales & Fonseca, *gUFO — A Gentle Foundational Ontology for OWL* (its design rationale: render the leaf categories as concrete OWL IRIs `gufo:Kind`, `gufo:Role`, `gufo:RoleMixin`, `gufo:Relator`, `gufo:Event`, `gufo:Quality`, … under `gufo:` so a modeller can *type against them*; **and** its explicit finding that the full UFO axiomatisation — modal distinctions, anti-rigidity, mereology — is **not** faithfully expressible in OWL 2 DL).

## Q1 — String literal vs resource (IRI)

**Verdict: REVISE. Ballot: FOR (the proposition as framed, with the amendment below).**

**Guizzardi:** A UFO category is not a label; it is an **entity with an identity and an axiomatic profile**. `gufo:Role` *is* "anti-rigid, externally founded, dependent on a relational context"; `gufo:Kind` *is* "rigid sortal supplying its own principle of identity"; `gufo:Relator` *is* "the truthmaker of a material relation, mediating two or more bound relata." These are not English glosses you can retype per file — they are the discriminators that the whole *UFO Story* (Applied Ontology, 2015, §3) is built on: a category is individuated by *which* identity/rigidity/dependence profile it carries. The string `"Substance Kind"` (and the corpus's slightly drifting variants — `"Substance Kind"` the class value vs `"Substance Kind label"` the scheme value, established fact 2) discards exactly that. It is a name with no referent, no `skos:definition`, no rigidity profile, no `skos:exactMatch` to the place where the profile is actually written down. A modeller cannot ask "which terms are anti-rigid roles?" of a literal; they can ask it of a resource. That is the entire reason we built gUFO with real IRIs rather than shipping UFO as a PDF glossary: *so the categories can be referenced rather than spelled* (gUFO design rationale). The status quo re-spells them, 86 times, by hand.

So the *value* must be a **resource**. But I will not affirm "a direct reference to `gufo:Kind` in the data" unconditionally — that is where I refine the framing. The principled representation is a **locally-minted SKOS concept** in a governed `opda:UFOCategoryScheme` (e.g. `opda:SubstanceKindCategory`, `opda:RelatorCategory`, `opda:RoleMixinCategory`), each carrying its own `skos:definition` (the rigidity/identity/dependence profile, in OPDA's own words and under OPDA's own governance and trailing-slash dereference policy) and a `skos:closeMatch`/`skos:exactMatch` edge to the corresponding `gufo:` IRI. `exactMatch` where OPDA's intended meaning is gUFO's leaf verbatim (`opda:RelatorCategory skos:exactMatch gufo:Relator`); `closeMatch` where OPDA narrows or register-defers (the quality-value / Quale-in-Region axis — see ODR-0030 Rule 2). This is the standard SKOS interoperability idiom (W3C *SKOS Reference*, 2009, §10 mapping properties): you do **not** reuse another vocabulary's IRIs as your own subjects/objects; you mint local concepts and *map*. It gives OPDA editorial control of the definition, a stable `/pdtf` URI it actually owns, typo-safety (a `sh:in` over the scheme members), and gUFO alignment in one move — without the data ever pointing into a namespace OPDA does not govern.

**Cross-talk — rebutting Cagle ("optional, droppable, keep it a string").** Kurt is right about one thing and wrong about its consequence. Right: at the wire level the category is inert — session-040 established, and I co-signed, that deleting every tag changes zero inferences and zero emitted bytes. Wrong: "inert ⇒ keep it a bare string." A provenance record being causally inert is an argument about *whether it reasons*, not about *whether it should be machine-resolvable*. We do not store `dcterms:creator` as a bare display string because authorship "doesn't reason"; we store an IRI so it resolves to an identified agent. The category is *documentation of a design commitment*, and the first duty of documentation in a linked-data artefact is to be **dereferenceable to its definition** — which a literal categorically cannot be. Kurt's own published position (*Do You Need An Upper Ontology?*, 2026) is that an upper ontology is "a methodology in disguise"; precisely — and a methodology's vocabulary, when you choose to record that you applied it, is exactly the kind of thing that earns a stable resource with a written-down definition, not a free-text sticky note that the next maintainer will spell `"Substance kind"` with a lowercase k. The thin-inert-string is *more* fragile than the resource, not less, because it has no governance surface at all.

## Q2 — Markup & graph placement

**Verdict: REVISE. Ballot: FOR, conditional on the placement amendment — this is the load-bearing answer of the session.**

**Guizzardi:** Three sub-answers.

**(a) Predicate typing.** Given a resource value (Q1), `opda:ufoCategory` becomes resource→resource and must be typed **`owl:AnnotationProperty`**, not `owl:DatatypeProperty` and not `owl:ObjectProperty`. The reason is not cosmetic and it is *mine to make*: the gUFO paper's central technical finding is that UFO's categorial apparatus is not faithfully expressible in OWL 2 DL — anti-rigidity, the modal force of "founds," the existential dependence of a Role on its Relator are second-order/modal and fall outside the description logic. The *correct* OWL surface for a commitment you have proven you cannot soundly reason over is the annotation surface, which the OWL 2 specification (*OWL 2 Structural Specification*, W3C 2012, §5.5 and §10 — annotations carry no semantic conditions and are excluded from the DL-expressible axiom set) defines as exactly that: a triple the formal semantics does not act on. An `owl:ObjectProperty` would (under a less timid regime than OPDA's current ODR-0029) license property-based entailments OPDA must not want; an `owl:DatatypeProperty` with `rdfs:range xsd:string` is simply *false* once the value is an IRI. `owl:AnnotationProperty` is the one typing that is simultaneously honest, resource-valued, and provably non-axiomatic. The declaration's *own `rdfs:comment`* (foundation.py:526) already says "records the foundational-ontology commitment **without entailing logical axioms**" — the typing must finally match that sentence.

**(b) Graph placement — the crux, and the place I am most insistent.** The tags MUST move to the annotation graph (`opda-annotations.ttl`). This is not an improvement; under the binding prior it is a **standing obligation that is currently in breach of its own warrant**. ODR-0030 Rule 1 is verbatim: the `opda:ufoCategory` tags "remain **annotation-graph-only** … the ODR-0029 quarantine is load-bearing — **retention lapses if it is breached**." The established facts are unambiguous (brief facts 3, 5; I re-verified): all 86-odd tags are physically asserted in the *non-annotation* reasoned-union files (`opda-classes.ttl`, `opda-agent.ttl`, `opda-claim.ttl`, `opda-property.ttl`, `opda-descriptive.ttl`, `opda-governance.ttl`, `opda-transaction.ttl`, `opda-vocabularies.ttl`); `opda-annotations.ttl` carries **zero**. So Rule 1 is *already* violated by placement, and only the accident of ODR-0029's shallow "RDFS-Plus" regime — which fires `rdfs:subClassOf` propagation only and ignores domain/range — keeps the breach from producing entailments. That is a latent fault, not a clean state: the warrant of retention is the *quarantine*, and the artefact is sitting outside it. Promoting the value to a resource (Q1) without simultaneously moving the triples to `opda-annotations.ttl` would deepen the fault — it is the literal text of **Cagle's re-open trigger (i)**: "the layer is ever made reasoned-over (moved … into a graph that drives inference or SHACL)." My position is the inverse of triggering it: this session is the occasion to *retire the latent breach* by relocating every `ufoCategory` triple into the designated quarantine (ODR-0004 §3a three-graph separation; ODR-0010 §Q7a annotation-graph home). Enrichment of the *value* and quarantine of the *graph* are not in tension — they are the same act done correctly. This is also the price of Knublauch's standing condition from session-040, which I accept without reservation: my Q1 AFFIRM there was conditional on the gUFO/`ufoCategory` quarantine remaining CI-enforced. Right now the CI gate (three-graph separation ASK) does not catch this because the gate watches for SHACL shapes leaking into the annotation graph, not for annotation tags leaking *out* into the class graph. The gate must be extended to assert the converse: `ASK { GRAPH <…/graph/classes> { ?s opda:ufoCategory ?o } } → FALSE`.

**(c) Governed scheme + SHACL `sh:in`.** Yes — the value space is a governed `opda:UFOCategoryScheme` (Q1), and a SHACL shape SHOULD constrain `opda:ufoCategory` to `sh:in` the scheme's members. This is consistent with ODR-0027 (classification-over-inheritance: kinds ship as `sh:in` facets via `sh:targetSubjectsOf`, not subclass trees) — the category facet is enforced *by value membership*, never by an `rdfs:subClassOf gufo:Role` edge that would drag gUFO into the reasoned tree. One sharp caveat I insist on, and it is the boundary of my own undecidability result: the `sh:in` shape validates *spelling* (the value is a known category), it does **not** and must **not** attempt to validate *correctness* (that `opda:Buyer` truly is anti-rigid). The rigidity judgement is OntoClean's, made by a human at design time (ODR-0027) — SHACL cannot derive `+R/−R` and must not pretend to. The shape is a typo-guard, not a reasoner.

**Cross-talk — Guarino's OntoClean meta-property position.** Nicola will argue (consistently with OntoClean, Guarino & Welty 2000–2002, and his session-040 swing) that the *meta-properties* — rigidity, identity, dependence — are the real load-bearing content and are separable from UFO's category names. I agree, and I want to be precise about the division of labour rather than let it be read as a rebuttal of resource-valued categories. OntoClean supplies the *discriminators* (`+R/+I/+D`); UFO supplies the *named cells of the resulting table* (Kind = `+R+I+D`-supplying-own-identity; Role = `−R` sortal externally-founded; RoleMixin = `−R` non-sortal; Relator = the mediation primitive OntoClean is silent on — exactly the wedge I pressed in session-040 at `opda:numberOfSellers`/`Proprietorship`). So if Q4 marks up the *meta-properties* as structured facets (and it should — see Q4), the `ufoCategory` resource and the OntoClean facets are **complementary, not redundant**: the category concept's `skos:definition` should literally *cite* its OntoClean profile (`opda:RoleCategory` definition: "anti-rigid (−R), sortal, externally founded"). That is the principled way to honour Guarino's point — make the meta-properties first-class *and* let the category resource reference them — rather than Cagle's way of dissolving both into prose.

## Q3 — Web pages / documentation IA

**Verdict: AFFIRM. Ballot: FOR.**

**Guizzardi:** Present the layer as first-class web pages, with one discipline I hold non-negotiable: **the honesty must be structural, not decorative.** Concretely:

- A dereferenceable **`/pdtf/ufoCategory`** term page (already exists per ADR-0044 Phase 5c) and **per-category pages** (`/pdtf/RelatorCategory`, `/pdtf/RoleCategory`, …) — one per governed SKOS concept, each rendering its `skos:definition` (the rigidity/identity/dependence profile), its `skos:closeMatch`/`exactMatch` to the `gufo:` IRI, and the list of OPDA terms it classifies. This is the dereferenceability Q1 buys, made visible. A category index page groups them.
- **Per-term UFO badges** on class pages, linking the badge to the category page (not to a literal). The badge is a *link to a definition*, which is the whole point.
- The single **`/ontology/foundational-ontology`** hub stays the narrative home — it already carries the four-stream sourced critique that produced ODR-0030. The per-category pages are the *reference* layer; the hub is the *rationale* layer.
- The **"UFO-informed, not UFO-grounded" disposition (ODR-0030 Rule 7)** must be machine-readable on the artefact, not buried in prose — this is *Guarino's* swing condition from session-040, which I endorsed and endorse again: the `skos:scopeNote` on `opda:ufoCategory` must state (i) the categories are UFO's, (ii) UFO's quality categories descend from DOLCE's Quality/Quale/Region (Masolo et al., WonderWeb D18, 2003), (iii) the majority of tagged *properties* fall under that DOLCE-derived quality apparatus. The page renders that scopeNote; it does not paraphrase it. And the page must say, in my own settled session-040 words, the honest verb: *"OPDA's modelling process is governed by a UFO/OntoClean decision procedure; the artefact carries an inert provenance record of that governance"* — never "the wire format reasons with UFO." Flag UFO-C (social/intentional/legal) as least-mature wherever the standard leans on it.

This honours ADR-0044 ("ontology as web pages") and ODR-0038 without overclaiming. The page *is* the dereference target the resource (Q1) promises.

**Cross-talk — Cagle.** Kurt will warn that pretty per-category pages *entrench* a layer session-040 called "optional, droppable" — that documentation IA is how an optional methodology quietly becomes load-bearing furniture. It is a fair caution and I take the sting of it. My answer: the pages entrench nothing that the binding prior did not already retain, *provided* the page foregrounds the optionality rather than hiding it. The `/ontology/foundational-ontology` hub already publishes the dissent and the re-open triggers — keep that. A category page that opens with "UFO-informed; this layer is design-time provenance, reasoned over by nothing; see the held dissent" is the *opposite* of entrenchment-by-decoration: it is the layer documenting its own contingency. The risk Kurt names is real only if the IA *suppresses* the honesty disposition. Rule 7 makes suppression a CI-checkable failure, not an aesthetic choice. That converts his objection into a guardrail I happily accept.

## Q4 — What else to mark up

**Verdict: REVISE. Ballot: FOR, with a sharp boundary between the two things that earn markup and the things that stay prose.**

**Guizzardi:** The question is which upper-ontology metadata is *the strictly irreducible thing* (worth a structured edge) versus narrative. My ruling, ordered by how hard the irreducibility case is:

**EARNS STRUCTURED MARKUP (1), THE HIGHEST-VALUE TARGET — the OntoClean meta-properties (rigidity +R/−R, identity +I/−I, dependence +D/−D, unity +U/−U).** These are the actual discriminators, and on live cross-talk Guarino sharpened my ranking and I adopt his: the meta-properties outrank the `ufoCategory` relabel itself. His framing, which I take as the strongest enrichment case in the session: *"we ship the verdicts and hide the inputs."* Unlike the category name — a *derived* label — the meta-property signature is the *primitive judgement a human made*, and it demonstrably moved emitted bytes (session-040: `tenureKind` `+R∧−I`, `VouchEvidence` `+D`, `RiskAssessment` `+I`), yet it lives nowhere as structured data, only as `sh:message` prose. Emitted verdict, hidden premise: that asymmetry is the defect to fix first. Mark the meta-properties up as structured annotation facets (e.g. `opda:ontoCleanRigidity opda:antiRigid`), in the annotation graph, under the same `sh:in`-governed-scheme + AnnotationProperty discipline as Q1/Q2. They make the design rationale *queryable* ("show me every anti-rigid term") and they let the category resource's `skos:definition` cite them (the complementarity below). Strict guard, again from my own OWL-2-DL result: they are recorded judgements, **never** reasoned premises — no SHACL rule may infer a subclass or a constraint *from* a rigidity value.

**EARNS STRUCTURED MARKUP (2) — the relational-reification primitive ("the Relator founds the RoleMixin" edges).** This is, in ODR-0030's own words and in my session-040 co-signed finding, **"the strictly irreducible thing."** It is irreducible in a sense the category tags are not: it is not provenance *about* a term, it is a *structural fact of the model* — that `opda:Proprietorship` (Relator) founds `opda:Seller`/`opda:Buyer` (RoleMixins), and that `opda:numberOfSellers` lives on the Relator *because* joint-tenancy-vs-tenants-in-common is a property of the mediated relation, not of the borne roles (verified `opda-agent.ttl:155` in session-040; Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4 §4.4). OntoClean's *monadic* `+R/+I/+D` cannot express mediation — that was the exact wedge that won the scoped AFFIRM. So a **`opda:foundsRole` / `opda:foundedBy` structured edge** between Relator and the roles it founds is the one piece of UFO content that is doing genuine *modelling* work, not documentary work. **But — and this is the boundary I am most careful about — this edge must obey ODR-0030 exactly: it lives in the annotation/provenance discipline and is NOT reasoned over.** My own gUFO undecidability result is the warrant: "founds" carries modal/existential-dependence force that OWL 2 DL cannot soundly express, so even though it is *structurally* load-bearing for human comprehension, asserting it as a reasoned `owl:ObjectProperty` with domain/range entailments would import exactly the non-computable commitment Q2 (session-040) forbade. Mark it up as a structured, dereferenceable edge for *navigation and documentation*; do **not** let it entail. (Where the relation is also expressed at the data level, that is the existing reified-node pattern on `opda:Proprietorship`/`opda:Transaction` — that node already carries the cardinality attributes; the `foundsRole` edge documents *why* it does.)

**STAYS PROSE (or stays quarantined-as-is) — three of them:**

- **The gUFO `rdf:type` markers (ADR-0034, the 5 `gufo:Quality` leaves).** Already quarantined, already correct. Do not promote, do not expand. They are the one place a `gufo:` IRI legitimately appears as an `rdf:type`, and they carry zero subclass axioms so they entail nothing. Leave them exactly where ADR-0034 put them. Touching them risks Knublauch's tripwire for no gain.
- **UFO-L Hohfeldian Claim-Right↔Duty correlativity.** This is genuinely load-bearing for the domain (charges, covenants, easements — the deontic core ODR-0030 Rule 6 keeps UFO-L-shaped) and a case *could* be made for structured correlativity edges. But I rule it **stays as modelled, not promoted to upper-ontology markup** *in this session*. Reason: the correlativity is already expressed where it belongs — at the *claim/duty class level* in the domain ontology, not as a `ufoCategory`-style provenance tag. Conflating "this term's UFO category" (provenance) with "this Claim-Right correlates with that Duty" (a domain axiom) would muddle the two graphs. If correlativity deserves first-class structured edges, that is a *domain-modelling* ODR about the Claim/Duty classes, not an extension of the `ufoCategory` provenance facet. Keep them separate.
- **The IAO crosswalk (ODR-0030 Rule 4, adopt-now).** Already has its disposition: a thin, *referenced-not-imported* set of `skos:exactMatch`/`closeMatch` edges to `obo:IAO`, in the annotation graph, on the `prov:Entity` precedent. That is the right treatment and it is orthogonal to the `ufoCategory` facet — do it per Rule 4, do not fold it into the UFO-category scheme. (Same one-file/two-clocks discipline for the held-in-reserve BFO spine, Rule 5.)

**Cross-talk — Guarino and Cagle, together, on the boundary.** To Guarino: marking up the meta-properties (my markup-(1)) is the fullest possible honouring of your OntoClean position — it makes the discriminators you authored first-class and queryable, rather than leaving them implicit in a category name; and your `opda:founds`/`foundedBy` edge IS my markup-(2), adopted verbatim (the monadic ±R/±I/±D vector structurally cannot express mediation, so the founding edge records the one relational fact the meta-property tags can't — complementary, not redundant). One correction I owe the record, having run your live corpus flag to ground: the breach you sensed disentangles into **two distinct quarantines in different states**. (a) The **gUFO `rdf:type` pass (ADR-0034) is NOT in breach** — the lone `gufo:` in the reasoned module `opda-descriptive.ttl` (line 65) is inside a `skos:scopeNote` *string literal* ("…gUFO: gufo:Object…"@en), prose not a triple; the 5 real `rdf:type gufo:Quality` triples all sit correctly in `opda-descriptive-annotations.ttl` (lines 31–47). Knublauch's tripwire is *not* tripped by `gufo:`. (b) The breach is the **`opda:ufoCategory` placement** (my Q2(b)): ~86 tags in the reasoned class files, zero in the annotation graph — a *different predicate* from `gufo:`. So the fault is real but it is the `ufoCategory` facet sitting outside its own quarantine, not a stray `gufo:` type — which is exactly why my Q2 fuses value-promotion and graph-relocation into one inseparable act, and why Knublauch's standing condition must be assessed against `ufoCategory`, not `gufo:`. To Cagle: notice that my "stays prose / stays quarantined" column is *longer and firmer* than my "earns markup" column. I am not enriching everything UFO-shaped — I am enriching exactly two things (the recorded meta-property judgements, and the one irreducible mediation primitive we *both* co-signed as load-bearing in session-040), under strict non-reasoned discipline, and explicitly declining to promote three others. That is the discipline your re-open triggers demand: enrichment that *narrows* to the irreducible core and keeps the quarantine, rather than enrichment that sprawls. If the only structured UFO content in the artefact is (a) the meta-properties that moved bytes and (b) the mediation primitive that won the scoped AFFIRM — both quarantined, neither reasoned — then the layer is *more* honest and *more* maintainable than the bag of hand-typed strings it replaces, not less.
