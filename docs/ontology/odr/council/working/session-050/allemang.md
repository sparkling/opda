# Dean Allemang — Linked Data Council, session-050

**Lens:** pragmatic RDF modelling, *Semantic Web for the Working Ontologist* (Allemang/Hendler/Gandon), "domain/range are documentation, not constraints," reuse-over-mint, the simplest model that works. Every axiom is a maintenance liability and must earn its keep with a real consumer.

**Decided premise I am working from (not re-litigating):** the directing authority has decided OPDA **adopts** hm/semantic-modelling's OWL coverage as baseline, and Q1 = hm ODR-0014 repeated-`rdfs:domain` "any-of" (superseding session-048's `owl:unionOf`). My charge is to **confirm the adoption is sound RDF, document the convention, and work out the consequences** — and to resolve the FP/IFP tension. I am the natural advocate for this adoption and I affirm it; my job is to make it *defensible on the record*, not merely assert it.

**Standing ground (cited up front):**
- SWWO, the RDFS-inferencing chapter: `rdfs:domain`/`rdfs:range` are **inference licences, not constraints** — "an ontology says what *can* be inferred, not what *must* be present." This is the book's most-misunderstood point and the doctrinal foundation of the whole adoption.
- SWWO Ch.7 (the additive/intersective reading of multiple domains) + **RDF Schema 1.1 §3.2** ("the subject is an instance of *all* the classes"): multiple `rdfs:domain` is **conjunction by entailment**. I led session-048 with the *opposite* claim and was correctly moved off it by Hendler/Gandon. I carry that correction into this session — it is load-bearing for *how* we document (a), and I will not pretend it away.
- hm **ODR-0014** — the prior art being adopted: declare `rdfs:domain`/`rdfs:range` on all properties with **domainIncludes semantics**, reasoning NEVER enabled, multiple `rdfs:domain` read as "any of," SHACL enforces per-class. This is **schema.org's `domainIncludes` idiom expressed in pure RDFS** — my preferred reuse-over-mint pattern.
- OPDA **ODR-0026 §R2** + its **session-035 amendment** (line 68): the corpus has already ruled, 8–0–0, that *"model-but-don't-evaluate does NOT apply where a safe, evaluable, native substitute exists — keep the information, drop the unsafe-and-redundant axiom."* Binding precedent; it decides Q2 more than any external citation.
- OPDA **ADR-0035** zero-domain/range-triple test — the *proof* that the closure evaluates none of this. The whole adoption rides on this test staying green.

## Q1 — Confirm hm ODR-0014 repeated-`rdfs:domain` "any-of" as sound RDF; document the convention; the SHACL `sh:or` dual

**Verdict: AFFIRM the adoption. Ballot: FOR.** With one mandatory rider: it is sound **only as documented convention + proof obligation**, and the documentation must be *honest about the standard reading*, not paper over it.

This is my wheelhouse and I want to be exact, because session-048 and session-050 are *not* asking the same question, and the difference is the whole point.

**What session-048 decided, and why it doesn't bind here.** session-048's panel (me included) compared (a) repeated-domain vs (b) `owl:unionOf` *as competing things to author*, with the AI-signal driver presupposing "the reader gets the right reading." On that framing I conceded (a) to (b): a competent LLM that internalised RDFS §3.2 reads `rdfs:domain Person, Organisation` as the **conjunction** "every bearer is both a Person and an Organisation" — the *negation* of the "any-of" intent, unsatisfiable for sibling kinds. (b) `owl:unionOf` states the union correctly for that reader. That reasoning was sound **for the question as posed**.

**Why the DA's decision is nonetheless right, and (a) is sound.** The directing authority added a premise session-048 didn't have in its brief: **cross-project consistency with hm, whose ODR-0014 is exactly this idiom, deliberately, with a documented convention.** That changes (a) from "an undocumented triple whose standard reading is inverted" into "a **conventioned documentary idiom** — schema.org `domainIncludes`-in-RDFS — whose standard intersection reading is *disarmed by construction* because OPDA provably never evaluates domain/range (ADR-0035)." Those are not the same artefact. hm ODR-0014 is not naïvely shipping a conjunction and hoping; it is adopting the single most widely-deployed convention in production KG engineering (schema.org softened to `domainIncludes` *precisely* because `rdfs:domain` intersection is impractical for multi-class properties; Dublin Core did the same in 2018; FIBO declares domain/range but enforces via SHACL — all four cited in hm ODR-0014, and all four are real, verifiable prior art). OPDA adopting that convention is **reuse of the mainstream**, not invention of a shibboleth.

So the honest synthesis of my two positions:

1. **The standard reading of multiple `rdfs:domain` is conjunction (RDFS §3.2). This is true and we state it.** (My session-048 concession stands — I do not retract it.)
2. **OPDA never computes that reading** (ODR-0025 §R1 frozen 7-rule closure excludes rdfs2/rdfs3; ADR-0035 proves zero domain/range triples materialise). So the conjunction is never *inferred*.
3. **The remaining question is documentation fidelity for the un-reasoned reader (human + LLM).** Here the resolution that survives both Hendler's §3.2 point *and* the DA's decision is: **the `rdfs:domain` list is the documentary echo; the SHACL `sh:or` is the authoritative disjunction; and a one-line module-header convention + per-property `rdfs:comment` makes the "any-of" reading explicit and overrides the default conjunction reading for anyone reading OPDA's corpus.** That is exactly hm ODR-0014's prescription (its Confirmation checklist mandates "header comments in all ontology files document the domainIncludes convention" + "multi-use properties have `rdfs:comment` explaining per-class usage"). The convention is not a secret handshake — it is a *published, in-corpus, machine-readable* override, the same one schema.org/DCMI/FIBO consumers already expect.

**This is the resolution of the session-048 shibboleth objection, not a dodge of it.** In session-048 Cagle and I agreed that an *undocumented* "any-of" reading is a shibboleth — "correct only if the reader has internalised a non-standard convention." hm ODR-0014's answer is: **don't leave it undocumented.** A `skos:editorialNote` on the ontology header + an `rdfs:comment` on each disjunctive property *is* the documentation that converts the shibboleth into a signal. The reader does not have to *guess* the convention from OPDA's silence (the session-048 problem); they read it stated, in the file, next to the triple. Combined with ADR-0035 (the triple is never evaluated, so even a reader who *ignores* the convention and applies §3.2 conjunction produces no wrong inference — the closure adds nothing), the residual risk is: a human/LLM who reads the bare triple, ignores the adjacent comment, and *manually* concludes "both at once." That residual is real but small, fully mitigated by the comment, and — decisively — **it is the residual the entire production KG industry already accepts every day** by using `domainIncludes`-style multi-domain. OPDA is not pioneering a hazard; it is joining the consensus hm already joined.

**The SHACL `sh:or` dual — already in place, and it is the authoritative carrier.** I verified the corpus is *already* in exactly the shape hm ODR-0014 prescribes for the disjunctions: `opda:founds`, `opda:plays`/`opda:playedBy` carry `sh:or` over the Role∪RoleMixin and Person∪Organisation unions (`opda-agent-shapes.ttl:42-43,76-77,104-105,132-133`), with `sh:or` on the focus node, **not** `rdfs:range`/`owl:unionOf`. That is the "SHACL enforces per-class with granularity domain/range can't express" half of ODR-0014, *already built* (session-047/ADR-0048). So adopting hm ODR-0014 for OPDA's disjunctions is **additive and cheap**: keep the `sh:or` shapes exactly as they are (authoritative enforcement, 100% per ODR-0029 §R3), and *add back* the documentary repeated-`rdfs:domain` "any-of" that session-047 stripped, with the convention note. No SHACL changes; the enforcement layer is untouched.

**Documenting the convention — concrete prescription (adopting hm ODR-0014's form):**

- **Module-header `skos:editorialNote`** on `opda-agent.ttl` (and any module bearing disjunctive object properties), text along the lines of:
  > "Domain/range-as-documentation (ODR-0014-adopted / ODR-0026 §R2). All `rdfs:domain`/`rdfs:range` in this file are informative, AI-legible documentation, NEVER entailed (ODR-0025 §R1; ADR-0035 proves zero domain/range triples). **Multiple `rdfs:domain` on one property reads as *any-of* (domainIncludes semantics), NOT the RDFS §3.2 conjunction.** SHACL `sh:or` is the authoritative disjunction and the sole enforcement."
- **Per-property `rdfs:comment`** on each disjunctive object property (`playedBy`/`plays`/`hasParticipant`/`hasAddress`), spelling out the any-of: e.g. *"any-of: bearer is opda:Person OR opda:Organisation (authoritative disjunction in opda:PlayedByBearerShape sh:or). Documentary domain/range only, never evaluated."* This is hm ODR-0014's "multi-use properties carry `rdfs:comment` explaining the per-class usage" rule, applied verbatim.
- **Single-class object properties** (`founds`, `mediates`, `concernsProperty`, `hasRegisteredTitle`, `hasEvidencedAuthority`) get **plain `rdfs:domain`/`rdfs:range`** — no convention needed, no ambiguity, the conjunction reading *is* the single-class reading. (Note: `founds`/`mediates` carry a "never reasoned" commitment per session-047; their documentary domain/range is sound under the same ADR-0035 proof — see my Q4 note on reconciling the session-047 "would entail" comments.)
- **Proof obligation, every time:** re-run the ADR-0035 zero-domain/range-triple test after authoring. This is the rider that makes (a) sound; without it I'd be back to (b). It exists and it is enforceable.

**Why this is *better* than session-048's `owl:unionOf` (b), now that hm is the baseline — and I'm the one who voted (b), so I say this against my own prior vote.** (b)'s entire advantage was "an external DL reasoner reads the union correctly." But (i) OPDA constitutionally opts out of being reasoned over and its own corollary tells external readers not to reason over it; (ii) (b) introduces the **first boolean class constructor** into a corpus hm ODR-0030 *excludes* (`owl:unionOf` is on hm's excluded list) — breaking exactly the cross-project consistency the DA's decision is buying; (iii) (b) makes three surfaces (TTL, per-section class diagrams, `ontology-model.json`) learn to render an anonymous union class. The DA's decision to adopt hm ODR-0014 *keeps the corpus boolean-constructor-free*, which is itself a consequence I'll defend under Q3/Q4: it means hm ODR-0030's excluded-construct meta-shape can keep failing `owl:unionOf` with no carve-out, a cleaner governance story than session-048's "admit unionOf only in domain/range position" special case. **The DA's decision is the simpler, more consistent model.** My simplicity lens, which pulled toward (c)/omit in session-048, now lands on (a)-with-convention: it is the least-machinery form that still carries documentary domain/range — which is ADR-0049's actual goal (regain documentary domain/range on the relationship layer).

**Honest concession I carry from session-048 (unchanged):** (a) is sound *only* as a rider on the frozen closure + ADR-0035 + the documented convention. Turn domain/range entailment on, or drop the convention note, and (a) silently asserts the everything-is-both-kinds garbage. The doctrine and the test are load-bearing for my FOR vote. They are airtight (the test exists; ODR-0026 §R2 spells out "treated as documentary, not as intersection-typing inference"; hm ODR-0014 mandates the convention note), so (a) stands.

## Q2 — Adopt hm ODR-0054 (author Functional/IFP as documentary) WHOLESALE, or carve out FP/IFP per OPDA's `owl:sameAs` ban + session-035?

**Verdict: REVISE — adopt hm ODR-0054's *philosophy* (OWL describes the domain, SHACL validates the data) but **carve out FP/IFP for OPDA**: do NOT author `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` at all; author `owl:FunctionalProperty` ONLY on a hand-curated set of genuine ontological-fact singletons, NOT as a blanket layer. Ballot: AGAINST wholesale adoption of ODR-0054; FOR a narrow, justified-each-one FP exception; FOR IFP-out-entirely.** This carries my session-048 Q2 position forward unchanged, and I now ground it additionally in the *difference between the two projects' constraint regimes* — which is the precise reason wholesale adoption is wrong even though hm ODR-0014/0030/0028b adoption is right.

**The asymmetry that decides this.** Q1/Q3 adopt hm coverage because the constructs are either harmless-when-unevaluated (domain/range under ADR-0035) or benign-and-irreplaceable (disjointness, conditionally — see Q3). FP/IFP are **different in kind for OPDA specifically**, because OPDA carries a constraint hm does not: the **categorical `owl:sameAs` ban** (ODR-0005 §5/§R5 line 138 "No `owl:sameAs` across the UPRN surfaces — unanimous, irreversible inference propagation"; ODR-0017 §R6 line 142). hm has no such ban — so for hm, authoring IFP as documentary is a low-stakes choice. For OPDA, **the published W3C semantics of IFP *is* `owl:sameAs` generation** (OWL 2 §2.3.5: IFP infers two subjects sharing a value are `owl:sameAs`). Authoring `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` as "documentary" plants, in the corpus, the exact axiom whose *meaning* is the thing ODR-0005 spent a whole council banning. That is not adopting prior art; it is importing a hazard the local regime explicitly outlawed.

**Apply the session-035 controlling test** (ODR-0026 §R2 line 68 — *"author documentary OWL only where no safe, evaluable, native substitute carries the same signal"*):

- **`owl:FunctionalProperty`** documents "at most one value per subject." The safe, evaluable, native substitute already exists and is already used corpus-wide: **SHACL `sh:maxCount 1`**. It fires in CI, it's the form the corpus already speaks, and it's *better* signal for human + LLM than the OWL axiom because it actually constrains. By session-035's rule, the OWL axiom is the unsafe-and-redundant one to drop. → **FP fails the test for the general case.**
- **`owl:InverseFunctionalProperty`** documents "this value uniquely identifies the subject" (UPRN, title number, company number). The safe substitute also exists: **`dash:uniqueValueForClass`** — the SHACL-land key constraint, *already named in OPDA's own ODR-0005* (line 86: "DASH (`dash:uniqueValueForClass`)") and ODR-0005's chosen keying mechanism ("Keyed operationally by SHACL/DASH uniqueness on UPRN"). So OPDA *already* carries the identifying-key signal, evaluably, in the idiom it already chose — and IFP would be the redundant *plus* dangerous duplicate. → **IFP fails the test twice: redundant AND re-arms the banned `sameAs`.**

**This is not me overriding hm; it's me applying hm's own principle.** hm ODR-0054's stated philosophy is "OWL describes the domain; SHACL validates the data" and its R9 says "no duplication removal — keep both." But hm ODR-0054 is reasoning *in a project with no `sameAs` ban and where reasoning over the Safe Group is partially ON* (hm ODR-0036). OPDA's regime is stricter on exactly the axis that matters: `sameAs` is banned, and the closure is frozen to 7 rules that exclude FP/IFP. hm's session-035 amendment (which OPDA inherited as ODR-0026 §R2's session-035 ruling) already established the carve-out logic: *where a safe native substitute exists, drop the axiom.* OPDA applying that to FP/IFP is **faithful to hm's own ruling**, not a departure from it. The wholesale-vs-carve-out question answers itself once you notice OPDA's local constraints (sameAs-ban + frozen closure + DASH-keying-already-chosen) are *stronger* than hm's on this exact construct.

**The narrow FP exception I grant (why REVISE not flat REJECT):** there is one thing `owl:FunctionalProperty` arguably signals that `sh:maxCount 1` doesn't — the **modal difference** between "single-valued as an ontological fact about the world" (a Person has exactly one date of birth; a RegisteredTitle has exactly one title-number lineage) vs "single-valued as a local data-shape rule" (we currently store one preferred phone). If the council judges that modal distinction worth a machine-readable AI-grounding marker, I can live with authoring `owl:FunctionalProperty` **only** on the handful of genuine world-fact singletons — identity-bearing codes, intrinsic singletons — as a **hand-curated, justify-each-one list**, explicitly NOT a blanket pass over every `sh:maxCount 1`. That's the opposite of wholesale ODR-0054.

**IFP I keep out entirely**, no exception, on the `sameAs`-landmine ground. `dash:uniqueValueForClass` carries the identifying-key signal *without* the entailment hazard, and it's already OPDA's chosen idiom. There is no modal-fact argument that rescues IFP the way it arguably rescues FP, because the "uniquely identifies" fact is *exactly* the `sameAs`-generating semantics OPDA bans — the documentary marker and the banned inference are inseparable for IFP in a way they aren't for FP. (FP's hazard under reasoning is also `sameAs` — "two values must be the same individual" — which is *why* even my FP exception is hand-curated and tiny, and why ODR-0026 §R2 line 62 lists Functional/InverseFunctional *together* as the `sameAs` anti-pattern. If the council prefers symmetry and zero `sameAs`-adjacent axioms, dropping FP entirely too is the *more* defensible line and I'd happily join it; my REVISE is the *most generous* reading toward hm-adoption, not my first preference.)

**Net:** session-048's panel reached AGAINST FP/IFP 4–0. I see no reason in the session-050 brief to reverse that — the DA's decision was about Q1 (domain/range form), and explicitly left Q2 open *because* OPDA's `sameAs` ban + session-035 pull against wholesale ODR-0054. My vote: **carve out, don't adopt wholesale.** The signal lives in SHACL (`sh:maxCount 1`, `dash:uniqueValueForClass`) where it's evaluable, enforced, and safe; OWL FP only on a tiny curated world-fact set if the council wants the modal marker; OWL IFP never.

## Q3 — Adopt hm ODR-0030 construct table + excluded-construct CI meta-shape + ODR-0028b disjointness as-is?

**Two parts, two verdicts.**

### Q3a — hm ODR-0030 permitted/excluded construct table + excluded-construct CI meta-shape

**Verdict: AFFIRM, adapted to OPDA's frozen closure (not hm's Safe-Group-ON closure). Ballot: FOR.**

Adopt hm ODR-0030's **structure** wholesale — a single per-construct permitted/excluded table governing all OWL constructs, plus a SPARQL-based SHACL meta-shape that fails CI if an excluded construct appears. This is exactly the governance ADR-0049 calls for (outstanding-work item 4) and it's pure reuse-over-mint: hm already wrote the meta-shape (the `ExcludedOWLConstructLinter` in hm ODR-0030's code appendix — `owl:disjointUnionOf`, `owl:Restriction`, `owl:oneOf`, `owl:hasKey`, cardinality restrictions, boolean class constructors). OPDA can adopt that SPARQL meta-shape near-verbatim.

**But the table's *Inferencing* column must be re-keyed to OPDA's regime, and this is the one place "adopt as-is" is wrong.** hm ODR-0030's table has an "Inferencing: YES/NO" column reflecting hm's **Safe-Group-partially-ON** closure (hm ODR-0036: subClassOf, subPropertyOf, inverseOf, TransitiveProperty, SymmetricProperty, AllDisjointClasses, disjointWith all inference YES in hm). **OPDA's closure is different**: ODR-0025 §R1 is a frozen 7-rule RDFS-Plus closure (subClassOf transitivity+type-prop, subPropertyOf transitivity+value-prop, inverseOf both directions, TransitiveProperty, SymmetricProperty) — and critically OPDA's ODR-0026 §R2 keeps disjointness as a **validation check (ADR-0035 negative consistency gate), NOT an entailment**, whereas hm runs `disjointWith`/`AllDisjointClasses` as active consistency *inference*. So OPDA's adopted table must read:
- **Permitted + inferred (the 7 rules):** subClassOf, subPropertyOf, inverseOf, TransitiveProperty, SymmetricProperty. (Matches hm's permitted-and-inferred for these five.)
- **Permitted + documentary-only (NEVER inferred):** `rdfs:domain`/`rdfs:range` (Q1), `owl:disjointWith` (Q3b — checked via ADR-0035 gate, not inferred), `owl:FunctionalProperty` (Q2, narrow), `owl:equivalentClass`/`owl:equivalentProperty` (ODR-0026 §R2). **This documentary-only band is *larger* for OPDA than for hm** — that's the consequence of OPDA's stricter frozen closure, and the table must show it explicitly so no one mistakes OPDA for hm's Safe-Group reasoner.
- **Excluded entirely:** `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` (Q2 — sameAs ban), `owl:disjointUnionOf`, `owl:Restriction`, `owl:unionOf`/`intersectionOf`/`complementOf`, cardinality restrictions, `owl:oneOf`, `owl:hasKey`. (Matches hm's excluded list, **plus IFP**, which hm permits-documentary but OPDA bans.)

**The meta-shape must therefore add one rule hm's doesn't have: fail on `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty`.** That's the CI enforcement of my Q2 IFP-out verdict, and it's the concrete divergence-point where OPDA's `sameAs` ban shows up in governance. (If the council also drops FP entirely per my Q2 symmetry note, add an FP rule too; if FP is kept narrow-curated, FP stays off the excluded list but the table flags it documentary-only-hand-curated.) Everything else in hm's meta-shape transfers verbatim. **Decisively: because Q1 adopted hm ODR-0014 repeated-domain (not session-048's `owl:unionOf`), the meta-shape can keep failing `owl:unionOf` with NO carve-out** — the corpus stays boolean-constructor-free, and hm ODR-0030's excluded list applies cleanly. That's a concrete win of the DA's Q1 decision over session-048's, and it belongs in the session-050 record.

### Q3b — hm ODR-0028b disjointness

**Verdict: REVISE — adopt the *scoping discipline* of hm ODR-0028b, but scoped HARD to OPDA's identity model: rigid Substance Kinds and Relator Kinds ONLY, NEVER anti-rigid Roles; pairwise `owl:disjointWith`, NOT `owl:AllDisjointClasses`; documentary-only, armed by the ADR-0035 gate. Ballot: FOR a tightly-scoped rule; AGAINST adopting hm ODR-0028b "as-is."** This tracks ADR-0049's Q3 disposition (rigid Kinds only, never anti-rigid Roles, pairwise) and my session-048 Q3 position, and I'll be honest that session-048 left me *converged with Cagle* on "no general disjointWith layer" — so my FOR here is narrow.

**Why not "as-is":** hm ODR-0028b's 6-family scoping rule is sound *for hm*, but it includes families and a mechanism that don't transfer cleanly:

1. **Mechanism — pairwise `owl:disjointWith`, not `owl:AllDisjointClasses`.** hm ODR-0028b uses `owl:AllDisjointClasses` for sibling-subtype families (Quarter, Time units). OPDA should NOT: no OPDA rule *consumes* the `AllDisjointClasses` list (it's not in the 7-rule closure, and the ADR-0035 gate checks pairwise), so `AllDisjointClasses` adds a list-structured triple no consumer reads — pure maintenance weight. Pairwise `owl:disjointWith` between the genuinely-disjoint pairs (Person/Organisation; the Relator Kinds) is the legible, gate-checkable form. (ADR-0049 already specifies "pairwise not `owl:AllDisjointClasses` — no rule consumes the list." I'm affirming that against hm's `AllDisjointClasses` default.)

2. **Scope — the OPDA-specific non-negotiable tripwire: NEVER between anti-rigid Roles sharing a bearer's identity.** This is the load-bearing caveat and it's stronger in OPDA than the corresponding hm rule. hm ODR-0028b's "NOT Disjoint (9-0)" list already excludes country roles on Guarino's identity-continuity ground — good, OPDA inherits that reasoning. But OPDA's Role model makes the tripwire *sharper*: `opda:Seller` and `opda:Buyer` are **RoleMixins over a shared bearer** — the *same Person* can be seller in one transaction and buyer in another. `Seller owl:disjointWith Buyer` would be flatly false and would reject legitimate data the instant any reasoner or the ADR-0035 gate keyed on it. Seller/Buyer exclusivity is **transaction-scoped** (you can't be both *in the same transaction*) and that's a SHACL constraint (session-047 Q4's whole `playedBy` qua-individual treatment exists for exactly this reason), NEVER a class-level `owl:disjointWith`. So: disjointness belongs between the **bearer Kinds** (Person/Organisation) and possibly the **Relator Kinds** where conditions hold — **never the roles**.

3. **The scoping rule OPDA adopts** (hm ODR-0028b's 3-condition rule, adapted): author `owl:disjointWith` only when **(1)** it reflects a genuine top-level rigid-Kind split (Person vs Organisation; distinct Relator kinds with incompatible identity criteria), AND **(2)** mutual exclusivity is a context-independent domain invariant (not "happens not to overlap in our data"), AND **(3)** misclassification is a real data-quality failure — AND **(4, OPDA-specific) NEVER between anti-rigid roles sharing a bearer's identity** (the Seller/Buyer/Proprietor RoleMixin tripwire). Hand-curated, each entry justified against the four conditions; NOT a blanket sweep.

**Why disjointWith clears the session-035 bar where FP/IFP didn't (the consistency check on my own Q2):**
- The SHACL substitute is **partial in a way `sh:maxCount`/`dash:` are not**. `sh:not`/`sh:xone` enforce non-membership per node-shape, instance-by-instance; `owl:disjointWith` documents a **symmetric, class-level ontological fact** ("no individual is ever both a Person and an Organisation"). *However* — and I concede this exactly as I did to Cagle in session-048 — a **dedicated mutual-exclusion NodeShape** (`sh:targetClass opda:Person ; sh:not [sh:class opda:Organisation]` + the dual) *does* concentrate that fact as one legible artefact AND it fires. So the "irreplaceable signal" claim is weak: Cagle was right that concentration is a formatting preference SHACL can match. The honest residual is the one I named in session-048: "is a single class-level *triple* worth authoring, given the gate + a NodeShape already carry it?"
- **The entailment hazard is benign under OPDA's closure** (unlike IFP). `owl:disjointWith` produces ZERO triples and arms NO merge; it can only make a graph inconsistent *if a reasoner runs consistency checking*, which OPDA's frozen closure provably doesn't — OPDA checks disjointness via the ADR-0035 **negative consistency gate**, a targeted local check, not entailment. Contrast IFP (a triple-producing, entity-fusing `sameAs` rule). So disjointWith is safe-to-author where IFP is not — the inverse of the Q2 calculus.

**Where I land, honestly (carrying session-048's convergence forward):** I am **FOR a tightly-scoped, hand-curated `owl:disjointWith` on the rigid Kind pairs (Person/Organisation, distinct Relator kinds)** — because (i) it arms OPDA's *currently-vacuous* ADR-0035 disjointness gate (ADR-0049 notes the gate exists but has nothing to check — authoring gives it teeth), (ii) the hazard is benign under the frozen closure, and (iii) it's bounded prior-art reuse from hm ODR-0028b's discipline. I am **AGAINST** any blanket disjointness sweep, **AGAINST** `owl:AllDisjointClasses` (no consumer), and the Seller/Buyer/Proprietor role tripwire is **absolute**. If the council prefers Cagle's pure-SHACL line (dedicated NodeShape + gate, no axiom), I regard that as a *defensible minority* — the signal-loss is small — but I tip FOR the scoped axiom because arming the existing-but-vacuous ADR-0035 gate is a concrete consumer (the gate *is* the OPDA rule that "consumes" disjointWith), which clears session-035 condition (i): the axiom feeds a real evaluable check no SHACL `sh:not` currently feeds. That distinction — **the ADR-0035 gate consumes `owl:disjointWith` as its input** — is the OPDA-specific fact that tips me FOR where in session-048 (reasoning about the gate as already-built-on-`sh:not`) I'd converged toward Cagle's REJECT. I'll want Guarino/Cagle to confirm whether the ADR-0035 gate reads authored `owl:disjointWith` or reconstructs disjointness from SHACL; if the former, FOR is clearly right; if the latter, it collapses toward Cagle's REJECT and I'd hold the axiom to the Person/Organisation pair only.

## Q4 — Consequences for the relationship layer / `ci-object-property-coverage` gate / `ontology-model.mjs` + supersession of session-048

**Verdict: AFFIRM the consequence-set in ADR-0049, with the specific reconciliations below. Ballot: FOR.** This is the "work out the consequences" half of my charge, and the consequences are mostly mechanical *given* Q1–Q3.

**C1 — Relationship layer: author back the documentary domain/range that session-047/ADR-0048 stripped.** This is the core consequence and it *reverses a specific session-047 decision*.
