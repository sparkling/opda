## Opening

### Frame: the engineering-act = ontological-act test, restated in identity terms

The panel (session-048) arrived at the right per-construct discipline, which I endorse and now ground: **author an R2-set construct as documentary signal only where its published W3C semantics AGREE with what OPDA actually enforces** (Gandon's "engineering-act = ontological-act"; ADR-0049 Decision Outcome). My lens sharpens the test into its identity-theoretic form:

> An axiom is publishable iff the proposition it asserts to the Web is one OPDA *holds true*. An axiom is NOT made publishable merely by being *inert under OPDA's loader*.

This is the distinction between **soundness-under-the-closure** (a property of OPDA's inference pipeline) and **truth-of-the-published-proposition** (a property of the model-theoretic content of the axiom, read by any conformant consumer). The ADR-0035 zero-triple gate proves the first. It says *nothing* about the second. For domain/range and `owl:disjointWith` the two coincide. For `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` they come apart catastrophically — and the reason is an identity-criteria confusion, which is the substance of my contribution.

### Q1 — Repeated-`rdfs:domain` "any-of" for disjoint bearers (Role∪RoleMixin; Person∪Organisation). Sound from an identity/sortal standpoint?

**Verdict: AFFIRM (the directing-authority decision). Ballot: FOR hm ODR-0014 repeated-`rdfs:domain` "any-of".**

The identity-theoretic question hidden in Q1 is: *can a single property legitimately carry a disjunctive domain whose disjuncts are sortals with different identity criteria?* (e.g. `playedBy` bearer = Role∪RoleMixin; `hasAddress` bearer = Property∪Person∪Organisation — three Substance Kinds with three distinct ICs from ODR-0005/0006.)

Under **standard RDFS** the answer would be alarming: multiple `rdfs:domain` is **conjunction** (RDFS 1.1 §3.2 — verified by the session-048 panel), so the closure would infer each subject is *simultaneously* a Person AND an Organisation AND a Property — three incompatible identity criteria forced onto one individual, which is exactly an OntoClean identity-violation (Guarino & Welty 2002: a sortal carries an IC; an individual cannot fall under two sortals supplying *incompatible* ICs). That is the "everything-becomes-a-Person" trap in its sharper, identity-theoretic dress.

But OPDA **does not evaluate `rdfs:domain`** (ODR-0025 §R2; ODR-0026 §R2 model-but-don't-evaluate; ODR-0029 §R1 inference/validation boundary). The closure adds zero domain-derived triples (ADR-0035; ODR-0029 line 18: "the closure adds 0 triples"). So the conjunction-vs-disjunction hazard simply **never fires**. The axiom is read by humans and LLMs as a *documentary* statement of the bearer set, under the documented "any-of" convention (module header + per-property `rdfs:comment`), and enforced — disjunctively and correctly — by SHACL `sh:or` (ODR-0029 §R3 dual, 100% enforcement).

Why repeated-`rdfs:domain` over `owl:unionOf` (the session-048 (b) verdict I am superseding) is also the *identity-correct* choice, not merely the cross-project-consistency choice:

1. **The disjuncts are sortals, and the union of sortals is not itself a sortal.** Person, Organisation, Property each supply their own IC; their union supplies *none* (Guarino & Welty 2009 §OntoClean: a class is a sortal iff it carries a single IC; a "dispersive" union of disjoint sortals is a **non-sortal / category**, like `owl:Thing` or DOLCE's top categories). An `owl:unionOf(Person, Organisation, Property)` would mint a **named, referenceable, subclassable `owl:Class`** — a first-class TBox entity — for something that is *ontologically not a kind*. Repeated-`rdfs:domain` asserts the weaker, correct thing: "the bearer is drawn from this *list* of kinds," with no claim that the list constitutes a kind. The "any-of" reading is the documentary disjunction; it does not reify a pseudo-sortal.

2. **Anti-rigidity reinforces this for the Role∪RoleMixin case.** `playedBy`'s bearer disjunction is over *anti-rigid* universals (Roles are anti-rigid; Guarino & Welty 2002 §3 — a Role is a universal R such that every instance can possibly not be an instance of R). A named union *class* over anti-rigid Roles is doubly ill-formed: it is neither a sortal (no IC) nor rigid (no stable membership). The repeated-domain list sidesteps both defects — it never claims the disjunction is a thing at all.

3. **Corpus coherence (ODR-0027/S036 classification-over-inheritance).** OPDA's doctrine is zero anonymous class expressions, facets-not-subclass-trees. `owl:unionOf` is the corpus's *first* blank-node class expression (Cagle session-048 line 10 verified: `owl:unionOf=0`). Repeated-`rdfs:domain` keeps the corpus boolean-constructor-free, which is the *engineering* expression of the *ontological* commitment that these unions are not kinds.

**Caveat I attach (binding):** the "any-of" convention is a documented departure from the standards-defined RDFS conjunction reading. It is *safe* only because OPDA never evaluates domain/range — the safety is doctrine-dependent, exactly the dependency ADR-0049 §Context admits was forgotten once (session-047). Therefore the module-header note + per-property `rdfs:comment` are **not optional dressing**; they are the only thing that makes the published axiom legible as disjunction rather than as the (false) conjunction its standard semantics assert. And the ADR-0035 zero-triple test is the standing proof that the conjunction never materialises. Author the convention; gate the convention.

**Citations:** RDFS 1.1 Semantics §3.2 (multiple `rdfs:domain` = conjunction); Guarino & Welty 2002 *Identity and subsumption* / 2009 *An Overview of OntoClean* (sortal carries IC; union of disjoint sortals is a non-sortal category; Role anti-rigidity); ODR-0025 §R2 / ODR-0026 §R2 / ODR-0029 §R1,§R3; ODR-0027 (classification-over-inheritance).

### Q2 — THE IDENTITY CRUX: adopt hm ODR-0054 author-FP/IFP, or carve out for OPDA?

**Verdict: REJECT authoring `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` entirely. REJECT `owl:FunctionalProperty` as a documentary layer (admissible at most as a hand-curated world-fact annotation on genuine single-valued-by-domain-truth properties, never the axiom). Carve OUT from hm ODR-0054 on the FP/IFP rule (R2). Ballot: AGAINST authoring FP/IFP as axioms; FOR `sh:maxCount 1` / `dash:uniqueValueForClass` + annotation as the home.**

This is the question my lens decides. The session-048 panel converged here 4-0, but the convergence rode two arguments that need formal identity-theoretic grounding to be *principled* rather than *defensive*. I supply it.

#### The core claim: IFP conflates an *identifier* with an *identity criterion*. These are different things, and OPDA's entire identity doctrine (ODR-0005) is built on keeping them apart.

`owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` has exactly one model-theoretic meaning (OWL 2 Direct Semantics §2.3.5; OWL 2 Primer §4.2 — the canonical `foaf:mbox` key pattern): **if two subjects share a value of an IFP, they are `owl:sameAs`.** There is no separate "this value is an identity key" proposition floating above the `sameAs` rule — *the key-ness IS the sameAs entailment* (Cagle session-048 line 124, which I co-own: "naming it ontologically and arming the merge are the same axiom"). So authoring IFP is authoring a **global, context-free identity criterion**: it says "across the entire universe of discourse, sharing this value is necessary and sufficient for being the same individual."

Now bring in identity-criteria theory (Guarino & Welty 2002 §2; Lowe 1989 on criteria of identity; the OntoClean Identity meta-property +I/+O). An **identity criterion** for a sortal F is a relation that holds between x and y iff x and y are the same F — it is an *intrinsic, constitutive* principle of individuation, true *in every context*, part of what it *is* to be an F. An **identifier** (a UPRN, an LEI, a title number, a Companies House number, an NI number) is something else entirely: an *extrinsic, administratively-assigned, contingent label* attached to an individual by some authority's bookkeeping. ODR-0005 makes this distinction the foundation of the whole Property module:

> "UPRN is a contingent identifier, **not the IC** — operationally, a UFO **Quality** on `opda:Property`." (ODR-0005 §Rules 6)
> "Treating UPRN or address as the identity criterion … (they are administratively contingent / a mode of presentation)." (ODR-0005 §Anti-patterns)

IFP **erases this distinction**. It promotes a contingent identifier to a constitutive identity criterion, and worse, a *global* one (`sameAs` is not context-relative). OPDA holds — as a ratified, load-bearing modelling commitment — that this proposition is **FALSE**:

1. **Shared identifier ⇏ same individual, because identifiers are contingent and re-assignable.** ODR-0005 §3a hard-cases this directly: a UPRN survives administrative re-numbering (`flat-with-split-uprn.ttl` exemplar), can be re-issued, retired, split, merged (§Rules 6, `prov:wasDerivedFrom` succession). Two records sharing a UPRN via re-numbering, data error, or a maintained boundary are **not** the same individual — they are the *correct* situation an IFP axiom would silently fuse. The session-048 panel said exactly this (Gandon line 117; Hendler line 121: "two records sharing a UPRN via re-numbering / data error / maintained boundary are NOT the same individual, and the IFP axiom tells the Web they are"). The formal name for the defect: **IFP asserts the identifier is a rigid, universal IC; ODR-0005 establishes it is an anti-rigid contingent Quality.** That is an OntoClean rigidity-clash at the level of the published axiom.

2. **Identity in OPDA is bounded-context, and `sameAs`/IFP is global.** ODR-0005's three-class split (`Property` / `LegalEstate` / `RegisteredTitle`) exists *precisely because* the same real-world situation bears **different identity criteria in different contexts**: spatial-material continuity (physical), rights-bundle persistence (legal), title-number lineage (registry). A UPRN appearing on a `Property` record and on an `energyEfficiency.certificate` record co-refers *via a controlled `opda:identifiesSameProperty` predicate or a SHACL key*, never via `owl:sameAs` (ODR-0005 Option G rejected unanimously 12-0; §Rules 5; Anti-patterns). IFP is `owl:sameAs` in a trenchcoat. Authoring it re-introduces, as a standing TBox axiom, the exact construct ODR-0005 spent a full council session (S005, Queen: me) excluding. The bounded-context identity doctrine and a global IFP are **logically incompatible** — not "in tension," incompatible: IFP entails the cross-context merge ODR-0005 forbids.

3. **The asymmetry with domain/range and disjointWith is an identity-theoretic asymmetry, not a matter of degree** (this is the formal core, and it is what makes the verdict principled):
   - A **wrong/over-broad `rdfs:domain`** read by an external reasoner adds a wrong *type* — a monotone, benign, recoverable error (the individual gains a spurious class; nothing is destroyed).
   - A **wrong `owl:disjointWith`** read externally yields *detect-and-reject* (an inconsistency report) — which is what OPDA *wants* on bad data (engineering-act = ontological-act: OPDA enforces the exclusion via `sh:not`; the axiom asserts the same exclusion).
   - A **wrong-or-even-correct IFP** read externally *merges individuals* — it collapses two distinct endurants into one, irreversibly under inference (RDF 1.1 Semantics §6 on `sameAs` propagation). This is the single operation OPDA's master-data identity doctrine bans by name (ODR-0005 §R5 "catastrophic in master data"; ODR-0017 §R6; ODR-0025 §R2 "Trigger `owl:sameAs` identity-merge").

   The first two pass the engineering-act = ontological-act test (the published proposition is one OPDA holds true, or whose external reading does what OPDA wants). IFP **fails it on both counts**: even a *true* IFP (a genuinely-identifying UPRN) asserts the *globally-merging* `sameAs` proposition OPDA holds false under bounded-context identity, AND the external reading performs the one banned operation. Domain/range and disjointWith are degree-compatible with the doctrine; IFP is *category*-incompatible.

#### Why `dash:uniqueValueForClass` is not a lesser substitute but the *ontologically correct* expression

This is the positive half, and again it is an identity-theoretic point. What OPDA actually believes about a UPRN is: *within the class `opda:Property`, no two distinct instances should share a UPRN* — a **closed-world, class-scoped uniqueness constraint that flags a collision as a data-quality violation**. That is *exactly* `dash:uniqueValueForClass` (TopQuadrant DASH; "no two focus nodes of the given class share a value for this property"). Note what it does and does not say:

- It does **not** say "sharing the value makes them the same" (no IC promotion, no merge).
- It **does** say "sharing the value is an *error to be reported*" (the collision surfaces as a violation; a human or process resolves it — possibly *as* a succession event under ODR-0005 §6a, possibly as a data error).

This is the **uniqueness-as-constraint** reading of an identifier, which is the only reading consistent with treating the identifier as a *contingent Quality* rather than a *constitutive IC*. IFP is the **uniqueness-as-identity** reading, which presupposes the identifier *is* the IC. ODR-0005 ruled, formally and unanimously, that it is not. So `dash:uniqueValueForClass` is not "the safe approximation of IFP" — it is the *correct ontological commitment*, and IFP is the *incorrect* one. The session-035 precedent (ODR-0026 §R3 amendment, 8-0-0) is then directly controlling: **where a safe, evaluable, native substitute carries the same signal, keep the information and drop the unsafe-and-redundant axiom.** Here the substitute carries *more* correct signal than the axiom: it carries the uniqueness *without* the false IC-promotion.

#### The `owl:FunctionalProperty` half (the weaker case)

FP is genuinely weaker — it asserts single-valuedness (at most one value per subject), and *if* evaluated it fires `sameAs` only on the *objects* (two values of a functional property are the same individual), not a cross-subject merge. So a mis-scoped FP at worst asserts a false max-cardinality; it does not merge subjects (Cagle session-048 line 159 conceded this; the "dangerous" framing was withdrawn). My disposition follows the panel: **AGAINST FP as a general documentary layer** (`sh:maxCount 1` states and *fires* the same single-valuedness — ODR-0029 §R3; it is already in the corpus, e.g. `opda-agent-shapes.ttl:148`), but I do **not block** a hand-curated `owl:FunctionalProperty` marker on a genuine single-valued-by-*domain-truth* world-fact (a Property has exactly one UPRN-keyed identity; a Title has exactly one title number) *if* the council wants the ontological-fact-vs-data-shape distinction marked in OWL rather than annotation. I record it as **admissible-but-unnecessary**: the distinction (Guarino & Welty 2009 — an *essential* single-valuedness is a domain truth, a *current-cardinality* single-valuedness is a storage artefact) rides an annotation at least as well as an axiom whose formal content `sh:maxCount 1` already states. The OntoClean discipline I'd impose if it is authored: FP only where single-valuedness is *essential/rigid* (holds in every world the individual exists in), never where it is a contingent data-shape — the characteristics analogue of the rigidity test.

#### Net Q2

Carve OUT of hm ODR-0054's FP/IFP rule (R2). hm authors FP/IFP because hm's identity doctrine is different — hm has no `owl:sameAs` ban of ODR-0005's strength and treats IFP as a benign "uniqueness hint" (hm ODR-0030 table: IFP "Uniqueness hint", inferencing NO). OPDA's local constraint is *stronger and more specific*: a ratified bounded-context identity criterion (ODR-0005) under which the IFP proposition is false. **OPDA's stronger local constraint overrides hm here** — this is the one place where adopting-hm's-coverage-as-baseline yields to OPDA's own prior identity ruling. The carve-out is not a deviation from the "adopt hm" decision; it is the per-construct application of the very test (engineering-act = ontological-act) that the adoption decision is built on.

**Citations:** OWL 2 Direct Semantics §2.3.5 (Functional/InverseFunctional → `owl:sameAs`); OWL 2 Primer §4.2 (IFP as key, `foaf:mbox`); RDF 1.1 Semantics (Hayes & Patel-Schneider 2014) §6 (`owl:sameAs` propagation, irreversible under inference); Guarino & Welty 2002 *Identity and subsumption: how to model …* + 2009 *An Overview of OntoClean* (identity criterion vs identifier; +I/+O meta-properties; rigidity); Lowe 1989 *Kinds of Being* (criteria of identity); ODR-0005 §Rules 5,6 + §Anti-patterns + §3a (UPRN as contingent Quality, bounded-context IC, `owl:sameAs` rejected 12-0); ODR-0017 §R6; ODR-0025 §R2; ODR-0026 §R2/§R3-amendment (session-035, 8-0-0); W3C SHACL §4.2.1 (`sh:maxCount`); DASH `dash:uniqueValueForClass`.

### Q3 — Adopt hm ODR-0028b disjointness (rigid Kinds only, never anti-rigid Roles, pairwise). Confirm the rigid/anti-rigid carve-out from OntoClean.

**Verdict: AFFIRM, scoped. Ballot: FOR authoring `owl:disjointWith` + SHACL `sh:not`/`sh:xone` dual, RIGID KINDS ONLY, NEVER anti-rigid Roles, PAIRWISE (not `owl:AllDisjointClasses`).**

The rigid/anti-rigid carve-out is **mine** in the prior-art (hm ODR-0028b §Vote line: "Identity continuity > anti-rigidity? 9-0 (Guarino)"; ODR-0028b §NOT-Disjoint "Country Roles — Identity Continuity (Guarino)"). I confirm it for OPDA and tighten its formal statement, because the session-048 record slightly under-specifies *why* the carve-out is at the level it is.

**The OntoClean grounding (Guarino & Welty 2002 §3-4):**

- **Disjointness is licensed between rigid Kinds with incompatible identity criteria.** `opda:Person` and `opda:Organisation` are both **rigid sortals** (+R, +I — Substance Kinds in UFO terms, ODR-0006): every instance is necessarily, in every world it exists, a Person (resp. Organisation), and each supplies its own IC (a Person's IC is biological/legal-personhood continuity; an Organisation's is constitutive-document/registration continuity — incompatible). Incompatible ICs on rigid sortals **entail** disjointness (Guarino & Welty 2002: two universals carrying incompatible identity conditions cannot share an instance). Authoring `opda:Person owl:disjointWith opda:Organisation` publishes a proposition OPDA holds **true**, whose external reading (detect-and-reject) does what OPDA wants, and whose internal effect is to **arm** the otherwise-vacuous ADR-0035 disjointness gate (ODR-0025 §R1; Hendler session-048 line 105: "a check needs the axiom to check against"). Engineering-act = ontological-act: passes.

- **Disjointness is FORBIDDEN between anti-rigid Roles that share a bearer's identity.** This is the load-bearing carve-out and it has two distinct grounds, which must not be conflated (the session-048 thread occasionally ran them together):

  **Ground A — anti-rigidity alone is NOT sufficient to forbid disjointness; identity-continuity is the real reason.** This is my correction in hm ODR-0028b (§NOT-Disjoint, 9-0: "Anti-rigidity alone is insufficient; shared identity principle is the formal basis"). `Seller` and `Buyer` are anti-rigid Roles (+anti-R, -I — they borrow their bearer's IC; a Role supplies no IC of its own, Guarino & Welty 2002 §4). The reason `Seller` and `Buyer` are not disjoint is **not** "they're anti-rigid" — it is that *the same Person can play both* (across transactions, or even reciprocally), so they **share the bearer's identity criterion** and an instance can fall under both. Forbidding co-typing at the Role-*kind* level (`Seller owl:disjointWith Buyer`) would publish a proposition OPDA holds **false**: it would tell the Web no individual is ever both a Seller and a Buyer, when in fact most repeat-market participants are exactly that.

  **Ground B — the exclusion OPDA *does* want is relational, not categorial.** What is true is "not both Seller and Buyer *of the same transaction*" — a constraint on the *qua-individuals* / the participation relation (the `playedBy` distinct-node discipline, session-047 Q4), which is **transaction-scoped SHACL** (`sh:not` / `sh:xone` keyed to the transaction), NOT a TBox `owl:disjointWith`. Putting it in `owl:disjointWith` would mis-locate a *relational* exclusivity as a *categorial* one — the exact role/relator confusion session-047 spent Q4 untangling, re-introduced in disjointness form (Hendler session-048 line 69).

  Formally: `owl:disjointWith` is correct between universals with **incompatible ICs**; it is wrong between universals with a **shared (borrowed) IC**. Anti-rigid Roles borrow their bearer's IC, so they share it whenever they share a bearer — hence the blanket prohibition at the Role-kind level. The same applies to the country-role family I analysed in hm ODR-0028b (FiscalCountry/LogisticCountry share the ISO-3166 IC of the underlying country — NOT disjoint).

**Scope I attach (binding, regardless of which doctrinal home the cost-discipline minority preferred):**
1. **Rigid Kinds only** — author `owl:disjointWith` between Substance Kinds with incompatible ICs (Person/Organisation; and the ODR-0005 three Property-family Kinds where their ICs are incompatible — Property/LegalEstate/RegisteredTitle are *co-referring*, not disjoint, so check IC-incompatibility case by case before authoring).
2. **Never anti-rigid Roles** — Seller/Buyer/Proprietor and the country-role family stay co-typable; their exclusivity (where it exists) is transaction/context-scoped SHACL.
3. **Pairwise `owl:disjointWith`, not `owl:AllDisjointClasses`** — no OPDA rule consumes the list form (ADR-0049 Q3); pairwise is the minimal-information form that arms the pairwise COUNT-gate (ADR-0035:132).
4. **SHACL dual mandatory** — `sh:not` (incompatible-IC, no common superclass) or `sh:xone` (sibling subtypes under a common superclass) carries 100% of *enforcement* (ODR-0029 §R3 two-layers-of-meaning; the `owl:disjointWith`-in-class-graph + `sh:not`-in-shapes-graph architecture is the identical dual to the ratified domain→SHACL dual — Hendler's §R3-dual precedent, which retired Cagle's doctrinal-home objection, session-048 line 142).

On the session-048 residual (Cagle's non-blocking cost-discipline HOLD, contingent on Q1): it is now **largely moot**. His residual was "the only OWL-only delta is the *named union class* serving the partition-coherence with Q1's `owl:unionOf`." But Q1 is now repeated-`rdfs:domain` "any-of" — **there is no `owl:unionOf`, hence no named union class** for disjointness to cohere with. So the partition-coherence argument that Hendler and I used to bundle disjointWith with Q1(b) *evaporates on the hm-adoption*, and with it the only residual cost-objection. `owl:disjointWith` now stands on its own footing — armed-gate + two-layers-of-meaning dual — with no boolean-constructor entanglement at all. The hm-adoption *strengthens* Q3's footing (it removes the one contested coupling) even as it supersedes Q1.

**Citations:** Guarino & Welty 2002 *Evaluating ontological decisions with OntoClean* / 2009 *An Overview of OntoClean* (rigidity +R/+anti-R; identity +I; incompatible-IC ⇒ disjointness; Roles borrow bearer IC); Guizzardi 2005 *Ontological Foundations* Ch. 4 (Substance Kind / Role / anti-rigidity); OWL 2 Direct Semantics §2.3.2 (`owl:disjointWith` = empty intersection of class extensions); hm ODR-0028b (rigid/anti-rigid carve-out, Guarino 9-0; country-role NOT-disjoint); ODR-0006 (Person/Organisation Kinds); ODR-0005 (three Property Kinds); ODR-0025 §R1 (disjointness consistency gate); ADR-0035:132 (pairwise closed-world COUNT-gate); ODR-0029 §R3 (domain→SHACL dual precedent).

### Q4 — Consequences for the relationship layer + supersession of session-048.

**Verdict: AFFIRM the consequences as set out in ADR-0049, with the identity-doctrine refinements below.**

1. **Relationship-layer object properties regain documentary domain/range (the Q1 consequence).** `playedBy`/`plays`/`hasParticipant`/`hasAddress` get repeated-`rdfs:domain` "any-of" (disjunctive bearers); `founds`/`mediates` get plain single-class domain/range. This **reverses** the session-047/ADR-0048 SHACL-only typing decision and the "would entail / NEVER reasoned / anti-pattern" comments at `opda-agent.ttl` (those comments encoded the standard-OWL-entailment reasoning that ADR-0049's corollary explicitly rejects for OPDA — "do not reason about OPDA from standard W3C OWL/RDFS entailment semantics"). From my lens the important point: restoring documentary domain/range on the *relationship* layer makes it **consistent with the ~750 datatype properties** that already carry domain/range + SHACL dual (ODR-0029 §R3). The relationship layer was the *anomaly*; ADR-0049 removes the anomaly. This also lets the class-graph model derivation see the edges natively (the `scripts/ontology-model.mjs` SHACL-derivation hack becomes belt-and-braces, not load-bearing — implementation T6).

2. **Supersession of session-048 is correctly scoped.** Session-048 is superseded **only on Q1** (`owl:unionOf` → repeated-`rdfs:domain`). Its Q2 (4-0 against FP/IFP) and Q3 (FOR-scoped `owl:disjointWith`) **stand and are re-affirmed by this session** on the deeper identity grounding above. The supersession is a *form* change on Q1 (which boolean-free disjunction syntax), not a reversal of the documentary-OWL doctrine — Q2/Q3 and the 750 datatype properties affirm that doctrine. I want this recorded precisely so the session-048 panel's substantive wins (especially the IFP-out verdict, which is the identity-load-bearing one) are not read as "overturned."

3. **The disjointness gate is armed (Q3 consequence).** Authoring scoped `owl:disjointWith` moves the ADR-0035 disjointness gate from vacuous to active — `Person owl:disjointWith Organisation` (and any IC-incompatible Property-family pairs) now gives the pairwise COUNT-gate something to check. This is a *net new* data-quality capability, not just documentation.

4. **The identity doctrine is left strictly intact (Q2 consequence) — and this is the consequence I most want on the record.** By carving FP/IFP out, ADR-0049 ensures the relationship-layer rework adds **zero** new identity-bearing OWL axioms. The only identity machinery remains: SHACL/DASH uniqueness keys (`dash:uniqueValueForClass`), controlled co-reference (`opda:identifiesSameProperty`), and PROV-O succession (`prov:wasDerivedFrom`) — exactly the ODR-0005 settlement. No `owl:sameAs`, no IFP, no `owl:hasKey`-as-sole-identity (ODR-0005 Option F rejected). The bounded-context identity doctrine survives the OWL-coverage adoption untouched, *because* the adoption is per-construct and the FP/IFP construct is the one that would have breached it.

5. **Proof obligations (mine to insist on, as the formal-ontology seat):** the ADR-0035 **zero-domain/range-triple consistency test** is the standing proof that "authored-but-never-evaluated" holds — it must pass after authoring (it will; no rule consumes repeated-domain, `owl:disjointWith`, or FP/IFP). Additionally the **negative consistency gate** (no `owl:sameAs`, no spurious `EPCCertificate ⊑ Property`) must remain green — and with FP/IFP carved out, there is no new `sameAs` surface for it to police. These two gates *are* the machine-checkable form of the engineering-act = ontological-act test. Regenerate `ontology-model.json` + `ontology-graph-elements.json` via `build:data` (the model-drift gate, per CI-gate-topology).

### Q1 — Repeated-`rdfs:domain` "any-of" for disjoint bearers. AFFIRM. Ballot: FOR.

Sound from an identity/sortal standpoint **because OPDA never evaluates domain/range** (ODR-0025 §R2 / ODR-0026 §R2 / ODR-0029 §R1) — the standard-RDFS conjunction reading (which would force incompatible ICs onto one individual, an OntoClean identity-violation) never fires. Repeated-`rdfs:domain` is *more* identity-correct than `owl:unionOf` (the superseded session-048 form), not merely more cross-project-consistent: the union of disjoint sortals is a non-sortal/category carrying no IC (Guarino & Welty 2009), so `owl:unionOf` would mint a named, subclassable `owl:Class` for something that is ontologically *not a kind*; repeated-domain asserts the weaker, correct "any-of-this-list" with no reification of a pseudo-sortal. For the Role∪RoleMixin case anti-rigidity doubles the defect (a union class over anti-rigid Roles is neither sortal nor rigid). **Binding caveat (I concur with Hendler, and it strengthens my opening):** the "any-of" convention is the standards conjunction read against its semantics — a *competent* LLM (the AI-signal audience ADR-0049 courts) reads the comma-list as conjunction and concludes the property is contradictory or, if Q3 makes the disjuncts disjoint, *unsatisfiable* (`owl:Nothing`). So the module-header note + per-property `rdfs:comment` are **load-bearing mitigation, CI-gated MUST, not decoration** — they are the only thing converting an incorrect-by-standards axiom into a legible one. The ADR-0035 zero-triple test is the standing proof the conjunction never materialises. Author the convention; gate the convention.

### Q2 — THE IDENTITY CRUX. REJECT authoring IFP entirely; REJECT FP as a documentary layer. Carve OUT from hm ODR-0054. Ballot: AGAINST authoring FP/IFP as axioms; FOR `sh:maxCount 1` / `dash:uniqueValueForClass` (within-sortal) + annotation.

This is my seat's decisive contribution and the panel adopted the grounding. **The binding reason is identity-doctrinal, not economy-of-axioms** (Hendler co-signed this ordering): IFP conflates an **identifier** (extrinsic, administratively-assigned, contingent — a UFO Quality) with an **identity criterion** (intrinsic, constitutive, context-invariant — Guarino & Welty 2002; Lowe 1989). IFP's entire model-theoretic content (OWL 2 §2.3.5) promotes the contingent identifier to a *constitutive, global* IC ("shared value ⟹ `owl:sameAs`, in every context"). ODR-0005 ruled — by ratified council verdict, 12-0 on the `owl:sameAs` rejection (Option G), and hard-cased against re-numbering / re-issue / split / merge — that the identifier is **not** the IC and that identity is **bounded-context**. So authoring IFP *publishes the negation of ODR-0005's identity ruling as a standing TBox axiom*. Formally: IFP asserts the identifier is a rigid universal IC; ODR-0005 establishes it as an anti-rigid contingent Quality — an OntoClean rigidity-clash at the published-axiom level. The asymmetry with domain/range and disjointWith is *categorial, not degree*: a wrong domain adds a benign type; a wrong disjointWith yields detect-and-reject (what OPDA wants); a wrong-**or-even-correct** IFP merges individuals (the one banned operation). Even a true IFP asserts the globally-merging `sameAs` OPDA holds false.

**The substitute is the *correct* ontological commitment, not a lesser approximation:** `dash:uniqueValueForClass` reads the identifier as *within-sortal uniqueness-as-constraint* (flag-not-fuse), which is the only reading consistent with treating it as a contingent Quality; IFP is the uniqueness-as-identity reading ODR-0005 forbids. **Refinement won in cross-talk with Cagle (binding):** the SHACL uniqueness must be scoped WITHIN each identity-bearing sortal, NEVER cross-sortal — because the same UPRN legitimately recurs across the three co-referring Property-family Kinds; a cross-sortal uniqueness would fire a violation on the *correct* co-reference and is `owl:InverseFunctionalProperty` in SHACL clothing. The within-sortal scope IS the bounded-context identity criterion expressed in SHACL. session-035 (ODR-0026 §R3, 8-0-0) is the controlling precedent on the corroborating prong: complete safe substitute ⇒ keep the information, drop the unsafe-redundant axiom. **FP:** AGAINST as a general layer (`sh:maxCount 1` states and fires it); a hand-curated `owl:FunctionalProperty` on a genuine *essential/rigid* single-valued world-fact is admissible-but-unnecessary if the council wants the ontological-fact-vs-data-shape distinction marked in OWL. This is the one place OPDA's stronger local constraint (ODR-0005) overrides the adopt-hm baseline — and it does so *by* the very engineering-act = ontological-act test the adoption is built on. Re-open trigger (constitutional bar, co-signed with Hendler): a real consumer needs the open-world identity-key *entailment* AND ODR-0005 is first revised to permit global `sameAs`. **The trigger is itself the proof that reason (ii) is binding, not (i)** (Hendler's independent argument, which I endorse): the trigger fires ONLY on overturning ODR-0005, never on "the signal isn't fully redundant." A redundancy-grounded verdict would be re-openable by a *modelling-economy* argument (e.g. the session-048 "scope lives in a human's head" worry); an identity-doctrine-grounded verdict is re-openable ONLY by a *constitutional* act (revising ODR-0005's 12-0 ruling). This fixes the verdict as a CLOSED question, not a deferred one.

### Q3 — Disjointness, rigid/anti-rigid carve-out. AFFIRM, scoped. Ballot: FOR pairwise `owl:disjointWith` + SHACL dual, rigid Kinds only, never anti-rigid Roles, pairwise not `owl:AllDisjointClasses`.

The rigid/anti-rigid carve-out is mine in the prior art (hm ODR-0028b, Guarino 9-0) and I confirm it for OPDA with the formal grounding the panel deferred to my seat (Hendler, Allemang, Cagle all defer explicitly). **Disjointness is licensed between rigid sortals with incompatible ICs** — `Person`/`Organisation` are rigid Substance Kinds whose ICs cannot co-inhere, and incompatible ICs on rigid sortals *entail* disjointness (Guarino & Welty 2002). **Disjointness is forbidden between anti-rigid Roles that share a bearer's (borrowed) IC** — `Seller`/`Buyer` are anti-rigid Roles a single Person plays across transactions; the reason they are not disjoint is **identity-continuity, NOT anti-rigidity alone** (my correction in hm ODR-0028b): they share the bearer's IC whenever they share a bearer, so an instance can fall under both, and asserting `Seller owl:disjointWith Buyer` publishes a proposition OPDA holds false. The exclusivity OPDA *does* want ("not both of the SAME transaction") is *relational*, living in transaction-scoped SHACL on the qua-individuals (session-047 Q4), never categorial TBox. **Scope (binding) — the bar is rigid Kinds with INCOMPATIBLE ICs, which is tighter than ADR-0049's "rigid Kinds only" (a refinement I ruled for Hendler):** author `owl:disjointWith` only between rigid Kinds whose identity criteria are *incompatible* — which excludes TWO classes, for two distinct formal reasons: (a) anti-rigid Roles/RoleMixins (no own IC to be incompatible — they borrow the bearer's; -I in OntoClean), AND (b) co-referring rigid Kinds (own ICs, but COMPLEMENTARY not incompatible — different identity lenses on one referent, joined by `opda:identifiesSameProperty`; complementary ICs make extensions co-referential, the precise opposite of disjoint). "Rigid Kinds only" is too loose — it would wrongly admit the three Property-family Kinds. **Pairwise only, never `owl:AllDisjointClasses`** (co-signed with Hendler — no rule consumes the list; the ADR-0035 pairwise COUNT-gate only reads pairwise; an authored AllDisjointClasses fails the no-unconsumed-axiom test); SHACL `sh:not`/`sh:xone` dual mandatory (the §R3-dual two-layers-of-meaning architecture, which retired Cagle's doctrinal-home objection). **Why disjointWith adopts where IFP cannot:** the ADR-0035 gate *consumes the authored axiom as its input* (verified ADR-0035:132; confirmed to Allemang, settling his FOR) — so the axiom is not redundant; it ARMS an otherwise-vacuous check. That is the engineering-act = ontological-act test passing where it fails for FP/IFP. **Positive-scope tightening (sharpened in cross-talk with Allemang — this is my final positive line):** the STANDING scope is `Person owl:disjointWith Organisation` ALONE; Relator-Kind disjointness (Transaction/Proprietorship etc.) is **deferred, not authored now**. The authoring test is three-part: author `owl:disjointWith` only where (i) both classes are rigid sortals carrying their own IC (rules out all Roles/RoleMixins), (ii) their ICs are genuinely incompatible (no instance can fall under both), AND (iii) misclassification is a *real, occurring* master-data hazard the gate should catch (hm ODR-0028b condition 2+3 — the domain-invariant test). Person/Organisation passes all three cleanly. Relator Kinds pass (i) and arguably (ii) but FAIL (iii): a relator's IC is the specific founded relationship (relata + relation-type + temporal frame; Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4), OPDA's relators are lifecycle-coupled and share relata (a Proprietorship is founded within a Transaction's completion), so the collision an axiom would guard against doesn't occur in practice (unconsumed axiom) AND the sharper separation risks over-authoring. So Relator-Kind disjointness is admissible-in-principle but gated on a named consumer + a verified per-pair IC-incompatibility check. **Caution I added to the scope:** the three Property-family Kinds (Property/LegalEstate/RegisteredTitle) are the counter-example — distinct Kinds but NOT disjoint, because they co-refer via `opda:identifiesSameProperty` (complementary perspectives, not incompatible ICs). Bright line: incompatible-IC + real-hazard ⇒ disjoint; shared-or-co-referring-IC ⇒ never. **Cagle's session-048 cost-discipline residual is now moot** (he and Hendler both confirm): it was contingent on Q1's `owl:unionOf`; with repeated-`rdfs:domain` there is no union class to cohere with, so disjointWith stands on its own with zero boolean-constructor entanglement. The hm-adoption *strengthens* Q3's footing even as it supersedes Q1.
