# Moreau — solo position on S013 (prov-time-extended; PROV-O × SHACL severity)

*Solo position file. PROV-O voice carrying OWL-Time-extended scope (S009 Q7 `sh:xone` evidence dispatch + S014 prov-time slice). Load-bearing on Q1 (severity-tier ratification for ODR-0017 SHACL-AF rules across all 11 citing sites) and Q6 (profile interaction with PROV-O-flavoured SHACL). Brief positions on Q2, Q3, Q4, Q5, Q7. Queen Knublauch; DA Cagle.*

## Stance summary

S013 is the Phase 6 CLOSING session for the SHACL validation stub. ODR-0013 was authored 2026-05-20 with three-tier severity already named (`sh:Violation` / `sh:Warning` / `sh:Info`); since then ODR-0017 has extracted the SHACL-AF non-blocking-quality-rules pattern across four sibling sites; S009 ratified the `sh:xone` evidence-type dispatch over PROV; ODR-0010 fixed the build-step graph-union for profile composition. My load on this session is to **ratify the severity tier as a normative table** — not invent it (the principle is already in ODR-0013 §Rules + ODR-0017 §2a) but pin every citing site to a specific severity and produce the machine-readable table downstream tooling (`odr-review` lint; the SHACL generator; LLM consumers per Hellmann et al. DBpedia 2017) can consume. Q6 is my secondary load: PROV-O-flavoured SHACL (S009 Q7 `sh:xone` evidence dispatch + the qualified-attribution shapes from ODR-0009) must compose cleanly with the profile mechanism (ODR-0010 §1a build-step graph-union) without leaking PROV's open-world derivation graph into the closed-world profile shape.

Per ODR-0001 §Two-artefact discipline (ratified 2026-05-27 amendments), this position is brief on the conceded questions and surgical on the two I carry.

## Per-question positions

### Q1 — Severity-tier ratification table (LOAD-BEARING)

**Principle re-stated from ODR-0013 §Rules (Guizzardi):** severity tracks **regulatory weight**, not schema nesting. The rarest, most damaging error must be the loudest. Three tiers, ratified across the 11 citing sites:

| Severity | Trigger discipline | Citing sites |
|---|---|---|
| **`sh:Violation`** | Normative-breaking: would corrupt a downstream consumer if the document advances. Reserved for (i) identity-contract breach per ODR-0005 Rule 3 (`dash:uniqueValueForClass` on `opda:uprn` — a `Property` with no resolvable identity key); (ii) identity-override by overlay per ODR-0010 §2a no-identity-override gate (a profile that restates a Kind's key is rejected at load); (iii) special-category PII without lawful-basis per ODR-0012 Q3 (Cagle dissent — un-annotated AML/conviction-adjacent data); (iv) unprovenanced `opda:Claim` with no `prov:wasDerivedFrom` AND no explicit "unverified" marker per ODR-0009 §SHACL-over-PROV. | ODR-0005 §3a; ODR-0006 (Role-without-Relator); ODR-0009 §SHACL-over-PROV; ODR-0010 §2a; ODR-0012 sensitivity gate (Cagle escalation only). |
| **`sh:Warning`** | Data-quality finding with a re-open trigger: the document may advance but a Council re-open is warranted if conditions persist. Reserved for (i) overdue milestone state per ODR-0007; (ii) same-variant disagreement per ODR-0008 (Address variant conflict); (iii) sensitivity-marker gaps on personal data per ODR-0012 (non-special-category — Cagle Q3 reserves `sh:Violation` for special-category); (iv) deprecation-without-succession per ODR-0011 §5a (Cagle S011 Q5 amendment); (v) profile-specific disclosure gaps per ODR-0010 (overlay-added required leaves). | ODR-0007; ODR-0008; ODR-0010; ODR-0011 §5a; ODR-0012 (general PII gaps). |
| **`sh:Info`** | Non-blocking data-quality assertion: correct under its temporal/contextual scope; informative, not normative-breaking. The full ODR-0017 §2a top-of-table case — substantive succession recorded. | ODR-0005 §6a (UPRN succession-with-chain); ODR-0011 §5a (deprecation-with-replacement); ODR-0015 §4a (INSPIRE / OS AddressBase succession); ODR-0009 §6a (PROV-O evidence-collection-event lineage, when S009's rule lands); ODR-0006 §X (Person-ID succession, when ODR-0006 produces its rule); absent optional attributes (ODR-0013 §Rules `sh:Info` floor). |

The principle binds: **every `sh:Violation` shape must guard a Kind's identity contract OR a normative-breaking finding the regulator would flag; no optional-attribute gap may carry `sh:Violation`** (ODR-0013 §Rules verbatim). Conversely, **every SHACL-AF rule declaring `sh:Info` or `sh:Warning` MUST `implements: ODR-0017`** (ODR-0017 §Consequences lint extension). The table is the operational contract; the lint reads it; the generator emits per row.

**The four normative-breaking categories for `sh:Violation`, named:**

1. **Identity-contract breach (ODR-0005 Rule 3).** `dash:uniqueValueForClass true` on `opda:uprn` is THE primary, checkable mechanism per ODR-0005 Rule 3. A `Property` with no resolvable identity key — UPRN-or-equivalent (HMLR title number for `RegisteredTitle`; INSPIRE / OS AddressBase ID for `Address` per ODR-0015 §2a) — is `sh:Violation` because downstream property-resolution heuristics will degrade silently into `owl:sameAs` fallback per Hellmann et al. (DBpedia 2017). The graceful-degradation test (ODR-0005 §3a) carves out unregistered-pre-first-registration cases — a `Property` with NO `opda:uprn` triple produces NO violation; only a `Property` claiming UPRN-equivalence WITHOUT resolving fires.
2. **Identity-override by overlay (ODR-0010 §2a Q6 amendment).** A profile that declares `sh:property [ sh:path opda:uprn ; sh:minCount 1 ; sh:datatype xsd:string ]` on `opda:Property` is admissible (it tightens presence). A profile that declares `dash:uniqueValueForClass false` on `opda:uprn` OR re-targets `dash:uniqueValueForClass` to a different property is REJECTED at profile load — identity belongs to the rigid Kind per Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4, not to a form context. The gate is structural (ODR-0013 §Rules class-graph drift check).
3. **Special-category PII without lawful-basis (ODR-0012 Q3 Cagle dissent).** Article-10 territory: `cautionOrConviction`, AML outcomes, conviction-adjacent terms. Per Cagle's Q3 dissent (recorded live in ODR-0012 §Consequences as unresolved), a special-category PII leaf without `dpv:hasPersonalDataCategory` AND `dpv:hasLegalBasis` annotation fires `sh:Violation`. General PII gaps (name/DOB/contact without `dpv:` annotation) fire `sh:Warning` — the Cagle escalation applies only to the special-category subset per Article 9 GDPR.
4. **Unprovenanced `opda:Claim` (ODR-0009 §SHACL-over-PROV bullet 1).** An `opda:Claim` with no `prov:wasDerivedFrom` AND no explicit "unverified" marker is a contradiction in a trust framework. The shape: `opda:Claim sh:property [ sh:path prov:wasDerivedFrom ; sh:minCount 1 ; sh:severity sh:Violation ; sh:message "unprovenanced claim — must derive from evidence or carry unverified marker" ]`. The carve-out "unverified" marker (`opda:assuranceLevel opda:Self-asserted` per ODR-0009 §Vocabulary delegation) satisfies the shape via `sh:or` branch.

**Why the four `sh:Violation` categories carve at the joints.** The S001 Q5 settlement bound severity to regulatory weight: identity-loss is catastrophic because property resolution silently fails; identity-override is catastrophic because the profile mechanism mutates the Kind contract; unprovenanced claim is catastrophic because the trust framework cannot deliver assurance on un-sourced data; special-category PII without lawful-basis is catastrophic because GDPR Article 9 makes the processing unlawful. All four are recoverable only by data-graph correction, not by profile-tweaking. The remaining citing sites (ODR-0007 milestones; ODR-0008 attribute variants; ODR-0010 disclosure gaps; ODR-0011 §5a deprecation; ODR-0012 general PII) are recoverable by Council re-open or data correction — `sh:Warning` floor. The ODR-0017 informative rules are correct under their temporal scope — `sh:Info` floor.

**Vote on Q1: FOR three-tier severity-table ratification + per-citing-site assignment as above + four-categories-for-`sh:Violation` discipline named + `odr-review` lint extension verifying (a) every `sh:Violation` traces to one of the four normative-breaking categories, (b) every `sh:Info`/`sh:Warning` SHACL-AF rule carries `implements: ODR-0017`, (c) the table is the canonical mechanically-readable artefact downstream tooling consumes.**

### Q6 — Profile interaction with PROV-O-flavoured SHACL (LOAD-BEARING)

**Two distinct PROV-flavoured SHACL bodies must compose with the profile mechanism:**

1. **S009 Q7 `sh:xone` evidence-type dispatch** (over `opda:DocumentEvidence` / `opda:ElectronicRecordEvidence` / `opda:VouchEvidence` from ODR-0009). These are normative shapes — they reproduce the JSON schema's `if/then` on `evidence.type` as closed-world dispatch. Per ODR-0009 §SHACL-over-PROV, every conformant evidence node MUST match exactly one branch.
2. **ODR-0017 SHACL-AF rules at `sh:Info` / `sh:Warning`** (the four-plus-citing-site pattern). These are informative — they materialise succession/deprecation/lineage into the validation report; they do NOT block the document.

**Composition with ODR-0010 §1a build-step graph-union:**

- **Normative PROV-O SHACL shapes (S009 Q7 `sh:xone`) are profile-EXTENSIBLE.** A loaded profile (BASPI5; TA6; the Conveyancer overlay) MAY tighten the `sh:xone` by adding a branch-specific `sh:property` constraint (e.g. BASPI5 requires `electronic_record.source.name` carry a specific authority URI). The composition rule mirrors ODR-0010 §Rules item 2 (enum union → merged `sh:in`): at build time, the profile-added constraint is graph-unioned with the base `sh:xone` branch; the loaded profile's branch supplants the base where overlap exists, never stacks two `sh:xone` constraints. A profile MAY NOT remove a `sh:xone` branch (that would weaken the closed-world dispatch — the no-weakening gate per Knublauch's S001 Q5 settlement).
- **ODR-0017 informative SHACL-AF rules are profile-INCLUDABLE/EXCLUDABLE.** Per the build-step graph-union, a profile MAY include the ODR-0017 rule (e.g. the Conveyancer overlay activates UPRN-succession reporting because conveyancers need to see lineage on stale-reference checks); a profile MAY exclude it (e.g. the AML-screening overlay suppresses INSPIRE succession noise because it is irrelevant to AML decisions). The exclusion is by NOT-including the rule's shape declaration in the profile graph; per ODR-0010 §Rules item 2 build-step semantics, "loaded profile = active rule". The default-loaded base profile MUST include all four ODR-0017 citing sites; per-overlay opt-out is the variation.
- **No-identity-override gate (ODR-0010 §2a) applies recursively.** A profile MAY NOT redefine `opda:uprn` as the identity key for `opda:Address` (it isn't — `opda:Address` has its own UPRN-or-INSPIRE-or-OS-AddressBase identity per ODR-0015 §2a). The gate's scope is unchanged; PROV-O shapes are not exempt.
- **PROV's open-world derivation graph DOES NOT leak.** The `prov:wasDerivedFrom` edge is open-world (PROV-O is an RDF vocabulary, not a SHACL constraint). The SHACL shape that VALIDATES the presence of `prov:wasDerivedFrom` (per ODR-0009 §SHACL-over-PROV bullet 1 — unprovenanced claim = `sh:Violation`) is closed-world. The two coexist in distinct graphs per ODR-0004 §3a three-graph separation: class graph carries the PROV ontology import; shapes graph carries the validation constraint. The CI test `ASK { GRAPH opda:shapes { ?s prov:wasDerivedFrom ?o } }` returns false — provenance data lives in the data graph, not the shapes graph.

**Worked composition example (BASPI5 profile × ODR-0009 evidence-type dispatch).** Base shapes graph carries `opda:EvidenceDispatchShape sh:targetClass opda:Evidence ; sh:xone ( opda:DocumentEvidenceShape opda:ElectronicRecordEvidenceShape opda:VouchEvidenceShape )`. BASPI5 profile graph tightens the `ElectronicRecordEvidence` branch: `opda:BASPI5ElectronicRecordTighten sh:targetClass opda:ElectronicRecordEvidence ; sh:property [ sh:path opda:recordSourceName ; sh:in ( <https://hmlr.gov.uk> <https://landregistry.data.gov.uk> ) ; sh:severity sh:Violation ]`. Build-step graph-union per ODR-0010 §Rules item 2: at build time, the BASPI5 branch is graph-unioned into the active shapes graph; the `sh:xone` outer structure is unchanged (no branch-removal); the inner `sh:property` is graph-unioned so a BASPI5-loaded validation checks both base presence AND BASPI5-tightened source-authority constraint. Per the regression guard, the build-step test asserts: (i) the active `sh:xone` still has three branches (no removal); (ii) the BASPI5 `sh:in` is the BASPI5-authority list (not stacked with a base `sh:in`). The AML-screening overlay opts out of the ODR-0017 INSPIRE-succession rule by NOT including `opda:INSPIRESuccessionNonBlockingRule` in its profile graph; the BASPI5 profile MUST include it (conveyancers need lineage). Per ODR-0010 §Rules item 5 DASH rendering: each branch's `dash:propertyRole`/`dash:viewer`/`dash:editor` carry through the graph-union unchanged — the form-generation surface is profile-aware automatically.

**Vote on Q6: FOR (a) normative PROV-O `sh:xone` shapes profile-extensible per ODR-0010 §1a build-step graph-union with branch-tightening admissible and branch-removal prohibited; (b) ODR-0017 SHACL-AF informative rules profile-includable/excludable per the same build-step semantics, base profile includes all four citing sites; (c) ODR-0010 §2a no-identity-override gate applies to PROV-flavoured shapes unchanged; (d) PROV's open-world derivation graph and the closed-world SHACL validation shape coexist in distinct graphs per ODR-0004 §3a.**

### Q2 — JSON-Schema → SHACL mapping for PROV-O properties (brief — CONCEDE)

PROV-O properties get standard SHACL datatype/class shapes: `prov:atTime` → `sh:datatype xsd:dateTime`; `prov:wasAttributedTo` → `sh:class prov:Agent`; `prov:qualifiedAttribution` → `sh:class prov:Attribution` with `sh:property` chains for `prov:hadRole` (per ODR-0009 §PROV-O backbone). The mapping table extends ODR-0013 §Rules constraint-mapping with one PROV-O row. **CONCEDE.**

### Q3 — DASH coverage (brief — CONCEDE)

DASH (`dash:propertyRole` / `dash:viewer` / `dash:editor` / `dash:uniqueValueForClass` / `dash:EnumSelectEditor`) reproduces the form on the shapes that validate (ODR-0013 §Rules DASH rendering). Acceptable because DASH is a documented W3C-community vocabulary; undocumented advisory terms (e.g. `opda:aiHint`) are NOT acceptable in the shapes graph (Q4). **CONCEDE.**

### Q4 — `aiHint` exile (brief — CONCEDE)

Advisory `opda:aiHint` annotations live in the separate annotation graph (`opda-annotations.ttl` per ODR-0004 §3a) keyed to shape IRIs (ODR-0013 §Rules class⊥shapes⊥annotation graph separation). Cagle's S001 Q5 inline-hint dissent (≈7-2) is recorded; the shapes graph carries no undocumented advisory terms. **CONCEDE.**

### Q5 — SHACL-native validation report consumed by LLM tooling (brief — CONCEDE)

The SHACL validation report (`sh:ValidationReport` with `sh:result` / `sh:resultMessage` / `sh:focusNode` triples) is the canonical machine-readable channel; LLM tooling consumes the rendered `sh:resultMessage` per Hellmann et al. (DBpedia 2017) — the structured template substitutes for the natural-language `rdfs:comment` fallback that LLMs misread. ODR-0017 §3a machine-consumability requirement applies: the report is consumed by (i) SHACL validators (canonical consumer), (ii) `odr-review` lint extensions (verifies severity-tier table compliance), (iii) LLM tooling (parses structured `sh:resultMessage` template form), (iv) audit-trail consumers (correlates `sh:Info`/`sh:Warning` entries with data-graph reified events per ODR-0009 §PROV-O backbone). **CONCEDE** — no bespoke channel; the W3C SHACL Recommendation §3.1 report vocabulary suffices.

### Q7 — Three-rule interface contract (brief — CONCEDE)

ODR-0013 §Rules constraint-mapping table + severity tiering + class⊥shapes⊥annotation separation are the three normative rules; ODR-0013 owns the severity-tier table I ratify in Q1; the interface contract for downstream consumers (lint, generator, LLM tooling, audit-trail) is fixed per ODR-0017 §3a. **CONCEDE.**

## PROV-time-extended scope note

This solo carries the prov-time-extended dimension: PROV-O's `prov:atTime` / `prov:startedAtTime` / `prov:endedAtTime` are instant-typed (`xsd:dateTime`); claim-validity *intervals* require OWL-Time (`time:Interval`, `time:hasBeginning`, `time:hasEnd`) per ODR-0009 §References (OWL-Time deferral to ODR-0014 retired → ODR-0002 §Change log; OWL-Time now in Core-tier per ODR-0002). The severity-tier table interaction: where an `opda:Verification` activity asserts `prov:endedAtTime` only and the claim-validity Quality (e.g. an AML check valid for six months per MLR 2017) requires an interval, the absent `time:Interval` is `sh:Warning` (data-quality re-open trigger — same-variant disagreement between PROV instant and OWL-Time interval) NOT `sh:Violation` (not normative-breaking unless interval is required by lawful basis). Lint extension: where a Verification activity's `prov:hadPlan` references a plan declaring `opda:requiresValidityInterval true`, the absent interval escalates to `sh:Violation`. The full interaction belongs in ODR-0014's Programme-time slot; this position records the discipline.

## Operational specifications — what lands in ODR-0013 (post-S013)

ODR-0013 §Rules absorbs three operational specifications from this position:

1. **Severity-tier table § (new — replaces the current §Rules `Severity tiering` bullet).** Three-tier severity table with eleven citing-site assignments as named in Q1 above; four-categories-for-`sh:Violation` discipline (identity-contract breach; identity-override; special-category PII without lawful-basis; unprovenanced claim); `dpv:hasPurpose`-on-special-category-PII escalation rule per the Q1 DA engagement. Lint MUST verify per-site assignment; generator emits per row.
2. **Profile-composition table § (new — interlocks with ODR-0010 §Rules item 2).** PROV-O `sh:xone` shapes are profile-extensible (branch-tightening admissible; branch-removal prohibited); ODR-0017 SHACL-AF informative rules are profile-includable/excludable (default-loaded base includes all four citing sites; per-overlay opt-out is the variation); no-identity-override gate applies unchanged. Build-step graph-union recurses into nested `sh:xone` × `sh:in` per the Q6 DA engagement; the regression guard extends to assert single-valued `sh:in` post-merge inside branches.
3. **PROV-time interaction § (new — points to ODR-0014 Programme-time slot).** PROV instant vs OWL-Time interval mismatch is `sh:Warning` (data-quality) UNLESS the Verification activity's `prov:hadPlan` declares `opda:requiresValidityInterval true`, in which case escalation to `sh:Violation`. The full discipline is deferred to ODR-0014; ODR-0013 records the pointer.

The three sections fit the §Rules layout per ODR-0001 A9 (`kind: architecture` discipline). The current ODR-0013 §Rules `Severity tiering` bullet is replaced by section 1; section 2 is added under §Rules as a new bullet (`Profile composition` — paragraph form); section 3 is recorded as a forward-pointer paragraph at the end of §Rules. No changes to §Decision; §Consequences gains the lint-extension bullet noting the three sections.

## DA anticipation (Cagle — inline-hint and LLM-consumability dissent)

**Anticipated DA line.** Cagle's S001 Q5 dissent (≈7-2) recorded preference for inline `opda:aiHint` annotations in the shapes graph; ODR-0013 §Rules exiled them to the separate annotation graph. Cagle may re-press: (i) the LLM consumer reading the validation report does NOT necessarily resolve into a second graph fetch; (ii) the `sh:message` template (ODR-0017 §1a) IS the inline-hint surface and SHOULD be permitted to carry richer prose. **Engagement on (i):** Conceded that LLM consumers may not fetch a second graph; the resolution is that the shapes graph carries machine-readable `sh:message` templates per ODR-0017 §1a (key-value or template-literal form), and the annotation graph carries the prose elaboration. The LLM reads the validation report's `sh:resultMessage` literal (which is the rendered template); the prose elaboration is optional, fetched only if the consumer wants it. **Engagement on (ii):** Conceded that `sh:message` carries some prose, but the prose form is constrained per ODR-0017 §1a — placeholders only, no natural-language paragraphs. The constraint preserves machine-parseability; richer prose lives in the annotation graph keyed to the shape IRI. Cagle DA withdraws on both conditions met.

**Net engagement:** Cagle's dissent sharpens the `sh:message` template discipline (ODR-0017 §1a) but does not invalidate the graph-separation (ODR-0013 §Rules + ODR-0004 §3a). The amendment that lands: the `odr-review` lint MUST verify (a) `sh:message` templates use the placeholder form (key-value or template-literal, not free prose), (b) no `opda:aiHint` triple appears in the shapes graph, (c) prose elaboration in the annotation graph dereferences to the shape IRI as `dct:source`. The S001 Q5 dissent stays recorded; the shapes-graph discipline is unchanged.

**Anticipated DA line on Q1 — severity-tier table.** Cagle may press: the four-categories-for-`sh:Violation` discipline I named is too narrow — what about a `dpv:hasPurpose` annotation gap on a personal-data leaf where the purpose is unstated? Is that `sh:Violation` or `sh:Warning`? **Engagement:** The four categories carve at regulatory-weight joints per Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4; `dpv:hasPurpose` unstated is `sh:Warning` (data-quality re-open trigger — the leaf is annotated as PII but the purpose is missing) NOT `sh:Violation` (the PROCESSING is not unlawful absent special-category status; the documentation is incomplete). The escalation rule: a `dpv:hasPurpose` gap ON a `dpv:hasSpecialCategoryPersonalData`-annotated leaf is `sh:Violation` (Article 9(2) GDPR requires explicit lawful basis tied to purpose for special-category processing). The lint encodes the escalation. Cagle DA withdraws on the escalation rule being declared in ODR-0013 §Rules.

**Anticipated DA line on Q6 — profile-extensibility for PROV-O `sh:xone`.** Cagle may press: BASPI5-tightening a `sh:xone` branch with `sh:in` over a specific authority list (HMLR, OS AddressBase) is itself an enum-merge — does ODR-0010 §Rules item 2 build-step graph-union handle nested `sh:xone` × `sh:in`? **Engagement:** Yes, by recursion. The build-step graph-union descends into nested SHACL structures; an `sh:xone` branch carrying `sh:in` is graph-unioned at the branch level — the BASPI5 `sh:in` replaces the base `sh:in` on that branch (single merged `sh:in`, not stacked per ODR-0010 §Rules item 2 the regression guard). The build-step test extends per ODR-0010 §Enforcement to assert `sh:in` constraints inside `sh:xone` branches are single-valued post-merge. Cagle DA withdraws on the test extension being declared in ODR-0013 §Consequences.
