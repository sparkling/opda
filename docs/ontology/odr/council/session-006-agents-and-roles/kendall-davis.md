## Per-question positions

### Q1 — Person / Organisation Kind identity criteria

**Kendall:** FIBO LegalEntity / LEI pattern is the load-bearing precedent. FIBO commits `fibo-be-le-fbo:FormalOrganization` as a UFO Substance Kind with `LegalEntity` IC supplied via the LEI succession discipline (`fibo-be-le-lei:LegalEntityIdentifier` governed by ISO 17442; GLEIF as global LEI authority; LEI succession captured by `fibo-be-le-lei:hasPredecessorLEI`). The IC under multi-jurisdiction is a *set* of registration identifiers (LEI + UK CRN + EU equivalents), not a single identifier — same FIBO pattern Kendall carried into S005 Q4 for UPRN. The ODR-0017 SHACL-AF non-blocking-data-quality-rules pattern applies directly: Organisation merger / demerger triggers `prov:wasDerivedFrom` succession with the chain materialised into the validation report at `sh:Info` severity. If S006 adopts, **ODR-0017 reaches its fifth citing site** (organisation-with-merger.ttl is the proof-by-exemplar; S005 §6a UPRN succession; S011 §5a deprecation; S015 §4a INSPIRE; ODR-0009 PROV-O anticipated; S006 Organisation succession — five sites confirms the pattern's stability beyond the fourth-site spawn threshold).

The FIBO LEI pattern is normatively settled at the Council level (S005 Q4 ratification; Allemang DA withdrew; Cagle's operational challenge satisfied via `dash:uniqueValueForClass`). S006 inherits without re-deciding the *pattern*; it instantiates the pattern for two new Substance Kinds (Person + Organisation) with their own IC over their own named hard cases. This is exactly the A9 §Per-kind discipline (a)/(b)/(c) discharge the methodology requires of every `kind: pattern` ODR; ODR-0006 becomes the **second `kind: pattern` ODR** to discharge A9 after S005 (S015 and S011 ratified contemporaneously as third+fourth `kind: pattern` discharges).

For Person IC: the FIBO pattern transfers as multi-identifier (alternative state-issued IDs — NI + passport + driver's licence). Date-of-birth alone is *not* sufficient (twins; nameless edge cases at birth registration) but is sufficient combined with state-issued ID. UK PDTF context: NI-number is the primary identifier; passport + driver's licence are alternatives carried via `dct:source` chains to the trust-framework registry. The person-with-name-change.ttl exemplar pressure-tests this directly: NI-number persists across name change; the IC must yield one Person individual across the 2019 deed-poll event without invoking `owl:sameAs` (ODR-0005 Anti-pattern §5 inherits; ODR-0017 SHACL-AF rule materialises the `prov:wasRevisionOf` chain at `sh:Info`).

The death hard case (analogous to S005 §3b LegalEstate determination + S005 §3c RegisteredTitle closure) requires explicit treatment. A deceased Person's IC persists for the regulatory retention window (typically 12y post-completion HMLR; per S011 §5a Pandit PII-retention amendment) — the Person individual remains dereferenceable for SAR matching and audit-trail purposes; `opda:personStatus "deceased"` flags the lifecycle state; PROV-O `prov:wasInvalidatedBy` captures the death event with `dct:source` to death-certificate evidence. After the retention window, deletion proceeds per ODR-0012 governance. This parallels the S005 §3c title-closure pattern: the individual retains identity in the historical record even when its active lifecycle has ended.

**Davis:** BBC `/programmes/` precedent is exact for the Person case. The BBC editorial database (PIPS) used a stable per-person `pid` with multiple alternative identifiers; the URI was the join key; PIPS was the source of truth. For OPDA Person: the trust-framework registry plays the PIPS role — NI-number is the primary identifier in UK context; passport + driver's licence are alternatives; the IC is per-record-lineage in the trust-framework registry, surfaced operationally via `dash:uniqueValueForClass` on `opda:niNumber` (with graceful degradation for the no-NI case — pre-16 minors; non-resident transactors; same Cagle-style degradation discipline S005 §6a settled).

For Organisation IC: concur with Kendall's FIBO LEI pattern. The organisation-with-merger.ttl exemplar's three-individual outcome (two predecessors + one successor; both predecessors dissolved, successor `prov:wasDerivedFrom` both) is the right answer — *not* one Organisation with mutated CRN (which would `owl:sameAs`-conflate three distinct legal entities). The IC must give three; the SHACL-AF rule materialises the lineage; the publish-first round-trip works under the resulting structure (estate-agency records pre- and post-merger join correctly via `prov:wasDerivedFrom`).

One operational note for Person: the date-of-birth quale carries PII regime sensitivity (per ODR-0012 + DPV co-annotations). The IC commits to state-issued-ID set as the primary identity mechanism; date-of-birth is supplementary (a Quality of the Person individual, NOT part of the IC's primary check). This matters for downstream PII processing: a Subject Access request reads the Person's identity surface; the date-of-birth must be available for SAR matching but is NOT itself the join key. `dash:uniqueValueForClass` on `opda:niNumber` operationalises the join; the date-of-birth fires no shape, attaches DPV PII flag, follows the ODR-0012 governance discipline. The person-with-name-change.ttl exemplar carries both `opda:dateOfBirth` and `opda:niNumber`; the name-change resolves under either, but the IC's primary check is the NI-number persistence.

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — Person IC = state-issued-ID set (NI primary in UK; passport + driver's licence alternatives); Organisation IC = (registration-number + jurisdiction) set per FIBO LEI pattern; ODR-0017 SHACL-AF rule applies for merger/demerger lineage (fifth citing site if adopted). person-with-name-change.ttl and organisation-with-merger.ttl pass under the adopted IC.

### Q2 — RoleMixin vs Role

**Kendall:** Persuaded by FIBO Ch. 13 (Allemang & Hendler 2020 *Semantic Web for the Working Ontologist* 3rd ed.; Guizzardi 2005 Ch. 4 on Role-played-by-different-Kind) — Seller can be Person OR Organisation in PDTF v3 (a developer or institutional vendor; a relocation-services LLP), AND the role behaviour (signing, capacity-asserting, agency-authorising) does not differ by bearer kind enough to warrant separating PersonSeller and OrganisationSeller as distinct sortal classes. The UFO RoleMixin construct (anti-rigid, played by multiple Kinds) is the right meta-category; the ODR-0006 Decision section already commits this ("`opda:Seller`, `opda:Buyer` — RoleMixins, played by a Person *or* Organisation"). FIBO models its Counterparty / Party-In-Role pattern analogously (`fibo-fbc-pas-fpas:Party` is a RoleMixin played by `LegalPerson` or `NaturalPerson`).

The S005 Anti-pattern §3 (never key a Role) inherits: `opda:Seller`, `opda:Buyer` etc. have no IC qua-Role; their identity is borrowed from the Person/Organisation bearer. The Role exists for the duration of the Transaction Relator (per ODR-0007 § Transaction lifecycle); when the Transaction completes or is abandoned, the Role ceases and the bearer persists. This is what `ufo:isRoleMixin true` commits in the ODR-0006 §Turtle stubs section.

**Davis:** Hesitant — show me the SPARQL query that fails under RoleMixin-as-Role-played-by-Person-OR-Org but succeeds under separate PersonSeller + OrganisationSeller. The publish-first test from BBC `/programmes/` (where sub-kinds were added only when a listings producer's query genuinely partitioned the population — Channel-4 Brands vs Channel-5 Episodes) applies here. The BASPI5 round-trip reads `participants[]` with `role` discriminating Seller / Buyer / Conveyancer; no leaf in the data dictionary partitions Seller-when-Person vs Seller-when-Organisation. *Linked Data Patterns* §"Resource Description" (Davis 2010) makes the same call: sub-class only when a predicate or query genuinely needs the discrimination.

The case I'd cede on: regulated-profession capacity. A Conveyancer-when-Organisation has SRA-registration capacity; a Conveyancer-when-Person has individual SRA registration. The capacity SKOS scheme (ODR-0011 `sellersCapacity` Method/plan code) reads differently for each bearer kind. If S006 settles capacity attached to the Role (per ODR-0006's Capacity split), the RoleMixin discriminator earns its keep at the *capacity-value* layer rather than the *Role-class* layer. I'd accept RoleMixin on that condition: Roles are RoleMixins; capacity discriminates by bearer kind via SKOS notation (`sra-individual` vs `sra-firm`).

The S005 Q5 precedent reinforces this — Davis held-as-live on the 2-class commitment with `RegisteredTitle ⊑ LegalEstate` upgradeable to peer; the held-as-live dissent recorded a named re-open trigger (BASPI5-round-trip-net-negative). The same pattern applies here: Davis's caveat on Q2 records a named re-open trigger (consumer query genuinely needing Seller-when-Person vs Seller-when-Organisation partition). If 18 months of downstream sessions surface zero such queries, the RoleMixin commitment is operationally confirmed; if a query surfaces, the partition re-opens. This is held-as-live dissent in the S005 mode, not a blocking objection.

The role-tenure interval question worth flagging: each Role instance has a tenure interval (when the Person/Organisation started playing the Role; when they ceased). OWL-Time (Conditional per ODR-0002) is the candidate vocabulary for tenure intervals if S006 chooses to model them at this layer; alternatively the tenure-interval modelling defers to ODR-0007 (Transactions) where the Transaction Relator's lifecycle governs Role tenure. Davis's publish-first instinct says: defer to ODR-0007 — the BASPI5 round-trip does not surface role-tenure intervals as first-class queryable facts; the Transaction's `propertyPack.transactionMilestones` already carries the dates. Kendall concurs deferral.

**Vote (pair):** AGREE CONDITIONAL — Seller/Buyer/Conveyancer/EstateAgent/Surveyor/Lender/Insurer as RoleMixins per ODR-0006 Decision section (FIBO Ch. 13 precedent; Allemang & Hendler 2020). Davis's condition: capacity discriminates by bearer kind via SKOS notation (`sra-individual` vs `sra-firm`), reading the `sellersCapacity` Method/plan code scheme per ODR-0011 §8a. No separate PersonSeller / OrganisationSeller sortal sub-classes unless a future consumer query forces partition (publish-first deferral). Role-tenure interval modelling deferred to ODR-0007 (Transaction Relator lifecycle).

### Q3 — Proprietorship Relator

**Kendall:** FIBO `Relator` precedent for legal-institutional binding. FIBO models `fibo-fnd-pas-pas:hasParty` and `fibo-be-oac-cctl:Control` as UFO Relator analogues — entities whose existence is dependent on the parties they bind. `opda:Proprietorship` is the same construct: a UFO Relator binding two `opda:Proprietor` Role instances (one per natural person on title) to one `opda:RegisteredTitle`. The Relator's IC = the (Title, Persons-set) tuple; it has no independent identity beyond the Title + Persons it mediates (S005 Anti-pattern §3 — never key a Role; the same anti-pattern applies to a Relator's mediating Roles).

The proprietorship-relator-multi-proprietor.ttl exemplar is the load-bearing joint-tenancy case. Two `opda:Person` individuals jointly own a freehold (HMLR Practice Guide 24); the Proprietorship Relator mediates two Proprietor Role instances bound to one Title. Joint-tenancy vs tenants-in-common is a property of the Relator (`opda:tenancyKind`), NOT a property of the Roles (Roles have no identity qua-Role; the Relator is where the legal-institutional binding lives). FIBO Ch. 13 (Allemang & Hendler 2020) names this pattern explicitly: the Relator is the *legal-institutional fact* that binds the participants; its IC inherits from the bound parties' ICs plus the bond-creating event (registration of joint proprietors in the HMLR proprietorship register).

**Davis:** Concur, with one operational note. The publish-first discipline says: model the Relator if and only if the round-trip needs to distinguish "this Person is on the title" from "this Person is bound jointly with that other Person under a tenancy kind". For OPDA, HMLR's title-register *records* the proprietorship as a list of registered proprietors plus the tenancy kind (joint-tenants or tenants-in-common) — the data dictionary leaf `propertyPack.titleNumber.proprietors` carries the list; the tenancy kind appears in the title register itself. So the Relator earns its keep at the BASPI5-round-trip layer (without it, joint-tenancy vs tenants-in-common is invisible at the schema layer).

The cardinality discipline that S005 settled applies here: the Proprietorship Relator is its own UFO individual (the *third entity with its own lifecycle* analogue Hendler named for RegisteredTitle in S005 Q5 — proprietorship lifecycle is distinct from the Persons' lifecycle and from the Title's lifecycle; the Relator persists across proprietor death and substitution; it ceases on title closure or full transfer). The BBC `/programmes/` analogue is the `po:Episode`-vs-`po:Brand` distinction: an Episode-as-broadcast had its own lifecycle distinct from the Brand's lifecycle distinct from the production team's lifecycle; each was its own individual; the discrimination earned its keep because consumer queries genuinely needed all three (Raimond et al. 2009 §3.3). Proprietorship is the same construct under HMLR's joint-tenancy regime.

One operational note for the exemplar set: the proprietorship-relator-multi-proprietor.ttl exemplar uses `opda:tenancyKind "joint-tenancy"` as a literal. Per S011 §8a precedent (Substance Kind label / Quale-in-Region patterns), the tenancy-kind value should resolve to a SKOS concept in an `opda:tenancyKindScheme` ConceptScheme (Quale-in-Region category — `{joint-tenancy, tenants-in-common, sole-proprietor}` is a closed Quality region governed by HMLR Practice Guide 24). The exemplar's literal form is provisional pre-S011-§8a-scheme-authoring; once the scheme is generator-emitted, the exemplar amendment is mechanical.

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — `opda:Proprietorship` as UFO Relator binding `opda:Proprietor` Role instances to `opda:RegisteredTitle`. IC = (Title, Persons-set) tuple. Joint-tenancy vs tenants-in-common is a Relator property (`opda:tenancyKind`), NOT a Role property. proprietorship-relator-multi-proprietor.ttl is the load-bearing joint-tenancy case. Exemplar amendment scheduled: lift `opda:tenancyKind` literal to SKOS concept reference once `opda:tenancyKindScheme` is generator-emitted per S011 §8a.

### Q4 — Capacity vs Authority

**Kendall:** Two predicates settle this cleanly. `opda:assertedCapacity` is SKOS-typed (per ODR-0011's `sellersCapacity` Method/plan code scheme — §8a UFO seven-category framework: the capacity is a *procedural method or plan code* authorising an Activity). `opda:evidencedAuthority` is a PROV-O link to evidence (per ODR-0009 PROV-O Activity reification). The two predicates separate *what the seller claims they can do* (asserted) from *what the evidence supports they can do* (evidenced). FIBO's analogue is `fibo-fnd-aap-agt:Authority` (the legal capacity granted by a constitutive act) vs `fibo-fnd-aap-agt:AppointmentTerms` (the asserted scope of the appointment).

The founding grant (probate, power of attorney) is modelled as the missing Relator that the Capacity / Authority split presupposes. ODR-0006 §Decision already names this ("the founding grant (probate, POA) is modelled as the missing Relator"); the Relator is `opda:GrantOfAuthority` (or whatever S006 finally settles); its existence is what makes the asserted capacity coherent. Without the Relator, the capacity is a free-text enum (the PDTF v3 defect). The Relator's IC = (grantor-Person/Org, grantee-Person/Org, grant-event); its lifecycle = grant→revocation/expiry; PROV-O Activity reification captures both grant and revocation events with `prov:wasGeneratedBy` / `prov:wasInvalidatedBy` triples per S005 §3c precedent applied to Relator lifecycle.

**Davis:** Concur. The BBC `/programmes/` analogue is the editorial-rights / broadcast-rights split — what a producer asserted they could broadcast (under their contractual rights) versus what the evidence (signed talent releases, music licences) supported. The two predicates separated cleanly; the assertion was an editorial decision; the evidence was an operational record; the audit trail needed both. For OPDA: the SHACL shape on `opda:Seller` (per ODR-0006 §SHACL constraints) requires `opda:evidencedAuthority` *when* `opda:assertedCapacity` is in a regulated-context scheme (executor under probate; donee under POA; trustee). The shape conditionally enforces the link without forcing it for non-regulated capacities (sole-proprietor selling their own home).

The publish-first discipline cuts here at the SKOS-scheme cardinality. `sellersCapacity` has a small, governable closed sub-scheme for regulated-context capacities (executor / administrator / donee-under-LPA / donee-under-EPA / trustee / receiver / liquidator — eight or so values; sourceable from HMLR Practice Guides 6 + 21 + the Mental Capacity Act 2005 codes); the SHACL shape's `sh:qualifiedValueShape` enumerates this sub-scheme; data without regulated capacity skips the evidence shape mechanically. This is Cagle's operational-test discipline (S005 §6a) applied to capacity: the shape is checkable, degrades gracefully, fires `sh:Violation` only when the regulated-context capacity asserts without evidence.

The Mental Capacity Act 2005 (MCA) intersection deserves a flag: a donee acting under LPA (Lasting Power of Attorney; introduced 2007 superseding EPA) or under EPA (Enduring Power of Attorney; created pre-Oct-2007, still valid) has different evidence requirements (OPG registration certificate for LPA; court endorsement for EPA when used post-loss-of-capacity). The capacity SKOS scheme distinguishes these as separate concepts (`donee-under-LPA-property-and-financial-affairs`, `donee-under-EPA`); the evidence requirements are encoded at the SHACL shape via `sh:qualifiedValueShape` per concept. This is mechanically governable; Davis's publish-first round-trip recovers the right capacity from the PDTF v3 `sellersCapacity` enum once the SKOS scheme is generator-emitted with regulator-sourced labels per S011 §4a verbatim citation discipline.

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — two predicates. `opda:assertedCapacity` SKOS-typed (ODR-0011 Method/plan code scheme); `opda:evidencedAuthority` PROV-O link to evidence (ODR-0009). The founding grant is a `opda:GrantOfAuthority` Relator; its existence makes the asserted capacity coherent. SHACL conditional enforcement on regulated-context capacity scheme members via `sh:qualifiedValueShape`.

### Q6 — W3C Org Ontology vs bespoke

**Kendall:** AGREE adopt W3C Org as superclass for interop. The pattern S015 used for `opda:Address rdfs:subClassOf vcard:Address` is the precedent. For OPDA Agents layer: `opda:Organisation rdfs:subClassOf org:Organization` (W3C Org Ontology — Reynolds ed. 2014 W3C Recommendation). The W3C Org Ontology is the correct choice on the substantive merits: it commits to Organisation as a class with sub-classes for `FormalOrganization` (registered) and `OrganizationalCollaboration` (informal/network); it supports site / membership / role / posting structure that OPDA may use selectively; it integrates with PROV-O via `org:reportsTo` and similar predicates that have provenance semantics.

OPDA's choice is between bespoke `opda:Person` (no superclass) and `prov:Agent` (S001 Q3 ruled `prov:Agent` retained only as provenance role, not Kind-layer). **Davis:** Concur. The same pattern S015 used for Address. The same pattern S005 used for `RegisteredTitle` aligning to HMLR conventions. The W3C Org Ontology is the interop substrate; OPDA refines via subclass restrictions where its IC / capacity / role structure needs to differ.

One additional operational observation: the BBC `/programmes/` ontology aligned to W3C-published vocabularies via `rdfs:subClassOf` rather than `owl:equivalentClass` for exactly the same publish-first reason — a consumer who imports the OPDA vocabulary gets the W3C Org interop for free, without inheriting OWL-DL membership reasoning over external Organisations. OPDA inherits a well-trodden pattern at three citing sites: S005 (HMLR), S015 (Address), S006 (Agents).

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — `opda:Organisation rdfs:subClassOf org:Organization` (W3C Org Ontology; Reynolds ed. 2014). `opda:Person` is bespoke with `rdfs:subClassOf prov:Agent` for the provenance hook. ### Q7 — `participantStatus` as Phase

**Conceded.** Q7 was settled by S011 §8a Phase label scheme (ODR-0011 seven-category UFO framework). `participantStatus` is a UFO Phase label per the per-scheme typed-output table — "Labels for intra-Kind phases a Substance Kind passes through" — with governance flag "DPV processing-event trigger" and SHACL targeting "Kind-in-phase (Participant)". S006 consumes the scheme without re-deciding; the `participantStatus` SKOS scheme attaches to `opda:Person` and `opda:Organisation` participants in transactions, with phase transitions captured as PROV-O Activity reifications (per the S011 §5a lifecycle discipline + ODR-0017 SHACL-AF rule pattern for phase-change materialisation if needed).

One operational note for S006 implementation: phase transitions are NOT existential changes to the Person/Organisation — a Buyer transitioning from `pending-buyer` to `confirmed-buyer` to `completed-buyer` remains the same `opda:Person` individual throughout (UFO Phase is intra-Kind by definition; it does not change the Substance Kind). This contrasts with the merger case (Q1) where two predecessor Organisations cease and a successor begins (cross-Kind individuation via `prov:wasDerivedFrom`). The two patterns must be operationally distinguishable; the SKOS scheme governs phase, the PROV-O lineage governs individuation. Cagle's BBC `/programmes/` operational test: phase transitions must NOT trigger an IC discontinuity event (`prov:wasInvalidatedBy`); only merger/dissolution/death does.

The phase scheme members worth flagging for the generator: `participantStatus` likely carries values like `pending`, `confirmed`, `awaiting-evidence`, `evidence-received`, `withdrawn`, `completed`, `disputed` — sourced from the BASPI5 vertical slice's transactional milestones plus the business glossary. Each phase is governable; transitions between phases produce PROV-O Activity reifications with timestamp + agent + reason; the ODR-0012 governance layer reads phase transitions as DPV processing-event triggers (per S011 §8a per-scheme typed-output table). The S006 §Decision capacity-evidence shape (per ODR-0006 §SHACL constraints) reads `participantStatus` indirectly — a Participant in `evidence-received` phase has fired the evidencedAuthority requirement; a Participant in `pending` has not yet.

**Vote (pair):** CONCEDED — settled by S011 §8a. S006 consumes the Phase label scheme without re-deciding. Phase transitions captured as PROV-O Activity reifications; ODR-0017 SHACL-AF rule applies if phase-change lineage needs materialisation in the validation report. Phase transitions are intra-Kind (no IC discontinuity); merger / dissolution / death are cross-Kind (IC discontinuity via `prov:wasInvalidatedBy`).

## Replies to anticipated DA attacks

- **Allemang (DA, S001 Q3 partition-by-ontological-concern anchor) may attack the Q1 multi-identifier set as conflating identifier with IC.** Reply: same FIBO LEI separation S005 Q4 settled. The IC is the (registration-number + jurisdiction) *set* — multiple identifiers can attest the same individual (NI + passport + driver's licence for Person; LEI + UK CRN + EU equivalent for Organisation), but the set itself is the IC, not any single identifier. The operational check is `dash:uniqueValueForClass` on each identifier-bearer, with graceful degradation when any one is absent (per Cagle's unrebutted S001 Q4 challenge, settled at S005 §6a). FIBO does the same separation; OPDA inherits. Allemang's S001 Q3 partition discipline is honoured — the IC lives in the Kind-layer module (Person/Organisation as Substance Kinds); identifiers live in their issuing-authority sub-modules sourced via `dct:source` per ODR-0004 §7a.

- **Guizzardi-Queen may attack the Q2 RoleMixin conditional as collapsing UFO meta-categories.** Reply: the conditional is on bearer-discrimination at the *capacity-value* layer (SKOS notation), not at the Role-class layer. The RoleMixin commitment is unconditional per FIBO Ch. 13 + UFO Ch. 4; Davis's condition is that capacity discriminates by bearer kind (`sra-individual` vs `sra-firm`) at the SKOS scheme layer, which is precisely the ODR-0011 §8a Method/plan code discipline — capacity codes are *procedural plans for authorising Activities*, and the regulatory authority differs by bearer kind. The UFO meta-category structure is preserved; the discrimination lives where ODR-0011 §8a says it lives.

- **Allemang (DA) may attack the Q3 Proprietorship Relator as over-modelling.** Reply: the Relator is forced by the joint-tenancy case — without it, joint-tenants vs tenants-in-common is invisible at the schema layer (the BASPI5 round-trip cannot recover it from `participants[].role` alone; the tenancy kind is a Relator property, not a Role property). proprietorship-relator-multi-proprietor.ttl is the proof-by-exemplar. The Relator earns its keep at the publish-first layer; this is Davis's BBC `/programmes/` discipline applied directly.

- **Cagle may attack the Q4 SHACL conditional enforcement as too loose.** Reply: the shape is conditional on capacity-scheme membership — *if* `opda:assertedCapacity` resolves to a member of the regulated-context sub-scheme (executor, donee-under-POA, trustee, conveyancer-in-firm-capacity), *then* `opda:evidencedAuthority` MUST be present (`sh:Violation` severity).
