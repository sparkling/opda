## Per-question positions

### Q1 — Endurant commitment

**Kendall:** Concur Endurant for `opda:Property`. DOLCE `Physical Endurant` (Masolo, Borgo, Gangemi, Guarino, Oltramari 2003, *The WonderWeb Library of Foundational Ontologies*, D18 §4.1) is the right meta-category for a physical dwelling with persistent identity through demolition-and-rebuild boundaries; UFO `Substance Sortal` (Guizzardi 2005 dissertation §4.3) is the equivalent commitment in UFO. FIBO commits its `fibo-be-le-lp:LegalPerson` analogously — DOLCE/UFO category named in the class annotation (`fibo-fnd-utl-av:logicalDescription`), not left implicit. OPDA inherits the discipline: `opda:Property` declares `dolce:PhysicalEndurant` in its `## Rules`, and `opda:LegalEstate` declares the legal-institutional commitment (DOLCE `Non-Physical Endurant` / UFO `Relator`-bearing Substance, depending on the cardinality resolution at Q5).

The sub-kind question is where I'd push back on speculative depth. Hendler's `Site/BuiltStructure` sub-division (Q4 S001) would give a more granular IC over demolition vs subdivision — a `Site` survives demolition, a `BuiltStructure` does not — but the BASPI5 round-trip does not require the sub-division to validate. The 935 annotated leaves at the descriptive layer (ODR-0008) reference *the property* and treat it as one referent; no leaf distinguishes Site from BuiltStructure. Without consumer-side evidence that the sub-kind earns its keep, defer to the `pattern`-extraction discipline in ODR-0001 A9 §Artefact identity test: if a third record (HMO leases, shared-ownership) ever forces the Site/BuiltStructure split, extract it then. For now, one Endurant `opda:Property` with the IC stated over demolition-as-edge-case is sufficient.

**Davis:** Concur Endurant, concur defer the sub-kind. At BBC the analogous question was whether `po:Programme` needed sub-kinds for `Episode`, `Brand`, `Series` — and the answer was *yes only because consumer queries demanded the discrimination*. Listings producers wanted to find "all Brands by Channel 4"; the discriminator earned its keep at the predicate-domain layer (Raimond, Smethurst, McParland 2009, "BBC Programmes Ontology" §3). For OPDA at S005 scale, no consumer query yet demands the Site/BuiltStructure split — the BASPI5 round-trip and the four-UPRN-surface join treat the property as one referent. *Linked Data Patterns* (Davis 2010, §"Resource Description") makes the same point: sub-class only when a predicate or query genuinely partitions the population. Time-box: one Endurant, IC stated, ship.

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — `opda:Property` as DOLCE `PhysicalEndurant` (equivalently UFO `Substance Sortal`). Sub-kind granularity deferred to a successor `pattern` ODR if and only if a future exemplar forces it.

### Q2 — IC for physical Property over demolition, subdivision, merger, replacement

**Kendall:** The IC must be spatial-material continuity, stated over the hard cases. Guarino's S001 Q4 framing — "spatial-material continuity, defined over demolition / subdivision / merger" — is the right formulation; FIBO's analogue is `fibo-fnd-pas-pas:hasOriginatedFrom` for entities that persist through structural change. The IC for `opda:Property`:

> A physical Property is the same individual across time iff (a) its spatial footprint is materially continuous (allowing for routine repair, refurbishment, and partial reconstruction within the same footprint), AND (b) the legal-institutional record (LegalEstate / RegisteredTitle) does not assert a discontinuity event (subdivision, merger, demolition-and-rebuild on a re-zoned footprint).

The unregistered-house exemplar is the test: the property persists as one individual across the eventual first registration; the IC must yield this answer without relying on the legal layer's existence. The flat-with-split-UPRN exemplar is the second test: the property persists across UPRN re-numbering (administrative event, not a discontinuity event). The cure passes both: spatial-material continuity holds in both cases; UPRN succession is a `prov:wasDerivedFrom` chain over the identifier, not over the Property.

For demolition-and-rebuild: the IC says these are two Properties if the footprint is materially re-zoned or the building is wholly replaced on a different footprint; one Property if the rebuild is within the same footprint and the legal record does not assert discontinuity (the routine "knock-down rebuild" on the same plot remains one Property by the legal-record test). This is FIBO's `fibo-be-fct-fct:hasOriginatedFrom` pattern: the lineage of physical-thing-derivation is itself a `prov:wasDerivedFrom` chain, not an `owl:sameAs`.

**Davis:** Concur the IC formulation, with one operational note. At BBC the analogous question was whether re-edited programmes (the same content broadcast under a new schedule entry) were the same `po:Episode` or a new one — and the resolution was: the editorial database (PIPS) was the authoritative source; if PIPS asserted a new `pid`, it was a new Episode. For OPDA, HMLR's title-register record is the analogue of PIPS — if HMLR closes a title and opens a new one with a demolition / subdivision marker, the legal layer asserts discontinuity and the physical Property is two individuals. If HMLR retains the title across the change, the physical Property remains one. The IC inherits the authoritative-source discipline; the IC says what counts, but HMLR's record is what the IC reads.

One sceptical note: the IC is stated; the operational check is harder. SHACL cannot fully validate "spatial-material continuity" — the predicate is not in the dataset. The pragmatic resolution is the same as Q4: state the IC as the *semantic commitment*; use `dash:uniqueValueForClass` on UPRN as the *checkable* surface; reify discontinuity events (demolition, subdivision, merger) as explicit `opda:PropertyDiscontinuityEvent` resources that downstream consumers can detect. This is what the flat-with-split-UPRN exemplar already prefigures with its `opda:UPRNSuccessionEvent`.

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — IC stated as spatial-material continuity over the hard cases, with the legal layer (where present) as the authoritative source for discontinuity assertions. Operational check via `dash:uniqueValueForClass` + reified discontinuity events.

### Q3 — IC for LegalEstate / RegisteredTitle over closure, merger, transfer between registers

**Kendall:** This is where the FIBO LEI pattern transfers most directly. The IC for a `LegalEstate` (and, if Q5 splits, `RegisteredTitle`) is title-register identity — the institutional record's own identity criterion. FIBO `fibo-be-le-lei:LegalEntityIdentifier` is governed by ISO 17442; its IC is "the LEI string assigned by the local operating unit"; LEI succession is captured by `fibo-be-le-lei:hasPredecessorLEI` (a `prov:wasDerivedFrom` analogue). The same pattern fits HMLR title numbers:

> A LegalEstate is the same individual across time iff (a) its title-register identity (typically the HMLR title number) is unchanged, OR (b) the register asserts a controlled-succession relationship (`opda:succeededBy`) from a prior title number to the current one, AND (c) no register-closure event has marked the title as terminated without succession.

For title transfer between registers (England and Wales → Scotland; freehold → commonhold conversion): the IC says the LegalEstate is a new individual under the receiving register, with `prov:wasDerivedFrom` to the prior. This is the FIBO `fibo-be-le-cb:hasOriginatedIn` pattern adapted — jurisdictional re-anchoring is a derivation event, not identity persistence.

The unregistered-house exemplar is the first test: no LegalEstate / RegisteredTitle individual exists pre-first-registration. The IC says nothing — there is no individual to identify. When first registration completes, a LegalEstate individual is minted with a fresh identity; the physical Property persists across the event (Q2 IC) but the legal layer is born at that moment. This is the right answer; the IC must allow it.

**Davis:** Concur, and the BBC `/programmes/` precedent is exact: the `po:Brand` IC was the PIPS `pid` (Production Information ID); transfers between BBC divisions did not change the `pid`; only re-commissioning under a new brand created a new `pid` (Raimond et al. 2009 §3.2). For HMLR, the title number plays the same role. The operational discipline is the same: `dash:uniqueValueForClass true` on `opda:titleNumber` for the LegalEstate / RegisteredTitle; uniqueness checkable; succession captured via `prov:wasDerivedFrom`.

One Cagle-style operational test worth pre-empting: "what does `owl:hasKey (opda:titleNumber)` *do* for a LegalEstate whose title has been closed?" Answer: it does nothing — `owl:hasKey` licenses inference, and a closed title produces no new triples to infer over. The `dash:uniqueValueForClass` shape produces a violation if duplicate title numbers appear in active titles; closed-title state is captured via `opda:titleStatus "closed"` and the shape is conditioned on `opda:titleStatus "active"`. This is the SHACL-as-checkable, OWL-as-semantic discipline that S001 Q4 ratified.

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — IC for LegalEstate/RegisteredTitle stated as title-register identity with `opda:succeededBy` for controlled succession; first registration mints a fresh LegalEstate identity; transfer-between-registers is a derivation event under `prov:wasDerivedFrom`.

### Q4 — UPRN status (checkable key vs contingent identifier vs both)

**Kendall:** Both, in the FIBO LEI pattern. The LEI is simultaneously (a) a checkable key on the rigid `fibo-be-le-lei:LegalEntity` Kind (FIBO authors annotate it with `fibo-fnd-utl-av:adaptedFrom` to ISO 17442 and validate uniqueness in the registry's SHACL profile), AND (b) a contingent administrative identifier governed by the GLEIF registration authority (LEIs can be retired, re-issued under controlled circumstances, transferred between local operating units). The two facets coexist in FIBO because the LEI's *operational* role is to be a checkable join surface, while its *ontological* role is to be a scheme-scoped identifier whose lifecycle is independent of the Legal Entity it identifies.

OPDA inherits the pattern. UPRN is (a) the primary checkable surface via `dash:uniqueValueForClass true` on `opda:uprn` — fires a violation report when duplicates appear, degrades gracefully when absent (Cagle's challenge to Guizzardi in S001 Q4 — unrebutted), AND (b) a contingent scheme-scoped identifier whose succession is captured by `prov:wasDerivedFrom` (the flat-with-split-UPRN exemplar pattern). Both facets simultaneously; neither is the IC.

The Cagle operational test (S001 Q4) is satisfied by this resolution: a consumer record with no UPRN gracefully degrades (no shape fires, no false violation; the Property exists, just without the operational key); a consumer record with a UPRN duplicated across the four surfaces fires the SHACL violation (the co-reference defect surfaces); a consumer record with a UPRN that has been re-issued reads the `prov:wasDerivedFrom` chain (succession captured, no `owl:sameAs` propagation).

Guarino's S001 Q4 dissent — that UPRN cannot be a rigid IC — is fully satisfied: UPRN is not the IC; the IC is spatial-material continuity (Q2); UPRN is the operational key over the typical case where one exists and the contingent identifier where it does not. The two are not in tension; they answer different questions.

**Davis:** Concur, reached via the BBC `/programmes/` route. The `po:Programme` had a `pid` (production identifier) that was simultaneously the URI suffix (the checkable, dereferenceable surface) and the contingent administrative identifier (the BBC could in principle re-issue a `pid` after retirement, though in practice they never did). The URI was the join key; the `pid` was the identifier. Same pattern: the IC of a `po:Programme` was its production lineage (PIPS-anchored), not its `pid`; the `pid` was the operational key over the lifetime of the production record.

For OPDA: the four UPRN surfaces (`propertyPack.uprn`, `energyEfficiency.certificate.uprn`, `chain.onwardPurchase[].uprn`, `valuationComparisonData.propertyDetails[].uprn`) are the consumer-facing join points. `dash:uniqueValueForClass` says "if two records claim the same UPRN, they MUST refer to the same Property — otherwise it is a co-reference defect, raise a violation". `prov:wasDerivedFrom` says "if a UPRN was re-issued, the new identifier is derived from the old". The two surfaces together solve the practical problem the four-leaf-path defect creates: consumer-side join becomes mechanically checkable; succession is captured without inference propagation.

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — UPRN is **both** the checkable operational key (via `dash:uniqueValueForClass`) **and** a contingent scheme-scoped identifier (with `prov:wasDerivedFrom` succession). Neither is the IC. FIBO LEI pattern.

### Q5 — 2 vs 3 class split

This is the question where the pair genuinely splits and the multi-title flat is the deciding exemplar. We record both positions and the converged recommendation.

**Kendall:** I-was-wrong-and-will-say-so. In S001 Q4 I argued one-Property-Kind-plus-alternative-identifiers (FIBO LEI pattern: one `LegalEntity` Kind, multiple identifiers via `fibo-fnd-rel-rel:hasIdentifier`). That position works when the physical referent and the legal interest are the same locus — a registered freehold house is the canonical case. It strains when they diverge: a flat under leasehold has a physical referent (the flat itself) AND a legal interest (the leasehold title) AND a separate legal interest (the freehold of the building that the leasehold is carved out of) — three distinct objects in the world that the schema must address as separable.

The multi-title flat (Glasshouse Mansions in the exemplar) is the case. The leasehold title `TGL654321` and the freehold of the building are distinct LegalEstate / RegisteredTitle individuals; the physical flat is a third locus. A 2-class split (`Property` + `LegalEstate`) lets the flat and the leasehold be distinct individuals — but it conflates `LegalEstate` (the abstract legal interest) with `RegisteredTitle` (the HMLR record of that interest). Hendler's S001 Q4 argument is that they are not the same thing: a LegalEstate can be unregistered (the flat freehold prior to first registration); a RegisteredTitle is the *record* of a registered interest. The unregistered-house exemplar pressure-tests this: pre-first-registration there is a LegalEstate (the owner-occupier holds a freehold) but no RegisteredTitle.

The 3-class commitment (`opda:Property` + `opda:LegalEstate` + `opda:RegisteredTitle`) costs one more class declaration, one more set of SHACL shapes, and one more IC to author. Hendler/Guizzardi's S001 argument is that the cost buys: (a) correct handling of unregistered estates (LegalEstate without RegisteredTitle); (b) correct handling of multi-title flats (multiple RegisteredTitles co-referring to one Property + one LegalEstate-bundle); (c) clean separation of legal-interest semantics from registry-record semantics. The exemplar evidence supports (a) directly (the unregistered-house case) and (b) directly (the multi-title flat).

I am persuaded to 3 classes. The condition I'd put on the Queen: write a fourth exemplar that isolates the LegalEstate-without-RegisteredTitle case (e.g. an unregistered freehold acquired pre-1990 in an area outside compulsory registration triggers, or a Manor that has never been registered) — the unregistered-house exemplar is close but does not separate LegalEstate-as-individual from "no legal layer at all". If the WG can produce this fourth exemplar and it forces three distinct individuals (Property + LegalEstate + no-RegisteredTitle), the 3-class commitment is settled. If the fourth exemplar collapses to 2 individuals (Property + nothing-legal), the 2-class is sufficient and `RegisteredTitle` is a sub-class of `LegalEstate` rather than a peer.

**Davis:** Not without consumer-side evidence. *Linked Data Patterns* §"Resource Description" (Davis 2010) and the BBC `/programmes/` precedent both say: add a class when a predicate or query genuinely partitions the population. The 2-class split (`Property` + `LegalEstate`) lets BASPI5 round-trip — every leaf in the data dictionary that references "the title" can target `LegalEstate`; every leaf that references "the property" can target `Property`; the four UPRN surfaces join on `Property`; the title-number leaves join on `LegalEstate`. Adding `RegisteredTitle` as a third peer requires a consumer query that genuinely needs to discriminate "this is the registry record" from "this is the legal interest the record records".

I am sceptical that BASPI5 surfaces this query. The BASPI5 round-trip reads the property pack and produces a form; the form's title-related leaves all target the registry record (title number, charges, restrictions, registered proprietor) — there is no leaf that references "the abstract legal interest as distinct from its registry record". The unregistered-house case (Kendall's condition (a)) is real but rare; the multi-title flat case (Kendall's condition (b)) is handled by 2 classes if we allow many-to-one between RegisteredTitle and LegalEstate within the 2-class model (a leasehold title and a freehold title both co-refer to the same flat-Property; they are distinct LegalEstate individuals — but only one class is needed for both).

Time-box: ship 2 classes for the BASPI5 vertical slice. If the multi-title flat exemplar genuinely forces 3 (i.e. if the IC for `LegalEstate` cannot be stated coherently without distinguishing the legal-interest from the registry-record), accept Kendall's resolution. The decision should be exemplar-driven, not theory-driven.

**Pair joint position:** We split on inclination and converge on the gate. The Queen should construct the cardinality-test exemplar Kendall named (LegalEstate-without-RegisteredTitle case; suggested: unregistered Manor or pre-1990 unregistered freehold outside compulsory triggers) and test both the 2-class and 3-class commitments against it. If the 2-class commitment yields a coherent IC for that case, ship 2 classes; if it does not, the 3-class is forced and ship 3. The exemplar evidence settles the dispute. Until then: provisional 2-class with `RegisteredTitle` as a sub-class of `LegalEstate`, upgradeable to a peer if the cardinality-test exemplar forces.

**Vote (pair):**
- Kendall: AGREE 3-class (conditional on the Queen authoring the cardinality-test exemplar; persuaded by Hendler/Guizzardi's S001 Q4 argument and by my own re-reading of the multi-title flat case).
- Davis: AGREE 2-class with `RegisteredTitle ⊑ LegalEstate`, upgradeable to peer on exemplar evidence. *Linked Data Patterns* discipline.

The Queen records this as a 1-1 internal split within the pair, resolved by the cardinality-test exemplar gate.

### Q6 — Address-as-mode-of-presentation

**Kendall:** Address is not the IC and is not a Kind. Guarino's S001 Q4 framing — "address-as-key is worse than UPRN-as-key (a mode of presentation, not a bearer)" — is the right diagnosis. The FIBO analogue is `fibo-fnd-arr-id:Address`, which FIBO models as an `Arrangement` (a structured value object) rather than as a participant in identity-bearing relationships. Address is *about* a Property; it is not the Property; multiple addresses may co-refer to the same Property (postal vs delivery vs INSPIRE-coordinate-derived) and the Property persists across address changes (postcode reorganisations, street renamings, council boundary changes).

The right modelling is co-reference via `opda:identifiesSameProperty` (the same predicate the exemplars use for title↔property co-reference). An address resource (with its constituent fields: street, town, postcode, country) is an `opda:PostalAddress` (or whatever the WG settles); it carries `opda:identifiesSameProperty <property>` when it serves as a co-referring identifier. This is the BBC `/programmes/` pattern of co-referring resources — the same `po:Programme` could be referenced by `pid`, by editorial title, by transmission slot; each was a co-referring resource, none was the Programme itself.

The detailed modelling — what constituent fields an address carries, how INSPIRE coordinates relate to postal addresses, whether geocoded coordinates earn `geo:Point` resources — is **ODR-0015 territory** (the Address gate per the depends-on chain). ODR-0005 should commit to *the IC of Address-relative-to-Property* (co-referring resource, not mode-of-presentation in any quale sense), and defer the detailed address modelling to ODR-0015.

I'd resist the temptation to model address as a DOLCE `Mode` or `Quale` (the qualia-region apparatus is overkill for what is, in practice, a structured value). FIBO does not invoke `dolce:Quality` or `dolce:Quale` for addresses; SKOS does not; the BBC `/programmes/` ontology did not; data.gov.uk's INSPIRE alignment did not. The quale apparatus earns its keep when the property *is* a quality (a colour, a temperature, a speed) — addresses are not qualities; they are structured identifiers.

**Davis:** Concur, time-box, defer to ODR-0015. The data.gov.uk linked-data cookbook §3 made the same call explicit: addresses are co-referring resources, not modes; the detailed modelling is INSPIRE-aligned (`http://inspire.ec.europa.eu/codelist/AddressRepresentation`) or PAF-aligned (Royal Mail PAF schema) but the *ontological* commitment is "address co-refers to a Property; the Property is not the address". For OPDA, ODR-0015 will settle whether OPDA inherits INSPIRE or PAF (or both with co-reference) — that is an architecture decision about which external standard to align with, not an IC decision.

The pragmatic note: ODR-0005 should declare `opda:identifiesSameProperty` as the co-reference predicate for ANY identifier-bearing resource that joins to the Property (UPRN, title number, address, INSPIRE ID). This is the BBC `/programmes/` pattern — co-reference is one mechanism applied uniformly; the resources that co-refer (`pid`, editorial title, transmission slot) are heterogeneous but the predicate is one. This keeps the ODR-0015 territory bounded to "what is an address" rather than "how does address join to property".

**Vote (pair):** AGREE — Address is a co-referring resource (joined via `opda:identifiesSameProperty`), not a mode of presentation in any quale sense, not a Kind, not the IC. Detailed address modelling deferred to ODR-0015 (Address gate). ODR-0005 declares the co-reference predicate uniformly across all identifier-bearing resources.
