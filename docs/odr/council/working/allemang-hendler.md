# Positions: Allemang + Hendler

## Expert: Dean Allemang

### Q1
**Position:** Genuinely deliberation-worthy, but not for the reasons people usually claim. The mechanical part ŌĆö `propertyPack.priceInformation.price` ŌåÆ `pdtf:price` `rdfs:domain pdtf:PriceInformation` ŌĆö is exactly that, mechanical, and we should write a generator for it rather than burning Council cycles on naming. The *deliberation*-worthy part is the part the generator can't do: where is the implicit aggregate boundary in a 3,324-line tree of nested objects? Which sibling fields are the same concept under different overlay framings (`baspi5.priceInformation` vs `ta6.price` vs `nts2.priceInfo`)? Which `oneOf` branches are sub-classes versus enumerated states versus discriminated unions of unrelated things? Those are modelling decisions, not translation.
**Reasoning (Allemang's methodology):** *Working Ontologist*, the running theme from chapter 6 onward ŌĆö "RDFS-Plus is what gets used in practice; OWL is what you reach for when RDFS-Plus runs out." JSON Schema has already done the easy half (named slots, datatypes, required/optional, enum lists), which lines up almost trivially with RDFS class/property hierarchy. What it *hasn't* done is identity, equivalence across overlay framings, or class-membership criteria ŌĆö and the *Working Ontologist* method is explicit that those are the things that need a modeller, not a tool. Pragmatic posture: don't argue about whether to model `price` as a class or property; we already know it's a `DatatypeProperty` with `xsd:decimal` range. Argue about whether `propertyPack` is an aggregate root or a documentation convenience.
**Vote:** AGREE
**Challenge or alternative:** I want the Council to commit to a generator-first stance: anything that *can* be translated mechanically *will be*, and the deliberation slots are reserved for the genuinely ambiguous moves. Otherwise we'll spend 200 Council sessions naming properties.

### Q2
**Position:** Core stays as ODR-0002 specifies. DASH in, PROV-O in, DPV (+ family) in, ODRL in ŌĆö but I want ODRL **scope-limited to data-rights expression**, not used to model the property-transaction contract semantics, because that's where modellers slip into "ontology of everything" territory. BBO exclusion: agree, strongly. For the four ArchiMate/OWL-Time/DCAT/SSSOM/SEMAPV calls: **ArchiMate OUT** this round (no capability-modelling task on the table), **OWL-Time OUT** (xsd:date + xsd:dateTime is sufficient for the leaf-level temporal facts in PDTF v3; bring OWL-Time in when we genuinely need interval algebra, which v3 doesn't), **DCAT OUT** (we're not publishing dataset catalogue records this round; we're modelling a transaction), **SSSOM/SEMAPV IN** but narrowly ŌĆö only to annotate the overlay-to-canonical mappings we *will* produce, not as a general modelling tool.
**Reasoning (Allemang's methodology):** *Working Ontologist* chapter 3 makes the case for the smallest sufficient vocabulary ŌĆö every vocabulary you adopt is a vocabulary you have to govern. TopBraid practice: the modellers who succeed are the ones who say "no" to vocabularies they can't operationalise this quarter. SSSOM/SEMAPV are the exception because the overlay system is *literally* a mappings system that today encodes its mappings as JSON annotations (`baspi5Ref`, `ntsRef`, `ta6Ref`) ŌĆö those need first-class machine-readable mapping records, and SSSOM is the right shape for that.
**Vote:** AGREE (with the scope-limit amendments above)
**Challenge or alternative:** None of this should be re-litigated per-file. Write the SHACL gate that enforces the per-layer vocabulary boundary now, or "Conditional" stays honour-system as ODR-0002 itself flags.

### Q4
**Position:** Single class `pdtf:Property` with a `pdtf:hasLegalEstate` relation to one-or-more `pdtf:LegalEstate` instances. The physical thing and the legal estate are *not* the same ŌĆö leasehold flats over commercial premises are the canonical counter-example, where one Property has a freehold estate, a leasehold estate, and potentially a head-leasehold estate, all referring to the same physical referent. Identity criterion for `Property`: **UPRN where present**, falling back to address-as-tuple where UPRN is missing; INSPIRE ID is *additional identity evidence*, not a substitute, because INSPIRE polygons can split or merge over time and UPRN is the closer match to "the property as a buyer would think of it." Use `owl:hasKey (pdtf:uprn)` for the UPRN case, and a SHACL shape with `sh:or` for the address-fallback. Do **not** use `owl:sameAs` to bridge UPRN-keyed and address-keyed instances ŌĆö Hendler will say the same, and rightly. Use a `skos:exactMatch` between them, or a custom `pdtf:sameProperty` property that *we* control the semantics of.
**Reasoning (Allemang's methodology):** *Working Ontologist* chapter 11 on identity and chapter 13 on the "hasKey" pattern ŌĆö `owl:hasKey` is the modeller's friend exactly because it lets you say "these instances are the same iff they agree on this slot" without the global semantic blast-radius of `owl:sameAs`. TopBraid practice: avoid `owl:sameAs` like it owes you money; it's how you end up with reasoner explosions and unintended inferences three layers downstream.
**Vote:** AGREE (with the Property/LegalEstate split)
**Challenge or alternative:** Resist the temptation to also model "title" as the identity surface. Title numbers exist in `titlesToBeSold[]` and at multiple levels of `ownership`/`onwardPurchase` ŌĆö they're identity for the *registration*, not for the *property*. Conflating them is how you produce a model where merging two titles dissolves a property, which is wrong.

### Q5
**Position:** Each overlay becomes a `sh:NodeShape` (or a tree of NodeShapes) that *extends* the base PDTF shape using `sh:and` composition. The current deep-merge semantics ŌĆö required-arrays unioned, oneOf branches concatenated, scalars overridden ŌĆö maps to SHACL almost cleanly because SHACL is already structured around shape combination. The interesting case is the form-reference annotations (`baspi5Ref`, `ntsRef`, `ta6Ref`): those are not just metadata, they are the *traceability spine* of the whole system ŌĆö each leaf in the merged schema records which form it came from and which question on that form it answers. Model those as `prov:wasDerivedFrom` relations from the *shape* (not from the data) back to a `pdtf:FormQuestion` instance, which itself is a `skos:Concept` in a per-form ConceptScheme. That way the same leaf can carry references to multiple forms (BASPI question 5.1 *and* NTS question 7.3 *and* TA6 8.2) and the traceability is machine-readable rather than baked into a property name.
**Reasoning (Allemang's methodology):** *Working Ontologist* chapter 14 on SHACL ŌĆö shapes describe what a graph *should* look like; they are the right home for overlay-conformance because conformance is the question "does this graph satisfy form X's requirements." Practical experience: every time I've seen form-traceability encoded as a per-property name (`somethingRef`), it stops working the moment two forms ask the same question ŌĆö and BASPI/TA6/NTS overlap heavily on listing-vs-conveyance facts about the same property. Model it once, properly.
**Vote:** AGREE
**Challenge or alternative:** Don't try to generate the SHACL shapes mechanically from the overlay JSONs at first pass ŌĆö the per-overlay deep-merge has enough idiosyncrasies (the custom arrayMerge, the oneOf concatenation) that hand-written shapes for the first two or three overlays will teach us what the generator needs to know.

### Q6
**Position:** Direct mapping, with one caveat. `claimant` ŌåÆ `prov:Agent` (specifically `prov:Person` or `prov:Organization`); `verifier` ŌåÆ another `prov:Agent` in the `prov:wasAttributedTo` relation but for the *verification activity*, not the claim itself; `evidence` (document / electronic_record / vouch) ŌåÆ three subclasses of a `pdtf:Evidence` class which is a `prov:Entity`; validation_method and verification_method ŌåÆ properties on a `prov:Activity` (the `pdtf:VerificationActivity`) that consumed the evidence and produced the verified claim; `issuer` of a document ŌåÆ another `prov:Agent` in `prov:wasGeneratedBy`/`prov:wasAttributedTo` relations on the evidence Entity. Jurisdiction is a `prov:atLocation` or a custom property ŌĆö I'd lean custom because PROV's location modelling is intentionally loose. The caveat: PROV-O alone won't tell you that "vouch" evidence is weaker than "document" evidence is weaker than "electronic_record from authoritative source" ŌĆö that's a `pdtf:EvidenceStrength` ranking that has to be modelled on top.
**Reasoning (Allemang's methodology):** *Working Ontologist* chapter 7 ŌĆö PROV-O is the example W3C ontology that "got it about right" precisely because it stops at the Entity/Activity/Agent core and lets domain modellers extend. TopBraid practice: clients always want to layer business-specific notions of evidence quality on PROV; PROV gives you the spine, you supply the muscle.
**Vote:** AGREE
**Challenge or alternative:** Don't try to also map the verified-claims structure to OpenID Connect Identity Assurance / eIDAS at the same time. That mapping is real and important ŌĆö verifiedClaims is *based on* the OIDC4IDA structure ŌĆö but mixing it with PROV-O at the same layer will produce a fog. Do PROV-O first; map to OIDC4IDA via SSSOM later.

### Q7
**Position:** Order of work, six rounds:
1. **Identity layer** ŌĆö Property/LegalEstate, Participant, Transaction, Title. Three classes plus identity criteria. Nothing else.
2. **Top-level aggregates** ŌĆö propertyPack, participants, contracts, chain. RDFS class skeleton, no properties beyond the structural links.
3. **Address/Location** ŌĆö because they're load-bearing and reused across Property, Participant, and (separately) school/transport contact points. Get the alignment with INSPIRE/OS done here.
4. **VerifiedClaims + PROV-O** ŌĆö the evidence/verification spine. Q6's work.
5. **Overlay SHACL profiles, two at a time** ŌĆö start with BASPI5 + TA6 (most-used pair); use what you learn to generate the rest.
6. **Temporal / lifecycle / process** ŌĆö *after* the static model is stable. This is where the Council will be tempted to bring in OWL-Time and BBO; resist until round 6.

Minimum viable subset for a first OPDA-published ontology: rounds 1, 2, 3, 4. That is the ontology that lets a third party assert "this is a verified claim about this property by this participant" ŌĆö which is the entire point of PDTF.
**Reasoning (Allemang's methodology):** *Working Ontologist* chapter 16 on rollout ŌĆö model the things people refer to *first*, model the things people *do* second, model how *processes* shape the data third. PDTF v3 is overwhelmingly about referring to things (the property, the participants, the documents); the process layer is much thinner and can wait.
**Vote:** AGREE
**Challenge or alternative:** Set a hard rule that no overlay SHACL profile is published until rounds 1ŌĆō4 are stable. Otherwise the overlays will drive the core, and the core will be retro-fitted to whichever overlay was loudest.

## Expert: Jim Hendler

### Q1
**Position:** Mechanical translation is the *trap*, not the work. The deliberation-worthy question is "what is a property in linked-data terms, such that someone outside the originating software vendor can dereference and reuse it?" ŌĆö and JSON Schema cannot answer that question because JSON Schema does not have URIs. A name in JSON Schema is a slot name, scoped to its enclosing object; a URI in linked data is a global identifier, the substrate of the entire architecture. Translating the *names* without re-thinking the *identifiers* would produce an ontology that *looked* like linked data but couldn't be linked to anything. The Council session is the right venue precisely because the right answers come from web architecture (dereferenceability, content negotiation, persistence), not from schema-to-RDF translation rules.
**Reasoning (Hendler's published positions):** *Spinning the Semantic Web* ŌĆö the recurring point that the *web* in Semantic Web is load-bearing; URIs aren't decoration, they are what makes the data linkable. W3C TAG findings on URI persistence. The Tetherless World / RPI work on linked-government-data ŌĆö every successful deployment started by deciding *what gets a URI* and *who controls dereferencing*, not by translating schemas.
**Vote:** AGREE (strongly ŌĆö but with the framing-amendment that the deliberation is about URIs and dereferenceability, not class names)
**Challenge or alternative:** Allemang and I will disagree gently here. He's right that the easy half is mechanical ŌĆö but I want the Council on record that *which things get URIs* is itself the modelling decision. PDTF v3 has at least six candidate URI subjects per transaction (the Transaction, the Property, each Participant, each Title, each Evidence document, each Claim). Naming those is more important than naming any property.

### Q2
**Position:** Core as ODR-0002 specifies, no quarrel. PROV-O in (W3C Rec, the right tool). DPV in (essential for any property data carrying personal data; non-negotiable given UK GDPR). ODRL in ŌĆö I want it in this round, not deferred. **Disagree with Allemang's scope-limit** to data-rights expression: ODRL has been used by the W3C ODRL Community Group precisely for the kind of stakeholder-permission expressions that property transactions need (buyer permits estate agent to share data with conveyancer for purposes X, Y, Z). That's modelling the *contract*, not modelling everything; the line is "machine-actionable permissions and prohibitions over PDTF data," and that's clearly in scope for a trust framework. BBO out: agree. ArchiMate out this round, no use case. OWL-Time **in** ŌĆö light use, but in. PDTF has dated leaves (`officialCopyDateTime`, `editionDate`, `lastDemandPeriod.from/to`, `lastDecoratedPeriod`), some of which are intervals and not instants, and OWL-Time gives you the Allen interval relations for free. DCAT **in**, lightly ŌĆö the OPDA-published ontology will itself be a dataset, and a single `dcat:Dataset` description of the ontology distribution is two paragraphs of work and a load of free interoperability with EU government catalogues later. SSSOM/SEMAPV in.
**Reasoning (Hendler's published positions):** W3C standards-first stance ŌĆö when there's a W3C Rec that fits, use it. *Spinning the Semantic Web* on the value of interoperable vocabularies over local ones. Tetherless World deployment experience ŌĆö OWL-Time pays for itself the first time you need to ask "did this seller's certificate cover the date of the buyer's offer," which is exactly the kind of thing PDTF will be asked.
**Vote:** DISAGREE with the framing of the question ŌĆö the question conflates "do we adopt the vocabulary" with "do we use it everywhere," and those are different. Adopt OWL-Time/DCAT now, scope-gate them with SHACL per ODR-0002's own adoption pattern.
**Challenge or alternative:** I'll abstain on ODRL scope (Allemang's narrower view is defensible) but I want ODRL adoption *itself* not deferred ŌĆö the consent-and-policy work cannot wait until v4.

### Q4
**Position:** Split, but more aggressively than Allemang proposes. `pdtf:Property` is the physical-referent class (what HM Land Registry calls "the land"), `pdtf:LegalEstate` is the alienable interest in it, and **`pdtf:RegisteredTitle`** is the third class ŌĆö the Land Registry's record of an estate. They are related but they are not the same, and conflating any two of them will cause grief when you hit common edge cases (commonhold, where ownership and title don't align tidily; first registration, where a property has no title yet; multi-title properties; properties in title-by-title chains). Identity: Property keyed by UPRN, **but** with the explicit modelling caveat that UPRN itself is administered by GeoPlace under license, so the URI form is `https://opda.uk/property/uprn/{n}` rather than something that pretends UPRN is a self-sovereign identifier. Title keyed by `titleNumber` (Land Registry-authoritative). Legal Estate has no natural key ŌĆö it's a `prov:Entity` instantiated when a participant asserts it. Hard rule on `owl:sameAs`: **do not use it across these classes**. Use `pdtf:identifiesSameProperty` (custom, with controlled inference) for cross-source property identity. Use `owl:hasKey` *only* on classes with a single legal authoritative key (Title, with `titleNumber`).
**Reasoning (Hendler's published positions):** OWL formal semantics (S&AS, which I co-authored) on `owl:sameAs` ŌĆö equating individuals in OWL has *global* effect; once you say `:A owl:sameAs :B`, every assertion about `:A` becomes an assertion about `:B`, and reasoners will eagerly propagate. *Spinning the Semantic Web* chapter on the dangers of cross-vocabulary equating. Tetherless World deployment lessons ŌĆö every project that used `owl:sameAs` between government datasets had to back it out because of unintended inferences (one famous case: equating UK postcodes to Royal Mail postcode-area entities, which then propagated to organisations *located in* those postcodes).
**Vote:** AGREE (with the three-class split and the no-`owl:sameAs` rule)
**Challenge or alternative:** Anticipating Guarino's likely attack on identity criteria: yes, I'm aware that `owl:hasKey` is a logical key, not a *rigid* identity criterion in the DOLCE sense. I'm not claiming it is. I'm claiming it's the strongest identity assertion available in standard OWL 2 tooling, and DOLCE-grade identity criteria can sit on top via custom annotation properties if the OPDA Modelling Sub-Committee later wants them.

### Q5
**Position:** Allemang's framing is correct in shape ŌĆö overlays as SHACL shapes, traceability via `prov:wasDerivedFrom` to form-question concepts. I want to add two web-architecture constraints. First: each overlay shape must be **independently dereferenceable** at a stable URI. `https://opda.uk/shapes/baspi5/`, `https://opda.uk/shapes/ta6/`, etc. ŌĆö and the *individual* form-question concepts must dereference too (`https://opda.uk/forms/baspi5/q/5.1`). This is what makes the traceability *usable* outside OPDA. Second: model the overlay-composition operator itself. `getTransactionSchema(BASE, ['baspi5','ta6'])` is a *function* that takes shapes and composes them; the linked-data equivalent is a `sh:NodeShape` constructed via `sh:and` with documented composition semantics (which the JSON Schema overlay system already has). That composition operator should have its own URI and its own description. Otherwise the merged shape is opaque ŌĆö you can't tell what overlay contributed which constraint, which is exactly the problem the `baspi5Ref` annotations were trying to solve in JSON.
**Reasoning (Hendler's published positions):** W3C Linked Data Principles 2 and 3 (URIs that dereference, with useful information). SHACL spec (which TopQuadrant drove but I supported at W3C) ŌĆö `sh:and` is the composition primitive, use it. Tetherless World practice on government-data overlays: the projects that worked were the ones where every overlay had a permanent URL and a curl-able description.
**Vote:** AGREE (with the dereferenceability and composition-operator amendments)
**Challenge or alternative:** If the OPDA web team can't commit to keeping `https://opda.uk/shapes/baspi5/` resolving for at least 10 years, drop the dereferenceability constraint and accept that the ontology is consumed only by people who hold local copies. That's a worse outcome but an honest one. Do not promise persistence you can't deliver.

### Q6
**Position:** I agree with Allemang's PROV-O mapping in the bones. Two additions. First: **be careful with `prov:wasAttributedTo` vs `prov:wasAssociatedWith`**. The claimant is a `prov:Agent` *attributed* to the claim (the entity); the verifier is a `prov:Agent` *associated with* the verification activity. Conflating those produces a model where the verifier is treated as having made the claim, which is wrong. Second: the three evidence types ŌĆö document, electronic_record, vouch ŌĆö are not just subclasses of `pdtf:Evidence`. They are subclasses with materially different *web-architectural* properties. A document evidence has a `prov:Entity` that points at an attachment URL (you can dereference it); an electronic_record has a `prov:Entity` that points at an authoritative source (HM Land Registry, Companies House), which should be a URI in *their* namespace, not in OPDA's; a vouch has no dereferenceable entity, only the vouch-er and a textual claim. The OPDA ontology must distinguish these on the dereferenceability axis, not just the conceptual one.
**Reasoning (Hendler's published positions):** PROV-O design rationale (I was on the working group). *Spinning the Semantic Web* on the difference between modelling something as a class and modelling it as a dereferenceable thing. Tetherless World work on government-data provenance ŌĆö the practical breakage is always at the "what does this URI resolve to" boundary, not at the class hierarchy.
**Vote:** AGREE
**Challenge or alternative:** Anticipating Guarino: he will ask whether the three evidence subtypes have distinct identity criteria, and I'll say yes ŌĆö `document` is identified by its document_details (jurisdiction + document_number + issuer is a candidate compound key); `electronic_record` is identified by its source + personal_number/record-key; `vouch` has no natural identity and is identified only by the verification activity instance. Three different identity criteria, three different sh:hasKey patterns.

### Q7
**Position:** Allemang's six-round order is correct. I want to add a Round 0: **URI policy and namespace strategy**. Before the first class gets named, the Council must decide: (a) what's the OPDA namespace (`https://opda.uk/ns/pdtf/` ŌĆö I'd argue for that and for `dct:created`/`dct:modified` on the ontology itself); (b) what's the URI shape for instances (`https://opda.uk/property/uprn/{n}` vs `https://opda.uk/property/{uuid}` etc.); (c) what's the content-negotiation strategy when someone GETs one of those URIs; (d) what's the persistence commitment. Without Round 0, every subsequent round will quietly re-litigate naming and the ontology will end up with three different URI shapes for properties.
**Reasoning (Hendler's published positions):** Linked Data Principles 1 and 2 ŌĆö URIs first. *Spinning the Semantic Web* on the cost of changing URIs after publication (effectively infinite). W3C TAG cool URIs / "Cool URIs don't change."
**Vote:** AGREE (with the Round 0 prepend)
**Challenge or alternative:** Round 0 might take a session by itself ŌĆö that's correct, not a problem. Better one session on URIs than three years of cleanup.
